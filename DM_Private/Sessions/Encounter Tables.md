# Encounter Tables – Campaign Travel & Location Encounters

**Context:** Party is currently in **Adaham** (port town), March 1st, 1225. Varin has departed. Active party: Morjor, Amdir, Tearius. Next leg: Spehlina mission via ship. Long-term thread: Lavalto, Father Maltrius Kael, political summit in one month.

---

## PART 1 – ADAHAM PORT TOWN ENCOUNTERS

*These are ready-to-drop encounters for the session(s) the party spends in Adaham. Marked as **[Mandatory]** if they should definitely happen; **[Optional]** if you need filler or pacing.*

---

### [MANDATORY] – The Word Has Spread

**When:** First hour in Adaham after docking  
**Location:** The main dock or nearby tavern

The naval battle against Iona's fleet was visible to passing merchant ships. Word has reached Adaham ahead of the party. Dock workers stare, two sailors whisper near a crate, and a harbormaster squints as they approach.

> **Harbormaster Drent** (gruff dwarf, 50s): *"You the ones that broke Iona's fleet? Five ships? By the tides... they're saying one man dived into the water and tore apart a hull with his bare hands. Captain Kirk's stories, I s'ppose. Well. The dockmaster wants a word. Something about salvage rights and unclaimed vessels."*

**Purpose:** Re-establish the party's reputation in the region. Sets up:
- Potential sale of captured ships (500–1,000 gp each if party still has them)
- Rumours about Kirk that build his legend and his discomfort with it
- Transition hook into harbour chatter

---

### [MANDATORY] – The Quiet Watcher

**When:** During any downtime in Adaham (shopping, tavern, etc.)  
**Location:** Marketplace or The Anchor's Rest tavern

A young woman, half-elf, sits alone at the edge of a crowd or market stall. She makes eye contact with **Amdir** briefly, then looks away.

If Amdir approaches or is perceptive: she slides a sealed note under a cup and leaves without speaking. The note reads:

> *"The package is still in motion. Don't make noise. The summit changes things. — M"*

**DM Note:** This is a contact from **Merelle/Myrelle**. "The package" refers to Morjor's ring. "The summit changes things" means Merelle has learned about the Queens' meeting and the Islefield connection — she's warning Amdir to be cautious about moving on the ring too early. The courier is just a hired messenger and knows nothing.

**For Amdir's player:** This is a private beat. Consider passing a note or holding a side scene. The timing pressure is real — Merelle is watching the political clock.

---

### [MANDATORY] – The Summit Crier

**When:** Day 1 or 2 in Adaham  
**Location:** Adaham town square

A public crier announces the summit between the **Queen of Braewood** and the **Queen of Islefield**, with **Wizard Number 8 of Kluimont** attending as neutral observer. The location: the great bridge between **Kadena** and **Rye**, locked down entirely. One month from today.

Reactions from bystanders (use 1–2):
- A merchant: *"Good — maybe they'll finally settle the Strait tariffs."*
- A sailor: *"Kluimont at the table? That's not a peace summit, that's a trap."*
- An old woman quietly: *"They shouldn't meet in the open. Bad things happen at the bridge."*

**Party DM note:** This is the hook Morjor feels most. He's been told by Balthazar to deliver the ring to the Queen of Islefield. She will be at that summit — one month away. He has never seen her face.

### [OPTIONAL] – The Drowned Man's Debt

**When:** Evening, near the docks  
**Location:** Back alley near the chandler's shop

A goblin named **Pip Nettlewick** grabs whoever is at the back of the group and begs them not to make noise. He's terrified, one eye bruised shut, clutching a satchel that clinks.

> *"Please. I owe coin to the Saltmaw boys. They'll gut me tonight if I can't pay. I've got half — I just need someone to stand near me at the exchange so they don't just take it all and do me anyway. Just stand there. Please."*

**The Saltmaw Boys:** 3 thugs, locally feared. They're not interesting enemies — they'll back down at a show of force (Intimidation DC 11 or just unsheathing a weapon while outnumbering them).

**What's in the satchel (besides coin):** A small sealed vial of dark red liquid labelled in a dead dialect. Any PC who speaks Abyssal or passes Arcana DC 16 recognises it as **infernal ink** — used to copy cursed documents or mark summoning circles. How a dock goblin got this is a story thread you can chase or ignore.

**Reward:** Pip, if helped, gives the party 12 gp and a tip: *"There's a ship that comes in quiet every ten days. No flags. Never sells anything. Just… watches the harbour. Left yesterday. I don't know what it wants."*

*(This unnamed ship is a vampire courier vessel if you want to develop it — or just flavour.)*

### [OPTIONAL] – Kirk's Reluctant Legend

**When:** Any moment Kirk is in a tavern or public place with the party  
**Trigger:** A sailor recognises Kirk

An older sailor stands up from his bench, knocks over his drink, and points.

> *"You — you're Kirk. You're the Dead Tide's Kirk. I sailed under you fifteen years back, on the Seraphim's March. You wouldn't remember me. I was just a deckhand."*

Kirk freezes. The sailor — **Oln Brace**, human, weathered, 50s — is not hostile. He just wants to shake the man's hand. But the name **"Dead Tide"** catches the room. It's an old naval campaign name. Some people flinch. Others lean in.

**If the party asks Kirk about it later:** The Dead Tide Campaign was a brutal naval engagement. Many ships sank. Kirk survived. He doesn't talk about it. (DM: this is the campaign where he killed Admiral Varn Frostwind — Iona's father. It predates the party's knowledge.)

**Purpose:** Gives Kirk depth, awkward public attention, and confirms that his past follows him. Reinforces the emotional weight of Session 18.

## PART 2 – SEA TRAVEL ENCOUNTER TABLE

*Use when the party is sailing — whether toward the Spehlina mission, a coastal leg, or any open-water travel. Roll **1d10 once per full day of sailing**. Reroll doubles unless specified.*

---

| Roll | Encounter |
|------|-----------|
| 1–2 | **Calm Crossing** — Uneventful. Good weather. One character moment opportunity (see Character Beats below). |
| 3 | **Squall Warning** — Sudden storm. Skill challenge (see below). |
| 4 | **Merchant Flag** — A merchant vessel signals for aid or trade. |
| 5 | **Ghost Sails** — Abandoned ship drifting. No crew visible. |
| 6 | **Naval Patrol** — Braewood naval vessel. Expects documentation. |
| 7 | **Sea Predator** — Large creature beneath the hull. |
| 8 | **Flotsam & Bodies** — Wreckage in the water with survivors or clues. |
| 9 | **Dark Sail** — An unlit ship shadows them for hours. Does not engage. Vanishes at dawn. |
| 10 | **Something From Below** — Story-linked encounter (see below). |

---

### Roll 3 – Squall Warning

A fast-moving storm drops visibility to 30 feet and rocks the ship violently.

**Skill Challenge:** 3 successes before 3 failures (DC 13 each)

| Action | Skill |
|--------|-------|
| Secure the sails | Athletics or Acrobatics |
| Navigate the waves | Survival or Perception |
| Calm the panicking crew | Persuasion or Intimidation |
| Brace the hull | Strength check or Carpenter's tools |

**Success:** The ship takes no damage. The party each gain **one level of exhaustion** from the effort — removed after a full rest.  
**Partial (2/3):** Ship takes **1d10 × 3 hull damage** (from 150 max). One crewmember swept overboard — DC 15 Athletics to retrieve.  
**Failure:** Ship takes **3d10 × 2 hull damage** and is pushed significantly off course (+1 day travel).

---

### Roll 4 – Merchant Flag

A two-masted cog flying Braewood merchant colours signals a greeting. They want to trade or ask about conditions ahead.

**Captain Sero Whalk** (human, nervous, 40s): He's transporting iron fittings and fabric toward Kadena. He's heard the summit is making all the bridges busy — he's trying to reach port before the bridge lockdown.

**What he can sell:**
- 2× Healer's Kit (5 gp each)
- Rope (50 ft) and rations (3 days, 1.5 gp total)
- A **sealed letter** he found floating in a bottle — he's wary of it, says it's addressed to someone called "The Chosen of the Black Shore." He'll give it away for free if they want the trouble.

**DM Note on the letter:** Contents are in a cipher. Arcana DC 15 or Code-breaking (thieves' tools) DC 17 to crack it. The message describes a meeting time and location near Lavalto — written in the voice of someone reporting to Father Maltrius Kael about a "retrieval". This is background lore. It confirms Maltrius has agents out in the world. Not urgent but excellent flavour and a future lead.

---

### Roll 5 – Ghost Sails

A single-masted sailing vessel is drifting, sails half-furled, no flag. No movement on deck. No response to signals.

**Boarders find:**
- Crew of 6 — all dead. No visible wounds. Expressions frozen in terror, mouths open.
- The hold contains **salted fish, rope, and one iron chest** (locked, DC 14 Thieves' Tools or Break DC 17).
- The ship's log ends mid-sentence three days ago: *"—something in the water last night. Rennick says he heard singing. I told him to sleep but now I also hear it and I don't think it's—"*

**Chest contents:** 180 gp in mixed coin, a **Potion of Water Breathing**, and a small carved bone figurine of a mermaid (non-magical but beautiful, worth 30 gp to a collector).

**What killed them (DM):** The singing was a **deep siren** — a creature tied to infernal corruption in the water (connected to the Spehlina's spreading influence or Maltrius's vent seepage). This is environmental foreshadowing. The bodies have faint black veining at the ears — the same kind of taint the party may eventually see near the Spehlina.

**Complication:** Kirk recognises the ship's name — *The Pellan Mast*. He sold cargo to her captain two years ago. He gets quiet for the rest of the day.

---

### Roll 6 – Naval Patrol

A Braewood naval sloop flies the crown pennant and pulls alongside.

**Lieutenant Cale Dorn** (human, polished, 30s): Professional and courteous but firm. Wants to see documentation, confirm they're not Iona's remnants, and ask about their destination.

**Easy outcome:** If the party names themselves (especially if Captain Kirk is present with his naval history), Dorn relaxes. He may share:
- *"We've had reports of ships going dark in the Strait — three in the past month. Crown hasn't officially responded yet."*
- *"If you're heading west — stay out of the Kadena-Rye passage. Bridge is being locked down for some bloody summit."*

**Tense outcome:** If the party is evasive or has outstanding warrants (Dawerton), Dorn asks them to heave-to for an inspection (DC 14 Deception or Persuasion to talk their way out without inspection, or just allow the inspection — nothing illegal is on board unless Amdir is carrying something).

---

### Roll 7 – Sea Predator

Something large is circling beneath the ship. The water discolours faintly. The crew grows uneasy.

**Option A — Giant Shark (CR 5, standard stats):** Attracted by blood or refuse. Rams the hull for **2d10 bludgeoning damage** if not driven off. Can be scared away with fire, loud noise, or a successful DC 14 Persuasion/Animal Handling check. Killing it takes 1–2 rounds of real combat.

**Option B — Corrupted Leviathan Eel (custom, use if closer to Lavalto/Dawerton area):**

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| **HP** | 78 |
| **AC** | 14 |
| **Speed** | Swim 50 ft |

**Actions:**
- **Bite:** +6 to hit, 2d10+4 piercing. Grappled on hit (DC 14 STR to escape).
- **Constrict:** +6 to hit, 3d8+4 bludgeoning. Restrained until escape (DC 14 STR).
- **Surface Slam (Recharge 5–6):** The eel breaches and slams onto the deck. All creatures on deck within 15 ft. DC 13 DEX save or knocked prone + 2d6 bludgeoning. Ship takes 3d10 hull damage.

**Trait — Infernal Taint:** The eel's wounds weep black ichor. Any creature bitten must succeed on a DC 12 CON save or be **Poisoned** for 1 hour (a second save at the start of each hour to shake it).

**Reward (if killed):** Eel glands are worth 75 gp to an alchemist. The ichor itself can be bottled (3 doses) — counts as a contact poison or alchemical ingredient (DM discretion).

### Roll 8 – Flotsam & Bodies

The party comes across wreckage floating in the water — shattered planks, a broken mast, scattered cargo. Two survivors are clinging to debris.

**Survivors:**
- **Mira Coldwell** (human woman, 30s, exhausted but tough): Merchant sailor. Her ship was rammed by a vessel flying no flag — *"Black sails, no lights, came out of nowhere at dusk."*
- **Bolek** (half-orc, young, terrified): Deckhand. Saw the other ship's crew. *"They weren't normal. They didn't shout or board — they just stood at the rail and watched. Watched us sink. Then they sailed away."*

**What the dark ship was (DM):** A vampire courier ship — the same type Pip the goblin mentioned in the port encounter. It wasn't there to raid; it was there to **collect something** the merchant was unknowingly carrying. The cargo manifest floating in the water (sodden but readable with a DC 12 Investigation) lists a crate labelled: *"Sealed Reliquary — Consignment for Lavalto Receiving, c/o F.M.K."* — Father Maltrius Kael's initials.

**The reliquary is gone** — the vampires have it. But the manifest is a hard piece of evidence tying Lavalto to active smuggling networks.

**Reward (if party rescues survivors):** Mira knows the Sapphire Strait well. She can serve as a navigator if you want her in a future session (+2 Survival checks for navigation). She owes the party a debt.

### Roll 9 – Dark Sail

As night falls, the crew spots a sail on the horizon — no lights, no flag, keeping exact pace.

It doesn't close. It doesn't flee. It simply **matches their speed and heading** for 1d4+1 hours, then is gone at first light.

**Perception DC 14 (with spyglass or similar, DC 10):** The ship has black sails and carries no crew visible. The ship appears to be moving against the wind in one section. It's silent.

**DM Note:** This is a vampire-aligned vessel. It is not there to attack — it is **observing**. It's tracking Morjor's ring (Morjor's player should feel vaguely uneasy even if no one else notices anything significant). Whether you develop this into a direct confrontation is your call.

**DC 18 Arcana or Religion:** Whatever is on that ship is not naturally alive. It carries a faint aura of necromantic influence that your senses pick up and can't explain.

### Roll 10 – Something From Below

This is reserved for use near the **Spehlina's territory** (approaching Dawerton's lake or coastal transit to Dawerton region). Foreshadowing encounter.

The water changes colour — deep greenish-black. Fish surface dead. A low moan travels through the hull as a sound rather than as a wave.

Then silence. Kirk grips the rail.

> *"It knows we're here,"* he says, quietly. *"It's not attacking. It's… grieving."*

**Nothing happens mechanically.** The encounter is purely atmospheric. It is the Spehlina — present in the water or in the lake's connected currents — and it is aware of the ship. Kirk is pale. This is the moment where the party should feel how wrong it would be to simply kill it.

**Optional follow-up:** One character (your choice or whoever is most perceptive/attuned) has a **vivid waking vision** lasting seconds: a massive serpentine creature underwater, lit from within by dim red light, surrounded by dead fish. It turns one enormous eye toward them. The eye is full of pain, not malice. Then it's gone.

### Sea Travel Character Beats (use on Calm Crossing rolls)

When a day passes uneventfully, offer one of these roleplay moments depending on who's present:

**For Morjor:**
> Late evening. Kirk is at the helm alone. He doesn't speak first. After a long silence he says: *"The ring you carry. I don't know what it is. But I know what something like that looks like — when a thing is waiting to be used."*
> Kirk won't push further. He's not accusing. He saw Morjor during the battle. He trusts him. But he noticed.

**For Amdir:**
> A seabird lands on the rail near Amdir and stays there longer than it should — watching him. When it finally takes off, there's a tiny piece of wax paper tied to its leg, already fallen to the deck. It's blank except for a date: **the 14th**. Thirteen days away.
> *(What happens on the 14th — Merelle is signalling a scheduled handoff or meeting. Amdir knows. The party doesn't.)*

**For Tearius:**
> The water at night is so dark it looks solid. The crew makes a joke about what lives below. Then one crewmember goes quiet and whispers something about the **Lavalto coast** — that his cousin tried to anchor there once and came back different. Pale. Never went outside again.
> Tearius should roll his vampire fear save (DC 10, low stakes) or simply feel the unease. No mechanical consequence — just narrative creep.

## PART 3 – LAND TRAVEL ENCOUNTER TABLE

*For any overland travel — arriving at a port and traveling inland, road to Dawerton, or crossing toward Lavalto. Roll **1d8 per half-day of travel**.*

---

| Roll | Encounter |
|------|-----------|
| 1–2 | **Clear Road** — Uneventful. Possible trader or traveller passed on road. |
| 3 | **Ambush: Road Bandits** — Low-threat combat encounter. |
| 4 | **Refugees Moving South** — People fleeing from inland. |
| 5 | **Corrupted Beast** — Animal touched by infernal seepage. |
| 6 | **Travelling Cleric** — An NPC priest with relevant information. |
| 7 | **Summit Travellers** — Nobles or officials heading toward Kadena. |
| 8 | **Night Visitor** — Someone approaches camp after dark. |

---

### Roll 3 – Ambush: Road Bandits

4 bandits (standard stats) with a **Bandit Captain** (standard stats). They've set up a log across the road and wait in the treeline.

**Bandit Captain — Fenn Gorrick:** Human, scarred, desperate. He's not affiliated with any major faction. His crew is hungry. If brought below half HP or if two of his four men drop, he calls for parley.

**Parley options:**
- **Pay a toll** (5 gp per party member, DC 8 Persuasion to talk him down to 2 gp each)
- **Intimidate them off** (Intimidation DC 13 — easier with Kirk present or obvious weapons)
- **Offer information** — Fenn knows the road well. He can tell the party that two nights ago a **large wagon** with iron-barred windows passed east, escorted by figures in grey. They moved at night. He didn't rob them. *"Some things aren't worth the coin."*

**DM Note on the grey-escorted wagon:** Optional vampire plot hook. Could be prisoners being transported to Lavalto. Can be developed into a side mission or left as background texture.

---

### Roll 4 – Refugees Moving South

A group of 12–15 people — mostly families, some elderly — are moving away from the interior. Pulled carts, tired faces, children clinging to parents.

**Spokesperson — Wren Ashby** (human woman, 40s, calm despite everything): They're from a farming hamlet three days northeast. They left after the third person went missing in two weeks.

> *"They went out at night. All three. The doors were locked from the inside. The beds were made. Just… gone."*

She has no explanations. She just knows it started when the **big crows appeared** — dozens of them, roosting on the granary roof. They're still there. The birds don't eat. They don't leave.

**DM Note:** The crows are a vampire familiar network — eyes and ears for a pureblood in the area. The missing villagers are alive but converted into thralls, now working in the Lavalto delivery network. This is not urgent plot — it's texture — but if the party investigates and clears the hamlet, they earn significant goodwill and Wren can be a recurring NPC.

**Possible help the party can offer:**
- Escort them to the nearest town (half-day detour)
- Investigate the hamlet (full session branch if desired)
- Give them supplies (basic decency moment, meaningful RP for Morjor or Tearius)

---

### Roll 5 – Corrupted Beast

A **Dire Wolf** (standard stats, CR 1) behaves abnormally — moving in daylight, not fleeing from fire, growling with a wet rattling sound. Its fur has patchy black discolouration. Its eyes are clouded red.

**In combat:** It fights past 0 HP once before dying (has temporary HP equal to 15 from the corruption). When it finally falls, the black patches in its fur rapidly spread and it dissolves into a foul-smelling ichor within one minute.

**Nature or Arcana DC 14:** This animal was exposed to **infernal seepage** — a long-term environmental contamination. It's not a summoned creature; it's a natural animal twisted by passive corruption. This is what the Spehlina is also experiencing, but on a vastly larger scale.

**Medicine DC 12 (on the remains):** The discolouration pattern originates in the spine and works outward. Whatever is doing this is in the **ground**, not the air. Groundwater contamination, most likely.

This encounter is low-stakes mechanically but high-stakes thematically — it shows the party what the seepage from Lavalto is doing to the surrounding ecosystem before they even reach the Spehlina.

---

### Roll 6 – Travelling Cleric

A lone cleric of **Avandra** (goddess of travel and change) is making her way along the road with a mule and a heavy pack. **Sister Halwynn** (gnome woman, elderly, eyes bright as coins).

She is not in danger. She is not a villain. She is simply well-travelled and sharp.

**What she knows (if the party talks to her):**
- She's heard of the Spehlina — *"Terrible business. A guardian turned toxic. I've prayed for it."* She believes it can be saved, but would need **multiple powerful clerics acting in concert**. She herself is too old for that kind of work.
- She's heard rumours that **Father Maltrius Kael** in Lavalto has been *"doing something with the mountain's fires — channelling them inward rather than letting them vent."* She doesn't know what that means spiritually, but it's wrong. *"Fires belong above ground."*
- If Varin's absence comes up: she looks at whoever mentions him with gentle recognition. *"A Bahamut-chosen who walked away doesn't walk away from Bahamut. He walks toward the place he'll be needed most. Don't mourn him yet."*

**What she can offer:**
- **Cure Wounds (2nd level)** cast once on any party member, gratis
- A hand-drawn map of the road network around Dawerton (adds +1 to relevant navigation checks)

---

### Roll 7 – Summit Travellers

A small noble convoy is moving toward Kadena — three carriages, a dozen mounted guards in Braewood livery.

**Lord Edric Saal** (human, 60s, portly, self-important): A minor Braewood noble attending the summit as a dignitary witness — not a real political player, but he thinks he is. He stops the party because they "look like they've been through something" and he is the sort of man who collects dramatic stories at dinners.

**Conversation options:**
- If the party is forthcoming about recent adventures, he's delighted and offers a night in his carriage camp (free rations, a proper fire, and relative safety)
- He mentions that **Wizard Number 8** of Kluimont is already en route to the bridge — *"arrived early, apparently. Very mysterious. Wouldn't let anyone see their face."*
- He complains about the bridge lockdown and the security. *"You'd think they were guarding the vault of Balthazar himself."* (This is an accidental reference, not a meaningful one — unless you want it to be.)

**For Morjor:** This is the first person who casually uses the word "Islefield" in a positive context — as the Queen is respected, even by Braewood nobles. If Morjor is listening, let him feel the weight of what Balthazar asked of him: this is the world he's being asked to quietly serve.

**For Amdir:** Lord Saal has a signet ring on his right hand. It's Braewood's minor gentry seal — worth 40 gp if removed. DC 16 Sleight of Hand if Amdir tries anything.

---

### Roll 8 – Night Visitor

During a long rest, one of the watch characters notices a lone figure standing at the edge of the firelight. They haven't announced themselves. They're just watching.

**If approached:** The figure is **Corin**, a half-elf man in his 30s, wearing a worn travelling cloak and no visible weapons. He raises his hands immediately. He's not hostile.

> *"I saw your fire. I've been following the road for two days without sleep. I just… I didn't want to be alone in the dark anymore."*

**What's actually going on (DM):**  
Corin escaped from the hamlet where the people went missing (see Refugees encounter). He's not missing — he hid in the granary rafters for three days and watched what happened. He can describe it clearly: grey-cloaked figures arrived at night, walked to specific houses, and the occupants opened their own doors and walked out **willingly**, in silence, with blank faces.

He's not a vampire. He's not a spy. He's a traumatised man who saw something he can't explain and hasn't processed. If the party lets him travel with them even briefly, he becomes a useful witness who can confirm vampiric compulsion is active in the countryside.

**If the party is suspicious and presses hard:** Corin passes any truthfulness test. Insight DC 8 — he's telling the truth and is genuinely afraid.

## DM NOTES – CONNECTING ENCOUNTERS TO STORY

- **Morjor's ring** is being tracked. The Dark Sail (Roll 9) and the Quiet Watcher in Adaham both connect to this. Use sparingly to build pressure, not to overwhelm.
- **Amdir's double-game** should be made uncomfortable but not exposed yet. The courier bird and Merelle's note are ticking clocks, not reveals.
- **The infernal corruption thread** (corrupted beast, Flotsam bodies, Something From Below) should feel like a consistent environmental warning. By the time the party meets the Spehlina, they should understand what is happening *to* it, not just *from* it.
- **Kirk** (if still with the party) reacts to several of these encounters in ways that deepen his character. His quiet recognition of the *Pellan Mast*, his comment about Morjor's ring, and his reaction to the Spehlina foreshadowing are all pre-loaded.
- **The summit countdown** should be mentioned organically 2–3 times before it becomes plot-urgent. Lord Saal, Lieutenant Dorn, and the town crier all reference it naturally.
