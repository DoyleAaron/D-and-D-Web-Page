# Option A(Part 2) — Lavalto Mission
## Travel

1. **Cleamond – 35 km by foot**
    
    - Average overland pace: 4–5 km/h with rest breaks.
        
    - Travel time: ~7–8 hours, so can easily be done in **one day**.
        
2. **Mortling Stronghold – 33 km by foot**
    
    - Similar pace as above.
        
    - Travel time: ~7–8 hours, **one day**.
        
3. **Dawerton – 60 km by boat**
    
    - Average small coastal or river vessel speed: 4–6 km/h (assuming standard rowed or small sailboat).
        
    - With calm weather: ~12–15 hours.
        
    - Realistically, likely split over **1–2 days**, depending on:
        
        - Weather (wind or storm delays)
            
        - Tides and currents (if sailing near shore)
            

---

### **Possible Encounters on the Boat Trip**

Since Lavalto is secretive and the queen’s envoys are traveling under scrutiny, you can have **moderate, non-lethal encounters** or minor hazards suitable for a level 2 party:

1. **Weather Hazards:**
    
    - Sudden squall or storm, requiring **Dexterity or Athletics checks** to manage sails and avoid damage.
        
    - High waves could **spill supplies or knock characters prone**.
        
2. **Wildlife / Sea Monsters (minor):**
    
    - Swarm of **giant seagulls or reef sharks** attacking provisions (AC 12, HP 5–10 each).
        
    - A curious **giant octopus tentacle sighting** (DC 13 Athletics check to avoid tipping the boat).
        
3. **Pirates / Smugglers:**
    
    - Small Lavalto or coastal raiding party looking for easy prey.
        
    - Could be **negotiated, avoided, or fought**.
        
    - If combat occurs, a few low-level enemies (1–3 bandits, AC 12, HP 11 each)
        
4. **Suspicious Observers:**
    
    - Strange boat following at a distance, trying to **spy or intercept royal envoys**.
        
    - Party can attempt **Perception, Stealth, or Deception checks** to detect or evade.
        
5. **Navigation Hazards:**
    
    - Hidden reefs, fog, or strong currents.
        
    - Could require **Survival or Nature checks** to avoid grounding or capsizing.


## City Intros
#### **Mortling Stronghold – Read-Aloud Intro**

_“You arrive at Mortling Stronghold, the first line of defense against the Kluimont threat. Massive stone walls rise before you, topped with watchtowers where sentries scan the horizon day and night. The streets are orderly and busy with soldiers drilling, merchants moving supplies, and citizens going about their daily routines. Every building seems to serve a purpose, whether it’s a barracks, armory, or training ground. The air is heavy with the clanging of steel and the scent of forge fires._

_The people here are disciplined and proud. Nearly every family has some connection to the military, and their conversations often drift to matters of defense, duty, and the Kluimont border. Despite the rigor and austerity, there’s a palpable sense of pride and community spirit. Mortling is not wealthy, but its citizens find fulfillment in purpose, in protecting the kingdom, and in standing ready for the call to arms.”_

---

### **Dawerton – Read-Aloud Intro**

_“Dawerton sprawls before you, a bustling port city filled with the chatter of merchants, the creaking of ships’ timbers, and the smells of exotic spices and salted fish. The streets are a maze of markets, guild halls, and narrow alleys, alive with constant movement. Ships unload cargo day and night, some mundane, some mysterious, their contents rarely questioned. Lanterns flicker along the canals and docks, hinting at secretive activities under the cover of darkness._

_The people of Dawerton are energetic and sharp, used to negotiation, trade, and secrecy. They speak quickly, bargain fiercely, and trust cautiously. While the city thrives on commerce, there’s a sense of underlying intrigue—shadows in alleys, whispered deals, and eyes watching from balconies. Here, information is currency, and one misstep can cost far more than gold.”

## Dawerton Escort

### **1. Arrive
al & Initial Impressions**

**Goal:** Set the scene, show contrast to Mortling, hint at underlying intrigue.

- **Escort Arrival:** The party is met at the port or city gates by a small contingent of local guards (2–4) and a **courier/liaison** from the city who introduces themselves.
    
- **Read-Aloud / Description:**  
    _“The city of Dawerton stretches along the harbor, lanterns flickering on the docks, voices of merchants and sailors echoing in the evening air. Your guards lead you through bustling streets crowded with traders, warehouses, and narrow alleys. Despite the crowds, you notice glances lingering on you—watchful, careful, but never hostile.”_
    
- **Checks / Interaction:**
    
    - **Perception:** Notices subtle symbols or attire suggesting some citizens have connections to powerful, hidden forces.
        
    - **Insight:** Guards and city officials are polite but slightly formal, hinting at unspoken rules.
        

---

### **2. Escort to Tavern**

**Goal:** Introduce the location, the tavernkeeper, and subtle vampire presence.

- **Walk Through the Streets:** NPC guide explains your stay is arranged at a **comfortable, secure tavern** known for hosting travelers.
    
- **Description:**  
    _“Eventually, you arrive at a two-story tavern with warm light spilling from its windows. The sign depicts a stylized cask of wine and a quill. The streets outside are quieter here, yet you notice a faint, elegant presence watching from the shadows. Nothing overt, but it feels… curated.”_
    
- **Tavernkeeper Introduction:**
    
    - Human, polite, welcoming, slightly formal.
        
    - Answers questions about rooms, food, and local customs.
        
    - Secretly answers to **a vampire patron**, who prefers discretion.
        
- **Checks / Interaction:**
    
    - **Insight / Perception:** Players might notice small hints of the tavernkeeper’s unusual loyalty or subtle gestures toward the shadows.
        
    - **Investigation:** Notices security measures or magical wards in subtle forms (depending on player curiosity).
        

---

### **3. Subtle Tension / Information Layer**

**Goal:** Introduce the vampire network, city politics, and the “eyes on the party” without immediate threat.

- **Hints & Rumors:**
    
    - Tavern has a **few regulars who vanish at odd hours**, or who seem too refined and oddly calm.
        
    - Whispers in corners hint at vampires integrated into business and trade—but never outright shown.
        
- **Vampire Presence:**
    
    - The one watching them is **part of the pro-outside-world faction**, neutral but vigilant.
        
    - NPC behavior: polite, observant, subtle gestures that **a careful eye would notice**.
        
- **Player Opportunities:**
    
    - **Social Checks:** Persuasion or Insight can earn trust or subtle information.
        
    - **Investigation / Stealth:** Players could try to spot unusual patterns or secret passageways.
        
    - **Rest / Downtime:** Opportunity to interact with other guests, gather rumors, or hear unusual tales.
        

---

### **4. Settling In**

**Goal:** Transition into the city exploration / rumor-gathering phase.

- **Room Assignment:** Comfortable but modest rooms.
    
- **Meals / Tavern Ambiance:**
    
    - Other travelers share stories of Dawerton: trade disputes, missing shipments, secretive nobles.
        
    - NPCs may drop hints about nocturnal activities, volcanic reagents, or political intrigue.
        
- **Scene Hook:**  
    _“As night falls and lanterns cast long shadows across the tavern’s courtyard, you feel the subtle watchfulness of unseen eyes. Nothing threatens you—yet you know that in Dawerton, the city itself is alive with secrets.”
    
## Rumors
### 1) “They say the manor inside the volcano houses thirty vampires — and four of them are true purebloods who never leave the lava halls.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★★☆ (commonly whispered; many believe there’s a manor)
    
- **Likely truth:** True: there is a grand manor built into the volcanic vent system where many vampires shelter; a small number are ancient/purebloods.
    
- **How to verify:** Ask dockworkers and old miners (Persuasion DC 12). Check guild ledgers for unusual shipments to the mountain (Investigation DC 14). Find a retired stonemason or smuggler who has delivered supplies (Insight/Persuasion DC 13).
    
- **Leads:** A smuggler named Kess in the lower quay who handled “special freight” (rumor source). A miner’s hollow with old service tunnels.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Those who approach the mountain openly draw attention (watchful vampire sentries); digging too deep will reveal wards and patrols.
    

---

### 2) “The purebloods are isolationist — they think they’re above Dawerton’s politics. They want nothing to do with trade or the diplomatic council.”

- **Credibility:** ★★☆☆☆ (half‑true, spread by merchants who resent the volcano elite)
    
- **Likely truth:** Partly true: at least one pureblood faction lives apart in the volcano and scorns civilian dealings — but not all purebloods are isolationist. Some vie for power.
    
- **How to verify:** Talk to a vampiric diplomat or Councilor Liora (Persuasion/Insight DC 15). Intercept correspondence between Adrian and the manor (sleight/stealth check or bribe a clerk).
    
- **Leads:** A housekeeper who once served both camps and left—she fears returning (possible hire).
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Pitting the two groups or revealing their splits can get the party in the middle of deadly politicking.
    

---

### 3) “There’s a soul‑forged relic in Dawerton — a blade/amulet that feeds on sacrificed souls and grants deadly power to its wielder.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★☆☆ (frightening tale, some merchants hush about ‘cursed goods’)
    
- **Likely truth:** A magical construct exists, but the “soul sacrifice” story is likely exaggerated. It may be an artifact infused with trapped spirits or rare reagents from volcanic rites. It could be ceremonial (and usable), or a decoy planted to derail seekers.
    
- **How to verify:** Query arcane dealers and ritualists (Arcana DC 14). Inspect black‑market paperwork (Investigation DC 15) or find a ship manifest listing “soul‑steel” or “seared onyx.”
    
- **Leads:** An apothecary who once sold reagents labelled “for binding” and a midship ledger listing an unmarked crate.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Seeking it invites cultists, vampires, and desperate buyers; using it may attract supernatural attention or moral consequences.
    

---

### 4) “On the last Friday of every month, some people slip out at dusk and are never seen again. Families lock their doors. The city hushes the missing.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★★☆ (widespread, supported by grieving families)
    
- **Likely truth:** People do vanish with troubling regularity. The cause is murky — could be vampire collection for rites, a cult’s harvest, or an enforced conscription for something in the volcano. Some vanishances are mundane (fugitives), some likely supernatural.
    
- **How to verify:** Visit the missing‑persons office or parish records (Investigation DC 12). Interview families (Persuasion DC 13). Stakeout the dock/alleys on the last Friday (Stealth/Perception DC 14).
    
- **Leads:** A grieving widower who says his wife went to a “charity meeting” that vanished; a pier guard who saw a pale figure guiding people onto a ship.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Investigating on the last Friday risks being targeted or observed; bodies are unlikely to be found — but you might catch a shipment or witness a ritual.
    

---

### 5) “The diplomatic vampires (Adrian’s faction) are trying to keep relations with Braewood — but the mountain purebloods want to reassert dominance and are sabotaging shipments.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★☆☆ (political, believed by merchants and officials)
    
- **Likely truth:** True: there is political friction. The diplomatic faction wants trade and appearances; the mountain purebloods are more traditionalists who see outside commerce as a dilution of power and may undercut agreements. Sabotage is plausible, but could be covert.
    
- **How to verify:** Intercept cargo manifests, speak to guildmaster Varis (Persuasion DC 14), or find signs of tampering on stores (Investigation DC 13).
    
- **Leads:** Smuggled letters showing threats to merchant captains; a burned supply cache that may have been “ordered” by a rival faction.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Exposing sabotage can lead to retaliation or being framed as the saboteurs.
    

---

### 6) “There’s a hidden pass into the lava manor — a cavern mouth sealed by runes. You need either a key kept by the treasurer or to perform a small volcanic rite to open it.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★★☆ (often repeated among adventuring folk)
    
- **Likely truth:** Partly true: there are service tunnels and ritual crypts, and some fortified gates require specific tokens/phrases to pass. The treasurer or a high‑ranking housekeeper likely controls one access key. There may also be a ritual route using reagents.
    
- **How to verify:** Question dockside smugglers (Persuasion/Insight DC 13), bribe a low servant for a sketch (Persuasion DC 15), or examine old maps (Investigation DC 14).
    
- **Leads:** A map fragment kept in a retired captain’s chest; an elderly treasurer’s clerk who says “there’s a soft chant” to the mountain.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Forcing entry triggers wards and alarms — stealth or diplomacy is safer. Using a ritual may require reagents tied to the soul‑relic rumor.
    

---

### 7) “Some vampires work openly with merchants and townsfolk; others only show their fangs in the deep lava halls. The latter think themselves superior and perform terrible rites.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★☆☆ (common suspicion; some have seen both sides)
    
- **Likely truth:** Sociopolitical split exists: outward‑facing vampires integrate into civic life; the deep lava group preserves archaic rites and superiority. The “terrible rites” are likely exaggerated but may include blood‑magic or binding ceremonies.
    
- **How to verify:** Seek sympathetic vampiric diplomat (Charm/Persuasion DC 16) or an ex‑sanguine servant (Insight/Persuasion DC 14). Trace cultural items in the market that could be ritual tools (Investigation DC 13).
    
- **Leads:** An old courier who once served both courts; a missing ledger with references to “offerings.”
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Revealing these rites could spark an internal purge or political crackdown.
    

---

### 8) “There’s a decoy industry — false relics and fake ‘soul‑blades’ being sold to distract would‑be treasure hunters while the real items move through the volcano.”

- **Credibility:** ★★☆☆☆ (told by tricked merchants and gullible adventurers)
    
- **Likely truth:** Very plausible. The black market profits from selling fakes; moreover, decoys are useful as political traps. Expect fraudsters, fenced goods, and some genuine artifacts mixed with forgeries.
    
- **How to verify:** Compare multiple sellers’ stories (Insight DC 12), run a test sale (Investigation/Arcana DC 14) or have an expert appraise an item (Arcana/Investigation DC 15).
    
- **Leads:** A fence in the curio market who sells both “real and real-ish” pieces; a burnt crate marked “replicas.”
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Chasing fakes wastes time and draws attention from whoever benefits from the distraction — possibly the purebloods.
    

---

### 9) “Some city officials are paid by the volcano rulers to look the other way — even Council members have ‘gifts’ from Lord Adrian.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★☆☆ (rumored, often whispered in taverns and guild halls)
    
- **Likely truth:** Corruption and patronage exist. Lord Adrian and his loyalists can influence trade and appointments via favors, coin, and subtle vampire persuasion. Some councilors may be compromised or indebted.
    
- **How to verify:** Audit council finances (Investigation DC 15 with official access) or bribe a scribe for ledgers (Persuasion/Deception DC 14). Observe odd voting patterns (Insight DC 13).
    
- **Leads:** A city scribe who keeps a second ledger; Councilor Varis’s private meetings.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Exposing bribery makes political enemies; bribed officials may retaliate quietly.

## Negation and Dinner Scene
### **Scene 1: Council Meeting**

#### **Purpose:**

- Introduce the Dawerton Council formally.
    
- Show political structure and city dynamics.
    
- Allow the party to **observe**, gather rumors, and sense underlying tension.
    
- Set up Liora’s suspicion of the party.
    

---

#### **Setting:**

- Council chamber in a **well-maintained government building near the harbor**.
    
- Long rectangular table, councilors seated in order of seniority.
    
- Warm lighting from lanterns; walls adorned with trade banners and minor arcane symbols.
    
- Liora sits near the head of the table, nearest to where the party is introduced.
    

---

#### **Scene Beats:**

1. **Introductions**
    
    - The party is presented as **royal contractors** here to witness negotiations and provide protection for the envoy.
        
    - Each councilor is briefly introduced (Elara, Fenric, Jorath, Miriam, Varis, Liora).
        
    - NPC roleplay tips:
        
        - Elara: Politely inquisitive, asks about trade goods.
            
        - Fenric: Observes guards closely, nods to security protocols.
            
        - Jorath: Eyes the party, subtle eyebrow raise, makes quiet notes.
            
        - Miriam: Friendly, asks general questions about travel.
            
        - Varis: Smiles wryly, occasionally interjects with political insight.
            
        - Liora: Calm, polite, but studies the party’s extra muscle.
            
2. **Discussion**
    
    - Envoy reads a letter of introduction from the Queen; Council acknowledges.
        
    - Topics include:
        
        - Ports, trade routes, and reagents (public, neutral topics).
            
        - Minor rumors of missing shipments or delays (low-key).
            
    - Party mostly **observes**, but can roll:
        
        - **Insight DC 12–14** to detect tension between Liora and other council members.
            
        - **Perception DC 13** to notice subtle gestures between vampires (eye contact, hand signals).
            
3. **Liora’s Observation**
    
    - She notices extra guards/muscle in the party.
        
    - Politely remarks:  
        _“Your presence is... notable. It is unusual for visitors to bring such numbers. Perhaps you would join me for dinner? I find discussions more... candid outside the chamber.”_
        
    - Offers an invitation without accusation—**just curiosity**.
        

---

### **Scene 2: Dinner at Liora’s Place**

#### **Purpose:**

- Roleplay intrigue; subtle investigation opportunity for the party.
    
- Introduce **diplomatic vampires’ personality** and the factional split.
    
- Give opportunity for **rumors or small secrets** to leak naturally.
    

---

#### **Setting:**

- Liora’s private quarters / townhouse near the city center.
    
- Elegant, dark wood and silver décor, understated arcane motifs.
    
- Candles and lanterns provide warm light; faint scent of herbs.
    
- A human servant (possibly the tavernkeeper) attends discreetly.
    
- Liora’s table seats the party, her translator, and her aides comfortably.
    

---

#### **Scene Beats:**

1. **Arrival & Seating**
    
    - The party is escorted to the dining room.
        
    - Liora welcomes them warmly, emphasizing diplomacy:  
        _“Please, relax. The council often must appear formal, but I prefer honesty over ceremony.”_
        
    - Translator interprets for party if necessary.
        
2. **Dinner Conversation**
    
    - Topics are mostly mundane, but Liora is gently probing:
        
        - Questions about the party’s journey, motivations, and previous missions.
            
        - Comments on “interesting security arrangements” (the extra muscle).
            
        - Observations on current trade, the harbor, or minor politics.
            
    - **Insight Opportunity:**
        
        - DC 12–14 Insight: detect Liora’s curiosity and cautious politeness.
            
        - DC 14–16 Insight: notice subtle hints she suspects unusual purpose beyond official duties.
            
    - **Optional Interaction:**
        
        - Party can try **persuasion or deception** to downplay their “muscle” role.
            
        - Liora may subtly test them with hypotheticals (“What would you do if a shipment went missing under your watch?”).
            
3. **Subtle Hints / Rumors During Dinner**
    
    - Liora may casually mention:
        
        - The existence of the volcanic purebloods (“Some of our oldest families prefer the heat of the mountains and see themselves above politics.”)
            
        - Missing people or unusual shipments (“It is… unsettling, the things we hear about the last Friday of the month.”)
            
        - Political friction with the purely human councilors or merchants, in a neutral tone.
            
    - Party can ask questions carefully—she answers **truthfully, but vaguely**.
        
4. **Scene Closure**
    
    - Dinner ends politely. Liora expresses gratitude:  
        _“Your vigilance is appreciated. Dawerton is safer when all eyes are open, even those of outsiders.”_
        
    - Party leaves with the sense of:
        
        - They are being **watched carefully but not threatened**.
            
        - There is **hidden tension between factions**.
            
        - Liora trusts them enough to let them leave unharmed, but remains **alert**.

## 1. Council Meeting – Topics & Angles

Use this as your “agenda sheet” behind the screen. You can turn each bullet into a small scene / social challenge.

### A. Opening Positions & Status

**Goal of this segment:** Set tone, let everyone stake out who they are.

- **Pasan (Braewood)**
    
    - States Braewood has come “in good faith” to ensure **safety of merchants, pilgrims, and diplomats** travelling between Braewood and Dawerton/Lavalto.
        
    - Stresses _co-operation_ and mutual benefit.
        
- **Tathtakmajal (Eimgend)**
    
    - Introduces himself as **Special Trade Adviser from Eimgend, Kluimont**.
        
    - Politely reminds the council that **Kluimont shipping carries a large share of grain, timber and ore** into Dawerton – and those losses hurt _both_ sides.
        
- **Liora / Council (Dawerton)**
    
    - Liora welcomes them as “valued partners of Dawerton and, by extension, Lavalto”.
        
    - Elara mentions Dawerton’s desire to “maintain prosperity despite… recent disruptions.”
        
    - Fenric notes that **security is being tightened**, subtly implying “we’ve got this, stay in your lane”.
        

---

### B. Missing Shipments & Attacks on the Route

**Core conflict:** Who’s to blame, and who pays when things go wrong?

- **Braewood wants:**
    
    - Clear **acknowledgment** that people and goods have gone missing near Dawerton 
        
    - **Assurances** that Dawerton will act – or Braewood will reconsider routing traffic there.
        
- **Tathtakmajal wants:**
    
    - **Written guarantees** that:
        
        - Dawerton patrols the approach channels and coastal roads more actively.
            
        - Proven losses to Eimgend-registered ships due to Dawerton negligence result in **partial compensation or tariff reductions**.
            
    - To slip in language creating a **joint Dawerton–Eimgend “Security Levy”**, giving Eimgend leverage over patrol funding & routes.
        
- **Council responses:**
    
    - **Elara:** Pushes back on compensation, blames “bandits, pirates, and the occasional storm”.
        
    - **Fenric:** Argues the solution is **more Dawerton authority** – foreign weapons and private guards make things “confused”.
        
    - **Varis:** Suggests only _licensed Dawerton guild ships_ should carry certain profitable cargos “for safety reasons”, which quietly hands Dawerton’s guilds a monopoly.
        

**Compromise hook:**

- Joint statement: “All parties will contribute to an expanded security effort”
    
- Hidden clause: Eimgend funds extra patrols BUT gets **oversight rights** (Tathtak win).
    
- Dawerton gets to **raise a small levy** on each foreign cargo to “pay” for those patrols (Elara/Varis win).
    
- Braewood gets **public assurances** and a clear complaint channel (Pasan win).
    

---

### C. Disappearances & Restless Dead in Dawerton

This is where your party & Sister Calene’s concerns can surface.

- **Braewood / Party angle:**
    
    - Calene raises rumours of **disappearances, restless spirits, and shared nightmares** around Dawerton.
        
    - Pasan frames it as: “Instability in Dawerton can spill across borders; we merely seek clarity and reassurance.”
        
- **Tathtakmajal’s public angle:**
    
    - Outwardly: Expresses concern that “unrest or terror in the streets” is bad for trade.
        
    - Secret: Uses the discussion to request **access to watch reports, morgue records, and slaver manifests**, under the guise of “understanding risk to Eimgend’s investments”.
        
- **Liora / Council:**
    
    - Liora smooths it over:
        
        - “Isolated incidents.”
            
        - “Criminal elements we are already addressing.”
            
    - Jorath hints at **minor arcane anomalies** but says they are contained.
        
    - Fenric resents foreign interference in internal law enforcement.
        

**Talking-point hooks:**

- Whether foreign envoys may **observe investigations**.
    
- Whether Braewood clerics (Calene) are allowed to **consecrate problem sites** or whether that’s “religious overreach”.
    
- Whether there are **formal reporting channels** when a foreign subject disappears in Dawerton.
    

---

### D. Harbour Rights & Tariffs

Classic money talk.

- **Tathtakmajal (key driver here):**
    
    - Wants:
        
        - Reduced tariffs for **Eimgend-registered ships** if they deliver minimum tonnage per season.
            
        - Priority unloading/berth access for Kluimont/Eimgend caravans.
            
        - The right to station an **Eimgend “liaison clerk”** in the harbourmaster’s office (really a spy).
            
- **Pasan / Braewood:**
    
    - Wants broadly fair treatment for **Braewood merchants**, but is more focused on safety and precedent than specific numbers.
        
    - Doesn’t want Braewood shut out by any exclusive Eimgend–Dawerton side deal.
        
- **Elara / Varis:**
    
    - Want higher tariffs overall, but are willing to offer **“bulk discounts”** in return for guaranteed trade volume and guild contracts.
        
    - Try to tie any discount to **using Dawerton guild factors and warehouses** (“for efficiency”).
        

**Potential agreement outline:**

- “Dawerton offers reduced duties to any kingdom meeting minimum tonnage quotas.”
    
- Eimgend is best positioned to exploit this (Tathtak win).
    
- Varis ensures the handling must go through **Dawerton guilds** (Council win).
    
- Pasan gets a line ensuring “no discriminatory tariffs against Braewood-flag ships” (Braewood save).
    

---

### E. Security & Jurisdiction in the City Itself

Who controls whose people once they’re inside the walls?

- **Fenric:**
    
    - Wants strict rules: foreign guards must **disarm or be clearly marked**, Dawerton watch has ultimate authority.
        
    - Proposes that any foreigner accused of a crime is tried in a **Dawerton court**, with envoys allowed to observe.
        
- **Pasan:**
    
    - Pushes for **limited extraterritorial privileges**:
        
        - Envoy compounds are sovereign.
            
        - Braewood citizens accused of a crime can be handed over to Braewood for trial, at least in political cases.
            
- **Tathtakmajal:**
    
    - Publicly: Sides with “efficiency” – doesn’t want Eimgend sailors dragged through Dawerton’s courts over bar fights.
        
    - Pushes a middle clause:
        
        - “For commercial disputes and minor offences, the offender’s home port may arbitrate, provided restitution is made.”
            
    - Quietly ensures Eimgend gets **first call** on any detained Kluimont citizens – giving him access to slave-trade and smuggling suspects.
        
- **Liora / Miriam:**
    
    - Try to frame a compromise that doesn’t look like Dawerton surrendering sovereignty:
        
        - Special **joint tribunals** for crimes involving foreign citizens.
            
        - Dawerton magistrate presiding, but envoys sit as “advisers”.
            

This section gives you plenty of room for:  
“Who has the right to interrogate that prisoner?” later in the story.

---

### F. Lavalto & “Future Cooperation”

Plant seeds for the volcano arc.

- **Liora:**
    
    - Mentions that Dawerton’s prosperity is tied to **Lavalto’s volcanic forges and resources**.
        
    - Floats the idea of **joint ventures** in Lavalto: financing mines, escorts for caravans, etc.
        
- **Pasan:**
    
    - Careful interest. He’ll want **written assurances** that Braewood workers/traders won’t end up “disappearing” in Lavalto due to local customs (read: vampires).
        
- **Tathtakmajal:**
    
    - Very interested in any regular slave or “indentured” traffic between Dawerton and Lavalto (secret reason: looking for his Grey Toad clanmates).
        
    - Officially, he presses for:

## 1. Envoy Pasan – Expanded Backstory & Roleplay

**Envoy Pasan of House Merrowind**  
Human · Bard (College of Eloquence) 3 (flavoured as diplomat)  
Alignment: Neutral Good

You already have his stats/skills – this is the deeper “why he cares” stuff.

### Origin & Early Life

Pasan was born the **second son of a minor Braewood noble house**, the Merrowinds, whose influence comes not from land or armies but from **customs duties and harbour fees**. While his older brother trained with sword and shield on the border with Kluimont, Pasan learned to **read ledgers, treaties, and people**.

As a teenager he was sent as a page to the royal court. There, he earned a quiet reputation as **“the boy who could get rivals to agree on something”** – even if that “something” was simply which wine to serve.

### The Failure That Shaped Him

Pasan’s first real posting was as junior attaché to a senior envoy in a grain negotiation with Islefield. The talks stalled, tempers flared, and his superior decided to play hardball. Pasan privately advised compromise, but was ignored.

The envoy pushed too far. **The deal collapsed.**  
Two winters later, a cluster of Braewood villages closer to the Kluimont border **suffered a harsh famine** because the grain routes never recovered. Pasan visited those villages during relief efforts and saw **children queuing for bread bearing his family’s crest** on the crates.

Since then, Pasan has been almost **fanatical about preventing avoidable suffering through diplomacy**. Trade to him isn’t just coin – it’s the difference between full granaries and mass graves.

### Relationship with the Queen & the Mission

Pasan rose to become one of the Queen’s more trusted envoys precisely because he **doesn’t crave glory**. He brings back **steady, boring, profitable agreements**, the kind that keep the realm quietly strong.

The Lavalto / Dawerton mission is his **most politically delicate job yet**:

- Lavalto’s vampires are feared but valuable partners.
    
- Dawerton is a web of guilds, council factions, and immortal egos.
    
- Sabotaged shipments or disappearances could easily **spiral into an international incident**.
    

The Queen chose Pasan because he’s:

- Calm under pressure
    
- Willing to **listen first**
    
- And, crucially, **not openly hostile to the idea of working with vampires**, seeing them as dangerous but potentially reliable partners if properly bound by treaty.
    

He knows the party is there **not just as bodyguards but as troubleshooters**. He’s too polite to say “do my dirty work,” but he will absolutely rely on them once things go sideways.

### Pasan & Maera

Maera was originally assigned to him “just” as a translator for a treaty draft with a Kluimont splinter lord. During the talks, **a single misread idiom** would have pledged Braewood to pay for the lord’s private mercenaries in perpetuity. Maera quietly corrected it **mid-session**, and Pasan rewrote the phrase on the spot, saving the kingdom **a fortune and potential embarrassment**.

Since then, Pasan has refused to travel without her.

- He **trusts her judgement** enough to pause a negotiation if she frowns.
    
- He sometimes lets her read people first, watching her notes rather than the opposition.
    
- He jokingly calls her his **“second conscience”**.
    

### Roleplay Hooks for Pasan

- He **collects small facts** about people and places (favourite drink, local expressions) and drops them into conversation to put others at ease.
    
- He is surprisingly bad at **physical danger awareness** – will walk into an ambush while composing a speech in his head.
    
- If a PC speaks up with a clever compromise, he lights up and **treats them as a junior colleague**, not just “hired swords.”
    
- Flaw in play: he can be too optimistic that “everyone wants a deal”, and may underreact to clearly malicious actors or cult nonsense in Dawerton.

When she was about fifteen, a travelling Kluimont preacher held a sermon

Only three people in town could have done it; Maera was one of them. She never confessed, but the incident taught her that **words can avert violence more effectively than any blade**, and that language itself can be a weapon.

### Service in the Royal Lexicorum

Maera was recruited to the **Royal Lexicorum**, a tiny, dusty office in the capital that:

- Standardises treaties
    
- Keeps multi-lingual glossaries
    
- Monitors “dangerous texts”.
    

Her talent for nuance got her noticed. She flagged a draft of a minor treaty where a mis-placed comma would have turned **“trade passage” into “military passage”** across a disputed pass. That catch prevented a diplomatic crisis.

She was formally attached to Envoy Pasan after the Kluimont idiom incident you’ve already got.
