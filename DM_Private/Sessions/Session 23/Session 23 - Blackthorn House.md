### 2. Visiting Liora — She's Gone

The party heads to Liora's office (or wherever she typically meets them). She is not there.

**Mayor Adrian** is present instead. He is agitated — controlled on the surface, but clearly rattled.

#### Mayor Adrian's Briefing

> **Read Aloud (Adrian):**
> "You're looking for Liora. So am I. She was last seen two nights ago making her rounds near the eastern quarter. She didn't return. Her contacts have gone silent. Her safe house was untouched — no struggle, meaning she either left willingly or was taken by someone skilled enough to not leave a trace."

**What Adrian knows:**
- Liora vanished **two nights ago** (March 6th).
- She had been investigating the **Valdris household at Blackthorn House** — a "noble family" that recently moved in outside town.
- She suspected them of being connected to the February 28th attack and the broader Maltrius cell.
- Adrian cannot act directly against vampires (compulsion/pact binding). He is trapped.
- He believes the Valdris brood took Liora. He doesn't know if she's alive or dead.
- He will release the **10,000 gp** immediately — no strings — if the party investigates Blackthorn House.

**What Adrian does NOT say openly:**
- Adrian is terrified. If the Valdris family discovered Liora was a vampire working against Maltrius, they may be trying to turn her loyalty or use her as leverage.
- Isolde Valdris may have already reported Liora's existence to Maltrius.

## The Basement — Liora's Prison

### What Changed

The Valdris brood captured Liora and dragged her to the basement of Blackthorn House. Isolde sees Liora as a trophy and a bargaining chip — a vampire who betrayed her own kind by working with mortals.

Liora is **chained to a stone pillar** in a ritual chamber in the basement. She is weakened (0 spell slots, half HP) but conscious and furious.

### The Locked Door

The basement door (located behind a heavy tapestry on the ground floor) is sealed with an **Arcane Lock** (requires *Knock*, *Dispel Magic* DC 15, or the key).

**The key** is a black iron skeleton key on a chain around **Isolde's neck**, tucked into her collar. It can be looted from her body or pickpocketed (Sleight of Hand DC 18 while she is distracted/grappled).

Without the key or magic:
- The door is reinforced iron-banded oak. STR check DC 25 to force open.
- Thieves' Tools check DC 20 (the lock is magically reinforced).

### Basement Layout

The basement is a **40×30 ft. stone chamber**, 10-ft. ceiling, cold and damp.

**Features:**
- **Two stone statues of Asmodeus** flanking the far wall — life-sized, carved obsidian, nine-pointed star on each pedestal. Detect Magic reveals faint abjuration (ward anchors, not animated).
- **Ritual circle** etched into the floor in dried blood — currently inactive but recently renewed. Religion DC 14 identifies it as an Asmodean binding rite.
- **Stone pillar** at centre — Liora is chained here with silver manacles (silver burns vampires; she has burn marks on her wrists). She can speak but not move.
- **Alcove shelves** with ritual components: black candles, bone dust, vials of blood (some labeled with names the party doesn't recognize).
- **Iron brazier** — cold, but residue shows infernal incense was burned recently.

The room is guarded by the **Lava Golem**.

> **Read Aloud (entering the basement):**
> "Stone steps descend into cold, still air that smells of old blood and sulphur. The basement opens into a low-ceilinged chamber lit by a dull amber glow from the far wall. Two obsidian statues of a horned figure stand sentinel, each bearing a nine-pointed star on its chest. A ritual circle — drawn in something dark and dry — covers most of the floor. At the centre, chained to a stone pillar with silver manacles, is Liora. She's pale. Paler than you remember. Her wrists are burned raw. She looks up at you with fury and relief.
>
> Between you and her, something moves. Heavy. Slow. Radiating heat like a furnace door left open. A hulking figure of cracked black stone and molten orange veins straightens to its full height and turns toward you. The ground beneath its feet scorches."

### Traits

**Heated Body.** A creature that touches the golem or hits it with a melee attack while within 5 ft. takes **7 (2d6) fire damage**.

**Molten Core.** When the golem drops below half HP (66 or fewer), cracks widen across its body. Its Heated Body damage increases to **10 (3d6) fire damage** and it gains +5 ft. movement speed.

**Immutable Form.** The golem is immune to any spell or effect that would alter its form.

**Magic Resistance.** The golem has advantage on saving throws against spells and other magical effects.

**Siege Monster.** The golem deals double damage to objects and structures.

---

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The golem makes two Slam attacks.

**Slam.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target.
*Hit:* 16 (2d10 + 5) bludgeoning damage plus 7 (2d6) fire damage.

**Magma Spray (Recharge 5–6).** The golem vomits a 15-ft. cone of molten rock. Each creature in the area must make a **DC 15 DEX save**, taking **28 (8d6) fire damage** on a failed save, or half as much on a success. The area becomes difficult terrain until the start of the golem's next turn.

---

### Reactions

**Molten Rebuke (1/round).** When a creature within 5 ft. deals damage to the golem with a melee weapon, the golem can force that creature to make a **DC 15 CON save** or take **10 (3d6) fire damage** as molten slag sprays from the wound.

---

### Tactical Notes

- The golem **will not leave the basement**. It is bound to the room by its summoning.
- It focuses on the **nearest threat**. It doesn't strategize.
- Its slow speed means ranged attackers can kite it if positioning allows, but the 40×30 room limits that.
- **Vulnerable to cold:** while not a listed weakness, cold damage temporarily suppresses Molten Core effects for 1 round (DM discretion to reward creative tactics).
- If the party tries to free Liora before killing the golem, the golem prioritizes whoever is closest to the pillar.

## Freeing Liora

**Silver Manacles:** Can be broken with STR DC 18, picked with Thieves' Tools DC 15, or shattered with radiant damage (any amount breaks them — silver is already weakened by vampire contact).

**Liora's State:**
- Half HP (~41/82)
- No spell slots remaining
- Exhaustion level 1
- Burn marks on wrists from silver
- Furious. She has information.

**What Liora Shares (Once Freed):**
- Isolde's brood took her while she was scouting the house perimeter alone.
- They knew she was a vampire. They could **smell it**.
- Isolde called her "the traitor" and said Maltrius had marked her.
- The ritual circle was being prepared to **bind Liora's will** to Maltrius's bloodline — forced loyalty rewrite. It hadn't been completed yet.
- Liora confirms: the Valdris family answers directly to **Father Maltrius Kael** in Lavalto.
- She overheard Isolde mention that Maltrius is preparing something for the **28th** that is "larger than Dawerton has seen."
- Liora is willing to share the 10,000 gp the moment they return to Dawerton. No negotiation needed. She owes them her existence.
