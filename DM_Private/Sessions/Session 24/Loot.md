# Session 24 — Loot Tracker

This file tracks what is available in Blackthorn House room by room.

**Assumption:** The party escaped fast and did **not** meaningfully loot the house on the way out. Everything below is still in play.

# Basement

---

## Blood Altar to Asmodeus

The basement centres on a blood-soaked stone altar engraved with Infernal script and the sigil of **Asmodeus** — an inverted pentagram wreathed in serpents. Black candles, dried blood channels, and silver ritual implements surround it.

### Altar Items

- **Mace of Disruption** — (wondrous weapon, rare, requires attunement). On a hit, fiends and undead take an extra **2d6 radiant damage**. If the target is a fiend or undead with 25 HP or fewer after taking this damage, it must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom save or be destroyed. The mace sheds bright light in a 20-foot radius and dim light for another 20 feet while you hold it.
- **+1 Shield** — +1 bonus to AC on top of the shield's normal +2. A steel shield etched with angular Infernal wards. Nonmagical etchings, but uncomfortable to look at.
- **Potion of Superior Healing** — restores **8d4 + 8 HP**
- **Infernal Ritual Dagger** — a black iron dagger with a bone hilt. Nonmagical, but clearly used in blood rituals. Worth 50 gp to the right collector. Any temple of good alignment would want this destroyed.
- **Asmodean Prayer Beads** — a string of nine obsidian beads, each carved with a tiny Infernal contract clause. Nonmagical. Worth 30 gp, or a cleric could identify them as devotional implements of Asmodeus. DM discretion on flavour use — holding them makes you feel watched.
- **Silver ritual bowl** — worth 75 gp raw. Engraved with Infernal summoning script. Could be repurposed or sold to an arcane supplier.

### Lava Golem (after destruction)

- **Infernal Control Shard** — a fist-sized chunk of obsidian veined with dull orange fire. This is the binding focus that animated the lava golem. Nonmagical in player hands, but a wizard or artificer (DC 15 Arcana) can identify it as a reusable construct core worth **200 gp** to the right buyer. Could theoretically be used to power a future construct with enough research and resources.

# Documents

These are the letters, orders, and reports found throughout the house. Keep them separate from the room descriptions so they're easy to hand out at the table.

---

## Isolde's Correspondence Bundle

**Found in:** War / Planning Room, on the table among maps and notes.

A stack of letters between Isolde and Father Maltrius Kael. Written in Common with some Infernal shorthand. The most recent letter, dated roughly two weeks ago, reads:

> *Isolde,*
>
> *The traitor is secured. Good. Keep her alive and contained — she is more useful to me breathing than bleeding. The collar will hold until I arrive.*
>
> *I will be there within the week. The binding must be performed by my hand. Do not attempt it yourselves. The last one who tried to shortcut a covenant of Asmodeus lost more than her tongue.*
>
> *The construct in the cellar is bound to protect the prisoner and the altar. It will not distinguish between intruders and your kin. Stay above the door, and keep it sealed. Only I carry the override word.*
>
> *Do not fail me in this.*
>
> *— M.K.*

**DM Notes:** "The traitor" is Liora. "The construct" is the lava golem. "The binding" is a loyalty-rewriting ritual Maltrius plans to perform personally. This confirms the golem attacks everyone except Liora (it's bound to protect her, not obey the brood), and that Maltrius is expected to arrive within a week of this letter.

---

## Maltrius's Orders

**Found in:** Study, locked desk drawer.

A formal directive on heavy parchment, sealed with black wax bearing Asmodeus's mark. Written in precise, cold language:

> *Standing orders for the Dawerton cell.*
>
> *The Grand Span meeting must not succeed. The alliance it would forge between Braewood and the western kingdoms would complicate our work for a generation. The creature in the lake can be guided. It follows death — bodies in the water, wreckage, blood. A chain of bait ships and corpses along the correct heading will drive it toward the bridge on the appointed day.*
>
> *Timing is critical. The delegations must be on the Span when the creature surfaces. If the bridge holds, the panic alone will collapse negotiations. If the bridge does not hold, better still.*
>
> *Begin preparations. Acquire the vessels quietly. The dead are easy to come by in a port town — use what the lake gives back.*
>
> *— M.K.*

**DM Notes:** This is the sabotage plan. Maltrius intends to guide the Spehlina toward the Grand Span by creating a trail of corpses and bait ships through the water. The goal is to disrupt the diplomatic meeting on March 28th. The party now has proof of the plan if they find this document.

---

## Operative Report — Braewood

**Found in:** Upstairs Hearing Room, folded and sealed with Valdris wax.

A brief status report from an unnamed operative. No signature, no name, no location given. Written in a careful, educated hand:

> *All remains well here. No suspicion raised since the last check.*
>
> *Braewood's reports have been fruitful so far. She's shown great recruitment skills. The court is comfortable with her presence and suspects nothing beyond mild ambition. Access to military correspondence continues without issue.*
>
> *The eastern contact is similarly positioned. She remains close to the crown and has yet to attract any attention that would warrant concern. The queen trusts her fully.*
>
> *Both assets remain productive. No intervention needed at this time.*
>
> *Will report again at the usual interval.*

**DM Notes:** This is the informant report. "She's shown great recruitment skills" and "Braewood's reports" is about **Mirelle** — but her name is deliberately absent. "The eastern contact" who is "close to the crown" and trusted fully by the queen refers to the **informant in Islefield**, the queen's right-hand. Neither name appears anywhere in this letter. If the party reads this, they learn that Maltrius has agents embedded in both Braewood and Islefield's courts — but they'll have to investigate further to learn who. The line about Braewood should make Amdir deeply uncomfortable without giving away the answer outright.

# Adrian's Reward Package

On success, Adrian gives the party:

- **10,000 gp total** as the promised Dawerton payment
- **4 vials of holy water** — each can be thrown (20/60 ft ranged attack) dealing **2d6 radiant damage** to fiends and undead on a hit
- **A Dawerton seal of quiet authority** — a wax-stamped document and signet that lets the party request discreet guard cooperation once before the 28th. One use, not renewable.

If Liora returns alive, Adrian gives all three without argument.

If Liora dies but the brood is destroyed, Adrian still pays the **10,000 gp**, but the holy water and political favour come only if the party handled the matter quietly.
