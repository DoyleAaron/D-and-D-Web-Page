# Exterior and Re-entry

## Visible Changes Since Last Time

- The escape window is **boarded from the inside**.
- Front approach looks quiet, but the quiet is intentional.
- Any obvious blood near the window has been partly scrubbed or covered with ash.
- The house gives the impression that someone is waiting for the party to commit.

---

## Read Aloud

> "The manor stands as still as a tomb. Fresh boards cover the window you fled through. The front door is shut, but not abandoned. Someone has cleaned enough to hide panic, not enough to hide violence. The place looks composed now. Deliberate. Like it learned from you."

---

## Entry Options

### Front Door

- Locked, reinforced from inside
- If forced loudly, the whole manor shifts into prepared response immediately

### Boarded Window

- Can be reopened from outside with effort
- Doing so takes time and noise unless magic or tools are used well

### Upper Window / Roof Entry

- The safest way to avoid the first choke point
- Theron is the most likely to punish this route if he notices

### Stealth Recon

- Good chance to spot servant movement patterns before entry
- Lets the party realize the defenders are **not** clustered where they were last time

---

## Exterior Pressure

Use one or more of these to make the re-entry tense:

- A servant silhouette appears briefly in an upstairs window, then vanishes.
- A board creaks from inside even when no one is visible.
- A faint metallic grinding sound hints that a golem has been moved into position.

---

## Defender Intent

The brood wants:

1. The party inside the house.
2. Their retreat routes limited.
3. The first hard fight to happen on vampire terms, not in open space.

They are not trying to scare the party away. They are trying to finish them.
