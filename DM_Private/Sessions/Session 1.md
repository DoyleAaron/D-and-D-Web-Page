
# Intro 
The city of **Sheyton** rises from the flatlands like a fortress of stone and smoke. Its outer walls are high, patched from centuries of repairs, and its gates bustle with merchants, travelers, and watchful guards in burnished steel. Within, crooked streets coil between looming buildings, their upper floors leaning so close they almost blot out the sky.

Lanterns glow dimly even in daylight, fighting against the haze of smoke from countless forges and hearths. The air is thick with the smells of ale, iron, and wet cobblestone. Sheyton is alive—crowded with dockhands, thieves, pilgrims, and nobles’ envoys all walking the same cramped roads, each with their own secrets.

Here, whispers carry more weight than gold. The wrong word in the wrong ear can vanish you into the alleys. But for those bold—or foolish—enough to make their mark, Sheyton offers both fortune and ruin in equal measure.
# **Location 1: The Mogged Pigeon Pub**

### **Atmosphere**

- A cramped, smoky tavern with low ceilings and crooked beams.
    
- The floor is sticky with spilled ale, and the faint smell of roast boar mixes with damp wood.
    
- Patrons are mostly **locals from the docks and market**, tired workers drinking off the day. A few **shady figures** sit in the back, speaking in hushed tones.
    
- A one-eyed bard plucks a lute in the corner — badly.
    

**Sensory hooks you can narrate:**

- _“The crack of dice and the slosh of mugs echo through the room.”_
    
- _“The fire in the hearth burns low, throwing long shadows.”_
    
- _“Most eyes glance up at you as you enter, then return to their cups.”_
    

---

### **Key NPCs**

You’ll want 2–3 NPCs to play with. Each should be distinct, with a simple voice/personality hook.

**1. Tilda Greybrow (Owner/Barmaid)**

- **Personality**: Gruff, practical, knows everyone’s business but keeps it close to her chest.
    
- **Motivation**: Run her tavern without trouble. She’s suspicious of newcomers.
    
- **Voice hook**: Dry, sharp tongue.
    
- **Sample lines**:
    
    - _“Coin first, chatter later.”_
        
    - _“Don’t bring trouble through my door. The Guard hardly pays me to clean up corpses.”_
        

**2. Old Brann (Drunk Patron)**

- **Personality**: Loud, friendly, quick to latch onto strangers.
    
- **Motivation**: Wants company for drinking, will spill rumours if encouraged.
    
- **Voice hook**: Slurred, jovial.
    
- **Rumour seeds**:
    
    - Guards have been more aggressive in Sheyton lately.
        
    - Some say thieves are being pressured to act by _“folk above their station.”_
        
    - Mentions _“talk of Kluimont stirring things up”_ (foreshadow).
        

**3. Lysa (The Girl)**

- **Personality**: Tearful, urgent, determined.
    
- **Motivation**: Get help for her mother.
    
- **Sample entrance**: _The door bursts open. A small girl in a ragged dress, clutching a patched doll, stumbles in. Tears streak her dirt-stained face._
    
    - Line: _“Please! Please, someone help! They took my mother’s locket—my papa’s gift before he passed! She’s hurt—please!”_
        

---

### **Roleplay Prompts**

- **Drinking / Gambling**: PCs can gamble, overhear gossip, or interact with shady patrons. (Insight/Perception checks for rumours.)
    
- **Information Gathering**: Asking about thieves may get them _“Not my business”_ responses, unless they bribe Tilda or charm Brann.
    
- **Skill Checks**:
    
    - Persuasion/Intimidation vs. Tilda for info on recent thefts.
        
    - Insight vs. Brann’s drunken rambling to separate fact from nonsense.
        

---

### **Transition into the Hook**

- After some banter or downtime, **Lysa bursts in**.
    
- Players can **ignore her** (Tilda shoos her out, but she clings to the PCs if they showed kindness before).
    
- If they **intervene**, she explains the robbery — mother injured outside, locket stolen.
    
- This pulls the group into **Location 2: The Street**.
# **Location 2: The Street (The Robbery Scene)**

### **Atmosphere**

- The cobblestones are wet from earlier rain, reflecting the flicker of lanterns.
    
- A small crowd has gathered, murmuring uneasily. Some shake their heads, others keep their distance.
    
- The mother sits against an overturned cart, clutching her side, face pale. Blood stains the **red scarf** around her neck, marking her as someone of note (and suspicious).
    
- A few townsfolk hover nearby, trying to help but clearly frightened.
    

---

### **Key NPCs**

**1. Elira (The Mother)**

- **Appearance**: Mid-30s, gaunt but striking. The **red scarf** is tied firmly around her neck, frayed at the edges.
    
- **Personality**: Brave but shaken, loyal to her daughter above all. Reluctant to reveal too much.
    
- **Motivation**: Protect Lysa and avoid drawing attention to her connection with the Red Scarves.
    
- **Sample lines**:
    
    - Weakly, _“Please… the locket… it’s all we have left of him…”_
        
    - If pressed about the scarf (Persuasion/Insight DC 15+): _“…It’s just a scarf. Nothing more. Please, don’t… ask about it here.”_ (deflects but plants suspicion).
        
    - If healed or aided: _“Thank you… I thought they’d take everything from us.”_
        

**2. Concerned Baker (Onlooker NPC)**

- **Appearance**: Flour-dusted apron, mid-40s, protective stance near Elira.
    
- **Personality**: Caring but blunt, distrustful of outsiders.
    
- **Motivation**: Wants Elira safe, suspicious of anyone sniffing around.
    
- **Sample lines**:
    
    - _“She’s had enough trouble without strangers pokin’ their noses in.”_
        
    - If PCs show genuine kindness: _“Thieves ran that way — towards the east alleys. One limped. You’ll catch ‘em if you hurry.”_
        

**3. Street Urchin (Optional Clue Dropper)**

- **Appearance**: Skinny teen, dirt-streaked, lurking at the edge of the crowd.
    
- **Personality**: Nervous, opportunistic, will only talk if bribed.
    
- **Motivation**: Wants coin or food.
    
- **Sample lines**:
    
    - _“I saw ‘em — seven of ‘em! Slipped into the old warehouse by the canal. Coin for more?”_
        
    - If refused: _“Fine, then. Hope you like gettin’ lost in the alleys.”_
        

---

### **Roleplay Prompts & Checks**

- **Medicine/Healing**: Help Elira recover (stabilises her, earns her gratitude).
    
- **Perception (DC 12)**: Notice muddy bootprints leading toward the eastern alleys.
    
- **Investigation (DC 14)**: Spot a broken dagger near the cart (belonging to a thief, hinting at their recent scuffle with guards).
    
- **Persuasion/Intimidation**: Crowd members may open up more if convinced or pressured.
    
- **Red Scarf Clue**: Any PC who succeeds on **History/Religion (DC 16)** recalls the _Red Scarves_ are an organisation with murky ties — not outright enemies of the Crown, but their recruitment and influence are controversial.
    

---

### **Transition to Next Location**

- Following the clues → alleyways → abandoned warehouse → **Thieves’ Guild Den (Location 3)**.
    
- If PCs stall, Lysa insists: _“They went that way — I swear! Please, hurry before they’re gone!”_
# **Location 3: The Thieves’ Guild Den (Abandoned Warehouse by the Canal)**

### **Atmosphere**

- The old warehouse leans against the canal, boards creaking, roof patched with tarps.
    
- Dim candlelight seeps through the gaps. Inside, smoke curls from a brazier where damp wood smoulders.
    
- The air is **tense and restless**: raised voices, hurried whispers, the smell of blood and sweat.
    
- Seven thieves in total: some bickering, some wounded, some desperate.
    

---

### **NPC Setup (7 Thieves)**

**1. Rook (Leader)**

- **Appearance**: Wiry, leather armour, dagger always in hand.
    
- **Personality**: Cocky but clearly fraying under pressure. Snaps at anyone who questions him.
    
- **Motivation**: Keep the crew together under guild orders. Fear of failing the higher-ups.
    
- **Lines**:
    
    - _“Shut it, all of you! We’ve survived worse. The guild needs us, so get your heads straight.”_
        
    - If confronted by PCs: _“You don’t know who we answer to. Walk away, while you still can.”_
        

**2. Jerrin (Injured Thief)**

- **Appearance**: Bloody bandage across his ribs, slumped against a crate.
    
- **Personality**: Bitter, scared, coughing between words.
    
- **Motivation**: Just wants out. Fears he won’t make it.
    
- **Lines**:
    
    - _“We barely made it past the guards… this is madness.”_
        
    - _“Rook, they’ll find us. They’ll kill us all.”_
        

**3. Mira (Caretaker of Jerrin, Frantic Thief)**

- **Appearance**: Dark hair tied back, trembling hands as she tends his wound.
    
- **Personality**: Guilty, loyal to her friend, cracks under pressure.
    
- **Motivation**: Wants to escape the guild’s orders. Hints at the truth if coaxed.
    
- **Lines**:
    
    - _“This isn’t what I signed up for… we’re just pawns.”_
        
    - If PCs succeed Persuasion/Insight (DC 14+): _“The guild doesn’t want coin this time. They want… pressure. To stir trouble in Sheyton.”_
        

**4–7. Other Thieves (Extras)**

- Dice-players, lookouts, gamblers. More bark than bite.
    
- Tired, uneasy, restless — not fully loyal.
    
- Lines:
    
    - _“Shut up, Mira!”_
        
    - _“The guards’ll string us up if the guild don’t first.”_
        

---

### **Stress Hooks**

- **Bickering**: PCs can overhear fragments before entering. _“We should’ve dumped the locket when we had the chance.”_
    
- **Injured Thief**: Constant coughing and groaning heightens the sense of weakness.
    
- **Frantic Mira**: If players appeal to her, she may crack and give away guild motives.
    
- **Leader’s Frustration**: Rook snaps at Mira, threatening to “make her shut up” — clear fault lines in the group.
    

---

### **Roleplay / Encounter Options**

- **Combat**: 7 low-level thieves, but two are weakened (Jerrin and Mira won’t fight effectively).
    
- **Negotiation**: PCs could talk Mira into handing over the locket quietly, or convince the group they’re not worth fighting.
    
- **Stealth/Trickery**: PCs could sneak in, steal the locket, and avoid a fight.
    
- **Checks**:
    
    - Insight (DC 13): Sense the group is under external pressure, not just random thieves.
        
    - Persuasion (DC 15): Convince Mira to reveal more.
        
    - Intimidation (DC 16): Rattle the weaker thieves into backing down.
        

---

### **Transition**

- Whether PCs fight or negotiate, they’ll eventually secure the locket.
    
- Before they can leave → **Guards surround the warehouse** (Location 4).
# **Location 4: The Guards’ Arrival (Warehouse Raid)**

### **Atmosphere**

- The moment the PCs finish with the thieves, they hear **boots pounding** and the clank of armour outside.
    
- Torches flare through the warehouse slats. Shadows stretch long.
    
- A barked order: _“Surround it! No one leaves.”_
    
- Doors splinter open — a wall of steel and discipline.
    

---

### **Key NPCs**

**1. Commander Serel Veynar** _(Oathbreaker Paladin)_

- **Appearance**: Early 40s, scarred face, blackened armour trimmed in silver, greatsword at her side. Her eyes carry cold fury.
    
- **Reputation**: Known for results, feared for her methods. Some whisper she broke with her original oath but was kept in command for sheer effectiveness.
    
- **Personality**: Brutal, commanding, zero tolerance for weakness.
    
- **Skill Level**: Far beyond what the PCs could challenge at this stage — her presence should feel overwhelming.
    
- **Lines**:
    
    - _“Pathetic. Rats scurrying in shadows, thinking they can defy the Crown.”_
        
    - If any thief is alive: _“You will serve as an example.”_ (Executes or tortures one in front of PCs and her men).
        
    - To PCs: _“You stand in their den, with their spoils. Explain yourselves.”_
        

**2. Young Guard Cadet — “Alren”**

- **Appearance**: Barely 18, polished armour a size too big, eyes darting nervously.
    
- **Personality**: Inexperienced, unsure, still clinging to the ideals of justice.
    
- **Role**: Potential ally for PCs later. Visibly unsettled by Serel’s brutality.
    
- **Lines**:
    
    - _“Commander… are we sure—?”_
        
    - If approached kindly later: _“I… I don’t think all of you belong in chains. But what can I do?”_
        

**3. Guards (6–8)**

- Trained, disciplined, faceless force. They move on command, no hesitation.
    

---

### **Scripted Beat (Confrontation)**

- If **any thieves survived** the PC encounter:
    
    - Serel orders them dragged forward.
        
    - She makes a speech: _“This is what happens to those who cross the Crown.”_
        
    - Either **executes** (swift strike) or **tortures** (drives a dagger into ribs, slowly twisting) one in full view.
        
    - Cadet Alren looks away, disturbed.
        
- Even if no thieves survive, Serel will accuse the PCs: _“You came here willingly. You fought beside them, didn’t you?”_ (They’re guilty by circumstance).
    

---

### **Roleplay / Player Impact**

- **Persuasion/Deception (DC 18)**: PCs can try to convince Serel they acted against the thieves, not with them. Success reduces harshness of their arrest.
    
- **Insight (DC 12)**: PCs can spot Alren’s discomfort, planting seeds to use him later as a sympathetic contact.
    
- **Intimidation (DC 20+)**: Serel is immune to low-level bluffing, but a truly bold move might earn her dark respect.
    

---

### **Transition**

- Regardless of PCs’ actions, Serel has them **bound and escorted**.
    
- The sense is clear: resistance = death.
    
- They are marched to the **City Hall**, with Alren stealing glances back at them, conflicted.
# **Location 5: City Hall (Before the Envoy)**

### **Atmosphere**

- A vast, echoing chamber of **stone and marble**, lined with Braewood banners.
    
- Heavy braziers burn, the air filled with incense masking the scent of sweat and steel.
    
- A long council table sits at the far end, raised slightly above the stone floor where prisoners are displayed.
    
- Guards line the walls, silent and unmoving. **Commander Serel** stands off to the side, arms crossed, watching with her cold eyes.
    
- The mood: official, but with an undercurrent of **calculated menace**.
    

---

### **Key NPCs**

**1. Lady Envoy Myrelle** _(Secret Traitor)_

- **Appearance**: Mid-30s, finely dressed in dark silks embroidered with Braewood insignia. Always composed, always smiling faintly.
    
- **Personality**: Silver-tongued, intelligent, and manipulative. Outwardly loyal to the Crown but secretly part of a hidden order hoarding magical artifacts.
    
- **Skill Level**: Too clever, too politically embedded to be caught. A natural manipulator.
    
- **Hidden Agenda**:
    
    - Uses the PCs’ arrest as an excuse to size them up as potential pawns or allies.
        
    - Is involved in the conspiracy to frame Kluimont for stolen magical items.
        
- **Lines**:
    
    - Opening: _“Well then… a sorry sight indeed. Caught in the company of thieves, yet still standing before me instead of in a gutter. Intriguing.”_
        
    - Offer: _“You are lucky, adventurers. You may serve your sentence in chains… or serve the Crown. Which sounds more appealing?”_
        
    - Subtle Malice (if pressed about her motives): _“…I wonder which of you can see the board, and which are just pieces upon it.”_
        
- **Perception DC 20**: PCs feel that something is _off_ about her words and eyes, but cannot place it.
    

**2. Commander Serel (Oathbreaker Paladin)**

- Present in the chamber. She stands silently, occasionally scoffing or smirking.
    
- She views the PCs as expendable tools, but obeys Myrelle’s word.
    
- If questioned: _“I enforce. She commands. You’ll do well to remember that.”_
    

**3. Young Guard Cadet Alren** _(Optional Role)_

- May be present as part of the procession, holding papers or standing behind Serel.
    
- Glances at PCs nervously. If they were kind to him earlier, he might later slip them a whispered warning:
    
    - _“Not everything in Sheyton is what it seems… be careful.”_
        

---

### **The Offer (The Hook)**

- Myrelle presents the PCs with a “choice” that isn’t really a choice:
    
    1. Rot in a cell as “accomplices to thieves.”
        
    2. Serve the Crown on a dangerous, expendable mission.
        
- Either way, they’re bound to the Crown’s will — but the **players feel they had agency** in choosing.
    
- Myrelle seals it with: _“Excellent. You’ll find the Crown pays well… for those who survive.”_
    

---

### **Roleplay / Checks**

- **Insight (DC 18)**: Detect that Myrelle is _studying them_ more than judging them, as if testing potential.
    
- **Perception (DC 20)**: Sense her malice — something about her gaze feels predatory, not administrative.
    
- **Persuasion/Performance (DC 15+)**: PCs who present themselves cleverly may earn her favour, which could shape how she treats them later.






# THIEVES (Location 3)

## Standard Thief (Level-1 Cutpurse)

**Look & Vibe:** Rag-wrapped hands, nervous eyes, sweat on the brow. Daggers nicked from a market stall.
**Personality:** Desperate, jumpy; blusters to hide fear.  
**Background:** Bottom-rung Sheyton thief pushed into riskier jobs “to make a point.”

- **AC** 13 (leather)
    
- **HP** 11 (2d8+2)
    
- **Speed** 30 ft.
    
- **STR 10 (+0), DEX 14 (+2), CON 12 (+1), INT 10 (+0), WIS 10 (+0), CHA 10 (+0)**
    
- **Proficiency** +2
    
- **Skills** Stealth +4, Sleight of Hand +4, Perception +2
    
- **Senses** passive Perception 12
    
- **Languages** Common, Thieves’ Cant
    

**Actions**

- **Shortsword.** _Melee Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, reach 5 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 1d6+2 piercing.
    
- **Light Crossbow.** _Ranged Weapon Attack:_ +4 to hit, range 80/320 ft., one target. _Hit:_ 1d8+2 piercing.
    

**Bonus Action**

- **Cunning Footwork.** The thief **Dash** or **Disengage** (choose one) as a bonus action.
    

**Feature (use on at most 2 thieves in the room)**

- **Sneak Attack (1/turn).** +1d6 damage on a weapon hit if the thief has advantage on the attack **or** an ally is within 5 ft. of the target.
    

**Tactics & Morale:** Fights if cornered, otherwise shoots from cover then Disengages. If reduced to **5 HP or fewer**, they shout for terms or bolt.

**Notes:** Won’t fight if offered a way out; spills rumours under pressure.

## Mira, the Frantic Caretaker (Level-1 Thief, talkative)

**Look & Vibe:** Shaking hands, hair pinned back, sleeves rolled for triage.  
**Personality:** Guilty, empathetic; breaks under calm kindness.  
**Background:** Joined for coin and community; shocked by the new “pressure” jobs.

- **AC** 13 (leather)
    
- **HP** 12 (2d8+3)
    
- **DEX 14 (+2), WIS 12 (+1)**
    
- **Skills** Medicine +3, Insight +3, Stealth +4

**Actions**

- **Shortsword.** +4 to hit, 1d6+2 piercing.

**Bonus Action**

- **Field Dressing (1/rest).** A creature she can reach regains **1d4+2 HP** (uses a grubby bandage kit).

**Notes:** On **Persuasion or Insight DC 14+**, she admits the guild’s orders are about **“causing pressure in Sheyton”** not coin.

# COMMANDER SEREL VEYNAR (Oathbreaker Paladin, “do not fight at level 1”)

**Look & Vibe:** Blackened plate, scarred lip, eyes like cold iron. She speaks softly and everyone moves.  
**Personality:** Absolute, pitiless, surgical in violence.  
**Background:** Once the pride of Sheyton; broke her sacred oath in a border war, kept on for results.

- **AC** 18 (plate)
    
- **HP** 102 (12d8+48)
    
- **Speed** 30 ft.
    
- **STR 18 (+4), DEX 12 (+1), CON 18 (+4), INT 11 (+0), WIS 14 (+2), CHA 16 (+3)**
    
- **Proficiency** +4
    
- **Saves** Con +8, Wis +6, Cha +7
    
- **Skills** Intimidation +7, Athletics +8, Perception +6
    
- **Senses** passive Perception 16
    
- **Alignment mask:** “Lawful Crown” (truth: herself)
    

**Actions**

- **Greatsword.** +8 to hit, reach 5 ft., _Hit:_ **2d6+4** slashing.
    
- **Profane Smite (3/day).** When Serel hits with a melee attack, add **+2d8 necrotic** damage.
    
- **Dreadful Command (Recharge 5–6).** Creatures of her choice within 30 ft. that can hear her must succeed on a **DC 15 WIS save** or become **frightened** until the end of their next turn. On success, immune for 24 hours.
    

**Bonus Action**

- **Relentless Advance.** Move up to 10 ft. without provoking opportunity attacks.
    

**Spellcasting (Paladin-like, DC 15, +7 to hit)**  
Slots: 1st (4), 2nd (3), 3rd (2)  
Typical spells prepared: **command, compelled duel, wrathful smite, branding smite, hold person** _(via grim rite),_ **silence** _(tactical)_.

> _Use sparingly—she doesn’t need them to terrify level-1 PCs._

**Aura of Dread.** Enemy creatures that start their turn within **10 ft.** of Serel must make a **DC 15 WIS save** or be **frightened** until the start of their next turn. Creatures that succeed are immune to this aura for 24 hours.

**Tactics:** Demonstrations, executions, breaking lines. If the party does something truly reckless, she maims rather than kills—then **twice as many guards** arrive. She is not here to be defeated.

**Morale:** Unshakeable. She withdraws only on political orders.
