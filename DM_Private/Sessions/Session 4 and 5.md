# The Dungeon Quick-Run (four rooms to the vault)

**Room 1 — Slab Walk (opener):**  
Perception/Investigation DC 11 to spot rocking slabs/wedges; careful pathing or pitons = pass. First misstep: Dex save DC 12 → 1d6 bludgeoning (noise may draw a **short nuisance** rat swarm). Door unlatches on last safe slab.

**Room 2 — Counterweight Door:**  
Two chains must be held **and** weight in the bowl simultaneously. If they faff: hidden fan blades each round (Dex save DC 13 for 2d4 slashing) until wedged (Athletics DC 12) or solved. Loot: 1× Potion of Healing, two 5-lb counter-bricks.

**Room 3 — Hall of Peacebinding (sentinels if they cheat):**  
Players must **stow or peacebind** all weapons/foci, then press the brass plate to unlock. If they draw steel, attack the door, or try to bypass: **Stone Sentinels ×2** activate (AC 15, HP 22; Slam +4 1d6+2; resist non-magical slashing; vulnerable to thunder). Disable runes with Arcana/Thieves’ tools (DC ~12–13). Loot after: Miner’s Salve ×1, Potions of Healing ×2.

**Room 4 — Quad Fountain Vault:**  
**Read-aloud gist:** Four plates, four doors, one question each; **what you yield is weighed and returned**; **all deceit is tithed**. **One sacrifice each**, no table talk while inside.  
**Mechanic:** Awards scale by **Market Power / Personal Weight / Irreplaceability / Immediate Cost**; **Apex** (20+) or dual **Greater** (17–19) in same minute releases **the Book** (or Wish). Overflow tumbles down to the next tier; you can award multiple lesser prizes if separate climbs form. Use the reward ladder for drops (Fatebrand, Mirror Vial, Regrowth Ampule, etc.). Close with the **Reward Pronouncement** boilerplate.  
**Pace tip:** Give each PC **1 minute real-time** in their private room.

**Exit:** One-way rune door to surface near the Den.

# How the Sacrificial Fountain works

**Rule text (what the party reads):**  
_“Yield what you cherish; receive in equal measure. All deceit is tithed.”_

## 1) Valuation rubric (behind the screen)

Score each offer on four axes (0–5 each). Let players argue their case; you adjust.

- **Market Power (0–5):** gp value / rarity / spell tier.
    
- **Personal Weight (0–5):** story importance, bonds, backstory ties.
    
- **Irreplaceability (0–5):** can it ever be reacquired or restored?
    
- **Immediate Cost (0–5):** mechanical loss now (HP max, stats, class feature access, limb, memory, feeling, name, etc.).
    

**Total Score → Tier**

- 0–4 Trifle
    
- 5–7 Minor
    
- 8–10 Modest
    
- 11–13 Significant
    
- 14–16 Major
    
- 17–19 Greater
    
- **20+ APEX** (maximum value)
    

> **Deceit clause:** If a player blatantly overstates value, the Fountain “makes it true” by **taking the difference** (e.g., also erasing a memory, or withering a hand) until the claimed tier is met.

## 2) Access condition for the Book

- To release **Balthazar’s codex**, the Fountain requires **one APEX sacrifice** (20+) **from at least one character** _or_ **two Greater sacrifices** (17–19) from two different characters **in the same minute**.
    
- In exchange for an APEX sacrifice you may choose **one** of:
    
    1. **The Book** (_Chart of the Threefold Way_), or
        
    2. **A Scroll of Wish** (see limits),
        
    3. **Another APEX reward from the table**.
        
- The Fountain **will not** grant both the Book and a Wish **unless** there are **two separate APEX sacrifices**.
    

## 3) Examples of non-physical sacrifices and suggested scores

- A treasured locket tied to plot (Personal 4–5, Irreplaceable 3–4, Market 1–2) → **11–13** Significant.
    
- **Your true name** (Personal 5, Irreplaceable 5, Immediate 4—disadv. vs true-name magic) → **19** Greater.
    
- **A hand** (Immediate 5; Irreplaceable 4 unless _regenerate_; Personal 3) → **12–14** Significant/Major.
    
- **All memories of a loved one** (Personal 5, Irreplaceable 5, Immediate 4—bond loss, Inspiration revoked) → **19** Greater.
    
- **Your fear** (Immediate 3—immune to frightened but −2 Insight and poor caution; Personal 3) → 10–11 Modest/Significant.

# Reward ladder (about 20 outcomes, top to bottom)

Use the tier gates; “APEX” items also appear as _choices_ if the cost hits that tier. Single-use unless noted.

### APEX (20+)

1. **The Chart of the Threefold Way (the Book)**
    
    - Lead-clasped codex. When wetted with Fountain water, reveals **three coordinate sets**; two are decoys. The real set shows a faint astral sigil that only the sacrificing PC perceives. _Arcane Lock_ (DC 17) and _Severance Sigil_ (scrying shows false location unless attuned by the giver’s blood).
        
2. **Scroll of Wish**
    
    - Works normally, **but** casting it brands the caster with the **Fountain’s Tithe**: until next level-up, **−2 to all saves** after the wish resolves, and the caster gains one GM “complication beat” later.
        
3. **Thread of Undoing**
    
    - Snap to **rewind the last 1 minute** of time once. Everyone repeats actions with metaknowledge suppressed except the holder (DC 15 Wis save to retain a single detail).
        
4. **Oath-Coin of Passage**
    
    - Present at a sealed border (yes, even Kluimont) to be let through once. The official “won’t remember why” afterwards.
        

### GREATER (17–19)

5. **Fatebrand**
    
    - Tattoo: **1/day** force any d20 (yours or seen) to be re-rolled and keep either result. Fades at level 6.
        
6. **Mirror Vial (Memory)**
    
    - Store one entire memory (yours or another’s via touch, DC 15 Cha contest). Can replay vividly or restore what was lost.
        
7. **Regrowth Ampule**
    
    - 10-minute ritual to **regenerate** one lost limb/eye or remove one permanent penalty—once.
        
8. **Lantern of Second Sight**
    
    - 10-minute burn reveals **all illusions/invisibilities** in a 60-ft cone for 1 hour.
        

### MAJOR (14–16)

9. **Ring of the Aegis (Homebrew “Protection I”)**
    
    - **1/long rest**, bonus action to gain **+1 AC & +1 saves** for **1 minute** (no attunement).
        
10. **Compass of Lines Unseen**
    

- Points to the **nearest safe path** toward a named destination within 100 miles; recharges at dawn.
    

11. **Balthazar’s Cache-Key (Decoy)**
    

- Opens any mundane/arcane lock of DC ≤ 15 once; turns to dust after.
    

12. **Debt-Stone of Plalisle**
    

- A dwarven foreman owes you a **serious favour** (escort, gear access, paperwork waved) redeemable once in Plalisle Den.
    

### SIGNIFICANT (11–13)

13. **Salve of Iron Skin**
    

- Apply as action: gain **resistance to slashing/piercing** for **10 minutes**.
    

14. **Echo Glyphs (3)**
    

- Leave a 10-word telepathic message in a room; next teammate who enters hears it.
    

15. **Silvered Ledger Page**
    

- Buy/sell once at **20% better rates** in a lawful market by presenting it; turns black after use.
    

### MODEST (8–10)

16. **Potion of the Vigil**
    

- No sleep needed for one night; gain **advantage on Perception** until next dawn, then **1 level of exhaustion**.
    

17. **Reckoner’s Chalk**
    

- Draw a 5-ft sigil: first creature that steps in must make a **Dex save DC 13 or be restrained** (1 min, Str/Dex end).
    

18. **Whisper-Token (pair)**
    

- Two tokens that work like **message** between holders within 500 ft for 24 hours.
    

### MINOR (5–7)

19. **Ration Stone**
    

- Turns a wineskin of water into a hearty meal for 4 once/day (tastes bland).
    

20. **Spark-Wire**
    

- 50 ft wire; as action create bright light and ignite kindling; can fuse two metal bits (counts as _mending_ on metal only).
    

### TRIFLE (0–4)

21. **Wayfarer’s Mark**
    

- A wrist mark that glows faintly when you walk **toward** your intended path; fades in a week.
    

> **Note:** Feel free to hand out _more than one_ reward at a given tier if multiple characters sacrifice—just keep the **Book/Wish** gated by the APEX/dual-Greater rule.

# Mechanical consequences for popular sacrifice types

- **Body part (eye/hand):** Immediate mechanical penalty (e.g., −2 Perception for lost eye; can’t use two-handed items for lost hand) until restored (only **Regrowth Ampule**, _regenerate_, or major side-quest).
    
- **Memory of a person:** Remove one Bond/Ideal tied to them; disadvantage on checks recalling that person or related places; inspiration tied to them is lost.
    
- **Feeling (fear, love, guilt):** Gain an ongoing trait (e.g., **immune to frightened** but disadvantage on Insight and “caution” checks; or cannot benefit from Help actions given by loved NPCs).
    
- **True name:** Creatures with your true name gain **+2 on spell DCs** targeting you; you can’t be targeted by _sending_ or _revivify_ unless the caster knows the new name (you may create one later via a quest).

# Adjudication quick rules

- **One ask per character.** They may combine sacrifices to hit a higher tier (simultaneous).
    
- **No “free” testing:** The Fountain **accepts instantly** and returns in kind; there’s no preview.
    
- **Counterfeit Key trigger:** If they offer Nyrelle’s fake key and claim it’s priceless, the Fountain takes it, **spits out a Minor reward**, and etches: _“False holds no weight.”_ (great reveal moment).
    
- **Mercy clause:** If someone overshoots and loses more than intended, the Fountain may also grant a **Regrowth Ampule** or **Mirror Vial** at your discretion to keep the game fun.

# Read-aloud: The Quadruple Fountains

Stone doors grind open on a circular hall. Four bronze **pressure plates** face four seamless stone doors. A black marble basin sits at the centre, its surface mirror-still. When you enter, starlight ripples across the floor. An old man in indigo robes appears only in the water’s reflection.

All four plates begin to glow.

> **Balthazar (illusory):**  
> “Four tread the threshold; four doors shall answer.  
> Step upon a plate and the door before you opens to a chamber of your own, where a fountain like this awaits. Within, a reflection of me will grant you **one question**—no more.
> 
> Hear the law of these waters, and mark it well:  
> **What you yield, the Fountain weighs and returns in equal measure.** It judges three things—what the world would pay, what **you** would pay, and what your choice **costs you now**. The gift may be coin or steel, oath or memory, feeling or **name**.
> 
> **All deceit is tithed.** Speak a value you have not truly paid, and the Fountain will take what is missing until your claim is made true.
> 
> Each of you may offer **once**. The waters will not reveal the weight of any gift until **all four** have chosen. Then the scales will settle, and the waters will rise with what has been earned.
> 
> Know this of the **balance**:
> 
> - **Like weights climb together.** Two of a kind rise to a single heavier weight; three climb further; four higher still.
>     
> - **Near weights may join**, step by step, until no lawful climb remains.
>     
> - At the summit waits a **boon of the highest order**—but the Fountain grants **only one** such prize in a visit, no matter how many paths might reach it. 
>     
> - Each climb yields **one** prize; separate climbs may yield separate, lesser prizes.
>
> 
> When you are ready, stand upon your plates. Your doors will open. Enter alone, ask your one question, and cast in what you will. When the last choice is made, the waters shall answer.”

As each of you steps onto a plate, the stone door directly ahead **slides open** to a small chamber with its **own fountain** and the same indigo-robed reflection waiting within. The other doors remain closed to you.

# Balthazar’s Reward Pronouncement (boilerplate)

_The four fountains thrum together. The waters rise in glassy globes, each holding a shard of starlight. The reflection of Balthazar tilts his head, listening to a music only he can hear._

> “The scales have settled. What was given has been counted—by the world’s price, by the weight upon your hearts, and by the wound of the moment. The fountains answer thus.”

**If a highest-order boon was reached (Book/Wish):**

> “One path climbs to the **summit**. Only one such height can be trod in a visit; the rest spills back to the step below.  
> The water bears forth a boon of the highest order: **{announce: The Chart of the Threefold Way / A Single Rewriting of Fate}**.”

**If overflow produces an additional prize beneath it:**

> “Excess weight does not vanish; it falls like rain to the ledge beneath. From that spill, the fountains shape **{Tier Name}** recompense: **{Item}**.”

**If no highest-order boon, but a near-top prize was formed (Epic or equivalent):**

> “Your gifts gathered and rose together. They do not reach the summit, yet they stand just below it. The waters give: **{Near-Top Prize}**.”

**If multiple separate climbs created multiple lesser rewards:**

> “More than one climb took shape. Each ascent earns its own answer. The fountains release:  
> — **{Prize A}** for the first ascent,  
> — **{Prize B}** for the second.”

**If exactly one solid mid-tier reward:**

> “Balance found its middle. No higher, no lower. Take this: **{Mid-Tier Prize}**.”

**If minor gifts only:**

> “Small weights make small ripples. Still, the water does not mock the thirsty. Receive: **{Modest/Minor Prize(s)}**.”

**If an offering was deceitful and the tithe was taken (optional line):**

> “A tongue strained the truth; the Fountain set it straight. The crack upon the reflection will fade when the debt is paid.”

**If an offering was judged unworthy (nothing gained):**

> “One bowl rang hollow. No echo returns.”

**If a named individual’s sacrifice keys/attunes the result (e.g., the Book binds to a giver):**

> “The foremost weight was laid by **{Character Name}**. The gift knows its giver. It answers to their hand, and opens at their will.”

**Closing line (use always):**

> “Thus ends the counting. Spend what you have won as wisely as you spent what you have lost.”

_The globes descend. Each shatters with a rain of cool light, leaving the prizes on black stone: **{List visible items}**. The fountains fall silent._

# Room 1 — Slab Walk (opener)

**Read-aloud**  
A 30-foot hall of big square stone slabs stretches ahead. Some show hairline cracks; chalk scuffs run along the walls. A plain stone door waits at the far end.

**Checks (pick what’s rolled)**

- **Perception DC 11**: You spot a few slabs that **rock** under weight.
    
- **Investigation DC 11**: Tiny wedges are missing beneath certain slab edges; those are **unsafe**.
    
- **Survival DC 10** (tracking the chalk): The chalk marks hug the **safe** slab edges.
    

**Pass**

- Step only on firm slabs, or wedge edges with pitons, or lay a board/pole: **auto**.
    

**Fail (first misstep)**

- **Dex save DC 12** or **1d6 bludgeoning**, knocked **prone**; loud clatter.
    

**(Optional) Nuisance on noise**

- **Swarm of Rats (minified):** AC 12, HP 12, +3 to hit, **1d4** piercing (1–2 rounds, then they scatter).
    

**To next room**  
Stepping on the last firm slab **clicks a latch**; the far door slides open.

# Room 2 — Counterweight Door (“two hands, one bowl”)

**Read-aloud**  
A dwarf statue holds a shallow stone **bowl**. Beside a heavy door hang two iron **chains** from ceiling drums. The air smells of old oil.

**What solves it (say clearly)**  
“Door opens only while **both chains are held down** and **enough weight** sits in the bowl **at the same time**.”

**Checks**

- **Investigation DC 12**: Grooves link bowl ↔ chain footings.
    
- **Arcana DC 10**: Faint weight-runes; it’s all one mechanism.
    

**Rulings (fast play)**

- If two characters hold the chains **and** they pile in lots of gear/rocks (packs, shields, loose stones), **door opens**.
    
- If they faff for ~1 real-time minute or let a chain rise, hidden **fan blades** deploy each round: **Dex save DC 13**, **2d4 slashing**/round until they wedge the fans (**Athletics DC 12** with a crowbar/pitons) or get timing/weight right.
    

**Treasure**  
Statue base (Thieves’ tools **DC 12**): **Potion of Healing ×1**, **two 5-lb counter-bricks**.

**To next room**  
Door remains open while the conditions are met; beyond is a short corridor to the next chamber.

# Room 3 — Hall of Peacebinding (sentinel room; disarm or fight)

**Read-aloud**  
Stone lockers line the left wall; a rack of linen **straps** and wax **seals** stands to the right. On the far door, a faint **star-sigil** glows above a palm-sized **brass plate**. Two dwarf-shaped stone suits rest on plinths, hammers crossed.  
Inscriptions read: **“Tools weigh more than threats.”** and **“Lay down what bites; bind what breaks.”**

**What the room expects (state plainly)**

- **Stow** all weapons, shields, and spellcasting foci **in lockers**, **or** **peacebind** them (wrap with linen and press a wax seal).
    
- Then **press the brass plate**. If everyone is stowed/bound, the far door **unlocks**.
    

**Checks**

- **Arcana DC 11**: The sigil senses **drawn or unbound** “threats”; peacebinding counts as safe.
    
- **Investigation DC 11**: Lockers are numbered; seals bear the same star-sigil (official system).
    
- **Perception DC 11**: Hairline glyphs on plinths—those stone suits are **guardians** on standby.
    

**Edge rulings (quick)**

- Foci = holy symbol / arcane focus / druidic sprig / component pouch. Must be **stowed or sealed**.
    
- Hidden daggers/bolts are fine if **sheathed + sealed**.
    
- Natural weapons don’t count.
    
- **Binding** takes **1 action** per item.
    

**If they cheat, draw steel, or attack the door/plinths** → the guardians wake.

**Stone Sentinel ×2 (minimal)**

- **AC 15, HP 22, Speed 20 ft**
    
- **Slam** +4 to hit, **1d6+2** bludgeoning
    
- **Stoneframe**: Resist non-magical **slashing**; **vulnerable to thunder**
    
- **Slow Turn**: If it moved and attacks this round, attacks are **−1**
    
- **Rune Disable**: Adjacent **Arcana DC 12** (spot rune) → next round **Thieves’ tools DC 12** or **Arcana DC 13** to power it down (it goes inert at start of its turn)
    

**Tactics**  
They step off plinths, guard the **door line**, and try to pin intruders between them. If one is disabled or someone presses the plate **after** everyone is bound/stowed, both **freeze**.

**Room tools**

- Using the official **wax seal** on a weapon/focus makes it **count as safe** immediately.
    
- Tossing the counter-bricks from Room 2 onto the plate impresses the system (no roll; just flavour).
    

**Loot (if you search after unlock)**  
Wall cupboard (Tools **DC 12**): **Miner’s Salve ×1** (apply: **resistance to bludgeoning** 10 min), **Potions of Healing ×2**.

**To the finale**  
Pressing the brass plate while everyone is stowed/bound plays a soft chime; the far door **slides open** to the vault.

# Room 4 — Quad Fountain Vault (split & sacrifice)

**Read-aloud (one speech to all)**  
A cruciform hall opens, four bronze **pressure plates** facing four identical doors. At the centre, a black marble **fountain** lies mirror-still. In the water stands the reflection of an old man in indigo robes.

> “Four tread the threshold; four doors shall answer. Step upon a plate and the door before you opens to a chamber of your own, where a reflection of me grants **one question**.  
> **What you yield, the Fountain weighs and returns in equal measure**—what the world would pay, what **you** would pay, and what it **costs you now**. **All deceit is tithed.**  
> Each of you may offer **once**. The waters reveal nothing until **all four** have chosen. Then the scales settle, and the waters rise with what has been earned.  
> **Like weights climb together; near weights may join.** At the summit waits a **boon of the highest order**, but only **one** such height can be taken in a visit; any excess weight spills to the gift below. Each climb yields **one** prize.”

**How to run it (quick)**

1. After the speech, each PC stands on a plate; **their** door opens to a **small fountain room**.
    
2. Inside, they may ask **one** literal question; answer briefly.
    
3. They declare **one sacrifice** (no backsies).
    
4. When all four finish, narrate outcomes using your **reward boilerplate** (you already have it).
    
    - Remember: **only one** highest-order prize (Book **or** Wish). Overflow becomes the next tier down. Separate climbs can yield extra **lesser** prizes.
        

**Optional checks**

- **Arcana DC 12** (before sacrificing): The fountains are sympathetic; they will **make an overstated claim true** by taking the difference.
    
- **Religion/History DC 12**: This rite is old dwarven-arcane practice; the **rules are binding**.
    

**Exit**  
A short passage beyond the central fountain ends at a **one-way rune door** to the surface near Plalisle Den.
