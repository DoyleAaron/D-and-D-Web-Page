# Mayor Adrian — Session 25 Notes

## Adrian's Position After Success

Adrian is relieved, but not calm.

- Liora being taken proved Maltrius can reach deeper into Dawerton than he feared.
- A successful rescue does not relax him. It convinces him things are accelerating.
- He becomes more useful once he knows the party can actually finish ugly work.

---

## Reward He Gives The Party

### Scroll of Purity

- Single use scroll. Any character can read it.
- Cleanses all diseases and curses affecting one target.
- Functions as a combined *Remove Curse* + *Greater Restoration* (disease only) cast simultaneously.
- No material components required.

### Wand of Fear

- Rare, requires attunement.
- Contains **3 charges**.
- Expend **1 charge** to cast *Command* (1st level, DC 15 WIS save).
- Expend **2 charges** to cast *Fear* (3rd level, DC 15 WIS save) — 30-ft cone, frightened creatures must dash away. Repeat save at end of each turn.
- Regains **1d3 charges at dawn**.
- If you expend the last charge, roll a d20 — on a 1, the wand crumbles to dust.

---

## Reward Conditions

- If Liora returns alive, Adrian gives both without question.
- If Liora dies but the brood is destroyed, Adrian gives the scroll but withholds the wand unless the party handled things cleanly.

---

## How Adrian Delivers It

He does not present it like gratitude. He presents it like grim business settled correctly.

Example tone:

> "You did what I could not. The city remembers results better than sentiment. Take these. If we survive the month, perhaps then we can discuss thanks."

---

## What Adrian Wants Next

- A full account of what Liora learned
- Confirmation of how directly Maltrius is now acting
- A realistic answer from the party on whether they intend to strike early or prepare for the 28th

---

## If Liora Dies

Adrian changes immediately.

- He still gives the scroll if the brood is destroyed
- He becomes colder and less trusting
- He stops speaking about "containing" Maltrius and starts speaking about surviving him
