# **Session 2 Layout – “The Envoy’s Task”**

### **1. The Hook (Envoy Scene)**

- **Envoy** explains:
    
    - A **Red Scarf council member** is suspected of treachery.
        
    - Whispers claim he uses a **stolen pendant** to manipulate the weak-willed into swelling Red Scarf ranks.
        
    - The envoy cannot move openly (would cause unrest), so _the adventurers will go instead._
        
- Stakes: Fail, and they’re traitors. Succeed, and their “slates may be wiped clean.”
    

---

### **2. Information Gathering (Optional, Roleplay-heavy)**

- Before heading to the cleric’s safehouse, players can gather intel:
    
    - **Persuasion/Intimidation**: Locals may whisper about “the haunted sermons.”
        
    - **Investigation/Insight**: Notices the “recruits” are pale, malnourished, obedient.
        
    - **Religion/Arcana**: Knowledge that pendants like this are _not divine_, but _arcane corruption_.
        

This section can be quick, but gives roleplayers a chance to shine.

---

### **3. Encounter – The Cleric’s Den**

**Location**: An abandoned chapel or cellar beneath Sheyton. Dim light, pews broken, a makeshift altar glowing with the pendant.

- **Enemies**:
    
    - **Cleric (Red Scarf Council Member)** – CR ~2–3 depending on party size.
        
        - Uses low-level Cleric spells (Bless, Command, Hold Person).
            
        - Can channel the pendant for extra effects.
            
    - **Controlled Commoners** – 6–12 weak humanoids (AC 10, 4–5 HP, club/farm tool attacks for 1d4).
        
        - Appear malnourished, shaky, but attack on command.
            
        - Use swarm tactics — more a distraction/threat than real killers.
            

---

### **4. Pendant Mechanics**

This is where we make the fight special.

**Pendant of Subjugation** _(working name)_

- **Effect**: Controls weak humanoids (commoners, peasants, malnourished thugs).
    
- **Control Limit**: Up to **12 creatures** at once.
    
- **Control Conditions**:
    
    - Only affects creatures with **<10 HP** or **CR 1/4 or lower**.
        
    - Does not affect PCs unless they are **0 HP but stable/unconscious** (so no mind control on the party mid-fight — avoids railroading).
        
- **Pendant Actions (Cleric can use 1/round)**:
    
    - _Command Horde_: As a bonus action, he can order all controlled commoners to move/attack.
        
    - _Drain Will_: Choose 1 controlled creature; it takes 1 damage but gains Advantage on its next attack.
        
    - _Dominate New Victim_: If a humanoid drops below 10 HP, the pendant can make them roll a **Wisdom save DC 13**. On a fail, they join the horde.
        

---

### **5. Resolution**

- **If cleric dies** → pendant falls inert (for now). Controlled commoners collapse, dazed and free.
    
- **If pendant is stolen mid-fight** → the commoners are confused, no longer hostile, but the cleric grows desperate.
    
- **If party tries to break it** →
    
    - Requires **Arcana or Religion check (DC 15)** + physical action (e.g. smashing, heating, dispelling).
        
    - Breaking releases a psychic scream (Wisdom save DC 12 or stunned 1 round).
        

---

### **6. Rewards**

- The **Pendant of Subjugation**:
    
    - Attunement required.
        
    - Player-usable but limited: Can control up to **3 commoners** (weaker version).
        
    - Risk: Each day of use, roll **Wisdom save DC 12** or gain 1 level of Exhaustion (representing the strain of channeling it).
        
- Envoy’s favour (and perhaps some coin).
    

---

### **7. Themes**

- Moral Dilemma: PCs may feel bad cutting down weak, starving commoners. They’re victims, not villains. Offer non-lethal combat options.
    
- Grey Politics: Taking the pendant back doesn’t mean destroying it — what if the envoy wants to _use_ it
