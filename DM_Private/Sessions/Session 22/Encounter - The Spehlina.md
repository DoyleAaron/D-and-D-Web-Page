# Encounter 4 — The Spehlina

**Type:** Combat (Deadly)  
**Location:** Lavalto Lake, open water  
**Trigger:** Party chooses to pursue the Spehlina bounty  
**Theme:** A creature in agony — not evil, but corrupted. The true enemy is the one who did this to it.

---

## Pre-Fight Reminders

- **Full stat block** is in [The Spehlina.md](../../Creatures/The%20Spehlina.md). This file provides combat-session quick reference and tactical notes.
- **Killing the Spehlina releases corruption** (Death Throes: 60 ft., DC 18 CON, 10d10 necrotic + poisoned 1 hr; water within 1 mile corrupted).
- **True solution:** Seal the infernal source (Maltrius/volcanic vents) FIRST, then cleanse with Greater Restoration + Remove Curse + Ceremony.
- **Captain Kirk** wants to save it, not kill it. If Kirk is with them, he'll argue against lethal force.
- The party may not know any of this. Let them discover through the fight.

## Dual-Track Boss System (HP + Purification)

Run the Spehlina with two bars:

- **Health:** 312/312 HP
- **Purification Meter:** 0/100

The Spehlina can be defeated by reducing HP to 0 (lethal route) or by reaching 100 Purification (mercy route).

### Gaining Purification Points

Holy/divine actions both damage the Spehlina and remove corruption.

| Source | Purification Gain |
|---|---:|
| Radiant damage from attack/spell | Half radiant damage dealt (round down, min 1) |
| Holy water splash | +4 |
| Lay on Hands used offensively | +1 per 5 HP spent |
| Lesser Restoration used as action on contact | +6 |
| Remove Curse (on Spehlina) | +12 |
| Greater Restoration (on Spehlina) | +20 |
| Ceremony (1 minute uninterrupted on stabilized Spehlina) | +15 |

### Corruption Pushback

At initiative 20 (after lair action), corruption pushes back:

- If no divine/radiant effect hit the Spehlina since last round, Purification Meter drops by **3**.
- Minimum meter is 0.

### Purification Threshold Effects

| Meter | Effect |
|---:|---|
| 25 | Corruption Aura radius shrinks from 30 ft. to 20 ft. |
| 50 | Loses one Legendary Action each round (3 to 2). |
| 75 | Corrupted Spray recharge becomes 6 only. |
| 100 | Spehlina collapses, corruption sloughs off, becomes docile and non-hostile for 24 hours. |

### End States

- **HP 0 before meter 100:** lethal kill, Death Throes trigger.
- **Meter 100 while HP above 0:** nonlethal purification success, no Death Throes.
- **HP below 80 and meter below 40:** Spehlina attempts to flee to vents.

## Encounter Setup

**Getting There:**
- Lavalto Lake is ~3–4 hours from Dawerton by road, then across the lake by boat.
- The Spehlina lurks in the deeper waters near the volcanic vents on the lake's south side.
- Local fishermen refuse to go near the south end. Party must acquire/hire a boat.

**Environmental Conditions:**
- Water is murky with volcanic sediment. Visibility: 15 ft. underwater.
- The lake surface occasionally bubbles with sulfuric gas (every 3 rounds, random 10-ft. square, DC 12 CON or nauseated — disadvantage on attacks for 1 turn).
- Corrupted bioluminescence: The water glows sickly orange-red in patches, marking the Spehlina's territory.

**The Approach:**
> "The lake is wrong. You can see it from the shore — the water near the south end glows with a sick orange light, pulsing slowly like a heartbeat. Dead fish float belly-up in clusters. The air tastes of sulfur and copper. As your boat cuts into the deeper water, the glow intensifies below you. Something massive moves in the depths — you see the shadow of it, bigger than your boat, trailing tendrils like a dark forest of kelp. It knows you're here."

## Fight Phases

### Phase 1: Emergence (Rounds 1–3)
- The Spehlina surfaces near the boat, tendrils first.
- Opens with **Corrupted Spray** if party is grouped.
- Uses **Tendril** attacks to grapple and separate.
- The Corruption Aura begins affecting anyone in the water.

### Phase 2: Fury (Rounds 4–8)
- Full **Multiattack** sequence. Slams the boat (Siege Monster = double damage to structures).
- Uses **Legendary Actions** to grab + pull underwater.
- Lair action summons Corrupted Fish Swarms to harass casters.
- If a character is grappled, attempts **Swallow** next turn.

### Phase 3: Agony (Below 150 HP)
- The Spehlina's behavior shifts. It's not fighting strategically — it's thrashing in pain.
- **Insight DC 15 or Medicine DC 17:** "This creature isn't attacking you. It's in agony. The corruption is hurting it as much as it hurts you."
- **Religion DC 16:** "There's an infernal taint woven through its body. This isn't natural corruption — someone did this to it."
- The Spehlina begins using **Agonized Thrash** more frequently (pushes away, not pulls in).
- It may try to **Submerge** and flee toward the volcanic vents.

### Phase 4: Death or Mercy
**If killed:** Death Throes trigger. Massive necrotic + poison explosion. Lake corrupted in 1-mile radius. Story consequence: Dawerton's water supply threatened. Maltrius wins this round.

**If subdued/driven off:** The Spehlina retreats to the deep volcanic vents. The party learns the creature can be saved — but only if Maltrius's infernal source is sealed first. Sets up the final arc.

**If party attempts a cleanse mid-fight:** Requires 3 consecutive rounds of sustained divine magic (Greater Restoration + Remove Curse + Ceremony) while the Spehlina is restrained. With the infernal source still active, the cleanse only partially works — the creature becomes docile for 1d4 hours, then re-corrupts. Shows the party the path but not the solution.

With the Purification Meter system, this partial cleanse can be represented as:
- Set meter to 70 if it is lower.
- Spehlina becomes neutral for 1d4 hours.
- Meter decays to 40 after that duration unless source corruption is addressed.

## Narrative Beats During Combat

**Round 1–2:** The Spehlina surfaces with a sound described as "a whale screaming through broken glass."

**Round 3–4 (if Varin uses divine magic):** The Spehlina recoils from Bahamut's radiance — not defensively, but like a wounded animal flinching from a torch. For a moment, its eyes clear. Then the corruption surges back.

**Round 5+ (if Morjor is present):** Morjor's ring pulses. The Spehlina turns toward him — not to attack, but as if recognizing something. Balthazar's residual energy in the ring resonates with the corruption's source.

**If the Spehlina reaches 0 HP:** Its body crumbles slowly. Beneath the corruption, for a brief moment, you see what it was: translucent blue-white, bioluminescent, gentle. Its moonstone eyes look at the party. Then the Death Throes detonate.
