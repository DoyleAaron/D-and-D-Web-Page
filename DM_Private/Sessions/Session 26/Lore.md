### The Spehlina, Corrupted Guardian

*Gargantuan aberration (corrupted elemental), chaotic evil (formerly neutral good)*

---

**Armor Class:** 16 (natural armor)  
**Hit Points:** 312 (25d20 + 50)  
**Speed:** 10 ft., swim 60 ft.

---

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 22 (+6) | 14 (+2) | 20 (+5) | 6 (-2) | 14 (+2) | 8 (-1) |

---

**Saving Throws:** Con +10, Wis +7  
**Skills:** Perception +7, Stealth +7 (underwater only)  
**Damage Resistances:** Fire, Poison; Bludgeoning, Piercing from nonmagical attacks  
**Damage Immunities:** Necrotic  
**Condition Immunities:** Charmed, Frightened, Poisoned  
**Senses:** Blindsight 120 ft., Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 17  
**Languages:** Understands Primordial but can't speak  
**Challenge:** 15 (13,000 XP)

---

### Traits

**Corruption Aura.** Any creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of the Spehlina while in water must succeed on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw or take 14 (4d6) necrotic damage and gain one level of exhaustion. On a success, the creature is immune to this effect for 24 hours.

**Legendary Resistance (3/day).** If the Spehlina fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Death Throes.** When the Spehlina dies, it releases all absorbed corruption. Every creature within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or take 55 (10d10) necrotic damage and be poisoned for 1 hour. On a success, half damage and not poisoned. The water within 1 mile becomes corrupted (see lore).

**Amphibious.** The Spehlina can breathe air and water.

**Siege Monster.** The Spehlina deals double damage to objects and structures.

---

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Spehlina makes three attacks: one with its slam and two with its tendrils. It can replace one tendril attack with Corrupted Spray.

**Slam.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 28 (4d10 + 6) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a ship or structure, it takes 56 damage instead.

**Tendril.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 30 ft., one target. *Hit:* 19 (3d8 + 6) bludgeoning damage plus 10 (3d6) necrotic damage. The target is grappled (escape DC 17). The Spehlina can grapple up to 6 creatures.

**Corrupted Spray (Recharge 5-6).** The Spehlina releases a 60-foot cone of tainted water. Each creature in the area must make a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw, taking 36 (8d8) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Creatures that fail are blinded until the end of their next turn.

**Swallow.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one Large or smaller creature the Spehlina is grappling. *Hit:* The creature is swallowed. A swallowed creature is blinded and restrained, has total cover from attacks outside the Spehlina, and takes 21 (6d6) acid damage plus 14 (4d6) necrotic damage at the start of each of the Spehlina's turns.

If the Spehlina takes 40 damage or more on a single turn from a creature inside it, it must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or regurgitate all swallowed creatures. If the Spehlina dies, swallowed creatures are no longer restrained and can escape using 20 feet of movement.

---

### Legendary Actions

The Spehlina can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Spehlina regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

**Tendril Strike.** The Spehlina makes one tendril attack.

**Corrupting Presence (Costs 2 Actions).** Each creature grappled by the Spehlina takes 14 (4d6) necrotic damage.

**Submerge (Costs 2 Actions).** The Spehlina sinks up to 60 feet into the water. If it has creatures grappled, they are pulled with it.

**Agonized Thrash (Costs 3 Actions).** The Spehlina thrashes violently. Each creature within 20 feet must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or take 22 (4d10) bludgeoning damage and be pushed 15 feet away. Boats within range take 44 damage.

---

### Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Spehlina can take a lair action while in its lake:

- **Corrupted Undercurrent:** The Spehlina creates a 30-foot-radius sphere of swirling corrupt water centered on a point within 120 feet. The area becomes difficult terrain. Creatures that start their turn there take 10 (3d6) necrotic damage.

- **Call the Tainted:** The Spehlina summons 1d4 **Corrupted Fish Swarms** (use Swarm of Quippers with necrotic damage instead of piercing).

- **Volcanic Vent:** Superheated water erupts in a 20-foot-radius, 40-foot-high cylinder. Creatures must make DC 16 Dexterity saves or take 22 (5d8) fire damage.

# Captain Kirk – Temporary Player Character Sheet

## 🎭 Character Concept

You are **Captain Kirk**, a retired naval captain from the town of **Adaham**. You were once the most prestigious captain in the Braewood navy—the one who captained the **King's personal ship and fleet** during royal voyages. 

You are also descended from the legendary **Pirate Queen, Ruler of the Reef Confederacy**—a fact you may keep private or reveal as you see fit.

---

## 📋 Required Story Elements

*The following backstory elements are SET and must be incorporated:*

### Your Connection to the Spehlina
- When you were younger, you were **saved by the Spehlina** (before its corruption)
- This event profoundly affected you
- You have heard the bounty on the creature and know people want it dead
- **Your goal:** Save the Spehlina before someone kills it
- *How it saved you and the details of that encounter are yours to create*

### Your Daughter – Marina (or rename her)
- Your daughter is a **sailor** like yourself
- She is **adopted**—you rescued her from a band of raiders where she was held as a slave
- She was traveling to **Quey** on business
- She **never arrived** and has not made contact
- This is extremely unlike her—she always checks in
- You are **desperate** to find her
- *Her personality, your relationship details, and other specifics are yours to define*

### Your Pact
- You have a **warlock pact** with a neutral water deity
- The deity is impressed by your talents but is **not good-aligned**
- **The price you paid:**
  - One of your eyes (you wear an eyepatch)
  - Your manhood
  - Your soul upon death
- The pact grants you power, especially at sea
- *The name of the deity, how you met, and the nature of your relationship are yours to create*

### Your Background
- You were a **military naval captain** for Braewood
- You captained the **King's personal ship** during royal voyages
- You are now **retired** and live in Adaham
- You are a descendant of the **Pirate Queen of the Reef Confederacy**
- *Your years of service, notable battles, why you retired, and other details are yours to define*

## 🌊 Water Empowerment

*When you are on a boat sailing on a natural body of water (lake, ocean, river), your patron's connection strengthens dramatically.*

**Your DM will provide you with your Water Form stats when this triggers.**

What you know:
- Your abilities are **significantly enhanced** while at sea
- You can **breathe underwater** if you enter the water
- Your attacks become more powerful
- New abilities become available
- Being on the deck of a ship on open water is enough—you don't need to be submerged

*The exact changes are detailed on your Water Form sheet, which your DM will give you.*

## 👁️ The Eye

Your sacrificed eye is a conduit to your patron.

**Communication:** You can remove your eyepatch and attempt to speak with your deity. Roll 1d20—on 15+, they respond. Their advice is cryptic and detached.

**Last Resort:** There is a powerful ability tied to your eye. If you ever feel truly desperate, ask your DM about "complete surrender." *This is not something to use lightly.*
