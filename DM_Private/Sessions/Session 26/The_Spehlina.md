# The Spehlina, Corrupted Guardian

*Gargantuan aberration (corrupted elemental), chaotic evil (formerly neutral good)*

### BOSS INTRO — When the Spehlina surfaces

> *The lake is silent. Unnaturally so. The birds are gone. Even the wind seems to hold its breath.*
>
> *Then the water changes colour.*
>
> *It starts at the centre — a deep, oily darkness spreading outward like ink dropped into water, shot through with veins of sickly red-orange light that pulse like a heartbeat. The smell hits you next. Rot and sulfur and something older, something wrong.*
>
> *The first thing you see are the eyes. Dozens of them. Bloodshot and weeping dark ichor, rising from the deep like lanterns in a flooded cathedral. Then the shape beneath them — massive, formless, filling the water from horizon to horizon.*
>
> *It breaches the surface with a sound like a ship breaking apart. Sixty feet across. Barbed tendrils trailing corruption wherever they drag through the water. Its body, once translucent and beautiful if the old stories are to be believed, is now the colour of a bruise — crimson-black flesh split by those pulsing veins of volcanic light.*
>
> *It screams. Not a roar. A scream. High and long and full of something that doesn't sound like rage.*
>
> *It sounds like pain.*

>

> *The Spehlina goes rigid.*
>
> *Every tendril freezes mid-lash. The pulsing veins in its flesh flicker — red to white to red to white — like a candle in a storm. Its eyes, all of them, roll back.*
>

## Traits

**Corruption Aura.** Any creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of the Spehlina while in water must succeed on a **DC 17 Constitution** saving throw or take **14 (4d6) necrotic damage** and gain one level of exhaustion. On a success, immune for 24 hours.

**Legendary Resistance (3/day).** If the Spehlina fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Death Throes.** When the Spehlina dies, it releases all absorbed corruption. Every creature within 60 feet must succeed on a **DC 18 Constitution** saving throw or take **55 (10d10) necrotic damage** and be poisoned for 1 hour. On a success, half damage and not poisoned. Water within 1 mile becomes corrupted.

**Amphibious.** Can breathe air and water.

**Siege Monster.** Deals double damage to objects and structures.

**Multiattack.** Three attacks: one slam and two tendrils. Can replace one tendril with Corrupted Spray.

**Tendril.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 30 ft., one target.
*Hit:* 19 (3d8 + 6) bludgeoning + 10 (3d6) necrotic. Target is **grappled** (escape DC 17). Can grapple up to 6 creatures.

**Corrupted Spray (Recharge 5-6).** 60-foot cone. **DC 17 Dexterity** saving throw. **36 (8d8) necrotic** on fail, half on success. Fail = **blinded** until end of next turn.

*Hit:* Creature is swallowed — **blinded, restrained**, total cover. Takes **21 (6d6) acid + 14 (4d6) necrotic** at start of Spehlina's turns. If Spehlina takes 40+ damage in one turn from inside, DC 20 CON save or regurgitates all.

## How to Run This Fight

### The Core Problem — Don't Just Attack Boats
The Spehlina has Siege Monster (double damage to objects/structures), which makes it tempting to just have it bash ships until they sink. **Don't do this.** It's not fun and it removes the players from the fight. The Spehlina doesn't want to sink ships — it wants the things that are hurting it.

**The key insight:** The Spehlina has INT 6 but WIS 14. It's not smart, but it's perceptive and instinct-driven. The corruption from Asmodeus has made it territorial and aggressive, but it still operates on creature instincts. It goes for threats — and the biggest threats are the ones dealing radiant damage to it.

---

### Legendary Actions — How They Work
You get **3 legendary actions per round**, spent one at a time, **at the end of other creatures' turns** (not on the Spehlina's own turn). They reset at the start of the Spehlina's turn.

**Example round:** Player A attacks → you spend 1 LA (Tendril Strike). Player B casts a spell → you spend 2 LA (Corrupting Presence on grappled targets). Player C moves → Spehlina's turn comes, it acts normally, LAs reset.

**Don't hoard them.** Use 1 or 2 LA most rounds. Save the 3-action Agonized Thrash for when a cluster of enemies is on deck.

---

### Lair Actions — How They Work
On **initiative count 20**, before anyone who rolled 20 or less acts, the Spehlina gets one free lair action. This costs nothing — it's not part of its turn, not a legendary action. Think of it as the lake itself acting.

**The initiative count 20 slot fires every single round.** Pick one lair action. It just happens.

---

### Ships vs. People — The Targeting Logic
The Spehlina doesn't hate ships — it's been conditioned to associate them with threat over 30–40 years of being attacked by hunters. Its opening instinct is to put ships out of action (stop them from running), but it doesn't sit there grinding on hull HP all fight. Think of ship damage as a threat to create pressure, not the actual combat.

**Targeting priority (run it in this order):**
1. **Round 1:** Slam the nearest ship's hull once to signal danger — 56 damage to the Wavecutter is a crisis. Now the crew are scared and pinned.
2. **Round 1–2:** Tendrils reach 30 ft — it starts *pulling people off the ships*. Anyone on deck within 30 ft of the Spehlina is a grapple target.
3. **Once someone is grappled:** It doesn't let go. It uses Corrupting Presence as a legendary action (2 LA) to punish grappled targets. It tries to drag one into the water and eventually Swallow them as a horror moment.
4. **Once it takes radiant damage:** This is the corruption's instinct kicking in. It starts prioritising whoever is dealing radiant — the clerics. It will specifically direct tendrils at them.
5. **Corrupted Spray:** Use this when 3+ creatures are clustered on a deck or in the water near it. The 60 ft cone easily covers a whole ship deck.

---

### Grappling and Ships — Rules Clarification
- **Ships are objects, not creatures.** They cannot take opportunity attacks.
- If the Spehlina moves away from a ship, no one gets an opportunity attack *from the ship*. Crew on deck might, if the Spehlina was within their weapon's reach.
- The Spehlina's tendril reach is **30 ft** — it almost never needs to be adjacent to a ship to grab crew on deck. It can lurk alongside and fish people off.
- **Can it grapple a ship?** No — grapple requires a creature. But it can push/slam a ship as an object using its Slam action.

| Round | Spehlina Behaviour |

| **1** | Surfaces (read intro). Slams the Wavecutter's hull (56 damage, alarm). Two tendrils reach for crew members on deck. Lair action: Corrupted Undercurrent near one ship to make the water around it dangerous. |

### Being Dragged — House Rules

When the Spehlina moves away from the ship while someone is grappled, the tendril goes taut and tries to yank them overboard. The Spehlina does **not** stop moving — the drag attempt happens as part of its movement, not as a separate action. It keeps going regardless of the result.

**The grappled player immediately makes a DC 15 Strength saving throw:**

| Result | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| **Pass** | They grab the railing, a rope, an ally — they hold. Still grappled, still on the ship. The Spehlina must try again next turn or use Submerge. |
| **Fail** | Yanked off the deck. They hit the water and are now being dragged alongside the Spehlina. |

This check does **not** use their action — it happens during the Spehlina's turn. On their own turn they can still attack, cast, or attempt to break the grapple normally (Athletics/Acrobatics vs DC 17).

---

**Submerge (2 LA) — Diving with a grappled creature:**

This is harder to resist. DC 17 Strength saving throw (the dive is sudden and violent):

| Result | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| **Pass** | They wrench free as it dives — they're in the water but no longer grappled. They still need to swim up. |
| **Fail** | Dragged 60 ft underwater. Grappled, in darkness, no air supply. Corruption Aura applies if within 30 ft of the body. |

Again — this does not stop the action. The Spehlina dives, the check happens, play continues.

---

### If Someone Falls in the Water
The **Corruption Aura** only applies to creatures that *start their turn* within 30 ft of the Spehlina while in the water. So falling in isn't instant death — but if they're still in the water at the start of their next turn near the creature, DC 17 CON or 4d6 necrotic and a level of exhaustion. Urgency without insta-kill.

**Drowning:** A creature can hold its breath for a number of minutes equal to 1 + its CON modifier (minimum 30 seconds). After that, it drops to 0 HP at the start of each turn. Most party members have CON +2 or better — they have 3 minutes underwater before drowning becomes a threat. Exhaustion from Corruption Aura is the real danger, not the water itself.
