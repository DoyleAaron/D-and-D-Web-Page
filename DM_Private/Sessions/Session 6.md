### **Encounter Title: Broken Chains by the Roadside**

**Setting:**  
A dirt trade road cutting through sparse woodland. The afternoon sun casts long shadows, and the distant creak of wagon wheels breaks the stillness.

---

### **Scene Description**

As your party travels, they round a bend and spot **three wagons** pulled to the side of the road.  
One of them has a **broken wheel**, lying at an awkward angle. Around it stands a group of people and animals:

- **6 ragged-looking individuals**, wearing collars and iron cuffs — clearly **slaves**.
    
- **4 rough-looking guards** with cudgels and short swords.
    
- A well-fed man in fine but dusty traveling clothes — the **slave trader**, shouting orders.
    
- A young boy (about 13) — the **trader’s son**, sitting atop a wagon, watching nervously.
    

The trader seems angry and anxious, gesturing to the broken wheel.

---

### **The Setup**

When the party approaches, the trader waves them down.

> “You there! Adventurers, are you? Perhaps you can help a businessman in need. One of my wheels has broken — if you’ve the skill to fix it, there’ll be coin in it for you.”

The guards eye the group warily, and the slaves avoid eye contact.

---

### **NPCs**

**Rughan Vale** – Human Male, Slave Trader

- Arrogant, dismissive, but cowardly if threatened.
    
- Believes slavery is “just business.”
    
- Offers 5 gp if the party helps repair the wagon.
    
- If pressed about the slaves, claims they are “criminals” or “debtors.”
    

**Joren Vale** – Human Male, 13 years old, Rughan’s son

- Quiet and uneasy. Doesn’t like what his father does but is too scared to speak up.
    
- Might whisper to a kind party member that he wants to run away someday.
    

**4 Mercenaries** – Bandit stat blocks (MM p.343)

- Hired muscle. Loyal to payment, not principle.
    
- If Rughan dies or flees, they might surrender or run.
    

**6 Slaves** – Commoners

- Exhausted and fearful, but one (a half-elf woman named **Mira**) dares to speak if the party seems trustworthy.
    
- She’ll beg for help escaping if approached privately.
    

---

### **Possible Approaches**

#### **1. Help the Trader (Neutral/Evil or Pragmatic Option)**

- The party repairs the wagon (DC 12 Strength or Dexterity check with tools).
    
- They receive **5 gp** and Rughan’s “thanks,” though he treats them with disdain.
    
- A successful **Insight (DC 10)** or **Perception (DC 12)** check reveals the slaves’ terrified expressions — hinting there’s more to the story.
    

#### **2. Free the Slaves (Heroic/Moral Option)**

- If the party tries to release them, Rughan orders his guards to attack.
    
- Combat ensues:
    
    - **Rughan Vale** (use **Bandit Captain** stats, but with 22 HP)
        
    - **4 Mercenaries** (Bandits)
        
- The slaves may join in (improvised weapons) after one round if inspired (Persuasion DC 12).
    
- After victory, the slaves thank the party. Mira knows of a **safe settlement** nearby (could lead to a future quest).
    

#### **3. Diplomacy or Intimidation**

- **Persuasion DC 15**: Convince Rughan to release the slaves for payment, favors, or moral reasoning.
    
- **Intimidation DC 13**: Force him to free them or flee.
    
- If Rughan flees, the mercenaries hesitate — 50% chance they don’t fight.
    

---

### **Twists / Hooks**

- One of the freed slaves (Mira) once worked for a **noble family** and carries valuable information — this can lead to a new quest or political intrigue.
    
- Joren (the son) might secretly follow the party later, seeking a better life.
    
- Rughan could survive and seek **revenge** down the line.

_“You arrive at Mortling Stronghold, the first line of defense against the Kluimont threat. Massive stone walls rise before you, topped with watchtowers where sentries scan the horizon day and night. The streets are orderly and busy with soldiers drilling, merchants moving supplies, and citizens going about their daily routines. ## Rumors
### 1) “They say the manor inside the volcano houses thirty vampires — and four of them are true purebloods who never leave the lava halls.”

- **Credibility:** ★★★★☆ (commonly whispered; many believe there’s a manor)
    
- **Likely truth:** True: there is a grand manor built into the volcanic vent system where many vampires shelter; a small number are ancient/purebloods.
    
- **How to verify:** Ask dockworkers and old miners (Persuasion DC 12). Check guild ledgers for unusual shipments to the mountain (Investigation DC 14). Find a retired stonemason or smuggler who has delivered supplies (Insight/Persuasion DC 13).
    
- **Leads:** A smuggler named Kess in the lower quay who handled “special freight” (rumor source). A miner’s hollow with old service tunnels.
    
- **Risk / Hook:** Those who approach the mountain openly draw attention (watchful vampire sentries); digging too deep will reveal wards and patrols.

## Negation and Dinner Scene
### **Scene 1: Council Meeting**

#### **Purpose:**

- Introduce the Dawerton Council formally.
    
- Show political structure and city dynamics.
    
- Allow the party to **observe**, gather rumors, and sense underlying tension.
    
- Set up Liora’s suspicion of the party.
    

---

#### **Setting:**

- Council chamber in a **well-maintained government building near the harbor**.
    
- Long rectangular table, councilors seated in order of seniority.
    
- Warm lighting from lanterns; walls adorned with trade banners and minor arcane symbols.
    
- Liora sits near the head of the table, nearest to where the party is introduced.
    

---

#### **Scene Beats:**

1. **Introductions**
    
    - The party is presented as **royal contractors** here to witness negotiations and provide protection for the envoy.
        
    - Each councilor is briefly introduced (Elara, Fenric, Jorath, Miriam, Varis, Liora).
        
    - NPC roleplay tips:
        
        - Elara: Politely inquisitive, asks about trade goods.
            
        - Fenric: Observes guards closely, nods to security protocols.
            
        - Jorath: Eyes the party, subtle eyebrow raise, makes quiet notes.
            
        - Miriam: Friendly, asks general questions about travel.
            
        - Varis: Smiles wryly, occasionally interjects with political insight.
            
        - Liora: Calm, polite, but studies the party’s extra muscle.
            
2. **Discussion**
    
    - Envoy reads a letter of introduction from the Queen; Council acknowledges.
        
    - Topics include:
        
        - Ports, trade routes, and reagents (public, neutral topics).
            
        - Minor rumors of missing shipments or delays (low-key).
            
    - Party mostly **observes**, but can roll:
        
        - **Insight DC 12–14** to detect tension between Liora and other council members.
            
        - **Perception DC 13** to notice subtle gestures between vampires (eye contact, hand signals).
            
3. **Liora’s Observation**
    
    - She notices extra guards/muscle in the party.
        
    - Politely remarks:  
        _“Your presence is... notable. It is unusual for visitors to bring such numbers. Perhaps you would join me for dinner? I find discussions more... candid outside the chamber.”_
        
    - Offers an invitation without accusation—**just curiosity**.

### **Scene 2: Dinner at Liora’s Place**

#### **Purpose:**

- Roleplay intrigue; subtle investigation opportunity for the party.
    
- Introduce **diplomatic vampires’ personality** and the factional split.
    
- Give opportunity for **rumors or small secrets** to leak naturally.
    

---

#### **Setting:**

- Liora’s private quarters / townhouse near the city center.
    
- Elegant, dark wood and silver décor, understated arcane motifs.
    
- Candles and lanterns provide warm light; faint scent of herbs.
    
- A human servant (possibly the tavernkeeper) attends discreetly.
    
- Liora’s table seats the party, her translator, and her aides comfortably.
    

---

#### **Scene Beats:**

1. **Arrival & Seating**
    
    - The party is escorted to the dining room.
        
    - Liora welcomes them warmly, emphasizing diplomacy:  
        _“Please, relax. The council often must appear formal, but I prefer honesty over ceremony.”_
        
    - Translator interprets for party if necessary.
        
2. **Dinner Conversation**
    
    - Topics are mostly mundane, but Liora is gently probing:
        
        - Questions about the party’s journey, motivations, and previous missions.
            
        - Comments on “interesting security arrangements” (the extra muscle).
            
        - Observations on current trade, the harbor, or minor politics.
            
    - **Insight Opportunity:**
        
        - DC 12–14 Insight: detect Liora’s curiosity and cautious politeness.
            
        - DC 14–16 Insight: notice subtle hints she suspects unusual purpose beyond official duties.
            
    - **Optional Interaction:**
        
        - Party can try **persuasion or deception** to downplay their “muscle” role.
            
        - Liora may subtly test them with hypotheticals (“What would you do if a shipment went missing under your watch?”).
            
3. **Subtle Hints / Rumors During Dinner**
    
    - Liora may casually mention:
        
        - The existence of the volcanic purebloods (“Some of our oldest families prefer the heat of the mountains and see themselves above politics.”)
            
        - Missing people or unusual shipments (“It is… unsettling, the things we hear about the last Friday of the month.”)
            
        - Political friction with the purely human councilors or merchants, in a neutral tone.
            
    - Party can ask questions carefully—she answers **truthfully, but vaguely**.
        
4. **Scene Closure**
    
    - Dinner ends politely. Liora expresses gratitude:  
        _“Your vigilance is appreciated. Dawerton is safer when all eyes are open, even those of outsiders.”_
        
    - Party leaves with the sense of:
        
        - They are being **watched carefully but not threatened**.
            
        - There is **hidden tension between factions**.
            
        - Liora trusts them enough to let them leave unharmed, but remains **alert**.
            

---

### **Optional Checks / Player Opportunities:**

- **Perception DC 13:** Notice small gestures from servants or subtle magical wards in the room.
    
- **Arcana DC 14:** Detect minor enchantments on silverware, candlesticks, or doors (warding or tracking).
    
- **Insight DC 15:** Detect tension between Liora and her human council hosts.
    
- **Persuasion / Deception:** Convince Liora of party’s honesty, potentially unlocking more information.
    

---

### **Narrative Hooks from the Dinner:**

1. **Factional intrigue:** Diplomats vs purebloods.
    
2. **Hints of missing people / nocturnal shipments.**
    
3. **Rumor seeds:** volcanic purebloods, soul-powered relics, secret access routes.
    
4. **Trust building:** Party can earn a small ally in Liora if they act honestly.

## Difficulty knobs (quick mathless tuning)

- **Medium → Hard:** add **one body** or turn on **one hazard** (fans, thunder pulse, dim-light invisibility, difficult terrain).
    
- **Hard → Deadly:** add **one elite** (veteran/helmed horror/spawn) **or** shrink the space (less kiting).
    
- **Make it merciful:** telegraph counters (bright light vs. shadows, holy water vs. spawn, antimagic vs. armor).
    
- **Short rest gates:** Aim for a short rest roughly every **2–3 beats** so resources breathe (fits your compass recharge rhythm from Item 4).

## The Clerk (coins, papers, rumour seed)

**When:** Immediately after the audience.  
**Setup:** Ledger table; coin scale; neat stacks.

> **Read aloud**  
> “Coin, slips, seals,” the clerk murmurs, sliding a tray. He pauses at a ledger line. “Hmn. **Stamping irregularity**… likely nothing.”

**Checks**

- **Investigation 12 / Perception 13:** Blank or out-of-sequence **key-intake line** spotted.
    
- **Insight 12:** He noticed too, but won’t blurt it publicly.
    

**Push lines**

- “Records can clarify. **Procurement East** keeps the older binders.”
    
- If pressed hard: “I’ll not malign a colleague **without proof**.”
    

**Success:** A written note of “irregular entry” for their folder.  
**Fail:** He closes the ledger; “Return with **proper request**.”

## 💬 Scene Flow and Dialogue Outline

As the party passes through the portal, the world slows for Morjor. He finds himself alone in the in-between — silence except for the rhythmic hum of magic. A figure coalesces.

_(He gestures to the space around — it flickers like a memory.)_

### **2. Recognition and Destiny**

## 💍 Magic Item Description: _The Queen’s Lost Ring_

_(Created by Archmage Balthazar)_

**Wondrous Item (Ring), Rare (Requires Attunement by a Lawful Good creature)**

### **Appearance:**

A slender silver ring with faint runes that shift like ripples in water. It occasionally emits a pulse of violet light, particularly when near a magical item.

---

### **Lore:**

This ring was crafted for **Queen Atheliana Aeristel** when she was a child — both a charm of protection and a vessel for greater destiny. It was lost to the tides but reclaimed by Balthazar, who reforged it into a conduit of restoration and judgement.

It is bound by soul-thread to Atheliana’s bloodline; its true potential can only be unlocked when it is returned to her.

---

### **Base Abilities:**

- **Attunement:** Only a creature of Good alignment can attune to the ring.
    
- **Recognition of Blood:** When within 1 mile of Queen Atheliana (or her direct descendants), the ring emits a faint warmth.
    
- **Truthmark:** Once per long rest, the wearer can cast _Zone of Truth_ (save DC 15) without using a spell slot.
    

---

### **Unique Power — Arcane Siphon**

- When the wearer touches a **magical item**, they can choose to **absorb** a fragment of its enchantment.
    
- The absorbed item becomes mundane (loses its magic), and the ring gains **one charge**.
    
- Each absorbed item grants the ring a **temporary property**, depending on the item’s nature.
    

For example:

|Item Absorbed|Temporary Power|
|---|---|
|+1 Weapon|Ring grants +1 to attack rolls for 24 hours|
|Cloak of Protection|Ring grants +1 AC for 24 hours|
|Wand of Fireballs|Ring stores one charge of _Fireball (3rd level)_|

- The ring can store **up to 3 absorbed properties** at a time.
    
- Absorbing one of Balthazar’s **Trinity Items** permanently binds their essence into the ring — awakening one-third of its **true form**.
    

---

### **Bound to the Queen:**

- The full magic of the ring (once the three Trinity essences are absorbed) can **only** be activated by **Queen Atheliana Aeristel**.
    
- Until then, it serves as a dormant vessel — waiting for its rightful bearer.
    

---

### **DM Notes (Secrets for You Only):**

- Balthazar designed the Trinity Items as vessels of cosmic equilibrium: **Creation**, **Preservation**, and **Destruction**.
    
- The ring’s purpose is to **contain** their volatile energies to prevent cataclysm — but others may see it as a weapon.
    
- The “corrupt advisor” is likely seeking the Trinity as well — but to use their combined force to **control** Atheliana and Islefield’s throne.

## Blessing of the Platinum Hand (Bahamut)

**Rare blessing (divine), one-time gift — 10 total charges, no recharge**  
**Prerequisite:** Devotion to Bahamut. **Restriction:** You cannot target yourself with this blessing.

**What it does (action, touch):** Expend **1 charge** to lay your scaled hand upon a creature and choose **one** of the following miracles. No spell slots, components, or checks are required.

- **Restore a Lost Limb (as _regenerate_):** A severed or missing body part (arm, leg, eye, etc.) is restored; the creature regains basic use immediately, and full regrowth completes in ~2 minutes.
    
- **End a Curse (as _remove curse/greater restoration_, 1 effect):** Ends one curse, including attunement to a cursed item or a single effect causing cursed afflictions.
    
- **Cure Blight (as _greater/lesser restoration_, 1 effect):** End **one** of: blinded, deafened, paralysed, petrified, or one disease/poison (mundane or magical).
    
- **Lift Malign Influence:** Ends one magical effect that states “this can’t be removed by normal means” if it is **not** death, undeath, a patron pact, or a wish backlash. (DM adjudication as needed.)
    

**Limits & clarity**

- Cannot restore life, create body parts from nothing for a dead creature, undo lichdom/vampirism, reverse ageing, or overturn the consequences of a _wish_.
    
- You must touch the target and intend mercy or justice, not personal gain.
    
- Using this power for cruelty or selfish profit risks Bahamut withdrawing the blessing.
    

**You are Chosen:** Divinations perceive a faint celestial aura on you. Metallic dragons and good-aligned celestials recognise the mark (usually favourable).

## The Arm’s Transformation (cosmetic + light mechanical flavour)

Each time you use a charge, your **remaining hand/arm** changes further:

- **Upon acceptance (0 uses):** Platinum pin-scales dust the hand. Your hand counts as a **holy symbol** for your cleric spells. You can cast **Spare the Dying** by touch, at will.
    
- **After 3 uses:** Heat-hardened scales; you can safely handle red-hot metal and open flames without harm to that **hand** or items held in it.
    
- **After 6 uses:** Grip like a dragon. Your unarmed strike with the platinum hand counts as **magical** (1d4 slashing).
    
- **After 8 uses:** Claws form (unarmed strike with that hand becomes **1d6 slashing**, magical).
    
- **After 10 uses (finale):** The arm is fully draconic—**immune to fire** (the arm itself; you can plunge it into lava, hold burning objects, etc.), and your claw with that arm becomes **1d8 slashing** (magical). The blessing’s 10 miracles are spent permanently, but the arm remains.
    

> **Tracking suggestion:** Ten tiny platinum scale-runes appear along the forearm; each dims as a charge is used.

## Bahamut’s Offer (read-aloud)

_A hush like snowfall over steel. The scent of clean rain and hot coin. A voice, vast and close, settles into your bones—measured, merciful, unyielding._

**“Varin,”** says the Platinum Dragon, **“I saw your choice. You offered your flesh for a higher good and were left uncompensated. Such imbalance is not justice—it is debt. I will not leave it unpaid.”**

**“Hear my offer.** Give me your willing hand—not in sacrifice, but in **service**. I will set my scales upon it and make of it a **Mercy-Hand**. With it, you shall knit bone and sinew, draw out venom and plague, break the barbs of curses, and make whole what mortals name ‘irreversible’. Ten times may you spend this grace. **Never upon yourself.** Each work of mercy will turn flesh to scale, until on the tenth your arm is as mine—clawed and fireless, a sign to all that you are **Chosen**.”

“**But know my terms.** Use this gift to lift the lowly, to right the crooked measure, to shield the innocent. Do not sell it, flaunt it, or spend it for vainglory. Should you twist mercy to cruelty, my light will depart from that hand, and its scales will dull.”

**“Swear, Varin.** Swear to wield this hand in justice tempered by mercy, and I will place my mark upon you. Refuse, and I shall count even your refusal as courage—for I compel no heart.”

_The air brightens like dawn on polished mail._ **“Will you take my scales, and with them, the weight they carry?”**
