# Tearitus — Vampire Transformation Mechanics

## Overview

Tearitus was bitten by Father Maltrius but the turning was **interrupted** before full venom transfer. He is a **Partial Vampire** — caught between mortal and undead. This is mechanically distinct from a full vampire and creates ongoing tension through the **Sanity Meter** system.

---

## The Daylight Ring

*Given to Tearitus by Liora after his escape — delivered via the traitor guard or in a later meeting.*

**Daylight Ring (Attunement Required)**
- While equipped: Tearitus can walk in sunlight without taking damage
- **Cost:** Maximum HP is **reduced by 25%** (round down)
- If removed in sunlight: Standard vampire sunlight hypersensitivity applies (20 radiant damage/start of turn)
- The ring glows faintly warm — enchanted with a fragment of true daylight

> **Example at Rogue Level 10 (HP ~63):**
> With ring: Max HP = 47
> Without ring: Max HP = 63 but cannot be in sunlight

---

## Day / Night Cycle

### Daytime (With Daylight Ring)
- HP reduced by 25% (ring cost)
- Normal rogue abilities only
- No vampire powers active
- No vampire weaknesses (ring protects)
- Appears mostly human — slightly pale, eyes occasionally flash red when emotional

### Nighttime (Ring On or Off)
- **HP returns to full maximum** (ring penalty does not apply at night)
- All vampire powers activate (see below)
- **All standard vampire weaknesses apply:**
  - Running water: 10 acid damage/round
  - Cannot enter a residence without invitation
  - Stake through heart: paralyzed (not destroyed — he's partial)
  - Garlic: disadvantage on attack rolls and ability checks within 10 ft
  - Holy symbols: DC 15 WIS save or frightened, must keep 10+ ft away
  - Silver: attacks with silvered weapons bypass his regeneration

**Night Powers:**
- **Vampire Regeneration:** Regain 5 HP at start of each turn (disabled by radiant damage until start of next turn)
- **Spider Climb**
- **Enhanced Senses:** Darkvision 120 ft, advantage on Perception checks
- **Vampiric Bite:** Melee attack, +DEX to hit, 1d6+DEX piercing + 2d6 necrotic. Tearitus regains HP equal to necrotic dealt. **Using this lowers Sanity by 5 points**

## The Sanity Meter

**Scale:** 0–100 | **Starting Value:** 75 *(partial turn — shaken but in control)*

### Sanity Thresholds

| Sanity | State | Mechanical Effects |
|--------|-------|--------------------|
| **100–76** | **Lucid** | Full control. No vampire urges. Resting takes **2× as long** (long rest = 16 hrs, short rest = 2 hrs). Bite does only 1d6 necrotic. Regeneration is 3 HP/turn |
| **75–51** | **Tempted** | Mostly in control. Occasional urges. Resting takes **1.5× as long** (long rest = 12 hrs, short rest = 1.5 hrs). Normal vampire power levels |
| **50–26** | **Hungry** | **25% chance per combat round** (DM rolls d4; on a 1) that Tearitus is distracted by an ally's blood. On trigger: must move toward nearest wounded ally and make DC 14 WIS save or use action to Bite. Bite does 3d6 necrotic. Regeneration is 8 HP/turn |
| **25–1** | **Feral** | **50% chance per round** (coin flip) to be compelled toward wounded allies. WIS save DC 18. Gains +2 to STR and DEX. Advantage on attack rolls at night. Speech becomes fragmented and snarling. Allies have disadvantage on Persuasion checks to calm him |
| **0** | **Lost** | **Tearitus fully devolves.** Becomes a hostile NPC vampire under DM control. Can only be restored by Greater Restoration (or equivalent) within 24 hours. After 24 hours, the transformation is permanent |

### What Changes Sanity

| Action | Sanity Change |
|--------|---------------|
| Drinking blood (Bite on a living creature) | **−5 per bite** |
| Drinking blood willingly (feeding from willing creature) | **−10** |
| Killing by draining | **−20** |
| Resisting an urge (passing the WIS save when compelled) | **+2** |
| Long rest without feeding | **+5** |
| Receiving *Lesser Restoration* | **+10** |
| Receiving *Greater Restoration* | **+25** |
| Eating normal food (forced) | **+1** (uncomfortable, tastes like ash) |
| Being in a temple or holy ground | **+3 per hour** (burns slightly but stabilises the mind) |
| Witnessing an ally get badly hurt (bloodied) | **−3** (triggers hunger) |
| Drinking animal blood | **−2** (lesser impact, but still lowers) |

### Feeding Mechanics

When Tearitus feeds (uses Vampiric Bite on a living creature):
- He **heals** HP equal to necrotic damage dealt
- His Sanity **drops** (−5, −10 if willing, −20 if lethal)
- He gains a **combat buff for 1 hour:**
  - +1 to attack rolls
  - +1d6 necrotic damage to weapon attacks
  - Regeneration improves by +3 HP/turn
- These buffs **stack up to 3 times** (feeding 3 times in an hour)
- At 3 stacks: eyes glow red, fangs extend fully, all nearby NPCs become uneasy (disadvantage on CHA checks with non-vampires)

## The Core Tension

```
More Human ←————————————→ More Vampire
  (Weaker)                      (Stronger)
  (In Control)                  (Losing Control)
  (Slow Recovery)               (Fast Recovery)
  (Allies Trust Him)            (Allies Fear Him)
```

- **If he stays clean:** Weaker, rests take longer, no daytime vampire powers. But he keeps his mind and his allies' trust.
- **If he feeds:** Stronger, heals faster, combat buffs. But he's losing himself — and eventually his allies become food.
- **The daylight ring** is both a lifeline (walk in sun) and a cage (25% HP penalty). Removing it at night grants full power but all the weaknesses.
