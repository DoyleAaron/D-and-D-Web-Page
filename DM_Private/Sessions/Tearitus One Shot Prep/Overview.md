## Timeline Reconciliation

- **February 10, 1225 (Session 2):** Tearitus initially left the party after the Red Scarf basement horror. Paynr killed Nehinyathu (his best friend), pushing him away.
- **February 12–15, 1225 (Sessions 3–5):** Tearitus returned briefly, befriended Adolf Smitler in Braewood, and entered the Balthazar dungeon together. The party left **injured Smitler with the horse** at the dungeon entrance near Plalisle.

## Session Flow

### Act 1 — "Finding Smitler" (RP Heavy)

**Scene 1: The Barge**
- Tearitus arrives at a small river settlement near Plalisle
- Finds Smitler working at a tavern, washing dishes
- Smitler is apologetic but matter-of-fact: he returned the borrowed horse to the stablemaster as agreed, but **sold the party's leftover gear and supplies** to pay for food and lodging while stranded
- **Items sold:** Whatever the party left on the horse — DM can decide specifics or keep vague ("your pack, some rations, that rope, a few potions")
- Smitler offers to make it up by working with Tearitus
- **RP opportunity:** Is Tearitus angry? Understanding? This sets the tone

**Scene 2: Odd Jobs (Montage)**
- Quick montage: the two run small cons, pick pockets, do a minor burglary
- Let Dylan roll a few Sleight of Hand / Deception checks for fun
- Earn enough to get by — maybe 50–80 gp between them

---

### Act 2 — "New Blood" (RP + Light Combat)

**Scene 3: Rami and Alora**
- While casing a wealthy merchant, Tearitus and Smitler realize two others are casing the **same target**
- **Skill challenge:** Both pairs try to rob the merchant simultaneously. It goes sideways. They have to work together to escape the city watch
- Escape together → mutual respect → team up over drinks

**Scene 4: The Campfire**
- Night. The four of them camped outside town
- Tearitus, loosened by drink, talks about why he hates vampires — the noble estate, the ritual, the eyes
- **Rami speaks up:** "We know those eyes." He and Alora tell the story of their parents — a family of traveling merchants ambushed on the road to Darewaeton. The vampires fed on their parents and left the children alive as a message
- **Alora (quiet, bitter):** "They left us alive because children aren't worth the blood."
- This triggers the plan: they're going to **hunt vampires**
- Rami has heard rumors of where they nest — the volcanic manor above Darewaeton

---

### Act 3 — "Into the Mountain" (Dungeon Crawl)

**Scene 5: Approach**
- The four sneak up the volcanic slopes toward the manor
- **Stealth checks** (DC 14) to avoid patrols of regular vampires
- They pass the estate house where Liora was being held — through a window, they **witness the capture happening in real time** (Liora being dragged into the building by vampire guards)
  - This is a glimpse only — they cannot intervene without being overwhelmed
  - It establishes the stakes

**Scene 6: The Lair**
- They find a hidden entrance into the volcanic tunnels beneath the manor
- **Encounter 1: Patrol** — 3 Regular Vampires (Level 6) guarding a tunnel junction
  - Can be ambushed with good Stealth rolls
- **Encounter 2: The Ritual Chamber** — Empty except for eerie chanting echoing from deeper in
  - Arcana check (DC 15): This is a consecration to Asmodeus. The walls are warm from volcanic vents
- **Encounter 3: The Heart of Blood**
  - The chamber opens into a vaulted volcanic hall, lit entirely by the glow of a single object on a stone altar: a **large deep-red gemstone mounted in a golden cross** — the symbol of Asmodeus
  - Read-aloud:

    > *The cross is taller than a man. The gemstone at its centre is the size of a fist, dark red and pulsing with inner light as if something alive is trapped inside. The air around it is warm. The chanting is loudest here.*

  - **The Heart of Blood** — this is a fabricated artefact. The vampires have spent years planting rumours through people they knew were hunting them: *destroy the Heart of Blood and all vampires die.* It is a deliberate trap to lure hunters into exactly this room
  - The gemstone is real. The cross is real. It does **absolutely nothing**
  - **Investigation check (DC 20):** The gemstone is just a gemstone — magically inert. The gold cross is ornate but ordinary. There is no enchantment here
  - **Arcana check (DC 18):** No abjuration, no binding magic, no connection to any undead creature. It's a very expensive piece of jewellery
  - Most hunters never roll either check. They smash it immediately
  - **If the party attacks or destroys the Heart of Blood:** Nothing happens to any vampire. The moment it shatters, the doors seal. Father Maltrius's voice fills the room — calm, amused, theatrical

**Scene 7: The Ambush**
- **Father Maltrius** steps into the candlelight, flanked by **6 Purebloods**
- Read-aloud (if they destroyed the Heart of Blood):

> *A pause. Then laughter — low, genuine, and deeply satisfied.*
>
> *"Did it work? Did destroying my very expensive paperweight kill me?"*
>
> *Six figures emerge from the shadows. Pale skin, burning eyes, the stillness of creatures that haven't needed to breathe in centuries.*
>
> *Maltrius steps forward, spreading his arms wide, entirely unharmed.*
>
> *"We have been telling that story for forty years. You would be amazed how many hunters walk straight into this room and smash it before saying a single word. You, I must say, are not the first. You are simply the most recent."*
>
> *He smiles. The fangs are very visible.*
>
> *"Welcome to my lair. I'm afraid the tour ends here."*

- Read-aloud (if they did NOT destroy it — rarer, but possible):

> *"Did you think you could enter my house uninvited? How... deliciously ironic."*
>
> *Six figures emerge from the shadows behind him. Pale skin, burning eyes, the stillness of creatures that haven't needed to breathe in centuries.*
>
> *Maltrius smiles, fangs gleaming in the candlelight.*
>
> *"I've been watching you since the tunnel. Four little mice, come to hunt the cats."*

- **This fight is UNWINNABLE.** The purpose is narrative, not mechanical
- Rami, Alora, and Smitler fight bravely but are overwhelmed:
  - Rami goes down first — Serath Valen cuts him apart
  - Alora screams, heals him once, then Seraphina drains her
  - Smitler uses his last gas bomb, blinds two purebloods, but Maltrius dispels the cloud
  - Smitler is killed by Theron Valdris
- Tearitus is pinned. Maltrius bites him — **but is interrupted**

**Scene 8: The Interruption**
- A vampire guard bursts in:

> *"My Lord! The prisoner — Liora — she's been taken! The outsiders from Dawerton, they—"*

- Maltrius snarls. He drops Tearitus
- **"Throw him in the cells. I'll finish him later."**
- The purebloods scatter to deal with the crisis *(this is when the main party rescued Liora)*

**Scene 9: The Escape**
- Tearitus wakes in a dungeon cell, chained. He feels... wrong. His veins burn. His teeth ache
- A guard approaches — not hostile. Speaks quietly:

> *"I serve Lord Adrian and Lady Liora. Not this madman. There's a passage behind the third stone on the left wall. It leads to the sewers beneath Darewaeton. Go. Now. Before the others return."*

- The guard unlocks his chains and walks away
- Tearitus escapes into the night — but he has **turned**
