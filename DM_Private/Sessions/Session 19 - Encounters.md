## Encounter 1: Dockside Disturbance (Mandatory)

**Type:** Skill challenge with optional combat escalation  
**Location:** Adaham loading docks, near the salt-freight cranes  
**Trigger:** Within the first hour in Adaham. Happens regardless of party choice.

### Setup
A cargo crane snaps while offloading salt crates from a Lavalto trade run. One crate splits open and leaks **black brine** onto the dock — fish in the adjacent harbour water die within 30 seconds. Dock workers panic, a second crate is swinging loose overhead, and a crowd is forming fast.

### Party Goals
- Prevent injuries from the swinging crate (immediately)
- Contain the brine spill before it reaches the open harbour
- Identify what the brine is and where it came from

### Skill Challenge (6 Successes Before 3 Failures)

| Check | DC | Outcome |
|-------|----|---------|
| Athletics / Acrobatics | 14 | Catch or brace the swinging crate before it falls |
| Athletics | 12 | Drag the cracked crate back from the water's edge |
| Medicine / Nature | 15 | Identify corruption pattern (infernal seepage, same signature as Spehlina) |
| Arcana / Religion | 16 | Trace contamination to Lavalto-origin infernal runoff |
| Persuasion / Intimidation | 13 | Calm the crowd and prevent dock authority shutdown |

**Success (4+ checks):** Party earns dock trust. One free local contact, open access for departure prep, and a rumour about a Lavalto salt ship that left early before inspectors arrived.  
**Mixed (2–3 checks):** Some injuries, hard crowd, port authority restricts one departure prep action until party explains the brine.  
**Failure (3 failed checks):** Brine reaches harbour water. Port authority detains the party for questioning. Hard RP out; departure delayed.

### Combat Escalation (Optional — only if party is hostile to dock authority)
If the party refuses to cooperate with the port authority or tries to cover up the brine source, dock enforcers step in.

**Roll20 Setup:** 3 tokens (Dock Bruiser), 1 token (Dock Foreman). Flat dock terrain, 2 large crates as difficult terrain obstacles.

## Encounter 3: The False Lanterns

**Type:** Combat (boarding), sea travel  
**Location:** Open water between Adaham and the Spehlina's territory, night  
**Trigger:** Third or fourth night at sea. Party spots two lanterns in a signal pattern ahead — normal navigation beacon, except the rocks don't appear on Kirk's charts.

### Setup
A reef corsair crew has strung false beacon lanterns on an uninhabited shoal to draw ships onto the rocks, then board the damaged hulls. What the party doesn't know yet: this crew has been paid by someone to watch the Adaham sea lanes. Whether they're working for Kluimont, Maltrius's people, or just opportunists is unknown until the Reef Captain is questioned.

**If the party spots the deception (DC 14 Perception or Wisdom check from Kirk):** No surprise. Skill challenge to navigate without damage, then the corsair ship closes to board.  
**If the party fails:** Their ship takes 2d10 hull damage (narrative only unless DM wants structural rules) and is caught on the rocks at the start of combat. All party members make DC 12 DEX saves or start prone.

### Party Goal
- Repel boarders and/or board the corsair vessel
- Capture the Reef Captain alive — he may have intelligence on who hired them
- Prevent own ship from sinking (if on the rocks)

### DM Notes
- Captain's name: **Davan Fetch**. Lean, 50s, salt-greyed beard, missing two fingers on his left hand. He was paid in Kluimont minted coin (distinctive stamp — DC 14 History or Arcana to recognise it as 3rd-tier Synod mint).
- If Fetch is cornered, he gives up the payment fact voluntarily to avoid execution: *"Someone out of Kluimont wanted eyes on this lane. I don't ask names."*
- Corsairs fight until Fetch is downed or half their number are out. Then they scatter.

### Roll20 Setup
**Tokens:** 8× Reef Corsair, 2× Corsair Bosun, 1× Reef Captain (Davan Fetch)  
**Terrain:** Two ship decks connected by a boarding plank (or 10-ft gap if they haven't fully closed). Mast bases and coiled rope = difficult terrain patches. Night — dim light beyond 20 ft. unless lanterns or darkvision.  
**Initiative Note:** Corsairs who grapple-hooked onto the party ship begin on the party's deck. Bosuns and Fetch remain on the corsair deck until round 2 or 3.

## Encounter 4: The Hunted Courier

**Type:** Combat (interception), road travel  
**Location:** A tree-lined trade road, 2–3 hours from the nearest settlement  
**Trigger:** Party hears someone running hard on the road ahead, then crossbow fire from the treeline.

### Setup
A young courier named **Fen Aldwick** (early 20s, Islefield livery, terrified) is being run down by Pureblood Trackers — religious hunters in service to the network connected to Father Maltrius Kael. Aldwick is carrying encoded routing for the Kadena-Rye Summit: schedule, access confirmation, the lockdown perimeter. The Inquisitor leading the cell wants that document and wants no witnesses.

**Aldwick starts the encounter at 5 HP.** He is not a combatant. He dives behind a tree when the fight starts and stays there.

### Party Goal
- Survive and defeat or drive off the hunters
- Keep Aldwick alive
- Recover the document or let Aldwick keep it — his choice to share it or not afterwards

### DM Notes
- The encoded document is a prop. If the party earns Aldwick's trust (he rolls a Persuasion impression after combat, DC 13 CHA or kind words), he shares a copy of the routing.
- Intel from the document: summit is in exactly 28 days; bridge to be sealed 3 days prior; Kluimont delegation arrives by private ship, not public road.
- **Inquisitor (Brother Tavnen)** fights with cold professionalism. No speeches. If two Trackers fall, he withdraws to a pre-planned extraction point — he does not die for a document.
- The Bloodhounds attack Aldwick and Amdir specifically (they were given scent markers). Redirect them with a DC 13 Animal Handling check or by putting a different creature in their path.

### Roll20 Setup
**Tokens:** 2× Pureblood Tracker, 2× Bloodhound, 1× Inquisitor (Brother Tavnen), 1× Courier (Fen Aldwick, NPC non-combatant)  
**Terrain:** 40-ft wide road through sparse forest. Trees = half cover at 10 ft. distance; full cover at 5 ft. (adjacent). One ditch on the west side (difficult terrain, −10 ft. speed to cross). Trackers begin hidden in treeline (DC 15 Perception to spot before initiative).  
**Initiative Note:** Trackers start hidden — they roll Stealth vs. party Perception passively. Any party member with Passive Perception ≥ 15 spots movement before the ambush fires.

**Roleplaying:**  
No speeches. No posturing. If two Trackers fall, Tavnen calls off the hounds with a short sharp whistle and withdraws into the forest without a word. He is not afraid, just pragmatic. If captured (not likely — he won't let himself be taken), he says only: *"The mountain sees further than you."* He does not name Maltrius.
