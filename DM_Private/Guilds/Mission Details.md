## D-01: "The Cheating Merchant"

### Overview
**Client:** Merchant's Guild (Anonymous)  
**Location:** Dawerton – Market District  
**Duration:** 2-3 days  
**Reward:** 40 gp  
**Combat Expected:** Unlikely (social/investigation)

### The Job
A cloth merchant named **Yorvin Thatch** is suspected of falsifying quality certifications on imported silks. The Merchant's Guild wants proof before taking action. The party must investigate discreetly—if Yorvin catches wind of the investigation, he'll destroy evidence.

### Key NPCs

**Yorvin Thatch** – The Target
- Human male, 45, portly with ink-stained fingers
- Nervous, sweats when pressed, overcompensates with bluster
- Runs "Thatch's Fine Fabrics" in the Market District
- Has a back office where he keeps real records
- **Secret:** Has been relabeling cheap Lavalto cloth as expensive Islefield silk

**Mira Pence** – Shop Assistant
- Human female, 22, eager to please
- Doesn't know about the fraud but notices "odd shipments at night"
- Can be convinced to talk with Persuasion DC 12

**Guildmaster Orin Darrow** – Client Contact
- Dwarf male, 60, stern and by-the-book
- Meets party in a private room at The Silver Cup tavern
- Won't reveal who filed the anonymous complaint

### Investigation Hooks
1. **Observe the shop** – Notice customers complaining about fabric quality
2. **Talk to suppliers** – Learn shipments come from Lavalto, not Islefield
3. **Nighttime surveillance** – Catch Yorvin relabeling goods after hours
4. **Break into back office** – Find dual ledgers (one real, one fake)

### Evidence Required
- Proof of false labeling (relabeled cloth samples)
- The real ledger showing actual suppliers
- Witness testimony (optional but helpful)

### Possible Complications
- Yorvin has hired a **thug** (use Thug stat block) for "security"
- If confronted, Yorvin may try to bribe the party (100 gp to walk away)
- A rival merchant is also investigating—could become competitor or ally

### Rewards
- **40 gp** from Merchant's Guild
- **Bonus:** If party refuses bribe, +10 gp and future guild discount

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Market District map (daytime investigation scenes)
- [ ] Shop interior map (back office infiltration)
- [ ] Tokens: Yorvin Thatch, Mira Pence, Thug (1)
- [ ] Handout: Fake vs Real ledger entries

## D-02: "Warehouse Rats"

### Overview
**Client:** Grain Collective  
**Location:** Dawerton – Dock Warehouse #7  
**Duration:** 1 day  
**Reward:** 25 gp  
**Combat Expected:** Yes (straightforward extermination)

### The Job
Giant rats have infested a grain warehouse. The Grain Collective needs them cleared before the next shipment arrives tomorrow.

### The Warehouse
- Large wooden structure, 60x40 feet
- Grain sacks stacked 10 feet high in rows
- Dim lighting (torches at entrances only)
- Rat nest in the back corner behind collapsed sacks
- Basement access (locked trapdoor) where the **Rat King** lurks

### Enemies

**Giant Rats** – 10 total
- Use standard Giant Rat stat block
- Scattered in groups of 2-3 throughout warehouse
- Flee toward nest when reduced to half HP

**Dire Rat (Optional Boss)**
- If party investigates basement: 1 Dire Rat (use Giant Rat with 14 HP, +1 to hit)
- Surrounded by 4 more Giant Rats

### Encounter Layout
| Area | Rats | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Entrance | 2 | Flee immediately |
| Middle rows | 3 | Hiding in grain sacks |
| Back corner | 5 | Near nest, fight to death |
| Basement | 5 (optional) | Dire Rat + 4 Giant Rats |

### Key NPC

**Foreman Gregor Holt**
- Human male, 50, gruff dockworker
- Waiting outside, won't enter
- Provides torches and a club if needed
- "Kill 'em all, don't burn down my warehouse"

### Complications
- Grain sacks provide half cover
- Knocking over sacks creates difficult terrain
- Fire is forbidden (instant mission failure if warehouse burns)
- One rat carries a **small key** (leads to a locked chest in basement with 15 gp—bonus loot)

### Rewards
- **25 gp** from Grain Collective
- **Bonus:** 15 gp if basement chest found
- **Hidden:** Foreman offers repeat work if impressed

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Warehouse map (60x40, grain sack rows)
- [ ] Basement map (small 20x20 room)
- [ ] Tokens: Giant Rat x10, Dire Rat x1
- [ ] Dynamic lighting for dim warehouse

## D-03: "The Missing Shipment"

### Overview
**Client:** Wine Merchant Varros  
**Location:** Cleamond Road (between Dawerton and Cleamond)  
**Duration:** 3-5 days  
**Reward:** 50 gp + 2 bottles fine wine  
**Combat Expected:** Possible (bandits or wild animals)

### The Job
A wagon carrying 20 casks of fine Islefield wine never arrived from Cleamond. Varros suspects theft or bandit activity. The party must find the shipment and either recover it or discover what happened.

### Travel
- **Cleamond** is ~35km from Dawerton
- 1 day travel each way on foot
- Road passes through light woodland

### What Actually Happened
The wagon was attacked by **Redtooth Bandits**—a small gang operating in the woods. The driver (now dead) tried to flee off-road and crashed into a ravine. The bandits took what they could carry and left the rest.

### Investigation Timeline

**Day 1: Travel & Search**
- Party travels toward Cleamond
- Survival DC 12: Find wagon tracks leaving the road
- Following tracks leads to the crashed wagon

**The Crash Site**
- Wagon overturned in a 15-foot ravine
- Driver's body (human male, Denton) – arrow wounds
- 12 casks remain (8 broken, 4 intact)
- Tracks lead into the woods (Survival DC 14 to follow)

**Day 2-3: Tracking the Bandits**
- Trail leads to a small camp 2 miles off-road
- Bandits have the remaining 8 casks

### The Bandit Camp
- 3 tents around a fire pit
- 6 bandits total (use Bandit stat block)
- 1 Bandit Captain (use Bandit Captain stat block)
- Wine casks stacked near the largest tent

### Key NPCs

**Varros Windell** – Client
- Human male, 55, nervous merchant
- Offers party 2 bottles as "sampling" before they leave
- Genuinely worried about his investment

**Denton (deceased)**
- Driver's body has a letter to his wife in Cleamond
- Party can deliver it for additional reward (20 gp from grateful widow)

**Rekka "Redtooth"** – Bandit Captain
- Half-orc female, 30, filed teeth painted red
- Will negotiate if outnumbered
- Demands "toll" of 2 casks to let party take the rest
- Fights to the death if insulted

### Options
1. **Fight bandits** – Kill or capture, recover all wine
2. **Negotiate** – Give up 2 casks, avoid combat
3. **Stealth** – Sneak in at night, steal back wine
4. **Report location** – Return to Dawerton, let city guard handle it (half reward)

### Rewards
- **50 gp** + 2 bottles fine wine (worth 25 gp each)
- **Bonus:** 20 gp for delivering Denton's letter
- **Bonus:** Bandit Captain has 35 gp in personal loot

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Road travel map (forest path)
- [ ] Crash site map (ravine with overturned wagon)
- [ ] Bandit camp map (3 tents, fire pit, forest clearing)
- [ ] Tokens: Bandit x6, Bandit Captain x1
- [ ] Handout: Denton's letter to his wife

## D-04: "Haunted? Or Hoax?"

### Overview
**Client:** Innkeeper Darla Moss, The Rusty Anchor  
**Location:** Dawerton – Dockside District  
**Duration:** 1-2 nights  
**Reward:** 35 gp + free lodging  
**Combat Expected:** Possible (depends on investigation)

### The Job
Staff at The Rusty Anchor refuse to enter the cellar after dark. They report cold spots, whispers, objects moving, and the feeling of being watched. The innkeeper wants answers—ghost, prank, or something else.

### The Truth
There's no ghost. A **smuggler's tunnel** connects the cellar to the docks. Local thieves use it to move stolen goods at night. One thief, **Pip Hollow**, has been using minor magic (Prestidigitation via a stolen wand) to scare staff away.

### The Rusty Anchor
- Two-story inn, 15 rooms
- Ground floor tavern, kitchen, storage
- Cellar: wine racks, dry goods, old furniture, and a hidden trapdoor

### Investigation

**Talking to Staff**
- **Marta** (cook): "I heard voices at midnight. Speaking in tongues!"
  - Reality: Thieves speaking Thieves' Cant
- **Young Tom** (busboy): "Something grabbed my ankle!"
  - Reality: He tripped over a rope they forgot
- **Old Gerrick** (regular drunk): "I seen a ghost! Floatin' light!"
  - Reality: Pip's Dancing Lights from the wand

**Cellar Investigation**
- Investigation DC 12: Cold spots are draft from hidden trapdoor
- Investigation DC 14: Scuff marks on floor near wine racks
- Investigation DC 16: Find the hidden trapdoor behind false wall

**Stakeout (Night)**
- Thieves arrive around 2 AM
- 3 Bandits + Pip Hollow enter from tunnel
- Moving crates of stolen dockside goods

### Key NPCs

**Darla Moss** – Innkeeper
- Human female, 40, tired but kind
- Inherited inn from her mother
- "I don't care if it's ghosts or goblins—I need my cellar back"

**Pip Hollow** – Thief with Wand
- Halfling male, 25, cocky and quick
- Has a Wand of Prestidigitation (3 charges left)
- Will flee rather than fight if possible
- Knows location of thieves' guild contact (leverage)

### Combat (If Triggered)
- **Pip Hollow:** Use Spy stat block (fleeing priority)
- **Bandits (3):** Use Bandit stat block
- Pip uses wand to create distractions (loud noises, sparks) during escape

### Options
1. **Catch thieves in the act** – Ambush during night operation
2. **Seal the tunnel** – Report to city watch, collect reward
3. **Negotiate** – Pip offers 50 gp bribe + information on bigger thieves
4. **Take over operation** – Morally questionable but profitable

### Rewards
- **35 gp** from Darla
- **Free lodging** at The Rusty Anchor (ongoing)
- **Bonus:** Wand of Prestidigitation if Pip is captured
- **Bonus:** Thieves have 45 gp in stolen goods

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Rusty Anchor tavern map (ground floor)
- [ ] Cellar map with hidden trapdoor and tunnel entrance
- [ ] Tunnel map (optional, connects to docks)
- [ ] Tokens: Pip Hollow, Bandit x3
- [ ] Handout: Wand of Prestidigitation description

## C-01: "Noble Escort – Lady Meren Ashvale"

### Overview
**Client:** House Ashvale  
**Location:** Dawerton → Cleamond → Dawerton  
**Duration:** 4 days round trip  
**Reward:** 200 gp  
**Combat Expected:** Likely (ambush on road)

### The Job
**Lady Meren Ashvale** has received threatening letters and requires protection for her journey to Cleamond to meet with a trade partner. The party must keep her alive for the round trip.

### Travel Schedule
| Day | Route | Distance | Events |
|-----|-------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Dawerton → Cleamond | 35 km | Possible ambush |
| 2 | Cleamond (waiting) | — | Free time / investigation |
| 3 | Cleamond → Dawerton | 35 km | Possible ambush |
| 4 | Arrive Dawerton | — | Payment |

### The Threat
Lady Meren's former business partner, **Aldric Crane**, sent the threats. He lost a fortune when she exposed his embezzlement. He's hired mercenaries to kidnap her for ransom—or kill her if capture fails.

### Key NPCs

**Lady Meren Ashvale**
- Human female, 35, elegant but practical
- Sharp businesswoman, not easily frightened
- Carries a concealed dagger (will fight if cornered)
- Travels with personal servant **Tomas** (non-combatant)
- "I won't cower. But I'm not foolish enough to travel alone."

**Aldric Crane** – The Mastermind (not present)
- Human male, 50, bitter ex-merchant
- Hiding in Cleamond, won't directly participate
- If party investigates threats, can discover him

**Captain Vorn** – Mercenary Leader
- Human male, 38, scarred veteran
- Hired for 500 gp to capture Lady Meren
- Commands 4 mercenaries
- Professional—will retreat if job becomes too costly

### The Ambush (Day 1 or Day 3)
**Location:** Forested section of road, 15 km from either city  
**Trigger:** Fallen tree blocking road

**Enemies:**
- Captain Vorn (Veteran stat block)
- Mercenaries x4 (Guard stat block with shortswords)
- Archer x1 (Scout stat block, hidden in trees)

**Tactics:**
- Tree blocks road, forces stop
- Archers fire from cover
- Mercenaries emerge from both sides
- Vorn demands surrender: "Hand over the lady, walk away rich"
- If party fights, mercenaries focus on grabbing Lady Meren

**Victory Conditions:**
- Keep Lady Meren alive
- Defeat or drive off attackers
- Optional: Capture Vorn for information about Aldric

### Cleamond (Day 2)
- Lady Meren meets with trade partner (4 hours)
- Party has free time to:
  - Investigate threatening letters (leads to Aldric)
  - Shop in Cleamond market
  - Gather information about road dangers

**Optional Discovery:**
- Gather Information DC 15: Learn a "bitter merchant" has been hiring thugs
- Investigation DC 18: Trace letters to Aldric Crane's rented room
- If confronted, Aldric (Commoner) surrenders immediately

### Rewards
- **200 gp** from House Ashvale
- **Bonus:** 50 gp if Aldric Crane is exposed
- **Bonus:** Lady Meren becomes a contact for future work
- **Reputation:** +1 with Dawerton nobility

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Forest road ambush map (fallen tree, flanking positions)
- [ ] Cleamond street/market map (optional)
- [ ] Tokens: Lady Meren, Tomas, Captain Vorn, Mercenary x4, Scout x1
- [ ] Tokens: Aldric Crane (if confronted)
- [ ] Handout: Threatening letter sample

## C-02: "The Drowned Man's Secret"

### Overview
**Client:** City Watch (Unofficial)  
**Location:** Dawerton – Docks & Temple District  
**Duration:** 3-7 days  
**Reward:** 150 gp  
**Combat Expected:** Yes (cult encounter)

### The Job
A dock worker named **Hennik Solt** was found floating in the harbor with strange symbols carved into his chest. The Watch is overwhelmed and needs discreet investigators. The symbols appear ritualistic—possibly cult activity.

### The Truth
Hennik stumbled onto a small **cult of the Drowned God** operating in Dawerton. They worship an ancient sea entity and are preparing a ritual to "call the depths." Hennik saw too much and was silenced.

### Investigation Timeline

**Day 1-2: Gathering Information**

**The Body (Morgue)**
- Religion DC 14: Symbols are associated with deep-sea worship
- Medicine DC 12: Hennik was drowned *before* being carved
- Investigation DC 14: Salt residue in lungs—seawater, not harbor water

**The Docks (Hennik's Workplace)**
- Coworkers say Hennik was nervous lately, talking about "secret meetings"
- Persuasion DC 13: He mentioned seeing "hooded figures" near **Warehouse 13**
- One coworker, **Delly**, knows more but is terrified (Persuasion DC 16)

**Warehouse 13**
- Abandoned, doors chained
- Investigation DC 12: Chains are theatrical—opens from inside
- Inside: Empty crates, fish smell, hidden trapdoor to flooded basement

**Day 3-4: Finding the Cult**

**The Flooded Basement**
- Ankle-deep seawater (difficult terrain)
- Shrine to the Drowned God (barnacle-encrusted altar)
- Evidence of rituals (candles, blood, fish offerings)
- Investigation DC 15: Find cult meeting schedule—next gathering in 2 days

**Day 5-6: The Ritual**

**Cult Gathering** (Night, Warehouse 13)
- 6 Cultists (use Cultist stat block)
- 1 Cult Fanatic (leads the ritual)
- They're attempting to summon a minor sea horror

**The Ritual:**
- If interrupted before completion: Cultists fight
- If allowed to complete: Summons 1 **Merrow** (dangerous!)
- Cult Fanatic will sacrifice himself to complete ritual if desperate

### Key NPCs

**Sergeant Kella Bryne** – Watch Contact
- Human female, 35, pragmatic investigator
- Provides access to body and basic info
- Pays upon proof of cult's destruction

**Delly Marsh** – Dock Worker
- Human female, 28, knows Hennik's secret
- She was his lover; he told her about the cult
- Terrified but can identify cult leader if promised protection

**Brother Ondrus** – Cult Fanatic Leader
- Human male, 45, formerly a fisherman
- Lost his family to a storm, now serves "the depths"
- Believes drowning is "returning to the true god"
- Fights fanatically, won't surrender

### Combat: Cult Gathering

**Enemies:**
- Brother Ondrus (Cult Fanatic)
- Cultists x6 (Cultist stat block)
- **Optional:** Merrow (if ritual completes)

**Environment:**
- Flooded basement (difficult terrain)
- Dim light (torches and candles)
- Altar can be destroyed to disrupt ritual

### Rewards
- **150 gp** from City Watch
- **Bonus:** 25 gp if cult leader captured alive
- **Evidence:** Ritual texts worth 50 gp to Temple of the Wavecaller
- **Dark Option:** Cult's ritual dagger (magic, +1, deals extra 1d4 necrotic to drowning victims)

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Docks investigation map (Warehouse 13 exterior)
- [ ] Flooded basement cult lair map
- [ ] Tokens: Cultist x6, Cult Fanatic x1, Merrow x1 (optional)
- [ ] Handout: Symbol carved on victim (drawing)
- [ ] Handout: Cult meeting schedule

## C-03: "The Sculptor's Debt"

### Overview
**Client:** Private (Sealed Letter from **Madame Elara Vance**)  
**Location:** Dawerton – Tidewatch Slums  
**Duration:** 2-4 days  
**Reward:** 175 gp  
**Combat Expected:** Likely (hired thugs)

### The Job
A marble statue was stolen by **Corvin Ashby**, a debtor who fled to the Tidewatch Slums. The party must retrieve the statue intact—it cannot be damaged. The client, Madame Vance, is a wealthy art collector.

### The Statue
- 3 feet tall, white marble
- Depicts a weeping woman
- Worth 500 gp (why Corvin stole it)
- **Secret:** Contains hidden compartment with love letters between Madame Vance and a married noble—the real reason she wants it back

### The Tidewatch Slums
- Poorest district of Dawerton
- Narrow alleys, leaning buildings, hostile residents
- Outsiders are noticed immediately
- Local gang: **The Mudlarks** control the area

### Key NPCs

**Madame Elara Vance** – Client
- Human female, 55, wealthy widow
- Calm, refined, but hiding desperation
- Will pay extra for discretion
- "The statue has... sentimental value. I need it returned without questions."

**Corvin Ashby** – The Debtor
- Human male, 30, former servant in Vance household
- Stole statue to pay gambling debts
- Hiding with his cousin in the slums
- Has hired 3 Mudlark thugs for protection
- Cowardly—will surrender if thugs are defeated

**Vella Ashby** – Corvin's Cousin
- Human female, 35, tough Tidewatch local
- Doesn't know statue's true value
- Can be bribed or convinced to give up Corvin's location

**Grim Hadley** – Mudlark Boss
- Half-orc male, 40, runs protection rackets
- Corvin paid 20 gp for 3 thugs
- Doesn't care about Corvin—only money
- Can be negotiated with (pay 30 gp, he withdraws protection)

### Finding Corvin

**Option 1: Ask Around**
- Gather Information DC 14: Learn Corvin is staying with cousin
- Gather Information DC 16: Get exact address

**Option 2: Bribe Locals**
- 10 gp to street urchins: "Saw a nervous man with something heavy, went to Vella's place"

**Option 3: Track from Entry**
- Survival DC 15: Follow wagon tracks to Vella's building

### Corvin's Hideout
- Second floor of crumbling tenement
- One entrance (front), one window (back alley)
- Statue wrapped in blankets in corner

**Inside:**
- Corvin (Commoner, cowering)
- Thugs x3 (Thug stat block)
- Vella (Commoner, angry but not fighting)

### Combat (If Triggered)
- Thugs fight but won't die for 20 gp
- If 2 thugs drop, the third surrenders
- Corvin begs for mercy, offers to reveal statue's secret for freedom
- **Critical:** If fighting near statue, Dexterity saves or risk damage

### Statue Secret (Optional Discovery)
- Investigation DC 14 on statue: Notice seam in base
- Opening reveals: Love letters between Elara Vance and **Lord Henric Dunmore** (married noble)
- **Leverage:** Could blackmail either party
- **Good Path:** Return letters sealed, Madame Vance adds 50 gp bonus

### Rewards
- **175 gp** from Madame Vance
- **Bonus:** 50 gp for returning letters without reading
- **Dark Option:** Blackmail potential (100+ gp, but creates enemies)
- **Bonus:** If Corvin captured, 25 gp from city watch for theft

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Tidewatch Slums alley map
- [ ] Tenement building interior (Corvin's hideout)
- [ ] Tokens: Corvin Ashby, Vella Ashby, Thug x3
- [ ] Handout: The weeping woman statue description
- [ ] Handout: Love letters (optional reveal)

## C-04: "Beast of the Southern Road"

### Overview
**Client:** Caravan Collective  
**Location:** Southern Road (Dawerton to Mortling Stronghold)  
**Duration:** 5-7 days  
**Reward:** 300 gp  
**Combat Expected:** Yes (monster hunt)

### The Job
Something is attacking caravans on the road to Mortling Stronghold. Three attacks in two weeks. Survivors describe it as "wolf-like but wrong." The Caravan Collective wants it dead.

### The Beast
A **Dire Wolf** corrupted by residual magic from an old battlefield. It's larger than normal, partially phased, and hunts with unnatural intelligence. It's not evil—just territorial and starving after its pack was killed.

### Travel
- **Mortling Stronghold** is ~33 km from Dawerton
- Road passes through rocky hills and sparse woodland
- Attack zone is 15-20 km from Dawerton

### Investigation

**Talking to Survivors** (Dawerton)
- Caravan survivor **Hennis**: "It came from nowhere. Like mist. Killed two horses before we could blink."
- Guard survivor **Koltar**: "I hit it with my sword—went right through! But it bled when Mira shot it with an arrow."

**Key Clues:**
- Beast is partially incorporeal (resistance to nonmagical melee)
- Still vulnerable to ranged attacks and magic
- Attacks at dusk when visibility is poor
- Targets horses first (food source)

### Tracking the Beast

**Day 1-2: Travel and Search**
- Reach attack zone
- Survival DC 14: Find massive paw prints, larger than normal wolf
- Survival DC 16: Tracks lead to rocky outcrop den

**The Battlefield Residue**
- Nature/Arcana DC 15: The area has old magical scarring
- 50 years ago, a battle with summoned creatures occurred here
- Residual planar energy corrupted local wildlife

### The Beast's Den
- Rocky outcrop with cave entrance
- Bones scattered outside (horses, deer, one human)
- Den is 30 feet deep, narrow entrance

### The Phase Wolf (Modified Dire Wolf)

**Phase Wolf**  
*Large monstrosity, neutral*

**AC:** 14 (natural armor)  
**HP:** 52 (5d10 + 25)  
**Speed:** 50 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 18 (+4) | 15 (+2) | 16 (+3) | 6 (-2) | 14 (+2) | 8 (-1) |

**Skills:** Perception +4, Stealth +6  
**Damage Resistances:** Bludgeoning, piercing, slashing from nonmagical melee attacks  
**Senses:** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 14

**Traits:**
- **Phase Shift (Recharge 5-6):** As a bonus action, become incorporeal until start of next turn. Can move through creatures and objects. Takes 1d10 force damage if ends turn inside an object.
- **Pack Tactics:** Advantage on attacks if ally within 5 ft (no longer applies—pack is dead)
- **Keen Hearing and Smell:** Advantage on Perception checks using hearing or smell

**Actions:**
- **Bite:** +6 to hit, 2d6+4 piercing. Target must succeed DC 13 Strength save or be knocked prone.

**Tactics:**
- Uses Phase Shift to close distance or escape
- Targets horses/mounts first
- Flees at half HP, returns to den
- If cornered in den, fights to death

### Optional: Non-Lethal Solution
- Animal Handling DC 18: Calm the beast
- If fed properly and planar corruption cleansed (Remove Curse), could be saved
- Druid or ranger might recognize it's not evil
- Cleansing requires magic the party may not have

### Rewards
- **300 gp** from Caravan Collective
- **Bonus:** Beast's pelt worth 100 gp
- **Bonus:** Phase Wolf fang (alchemical component, 50 gp)
- **Hidden:** Battlefield site has buried soldier with 75 gp in old coins

### Roll20 Prep Checklist
- [ ] Rocky hills road map (ambush terrain)
- [ ] Cave den map (30 ft tunnel, narrow)
- [ ] Token: Phase Wolf (custom dire wolf token with mist effect)
- [ ] Handout: Survivor testimonies
- [ ] Handout: Phase Wolf stat block (for DM reference)
