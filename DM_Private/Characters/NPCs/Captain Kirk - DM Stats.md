# Captain Kirk – DM Reference & Full Statistics

## 🎭 Character Overview

**Captain Kirk** is a temporary PC for the Spehlina arc. He is a retired naval captain with a secret pact with a neutral water deity.

### Key Story Points (DM Only)
- His daughter **Marina** was kidnapped by **Captain Iona**, a menacing pirate who roams the Rimduff Isles
- The player does NOT know who took her—this is a mystery to be solved
- Kirk wants to **save** the Spehlina, not kill it
- His true power awakens on the water
- He has a nuclear option: full transformation into **Hestaria**

### Background Summary
- Descended from the **Pirate Queen, Ruler of the Reef Confederacy**
- Was the captain of the **King's personal ship and fleet**
- Now retired, lives in **Adaham**
- Adopted daughter rescued from raiders (was a slave)
- Saved by the Spehlina when he was younger (before corruption)
- Made a pact with **Zephyrion, the Indifferent Tide** (neutral water deity)
- Sacrificed: one eye, his manhood, his soul after death

## 👁️ THE EYE – Zephyrion's Mark

Kirk's sacrificed eye is now a direct conduit to his patron, **Zephyrion, the Indifferent Tide**.

### Communication (No Action Cost)
- Kirk can remove his eyepatch and attempt to speak with Zephyrion
- **Zephyrion may or may not respond**—he is indifferent and capricious
- If he responds, his advice is cryptic and detached
- Roll d20: on 15+, Zephyrion responds; otherwise, silence

### The Trump Card – "I Allow Complete Surrender"

If Kirk removes his eyepatch and speaks these exact words, he triggers his **Final Transformation**.

**Consequences:**
- Kirk transforms into **Hestaria** (see separate stat block)
- The transformation lasts **10 minutes**
- After the 10 minutes, **Kirk dies**—his soul is collected by Zephyrion
- This cannot be undone by any means short of divine intervention
- Kirk knows this is fatal

**When to Use:**
- Only if the fight against Spehlina is going catastrophically wrong
- Kirk will not use this lightly—he wants to live
- This is the "pull in case of TPK" lever

## 🦑 HESTARIA – The Tide Made Flesh

*What Kirk becomes when he fully surrenders to Zephyrion*

---

### Lore

**Hestaria** is the manifestation of Zephyrion's power channeled through a mortal vessel. The vessel's soul and personality shape the form—Kirk's sense of duty and protective nature creates a guardian-type sea monster rather than a predator.

**Appearance:**
- 40 feet long, serpentine body with Kirk's features barely visible in the face
- Scales that shift between deep blue and storm gray
- Multiple fin-like limbs that can function as arms
- Eyes glow with pale, emotionless light—one is Kirk's remaining eye, the other is Zephyrion's socket blazing with power
- Water constantly swirls around the form
- Voice echoes with two tones: Kirk's and something ancient

---

### Stat Block

**Hestaria, Avatar of the Indifferent Tide**  
*Huge elemental (transformed humanoid), true neutral*

**Armor Class:** 19 (natural armor)  
**Hit Points:** 230  
**Speed:** 20 ft., swim 80 ft.

---

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| 24 (+7) | 18 (+4) | 22 (+6) | 10 (+0) | 16 (+3) | 20 (+5) |

---

**Saving Throws:** Str +12, Con +11, Wis +8, Cha +10  
**Damage Resistances:** Bludgeoning, Piercing, Slashing from nonmagical attacks  
**Damage Immunities:** Cold, Poison  
**Condition Immunities:** Exhaustion, Frightened, Paralyzed, Poisoned  
**Senses:** Blindsight 60 ft., Darkvision 120 ft., passive Perception 18  
**Languages:** Common, Primordial  
**Challenge:** 17 (18,000 XP)

---

### Traits

**Legendary Resistance (2/Day).** If Hestaria fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**The Mortal Anchor.** Hestaria retains Kirk's alignment and memories. It will not attack allies and will prioritize protecting them. However, its methods may be more brutal than Kirk would normally allow.

**Tidal Presence.** The water within 60 feet of Hestaria is difficult terrain for enemies. Allies are unaffected.

**Time Limit.** Hestaria exists for exactly 10 minutes. When the time expires, Kirk dies and his body floats to the surface.

**Water Form.** Hestaria can move through spaces as narrow as 1 inch without squeezing while in water.

---

### Actions

**Multiattack.** Hestaria makes three attacks: one Bite and two Tail Lashes. It can replace one attack with Drowning Grasp.

**Bite.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +12 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.  
*Hit:* 25 (4d8 + 7) piercing damage plus 14 (4d6) cold damage.

**Tail Lash.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +12 to hit, reach 20 ft., one target.  
*Hit:* 20 (3d8 + 7) bludgeoning damage. Target must succeed on DC 18 Strength save or be knocked prone.

**Drowning Grasp.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +12 to hit, reach 15 ft., one creature.  
*Hit:* Target is grappled (escape DC 18). While grappled, the target cannot breathe and takes 21 (6d6) cold damage at the start of each of Hestaria's turns.

**Maelstrom (Recharge 5-6).** Hestaria creates a 40-foot-radius whirlpool centered on itself. All creatures in the area must make a DC 18 Strength saving throw. On a failure, they take 36 (8d8) bludgeoning damage, are pulled 20 feet toward Hestaria, and are restrained until the end of their next turn. On a success, half damage and not pulled or restrained.

**Zephyrion's Judgment (1/Transformation).** Hestaria calls upon its patron for one devastating strike. One creature within 60 feet must make a DC 18 Constitution saving throw. On a failure, the target takes 55 (10d10) cold damage and is stunned for 1 minute (save ends at end of each turn). On a success, half damage and not stunned. This attack ignores resistance to cold damage.

---

### Legendary Actions (2/Round)

**Surge.** Hestaria moves up to half its swim speed without provoking opportunity attacks.

**Tail Strike.** Hestaria makes one Tail Lash attack.

**Tidal Pull (Costs 2 Actions).** Each creature within 30 feet must succeed on a DC 18 Strength save or be pulled 15 feet toward Hestaria.

## ⚔️ COMBAT BALANCE NOTES

### Spehlina Fight Analysis

**Spehlina Stats:**
- CR 15, 312 HP, AC 16
- Legendary Resistance 3/day
- Legendary Actions 3/round
- Death Throes on death

**Party (Assumed Level 7-8):** ~3-4 members, mixed capabilities

**Kirk (Water Form):**
- Effective CR ~10-11
- 104 HP, AC 18
- 3 attacks at +10 for 2d8+8+2d6 each
- Riptide Strike and Tidal Wave for AoE

### Expected Balance

**Without Hestaria:**
- The fight should be **very hard but winnable** with:
  - Good preparation (potions, water breathing, strategy)
  - Kirk's water form contribution
  - Smart use of the environment
  - Managing legendary actions
- Expected to burn most resources, possible character deaths if reckless

**With Hestaria (Emergency):**
- Turns the fight decisively in the party's favor
- Hestaria can tank and deal significant damage
- The 10-minute timer creates urgency
- Kirk's sacrifice should feel meaningful, not wasted

### Recommended Adjustments
- If the party is higher level (9+), boost Spehlina HP to 350-380
- If the party struggles significantly, have Kirk enter water to gain his enhanced form earlier
- The Hestaria option should only come up if 2+ party members are down or Kirk is desperate

## 🎬 INTRODUCTION SCENE

### At the Wayfarers' Accord

When the party arrives at the guild:

> *An older man in a weathered naval coat is arguing with Yenna Cole at the counter. He wears an eyepatch over his left eye and carries a curved sword at his hip. Despite his age, he carries himself with the authority of someone used to being obeyed.*
>
> **Kirk:** "We don't have time for your evaluation process! My daughter's been missing for over a week—she was supposed to be in Quey days ago!"
>
> **Yenna:** "Sir, I understand your urgency, but the Accord has procedures—"
>
> **Kirk:** "Damn your procedures! I'll pay two thousand gold to anyone who helps me find her. Right now."

**If the party intervenes:**
- Kirk will size them up quickly (old military habit)
- If they seem capable, he'll accept their help immediately
- He's desperate but not stupid—he'll ask what they can do

**If the party doesn't intervene:**
- Kirk will notice them and approach directly
- "You lot. You're adventurers, aren't you? I have gold and a job. Interested?"

### Key Information Kirk Will Share
- Daughter: sailor, adopted, was heading to Quey
- Never arrived, no contact (extremely unlike her)
- He's from Adaham, retired navy captain
- Offering 2,000 gp for her safe return

### Information Kirk Will NOT Share (Initially)
- His pact with Zephyrion
- His connection to the Spehlina
- His Pirate Queen heritage
- The nature of his enhanced abilities
