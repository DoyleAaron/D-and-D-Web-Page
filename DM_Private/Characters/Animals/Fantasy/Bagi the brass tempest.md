# Bagi, the Brass Tempest — from free oath-keeper to bound storm

## Before the kingdoms (pre-900s C.C.)

In the long, messy centuries after the Tharnic Empire fell and the mainland fractured into feuding fiefs, the western seas were held together more by custom than crowns. A talkative brass wyrm began policing those customs. Sailors learned his name—**Bagi**—and his three rules for safe waters: keep your oaths, keep fair tolls, and keep fireside hospitality for the desperate. This “lighthouse law” quietly safeguarded the capes and coves that would later feed into **Kluimont’s** maritime rise. Over these same centuries, the island enclaves that would become Kluimont coalesced around arcane leaders and sea-lords, seeking secure trade lanes against pirates and storms.

## The island that would be Kluimont (900–975 C.C.)

As the **three kingdoms** took shape, the northwestern isles leaned into wards and seamanship. Kluimont’s proto-navy and wizard councils valued any pact that kept routes open; Bagi’s code fit perfectly. When Kluimont finally emerges as a distinct realm, it invests in arcane protection for shipping while mainland powers bicker—again aligning with Bagi’s “open lanes, honest tolls” stance.

## The Rimdiff era & tightening secrecy (975–1005 C.C.)

By **975 C.C.**, Kluimont reasserts control over the **Rimdiff Isles**, tamping down pirate independence and formalizing fees and escorts. Bagi continues to intervene against slavers and oath-breakers along the reefs north of **Qrey** (the northern harbor city), and his reputation as a conversational but immovable arbiter spreads. Kluimont’s prestige rises—but so does its taste for keeping arcane advantages quiet.

## The pact that soured (1101–1109 C.C.)

Around **1101 C.C.**, rumors begin: an **ancient brass dragon** is “subdued” somewhere in Kluimont’s **western old-growth**—not slain, but caught in a net of names, stones, and sigils. Official denials follow even as odd experiments multiply and the arcane academies **surge** by **1105**. Scholars from abroad are let in under strict protocols; a Braewood diplomat is **expelled** in **1109**, allegedly for snooping near “the site.” The story that fits: Kluimont’s rulers cut a baited oath with Bagi and then **looped his breath and will into a ward-grid**, turning a guardian into a living battery. Publicly, they called it clever wardcraft. Privately, the **Arcane Synod** discovered they could drive devices off a dragon’s heart-beat.

From Bagi’s side, the betrayal is simple: they converted hospitality into leverage. He was **chained in place**—not by iron bars but by **meteoric standing stones, clockwork timings, and bound names**—and the forest itself was taught to sing the refrain that kept him compliant. Over **a century** has passed since those bindings settled in; his temper is thin now, but not lost to hatred.

## A century of “quiet” progress (1110–1190 C.C.)

Kluimont’s **secrecy and arcane boom** reshape the balance of power: the kingdom presents as modest militarily while quietly becoming terrifying when its projects wake up. Ports like **Qrey** grow into well-warded hubs; smuggling and black-market reagent routes blossom in the shadows to feed Synod experiments. Through it all, Bagi’s power hums under the western forest—felt in compasses that drift, in copper that greens overnight, in the hush before a storm that isn’t purely natural.

Meanwhile elsewhere, the world lurches: **Darewaeton** on Lavalto Isle suffers the **Night of Red Embers** (**1191 C.C.**), and Kluimont quietly opens trade for volcanic reagents (**1195**). These external pressures only harden the Synod’s instinct: keep the island’s trump cards secret—especially the bound dragon in the woods.

## The Storm of Unbound Chains (1220 C.C.)

Bagi never stopped working at the knots. Through patient, lawyerly draconic magic he taught oaks, stones, and way-markers to **mispronounce the binding names**. In **1220 C.C.**, enough failure cascaded at once to crack the air: an **arcane storm** ripped the sky above the western forest for **three nights**, scorching fields near Qrey and forcing evacuations. Kluimont’s mages contained it, but the event made the rumor undeniable—the “subdued brass dragon” was **still thinking** and **still fighting**. The High Archon doubled the lockdown and scrubbed records while quietly ramping research to prevent a second breach.

## Present day (1225 C.C.)

Bagi is wary, quick-sparked, yet **not consumed by hate**. He still honors his old rules with a narrower patience:

- **Oaths matter.** Swear truth under stormclouds, and he will believe you; break it, and even the wind turns against you.
    
- **Hospitium stands.** Those who share food and signal peace in his woods get a warning roar before wrath. Pirates, slavers, and forgers of toll-flags get none.
    
- **No monopoly on the sea.** He wants the aether grid severed from his heartbeats and replaced with a **clean oath**: lanes open, tolls honest, research on dragon-derived magic **published** instead of hoarded. Kluimont’s elites—whose power rests on secrecy and arcane commerce—are not eager to oblige.
    

### What everyone else “officially” says

Kluimont insists the western forest has **“fey acoustics”** and that the 1220 incident was a freak phenomenon. Unofficially, the **High Archon** maintains the clampdown, because if Bagi truly breaks free (or the truth about his bondage breaks wide), the island’s careful balance with Braewood’s armies and Islefield’s wealth could **snap**.

## How to use Bagi in play (quick hooks)

- **The New Lighthouse-Oath.** PCs broker terms to detach the grid from Bagi in exchange for him resuming open-sea guardianship—politics in Kluimont, pirates in Rimdiff, and a vault that stores the binding names.
    
- **Storm Grammar.** After a squall, spiral scorch-script appears in the woods; reading it safely requires bargaining with the dragon through a ward-stone. Failure risks a local “mini-storm” echo of **1220**.
    
- **Smugglers & Secrets.** Synod hardliners plan to **increase** the siphon to meet demand for devices. Bagi starts sinking only ships flying **forged toll-flags**—the party must expose the forgery ring or watch a blockade form around Qrey.
