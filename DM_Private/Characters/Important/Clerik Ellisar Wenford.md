## 🧾 **Name:** _Clerk Ellisar Wrenford_

**Race/Class:** Human Commoner (expert in Bureaucracy / Scribe template)  
**Age:** 68  
**Role:** Senior Administrative Clerk of the Crown’s Vault-Ledger Office — responsible for recording, stamping, and tracking all **magical item loans**, **quartermaster chits**, and **curio assignments** for the Queen’s free contractors.

---

### 👤 **Character Description**

Ellisar is a stooped, narrow-framed man with a gentle face and watery blue eyes. His once-dark hair has faded to wisps of silver tied back with a frayed ribbon. He wears a simple navy court robe, ink-stained at the cuffs, and carries a brass-stamped ledger chain at his belt — a mark of his authority to sign for crown property.

He speaks softly and avoids eye contact, often wringing his hands or adjusting his spectacles. To outsiders he seems harmless — just another weary bureaucrat — but when pressed, his quill hand trembles slightly and his eyes dart to the guards at the door.


### 💬 **Personality and Motivations**

- **Loyal by nature, but broken by fear.**  
    Once proud of his precision, Ellisar now lives in quiet terror. Myrelle’s cell threatened his **daughter, Mirra**, who works as a shipping ledger assistant in the merchant quarter.
    
- **Nervous and conflicted.**  
    He believes Myrelle when she says the “artefact diversion” is temporary and sanctioned by “higher powers.” But every day, it eats at him.
    
- **Habits that betray guilt:** Overexplains, double-checks seals that don’t need checking, scribbles corrections over old stamps, sweats when magical items are mentioned.

### 🕵️‍♂️ **How He Betrays the Queen**

Ellisar’s position grants him **exclusive access** to the magical-artefact intake ledgers and distribution forms — perfect for quietly diverting relics from the Crown’s custody to Myrelle’s buyers under the guise of “loaner returns” or “clerical disposal.”

He doesn’t steal himself — he **adjusts paperwork** to make the thefts invisible. Myrelle’s agents handle the physical handoffs.
