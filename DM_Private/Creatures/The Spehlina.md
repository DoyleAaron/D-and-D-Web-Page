# The Spehlina – Corrupted Guardian of Lavalto Lake

## Lore

### What It Once Was

The **Spehlina** (pronounced *speh-LEE-nah*) is an ancient species of aquatic guardian, found only in volcanic lake ecosystems. For millennia, these gentle giants served as living water purifiers—massive, bioluminescent creatures that drift through lake waters absorbing toxins, pollutants, and harmful magical residue.

**Natural Appearance (Uncorrupted):**
- Resembled a vast jellyfish crossed with a manta ray
- Translucent blue-white flesh with soft internal glow
- Tendrils that sifted water like a whale's baleen
- Eyes like moonstones—gentle, watchful
- Size: Up to 40 feet across, 15 feet tall

The Spehlina of Lavalto Lake had protected those waters for over 800 years. Fishermen considered it sacred. Villages left offerings of flowers at the shore. It was never seen as a threat—only a blessing.

---

### The Corruption

When **Father Maltrius Kael** established his domain in the Lavalto volcanic range, his worship of **Asmodeus** began to seep into the land. Not through direct action, but through the slow, inevitable bleed of infernal energy. The mountain's volcanic vents, once purely natural, became conduits for something darker.

**The Corruption Process:**
1. Infernal essence began seeping into the volcanic springs feeding the lake
2. The Spehlina, doing what it always did, absorbed the corruption
3. For decades, it contained the evil—growing sicker, angrier, but holding
4. Eventually, the corruption exceeded its capacity
5. The guardian became the monster

**Timeline:**
- ~50 years ago: Father Maltrius Kael arrives in Lavalto
- ~40 years ago: First signs of corruption in the lake
- ~30 years ago: Spehlina begins behaving erratically
- ~15 years ago: First ship attacks begin
- Present: A nightmare haunts the waters

---

### Current State

The Spehlina is now a **twisted horror**—a creature in constant agony, driven mad by the evil festering inside it. It attacks not from malice but from pain. Every ship, every creature, every *thing* in the water feels like another source of corruption to absorb, another torment to endure.

**Corrupted Appearance:**
- Flesh has turned from translucent blue to **murky crimson-black**
- Bioluminescence now pulses **sickly orange-red** like volcanic veins
- Tendrils have become **barbed and necrotic**
- Multiple eyes have grown—**bloodshot, weeping ichor**
- Size has increased: **60 feet across, 25 feet tall**
- Constantly oozes dark corruption into the water around it

---

### The Terrible Dilemma

**If the Spehlina is killed:**
- The corruption it has absorbed for decades will be **released**
- The infernal essence still seeping from Maltrius's domain will have **nothing to contain it**
- The lake will become **toxic within months**
- Shores touching the lake (Dawerton, parts of Lavalto, fishing villages) will suffer
- Wildlife will die or mutate
- The water will become undrinkable
- **Long-term:** A creeping blight spreads across the region

**True Solution (Optional Discovery):**
- Purify the source (destroy Maltrius or seal the infernal vents)
- Then either:
  - Cleanse the Spehlina with powerful divine magic (Greater Restoration + Remove Curse + Ceremony, all at once, requiring multiple casters)
  - Or mercy-kill it AFTER the source is sealed, minimizing release

**The party likely won't know this.** The guild certainly doesn't.

### Traits

**Corruption Aura.** Any creature that starts its turn within 30 feet of the Spehlina while in water must succeed on a DC 17 Constitution saving throw or take 14 (4d6) necrotic damage and gain one level of exhaustion. On a success, the creature is immune to this effect for 24 hours.

**Legendary Resistance (3/day).** If the Spehlina fails a saving throw, it can choose to succeed instead.

**Death Throes.** When the Spehlina dies, it releases all absorbed corruption. Every creature within 60 feet must succeed on a DC 18 Constitution saving throw or take 55 (10d10) necrotic damage and be poisoned for 1 hour. On a success, half damage and not poisoned. The water within 1 mile becomes corrupted (see lore).

**Amphibious.** The Spehlina can breathe air and water.

**Siege Monster.** The Spehlina deals double damage to objects and structures.

---

### Actions

**Multiattack.** The Spehlina makes three attacks: one with its slam and two with its tendrils. It can replace one tendril attack with Corrupted Spray.

**Slam.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 15 ft., one target. *Hit:* 28 (4d10 + 6) bludgeoning damage. If the target is a ship or structure, it takes 56 damage instead.

**Tendril.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 30 ft., one target. *Hit:* 19 (3d8 + 6) bludgeoning damage plus 10 (3d6) necrotic damage. The target is grappled (escape DC 17). The Spehlina can grapple up to 6 creatures.

**Corrupted Spray (Recharge 5-6).** The Spehlina releases a 60-foot cone of tainted water. Each creature in the area must make a DC 17 Dexterity saving throw, taking 36 (8d8) necrotic damage on a failed save, or half as much on a successful one. Creatures that fail are blinded until the end of their next turn.

**Swallow.** *Melee Weapon Attack:* +11 to hit, reach 5 ft., one Large or smaller creature the Spehlina is grappling. *Hit:* The creature is swallowed. A swallowed creature is blinded and restrained, has total cover from attacks outside the Spehlina, and takes 21 (6d6) acid damage plus 14 (4d6) necrotic damage at the start of each of the Spehlina's turns.

If the Spehlina takes 40 damage or more on a single turn from a creature inside it, it must succeed on a DC 20 Constitution saving throw or regurgitate all swallowed creatures. If the Spehlina dies, swallowed creatures are no longer restrained and can escape using 20 feet of movement.

---

### Legendary Actions

The Spehlina can take 3 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Spehlina regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.

**Tendril Strike.** The Spehlina makes one tendril attack.

**Corrupting Presence (Costs 2 Actions).** Each creature grappled by the Spehlina takes 14 (4d6) necrotic damage.

**Submerge (Costs 2 Actions).** The Spehlina sinks up to 60 feet into the water. If it has creatures grappled, they are pulled with it.

**Agonized Thrash (Costs 3 Actions).** The Spehlina thrashes violently. Each creature within 20 feet must succeed on a DC 17 Strength saving throw or take 22 (4d10) bludgeoning damage and be pushed 15 feet away. Boats within range take 44 damage.

---

### Lair Actions

On initiative count 20 (losing initiative ties), the Spehlina can take a lair action while in its lake:

- **Corrupted Undercurrent:** The Spehlina creates a 30-foot-radius sphere of swirling corrupt water centered on a point within 120 feet. The area becomes difficult terrain. Creatures that start their turn there take 10 (3d6) necrotic damage.

- **Call the Tainted:** The Spehlina summons 1d4 **Corrupted Fish Swarms** (use Swarm of Quippers with necrotic damage instead of piercing).

- **Volcanic Vent:** Superheated water erupts in a 20-foot-radius, 40-foot-high cylinder. Creatures must make DC 16 Dexterity saves or take 22 (5d8) fire damage.

---

### Tactics

1. **Opening:** Stays submerged, uses Corruption Aura passively
2. **Engagement:** Surfaces near boats, uses Slam on vessels
3. **Combat:** Prioritizes grappling with tendrils, then Swallow
4. **Wounded:** Uses Submerge to retreat and regenerate position
5. **Desperate:** Corrupted Spray on grouped enemies, Agonized Thrash

## Signs of the Spehlina

**Before Encounter:**
- Dead fish floating, bodies blackened
- Water near shore has oily sheen
- Fishermen speak of "the shadow beneath"
- Boats return with hull damage—or don't return at all
- Sailors report "screaming" from the water on still nights

**The Screaming:** The Spehlina is in constant pain. At night, it releases subsonic wails that sensitive creatures can hear. This isn't aggression—it's suffering.

## DM Notes

### Party Level Recommendation
- **Minimum:** Level 8-9 with full resources and a plan
- **Comfortable:** Level 10-11
- **The guild rates this A-Tier for good reason**

### Possible Approaches
1. **Direct combat** (hardest, most rewarding XP-wise)
2. **Lure to surface** with bait/illusions
3. **Poison/weaken** over multiple encounters
4. **Discover the source** and attempt purification (requires investigation)

### Foreshadowing the Dilemma
- Have a druid or sage NPC mention "something feels wrong" about the creature
- Old texts describe Spehlinas as "sacred guardians"
- Villagers remember when the lake was safe and speak of "the gentle one"

### If They Kill It Without Knowing
- Don't punish them—this is a tragedy, not a failure
- Show the consequences over future sessions
- Lake corruption becomes a new problem to solve
- Creates personal investment in stopping Maltrius
