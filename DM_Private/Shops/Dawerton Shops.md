# Dawerton Shops

*Comprehensive market toolkit for a large coastal trade city*

Dawerton is a major port and trade hub — significantly larger than Adaham. It has permanent shops, a rotating market square, and black-market contacts through Liora's network. The economy mixes maritime trade, overland caravans from Braewood/Cleamond, and discreet dealings due to the vampire undercurrent.

---

## How To Use This File Fast

1. Pick a shop the party visits.
2. Choose stock mode based on the day.
3. Pull items from tables. Mark sold items.
4. Use the Daily Market Roll for variety.

### Stock Modes

| Mode | When | Items Visible |
|---|---|---|
| Light | After crisis / unrest (post-28th) | 6–10 per shop |
| Normal | Standard trading day | 12–18 per shop |
| Market Day (every 3rd day) | Large caravans arrive | 20–30 per shop |

### Daily Market Roll (1d6)

| Roll | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1 | Tense day. Guards patrolling. Some shops closed early. Light stock. |
| 2–3 | Normal trade. Standard prices. |
| 4–5 | Caravan arrival. +4 items per shop. One rare item available. |
| 6 | Festival/celebration prep. Busy market. Premium items appear. Prices +10%. |

## Price Notes

- Prices are **standard** post-February 28th. Townspeople are nervous but trade continues.
- **Holy water** is sold out at the shrine (Liora bought it all). Party can source from Ereni's alchemy shop at double price (50 gp) or wait 3 days for shrine restock.
- **Silver weapons** are in higher demand than usual. Bormund has them but won't explain why unless the party pushes (DC 13 Persuasion: "Something's been wrong on the roads. People want insurance.").
- **Black market** access requires knowing Liora or succeeding on a DC 16 Investigation check in the docks.
