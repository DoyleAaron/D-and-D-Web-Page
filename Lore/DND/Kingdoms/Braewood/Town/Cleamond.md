## **Name:**

**Cleamond**

---

### **Kingdom:**

**Braewood**

---

### **Leader / Mayor:**

**Warden-Mayor Lysa Thorne**, a shrewd half-elf with a merchant’s charm and a soldier’s caution. Formerly a caravan captain, she was appointed after her predecessor vanished during a southern raid. Known for fairness but also a sharp tongue.

---

### **Population:**

**~9,000** within the walls; **~12,000** including nearby farmsteads and river hamlets.  
Though technically a **large town**, Cleamond moves with the pulse of a city—its position as the **southern gate of Braewood’s trade routes** keeps its markets crowded and its inns full year-round.

---

### **Wealth:**

★★★★ (4 stars)

- Cleamond sits astride the **Golden Route**, connecting **Braewood City** to **Eaveton**, **Morting Stronghold**, and the southern realms beyond.
    
- Every wagon south must pass through Cleamond’s customs square—creating a steady flow of tolls, tariffs, and trade.
    
- Warehouses, stables, and inns line the cobbled causeway, while **merchant guilds** from the capital maintain depots here.
    
- Seasonal fairs draw traders from half the kingdom, but profits ebb when raids or bandit rumours rise in the south.
    
- While wealth flows freely, it rarely lingers—Cleamond is a place of **motion**, not accumulation.
    

---

### **Military:**

★★★ (3 stars)

- The town guard is well-drilled but small; they rely on a **rotating detachment of Crown soldiers** escorting merchant convoys south.
    
- The **Southern Gate Barracks** can house only a few hundred troops at most.
    
- Locals favour mobility over fortification—mounted scouts, signal towers, and fast messengers keep Cleamond informed rather than fortified.
    
- If attacked, the town can delay invaders, but not withstand a siege; its strength lies in warning others, not holding ground.
    

---

### **Happiness:**

★★★½ (3.5 stars)

- Cleamond thrives on diversity and trade—its people are outgoing, industrious, and proud of their role as **the kingdom’s link to the south**.
    
- Travellers from Morting and Eaveton fill its taverns, bringing new tales and coin.
    
- Life is lively, but instability in the southern hills brings anxiety—raids, missing caravans, and whispers of unrest weigh on the locals.
    
- Still, most folk hold fast to optimism, with frequent markets, open-air festivals, and music by the riverside.
    

---

### **Current Climate:**

- **Trade on a Knife’s Edge:**  
    Caravans from Morting report **ambushes along the southern passes**, while Eaveton merchants claim **new tariffs and bandit bribes**. Cleamond’s merchants walk a tightrope between profit and loss, with tempers flaring at every delay.
    
- **The River Dispute:**  
    The **River Vael**, once a peaceful artery of trade, is now a border of contention. A southern barony claims ownership of its ferries, taxing ships twice over. Warden-Mayor Thorne struggles to keep peace while ensuring Braewood’s trade keeps flowing.
    
- **Rumours of the Lost Convoy:**  
    Three weeks ago, a **caravan carrying Crown grain** to Morting vanished—no wreckage, no survivors. Locals whisper of **ghost lights in the southern marshes**, while the guard suspects organized raiders. The town’s trust in the Crown’s protection is wavering.
    
- **Cultural Crossroads:**  
    Cleamond is a melting pot—human merchants, elven artisans, and southern mercenaries mingle daily. This diversity fuels both **vibrant culture** and **occasional xenophobia**, depending on who profits that week.
    
- **A Candle Against the Dark:**  
    At night, the roads glow with lanterns strung between rooftops—a custom said to honour those “still travelling.” The lights have become both a charm and a warning: **keep moving, stay safe, and never travel alone past dusk.**
