>_All star ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

## **Name:**
**Cleamond**

---

### **Kingdom:**
**Braewood**

### **Settlement Type:**
Town

---

### **Leader / Mayor:**
**Warden-Mayor Lysa Thorne**, a shrewd half-elf with a merchant's charm and a soldier's caution. Formerly a caravan captain, she was appointed after her predecessor vanished during a southern raid. Known for fairness but also a sharp tongue.

---

### **Population:**
**~9,000** within the walls; **~12,000** including nearby farmsteads and river hamlets.  
Though technically a **large town**, Cleamond moves with the pulse of a city—its position as the **crossroads of Southern Braewood** keeps its markets crowded and its inns full year-round.

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Human (72%):** Merchants, farmers, and transport workers. Most families have lived here for generations.
- **Dwarf (10%):** Craftsmen and caravan guards. Less politically charged than in the north.
- **Half-Elf (8%):** Surprisingly common due to trade bringing diverse peoples together. Warden-Mayor Thorne is the most prominent.
- **Halfling (5%):** Innkeepers, cooks, and provisioners. Run several of the best establishments.
- **Other (5%):** Traveling merchants, seasonal workers, and southern visitors.

---

### **Geography & Climate:**

**Position:**
- Cleamond sits at the **crossroads of Southern Braewood**, where the Western Military Road meets the Sheyton Trade Road.
- Located approximately **35 km west of Braewood City**, serving as the gateway to the western territories.
- From Cleamond, roads branch: northwest to **Mortling Stronghold** (20 km), and south to **Sheyton** (28 km).
- The town sits at the **halfway point** between Mortling and Sheyton—directly west from the capital, then travelers choose north or south.

**Distances:**
- 100 km to eastern coast
- 155 km to northern coast
- 160 km to southern coast
- 35 km east to Braewood City
- 20 km northwest to Mortling Stronghold
- 28 km south to Sheyton
- 14 km south to the Ineross turnoff (branch road leading 20 km to Ineross village)

**Terrain:**
- Rolling farmland surrounds the town, with the **River Vael** flowing from the northwest.
- The roads are well-maintained cobblestone within 5 km of town, then packed earth beyond.
- Small copses of woodland dot the landscape, remnants of ancient forests.

**Climate:**
- Mild and temperate, benefiting from central Braewood's moderate weather.
- Warm summers with occasional droughts; winters are cold but rarely harsh.
- The River Vael occasionally floods in spring, making low-lying roads impassable.

---

### **Wealth:**
★★★★ (4 stars)

- Cleamond sits astride the **Golden Route**, connecting **Braewood City** to **Mortling Stronghold**, **Sheyton**, and the southern realms beyond.
- Every wagon west or south must pass through Cleamond's customs square—creating a steady flow of tolls, tariffs, and trade.
- Warehouses, stables, and inns line the cobbled causeway, while **merchant guilds** from the capital maintain depots here.
- Seasonal fairs draw traders from half the kingdom, but profits ebb when raids or bandit rumours rise.
- While wealth flows freely, it rarely lingers—Cleamond is a place of **motion**, not accumulation.

---

### **Military:**
★★★ (3 stars)

- The town guard is well-drilled but small; they rely on a **rotating detachment of Crown soldiers** escorting merchant convoys.
- The **Southern Gate Barracks** can house only a few hundred troops at most.
- Locals favour mobility over fortification—mounted scouts, signal towers, and fast messengers keep Cleamond informed rather than fortified.
- If attacked, the town can delay invaders, but not withstand a siege; its strength lies in warning others, not holding ground.

---

### **Happiness:**
★★★½ (3.5 stars)

- Cleamond thrives on diversity and trade—its people are outgoing, industrious, and proud of their role as **the kingdom's crossroads**.
- Travellers from Mortling, Sheyton, and the capital fill its taverns, bringing new tales and coin.
- Life is lively, but instability in the southern hills brings anxiety—raids, missing caravans, and whispers of unrest weigh on the locals.
- Still, most folk hold fast to optimism, with frequent markets, open-air festivals, and music by the riverside.

---

### **History:**

- **Founded (~580 C.C.):** Began as a waystation for troops moving to the newly-expanded Mortling Stronghold.
- **Trade Hub Established (700-800 C.C.):** As Mortling grew, Cleamond became the supply depot—then expanded into a proper town.
- **The River Compact (890 C.C.):** Treaty established Cleamond's customs authority over all River Vael crossings—still contested by southern barons.
- **Warden-Mayor System (1150 C.C.):** Crown created the unique "Warden-Mayor" position, combining military and civil authority for border security.
- **Modern Era:** Growth continues as trade intensifies; recent bandit activity has strained resources.

---

### **Notable Locations:**

- **The Customs Square:** Heart of Cleamond—a massive cobbled plaza where all caravans must register, pay tolls, and receive road passes.
- **The Golden Gate:** Stone archway marking the western entrance, decorated with trade symbols. Said to bring luck if touched when departing.
- **River Vael Ferry Docks:** Three ferries operate here, connecting to farmsteads and hamlets across the river. Source of the taxation dispute.
- **The Wayward Lantern:** Largest inn in Cleamond, famous for its hot meals and warm beds. Caravan masters meet here to share route intelligence.
- **The Southern Gate Barracks:** Small military compound housing the rotating Crown garrison.
- **Thorne Manor:** Warden-Mayor Lysa Thorne's residence, a modest but well-defended house near the Customs Square.
- **The Lantern Way:** Main street through town, lit by hundreds of hanging lanterns at night—the town's signature tradition.

---

### **Key Figures:**

- **Warden-Mayor Lysa Thorne:** Half-elf, 80s (appears 40s). Former caravan captain, now governs with merchant's practicality and soldier's vigilance. Knows every trade route personally.
- **Captain Marcus Dunley:** Human, 40s. Commands the town guard. Frustrated by limited resources but loyal to Thorne.
- **Guildmaster Orvyn Cartwright:** Human, 60s. Head of the Caravan Guild, controls most transport through Cleamond. Wealthy but unpopular.
- **Sister Evalyn Ashmore:** Human, 50s. Runs the Mercy House almshouse. Beloved by the poor; knows everyone's secrets.
- **Ferryman Jorl Vatton:** Human, 30s. Controls the largest ferry operation. At the center of the River Vael taxation dispute.

---

### **Current Events & Hooks:**

- **Trade on a Knife's Edge:** Caravans from Mortling report **ambushes along the southern passes**, while Sheyton merchants claim **new tariffs and bandit bribes**. Cleamond's merchants walk a tightrope between profit and loss.
- **The River Dispute:** The **River Vael**, once a peaceful artery of trade, is now a border of contention. A southern barony claims ownership of its ferries, taxing ships twice over. Warden-Mayor Thorne struggles to keep peace.
- **Rumours of the Lost Convoy:** Three weeks ago, a **caravan carrying Crown grain** to Mortling vanished—no wreckage, no survivors. Locals whisper of **ghost lights in the southern marshes**, while the guard suspects organized raiders.
- **The Ineross Problem:** Shepherds from Ineross village report strange disappearances—sheep first, now people. The branch road grows quieter each week.
- **A Candle Against the Dark:** At night, the roads glow with lanterns strung between rooftops—a custom said to honour those "still travelling." The lights have become both a charm and a warning: **keep moving, stay safe, and never travel alone past dusk.**

---

### **Relationships:**

- **Mortling Stronghold:** Primary client. Cleamond exists to supply Mortling; the military city's needs drive Cleamond's economy. 20 km to the northwest.
- **Braewood City:** Political master. Crown authority flows through Cleamond to the western territories. 35 km to the east.
- **Sheyton:** Trade partner to the south (28 km). Goods from Sheyton's markets flow north through Cleamond; there's friendly rivalry over which town is more prosperous.
- **Ineross:** Small village to the south (14 km to turnoff, then 20 km down branch road). Cleamond provides goods; Ineross provides wool and hides.
- **Southern Baronies:** Contentious. The River Vael dispute has soured relations with unnamed southern nobles.
