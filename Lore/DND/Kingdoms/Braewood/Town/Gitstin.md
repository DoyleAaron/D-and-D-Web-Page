>_All star ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

### **Name:**
Gitstin

### **Kingdom:**
Braewood

### **Settlement Type:**
Town

### **Leader:**
**Reeve Arton Fellmark** — A pragmatic human in his early fifties who rose from caravan guard to town leader through sheer persistence. His weathered face and calloused hands betray decades on the trade roads before settling into governance. Known for fair dealing and a genuine care for both merchant and farmer alike.

### **Population:**
**~3,800** within the town proper; **~5,200** including surrounding farmsteads and waypoints. A crucial junction town connecting the western bridge to Eaveton and serving as a gateway to southern Braewood.

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Human (72%):** Merchants, farmers, innkeepers, and caravan workers. The backbone of the town's economy.
- **Halfling (12%):** Many run the inns, taverns, and bakeries that serve weary travelers. Known for their hospitality.
- **Dwarf (8%):** Craftsmen and blacksmiths who maintain wagons and equipment. Generally integrated and respected.
- **Half-Elf (5%):** Traders and scribes, often with connections to Islefield across the bridge.
- **Other (3%):** Traveling merchants and seasonal workers.

---

### **Geography & Climate:**

**Position:**
- Located 40 km southwest of Eaveton (more west than south), Gitstin sits at a vital crossroads.
- From here, roads branch to Kadena (50km west-southwest), Osegas (27km south), and Eaveton (40km northeast).
- 23 km east of the Great Lavalto Lake shore.

**Road Network:**
- **To Eaveton (40km NE):** Main road, well-maintained. At 26km from Eaveton, a branch heads south toward Osegas. At 35km, another branch heads north to Eimgend village.
- **To Kadena (50km WSW):** Western road to the great bridge. At 15km, a branch leads 10km south to Olkgend military village.
- **To Osegas (27km S):** Southern road to the heart of the deep south.

**Distances (Straight Line):**
- 250 km to northern coast (110 km north is Mortling)
- 60 km to southern coast
- 130 km to eastern coast
- 23 km west to the Great Lavalto Lake

**Terrain:**
- Rolling farmland surrounds the town, with well-maintained roads in all directions.
- The western horizon shows occasional glimpses of the Great Lavalto Lake on clear days.
- Orchards and vineyards dot the countryside, benefiting from the moderate climate.

**Climate:**
- Warm summers, mild winters with minimal snow.
- Proximity to the Great Lavalto Lake moderates temperatures.
- Occasional lake mist drifts in from the west during autumn mornings.

### **Wealth:**
★★★★☆ (4 stars)

- Gitstin thrives as the essential connector between Kadena's bridge traffic and Eaveton's markets.
- Every caravan crossing from Islefield must pass through here to reach the capital region.
- Robust innkeeping trade—the town boasts seven major inns and countless taverns.
- Wagon repair and horse trading provide steady income.
- Influence from nearby Osegas has brought cultural development beyond typical trade towns.

---

### **Military:**
★★★☆☆ (3 stars)

- A capable town watch of about 80 guards, well-equipped but not militarized.
- Relies on proximity to Kadena's garrison for serious threats.
- Caravan guards passing through bolster the town's security presence.
- Wooden palisade walls with stone gatehouse—functional but not formidable.

---

### **Happiness:**
★★★★½ (4.5 stars)

- Strong sense of community and opportunity. The constant flow of travelers brings news, entertainment, and coin.
- Influence from Osegas's progressive culture has created an unusually tolerant atmosphere.
- Multiple faiths are respected, and the town hosts festivals from various traditions.
- Good employment prospects for all ages and backgrounds.
- The only tension: occasional frustration at being "just a stop" rather than a destination.

---

### **Economy & Trade:**

**Primary Industries:**
- **Innkeeping & Hospitality:** The lifeblood of Gitstin. Seven major inns compete for caravan business.
- **Wagon Repair & Smithing:** Essential services for travelers crossing from Islefield.
- **Horse Trading:** A weekly market draws buyers from across southern Braewood.
- **Agriculture:** Orchards and vineyards in the surrounding countryside.

**Trade Relationships:**
- **Kadena:** Receives bridge traffic bound for the interior.
- **Eaveton:** Sends goods north to the major markets.
- **Osegas:** Close cultural and economic ties; shared festivals and traditions.
- **Olkgend:** Supplies the military training village.

---

### **History:**

**Tharnic Era:**
- Founded during the Tharnic Empire's expansion as a supply depot between the great bridge and the interior.
- One of dozens of settlements hastily established when the Empire overextended.
- Survived the collapse era by becoming essential to bridge traffic—too useful to abandon.

**Post-Tharnic Era:**
- Grew steadily as Braewood consolidated the south.
- Strong ties with Osegas developed after that town helped stabilize the region.
- The current prosperity dates to King Tarvin III's investment in the southern road network.

**Recent History:**
- Trade has increased following improved relations with Islefield.
- The town has expanded beyond its original walls, with new districts along the main roads.

---

### **Notable Locations:**

- **The Seven Bells:** The largest inn in town, named for the seven inns of Gitstin. Run by a consortium of halfling families who rotate management duties. Famous for its communal dining hall where travelers share stories.

- **Fellmark's Hall:** The town hall where Reeve Arton Fellmark holds court. A practical building with a large public square for markets and announcements.

- **The Crossroads Shrine:** A small temple maintained by priests from multiple faiths. Travelers often leave offerings before continuing their journey.

- **Wheelers' Row:** A street of wagon repair shops, smithies, and harness makers. The sound of hammering never stops during daylight hours.

- **The Orchardist's Market:** A covered market where local farmers sell produce, wine, and preserves. Particularly busy during harvest season.

### **Relationships:**

- **Eaveton (40km NE):** Economic lifeline—most trade flows to and through the large city.
- **Kadena (50km WSW):** Bridge traffic partner. Gitstin handles the travelers; Kadena handles security.
- **Osegas (27km S):** Cultural sibling. The towns share festivals, traditions, and a progressive outlook.
- **Olkgend (25km SW via road):** Supplies the military village; soldiers come to Gitstin for leave.
- **Eimgend (road connection via Eaveton route):** Occasional messenger traffic to the Crown watch post.
