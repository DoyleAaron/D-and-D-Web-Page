## Geography

### Location
Borugham sits at the end of a road branching from the main [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) route — a geographic cul-de-sac with no through-traffic. The town lies 30km southwest of [Wynlants Cavity](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Wynlants%20Cavity.md), in the northwestern "heel" region of [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md). [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) lies mainly south but slightly west, accessible via the only road out.

### Borugh Lake
A circular lake with a 4km radius lies immediately southeast of the town. The lake feeds a river that flows 45km north to the ocean — a lifeline for fishing and the only water route out. The lake is deep, cold, and according to local legend, connected to the old mines.

### Terrain
- **Southeast:** [Borugh](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Thane%20Aldric%20Borugh.md) Lake, fed by mountain runoff
- **North:** River flowing to the northern ocean (45km)
- **East:** The river border with [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) (separate from the lake's river)
- **West/Northwest:** Forested hills toward the coastal settlements ([Slada](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md), [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md))

### Climate
Cold continental. Harsh winters, short summers. The lake moderates temperatures slightly but fog is common. Snow covers the ground for five months of the year.

## Economy

### Primary Industries
- **Lake Fishing** — Primary food source, some trade south
- **Forestry** — Controlled logging in surrounding woods
- **Mining Heritage** — Small-scale ore extraction, legacy of deeper operations
- **Smithing** — Dwarven craftsmen produce quality tools and arms

### Trade Relations
- Trades fish, timber, and metalwork south to [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md)
- Receives grain and manufactured goods from [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md)
- Some quiet trade northwest with [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) and [Slada](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md) (kinship goods)
- No trade east — the [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) border is watched but not crossed

### Economic Outlook
Borugham is stable but isolated. The dead-end road limits commerce. The population prefers it this way — fewer outsiders, fewer questions, fewer Crown officials.

## History

### Tharnic Era (pre-730 C.C.)
The site was originally a Tharnic mining camp, extracting ore from the nearby hills. Dwarven slaves worked the mines under brutal conditions. When the War of the Sundered Chains began, the camp's overseers were among the first killed — their bodies thrown into the lake, according to local stories.

### The Liberation (710-730 C.C.)
During the rebellion, fleeing dwarves used Borugham's isolated position as a staging ground. The mines became hideouts. The lake provided food. When the Tharnic Empire fell, the survivors stayed. The [Borugh](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Thane%20Aldric%20Borugh.md) clan — named for the lake they liberated — became hereditary leaders.

### Post-War Integration
When [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) formed as a kingdom, Borugham reluctantly joined. They pay taxes. They acknowledge the Crown. But they remember who enslaved their ancestors — humans — and they remember who freed them — dwarves. This colors every interaction with the outside world.

### Modern Era
Borugham has changed little in five centuries. The dwarven majority maintains traditions honoring the Sundered Chain heroes. Statues of High Thane Broldir Flameforged stand in the town square. Songs of liberation are sung at festivals. The Borughs still rule, their authority rooted in blood shed during the rebellion.

## Notable Locations

### Borugh Hall
A fortified stone hall at the town's heart, built in dwarven style — low, thick-walled, with deep cellars connecting to old mine tunnels. The Borughs have ruled from here since the liberation. The great hall contains murals depicting the War of the Sundered Chains.

### The Lakeside Docks
Wooden piers extending into [Borugh](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Thane%20Aldric%20Borugh.md) Lake. Fishing boats launch daily. A small ferry runs north along the river during summer months, connecting to coastal settlements. The docks are the town's lifeline.

### The Liberation Monument
A granite plaza featuring statues of High Thane Broldir Flameforged and other rebellion heroes. Every anniversary of the Siege of Treadfast Hold, the town gathers here for ceremonies. Outsiders are not forbidden from attending — but they are not welcome either.

### The Old Mines
Sealed tunnel entrances in the hills southeast of town. The Tharnic-era mines where dwarven slaves once labored. Now considered sacred ground. Some tunnels connect to the lake's depths. Locals claim the spirits of the enslaved still walk the deeper passages.

### The Crossroads Inn
The only establishment serving outsiders, located where the town's road meets the main route to [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md). Run by the Wheelock family — humans who've earned tolerance through generations of keeping outsiders contained and comfortable until they leave.

## Key Figures

### Thane Aldric Borugh
**Leader.** A stern dwarf in his 180s, direct descendant of the liberation-era Borughs. His family's authority derives from blood shed freeing their people. He distrusts humans instinctively — not hatred exactly, but wariness bred into his bones. He respects strength and remembers insults.

### Greta Borugh
**Administrator.** Aldric's younger sister (150s), handles trade negotiations with [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md). More pragmatic than her brother, she recognizes that total isolation is impossible. Still, she ensures every interaction reminds outsiders that Borugham owes them nothing.

### Hagen Wheelock
**Innkeeper.** Human, runs the Crossroads Inn. His family has served this role for generations — the acceptable face of Borugham to the outside world. He's genuinely friendly, which makes some dwarves suspicious, but he's proven his loyalty repeatedly.

### Fenris Thorne
**Master Logger.** Dwarf, oversees the forestry operations. Practical, focused on work rather than politics. Knows the surrounding woods intimately. Veterans of the 1207 Dwarven Revolt of Northpeak sometimes pass through seeking his counsel.

## Cultural Notes

### The Dwarven Republican Sentiment
Borugham sits at a moderate point on the spectrum of dwarven independence sentiment:
- **Barge:** Mixed population, tension exists but Crown authority is accepted
- **Borugham:** Dwarven majority, deeply proud, pays taxes but resents the Crown
- **Yotherdon:** Largest independence-focused town, open defiance of some Crown edicts
- **Slada:** Extreme — some refuse to acknowledge [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s authority entirely

In Borugham, the sentiment manifests as:
- Celebration of Sundered Chain heroes
- Distrust of human authority figures
- Quiet support for more radical settlements
- Teaching children the old songs of liberation

### Attitude Toward Humans
Borugham doesn't hate humans — but it remembers. The Tharnic Empire was human-led. The slavers were human. The current monarchy is human. Every human who enters is measured against that history. Those who show respect for dwarven suffering earn tolerance. Those who don't are shown the door.

## Relationships

### With Other Settlements
- **Barge:** Trade partner, necessary but not warm. Human officials are treated with cold formality.
- **Yotherdon:** Kinship and respect. Young dwarves sometimes travel there seeking more radical communities.
- **Slada:** Admired for their purity of conviction, even if Borugham considers them impractical.
- **Braewood City:** Distant, resentful. The Crown is tolerated, never embraced.

### With the Crown
Borugham pays taxes. It obeys laws that don't conflict with dwarven tradition. It provides militia when called — dwarven soldiers fight well, and the Crown knows better than to refuse them.

But there is no loyalty here. If circumstances changed, if a new rebellion arose, Borugham would remember which side their ancestors fought on.

---

## Character & Atmosphere

Borugham is proud. Not hostile, but not welcoming either. Travelers are served at the Crossroads Inn, directed away from the town proper, and encouraged to complete their business quickly.

The atmosphere is one of memory. Every building, every monument, every song reminds residents of what their ancestors suffered and how they won their freedom. The dwarven majority doesn't live in the past — but they haven't forgotten it.

To outsiders, especially humans, this can feel oppressive. Every interaction carries weight. Every glance measures whether you're worthy of respect or merely tolerated. Most travelers find it easier to push on to [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md).
