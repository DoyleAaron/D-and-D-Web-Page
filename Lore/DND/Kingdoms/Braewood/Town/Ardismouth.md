### **Name:**
Ardismouth

### **Kingdom:**
[Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)

### **Settlement Type:**
Town

### **Leader:**
**Portreeve Garrett Dunholm** — A Crown-appointed administrator in his late 40s, tasked with the impossible job of maintaining peace between humans, dwarves, and the Crown. Perpetually exhausted, genuinely trying to do right by everyone.

### **Population:**
**~4,200** (significant dwarven minority)

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Humans (52%):** Crown officials, fishermen, coastal traders
- **Dwarves (35%):** Unusually high — trade connection to northern dwarven settlements
- **Halflings (7%):** Merchants, innkeepers
- **Other (6%):** Mixed travelers on the coastal road

### **Geography & Climate:**

**Position:**
Ardismouth occupies a natural harbor on [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s east coast:
- **35km northeast of Braewood City** (right road from capital)
- On the **coastal road to Yotherdon** (which passes the [Debourg Grotto](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md))
- Part of the **ring road network**: [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City → Ardismouth → [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) → [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) → [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City

**The Harbor:**
A decent natural harbor, though nothing compared to Mortling. Small to medium vessels can dock safely. The harbor serves two purposes: coastal fishing and trade with dwarven caravans heading to/from the northern settlements.

**Key Distances:**
- **To Braewood City:** 35km southwest
- **To Yotherdon:** ~80km north along the coastal road (passing [Debourg Grotto](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md))
- **To Barge:** ~110km northwest via [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md)
- **To Oyard:** 14km up the [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) road, then 35km down a side road

**Terrain:**
Coastal cliffs and rocky beaches. The town is built where a river meets the sea, providing fresh water and a defensible position. The land rises steeply inland — the road to [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) climbs through forested hills.

**Climate:**
Maritime, with salt spray and frequent storms. Milder than inland but windier. Fog rolls in regularly.

### **Economy & Trade:**

**Primary Industries:**
- **Dwarven Trade:** Ardismouth is the preferred route for dwarves who want to avoid [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md)'s crowds (and its tensions)
- **Fishing:** Coastal waters provide abundant catch
- **Transshipment:** Goods from the northern heel move through Ardismouth to the capital
- **Metalwork Finishing:** Some dwarven smiths have set up shops here, finishing goods for southern markets

**The Dwarven Factor:**
Many dwarves from the northern settlements ([Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md), the [Meriver Mountains](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Meriver%20Mountains.md), [Buchrior Hollows](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Buchrior%20Hollows.md)) use Ardismouth as their gateway to [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) proper. They consider [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) too human-dominated and too prone to Crown interference. This preference has made Ardismouth wealthy but tense.

**Trade Networks:**
- **North (Yotherdon/heel):** Raw ore, worked metal, mountain goods
- **South (Braewood City):** Finished goods, grain, luxury items
- **Coast:** Fish, salt, maritime supplies

### **History:**

**Tharnic Era (pre-730 C.C.):**
A fishing village and minor port. The Tharnics considered the coastal road to the north too dangerous to develop fully — the terrain favored ambushes.

**Post-Empire Period:**
Ardismouth remained small until dwarven trade from the north increased. As [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) grew crowded, some dwarven merchants sought alternatives. Ardismouth was perfectly positioned.

**The Debourg Grotto Catastrophe (~890 C.C.):**
The mines near [Debourg Grotto](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md), operated by an extreme dwarven faction, fell silent. Something terrible happened there. The coastal road became dangerous. Ardismouth suffered economically but survived because the alternative route through [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) was even worse for dwarves who feared Crown interference.

**Modern Era:**
Ardismouth has become a powder keg. Dwarven merchants have significant economic power. Some factions push for special privileges, autonomy, even secession from [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md). The Crown responds with bureaucracy and occasional shows of force. No one is happy. The Portreeve tries to mediate while everyone shouts at him.

### **Notable Locations:**

- **The Stone Pier:** Dwarven-built dock extension, paid for by merchant guilds, source of controversy (humans say it was built without proper permission)
- **Dunholm House:** Portreeve's residence and administrative center, built on the cliff overlooking the harbor
- **The Hammer Hall:** Dwarven meeting house, officially a "trade guild" but functions as a political headquarters
- **The Sailor's Rest:** Human-majority tavern, counterweight to dwarven influence, sometimes used for Crown meetings
- **The Coastal Watch:** Stone tower overlooking the road north, watching for... something. The guards are vague about what.

---

### **Key Figures:**

- **Portreeve Garrett Dunholm:** Crown administrator, mediator, exhausted by the impossible job of keeping peace
- **Guildmaster Brogar Flamevein:** Leader of the dwarven merchant faction, politically aggressive, pushes for special privileges
- **Captain Mira Blackshore:** Commands the town garrison, human loyalist, deeply suspicious of dwarven "autonomy" talk
- **Elder Mordin Greymantle:** Moderate dwarven voice, wants cooperation not confrontation, increasingly marginalized
- **Father Osric Tidewalker:** Local priest, one of the few figures both sides respect

---

### **Relationships:**

**With Other Settlements:**
- **Braewood City:** The Crown watches Ardismouth carefully; the town is a test case for human-dwarf relations
- **Yotherdon:** Strong trade connections; many Ardismouth dwarves have family in [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md)
- **Barge:** Rivalry for dwarven trade; [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) is larger but Ardismouth is preferred by dwarf nationalists
- **Debourg Grotto:** The horror to the north; no one goes there; the road past it is traveled quickly and nervously

**With the Crown:**
The Crown sees Ardismouth as a potential flashpoint. Too much autonomy could encourage other dwarven settlements to demand the same. Too much control could spark rebellion. The Portreeve is supposed to find a middle path. He's failing.

---

### **Current Events & Hooks:**

- **The Autonomy Petition:** Guildmaster [Flamevein](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Guildmaster%20Brogar%20Flamevein.md) is circulating a petition demanding dwarven self-governance within Ardismouth. The Crown has not yet responded. Tension is mounting.
- **The Road Attacks:** Caravans on the coastal road have been attacked near [Debourg Grotto](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md). Not bandits — something else. Survivors refuse to describe what they saw.
- **The Missing Moderate:** [Elder Mordin Greymantle](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Elder%20Mordin%20Greymantle.md) disappeared last week. His supporters suspect foul play by the nationalist faction. [Flamevein](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Guildmaster%20Brogar%20Flamevein.md) claims he simply left town.
- **Crown Reinforcements:** Rumors say the Crown is sending additional soldiers. The dwarves see this as provocation. Captain [Blackshore](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Captain%20Mira%20Blackshore.md) sees it as overdue.
