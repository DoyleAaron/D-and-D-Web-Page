# Debourg Grotto

## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Debourg Grotto |
| **Type** | Point of Interest (Abandoned Mine / Horror Site) |
| **Kingdom** | Braewood (officially) |
| **Region** | East coast, between Ardismouth and Yotherdon |
| **Status** | **FORBIDDEN ZONE** — Crown edict prohibits entry; the road passes by but no one stops |

---

## ⚠️ WARNING

**Do not enter Debourg Grotto.**

The Crown has forbidden access to the mines and surrounding tunnels since 892 C.C. Patrols do not approach. Rescue parties are not sent. Those who enter do not return.

What lives in the Grotto is no longer dwarven.

---

## Geography

### Location
Debourg Grotto lies on the coastal road between Ardismouth and Yotherdon, approximately:
- **40km north of Ardismouth**
- **40km south of Yotherdon**
- **~1km from the coastal cliffs**
- The main road passes within sight of the mine entrance

### Terrain
The area around Debourg is characterized by dry, hilly terrain — unusual for this section of coast. The hills once provided excellent defensive positions; during the Tharnic War, both sides used them for ambushes. The soil is thin, the vegetation sparse, the rock exposed. Caves and natural hollows dot the landscape, many connected to the mine complex below.

The main grotto entrance is a massive carved archway in a cliff face, now blackened and crumbling. Dwarven runes are still visible, though many have been defaced or... altered.

### The Road
The coastal road from Ardismouth to Yotherdon passes within a few hundred meters of the Grotto entrance. Travelers move quickly through this section. Caravans travel in groups. No one camps nearby. Even in daylight, the area feels wrong — too quiet, too still, too watched.

## History

### The Golden Age (750-850 C.C.)

Debourg Grotto was one of the richest mines in northern Braewood. Established by dwarven clans freed during the Tharnic collapse, the mine produced:
- High-quality iron ore
- Copper and tin
- Semi-precious gems
- Small amounts of silver

The dwarves who settled here were among the most extreme anti-Tharnic factions — those who had suffered worst in the slave mines, who harbored the deepest hatred for their former masters. They were prosperous but isolated, rarely trading with humans, barely tolerating Crown oversight.

### The Darkening (850-890 C.C.)

As the mine deepened, the dwarves of Debourg became increasingly extreme. They:
- Refused Crown inspectors
- Stopped trading at Ardismouth
- Began capturing Tharnic refugees (humans descended from the empire's servant classes)
- Built a prison complex beneath the mines

Reports from this period are fragmentary. Traders who approached were turned away. Crown investigators disappeared. Rumors spread of:
- Torture chambers in the deep tunnels
- Tharnic prisoners worked to death in the mines
- "Justice" inflicted for the sins of ancestors

The Crown discussed intervention. The debates lasted decades. By the time anyone acted, it was too late.

### The Isolation (890-892 C.C.)

In 890, a tunnel collapse (natural? deliberate?) sealed the main entrance to Debourg Grotto. Contact with the outside world ceased entirely. For two years, nothing emerged.

Inside — based on later fragmentary evidence — the dwarves faced starvation. Their food stores depleted. Their prisoners became their food. They ate human flesh to survive. And in their desperation, in their madness, in the dark beneath the earth... they prayed.

**Something answered.**

### The Opening (892 C.C.)

When the tunnel was finally cleared — by internal efforts, not rescue — what emerged was not dwarven.

A Crown patrol approaching the mine entrance encountered creatures that had once been dwarves. Twisted. Pale. Hungry in ways that had nothing to do with food. The patrol was destroyed. One survivor reached Ardismouth before dying of his wounds. His final words were recorded:

*"They are not dwarves anymore. They are not alive. They are not dead. They made a deal. They paid a price. And now they are... hungry. Always hungry. For something we cannot give."*

The Crown sealed the area. No further investigation was attempted.

### Modern Era (892-Present)

The Grotto remains occupied by... something. Travelers on the coastal road occasionally see:
- Pale figures watching from the hills
- Lights in the mine entrance at night
- Shadows that move against the wind

Attacks are rare but documented. Caravans that camp nearby sometimes lose people. Travelers who wander off the road are never found. The Crown maintains a nominal "patrol" but the soldiers never approach within a kilometer of the entrance.

## What Lives There Now

**CLASSIFIED — Crown Intelligence Only**

The creatures that emerged from Debourg Grotto in 892 C.C. appear to be:
- Former dwarves, physically corrupted beyond recognition
- Pale, elongated, with features that suggest extreme starvation taken beyond death
- Capable of moving in daylight but preferring darkness
- Hostile to all living things, especially humans with Tharnic ancestry
- Organized — they do not simply attack, they hunt, they plan, they wait

The entity they made a pact with remains unknown. Possibilities include:
- A forgotten god of the deep earth
- Something that slept beneath the mountains
- A demon summoned by their hatred and fed by their crimes
- Something older, stranger, with no name in any mortal language

Vampires who have observed the Debourg creatures (at a distance) reportedly refuse to discuss them. One was quoted as saying: *"Even we would not become that. Even we have limits. What they are... is a warning about what desperation can create."*

## The Mine Complex

Based on pre-collapse records, Debourg Grotto includes:
- **The Main Entrance:** Massive carved archway, now partially collapsed and defaced
- **The Upper Mines:** Iron and copper extraction, relatively shallow
- **The Deep Shafts:** Silver veins, gem deposits, extending far underground
- **The Prison Levels:** Built after 850 C.C., location unknown
- **The Ritual Chamber:** Reported by the dying patrol survivor — a space deep within where "the deal was made"

Current layout is unknown. The creatures may have expanded the complex significantly.

---

## Notable Features

### The Archway
The original entrance features magnificent dwarven stonework — or did. The runes have been altered, scratched over, replaced with symbols no one recognizes. Crown scholars who studied rubbings of these symbols reported nightmares for weeks.

### The Watching Hills
The dry hills surrounding Debourg contain numerous caves and hollows. Creatures from the Grotto use these for observation. Travelers report feeling watched. They are correct.

### The Pit
Approximately 500 meters from the main entrance, a sinkhole opened in 915 C.C. It appears to connect to the lower tunnels. Sometimes sounds emerge from it at night. No one investigates.

---

## Current Situation

**Official Status:** Forbidden zone. Crown edict prohibits approach.

**Actual Status:** The creatures rarely leave the immediate area, but attacks have increased slightly in recent years. Three caravans have been hit in the past decade. One survivor was recovered — she had been kept alive for days, apparently for questioning. She refused to say what they asked her.

**The Coastal Road:** Still used because the alternative (overland through the interior) is even longer and passes through other dangers. Travelers accept the risk. They move fast, they don't stop, and they pray.

---

## Hooks & Mysteries

- **The Increasing Activity:** Something has stirred the Debourg creatures. Their scouts have been seen further from the Grotto than ever. What changed?
- **The Ritual Chamber:** If someone could reach the deepest level, they might learn what god or power the dwarves contacted. That knowledge could be valuable — or catastrophic.
- **Prisoner 7:** An elderly dwarf in Oyard Prison was extracted from Debourg before the collapse. He saw the early signs of what was happening. He has never spoken of it — but his recent ravings suggest he knows more than he's told.
- **The Survivor's Questions:** The woman kept for questioning was asked repeatedly about "the bloodline" and "the children of the empire." What are the creatures looking for?
