## Geography

### Location
The Meriver Mountains rise along the eastern coast of [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s northern heel, approximately 40km east of [Buchrior Hollows](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Buchrior%20Hollows.md). The range runs roughly north-south, with the sea to the east and dense forest to the west. Peaks reach 800-1200 meters, modest by continental standards but imposing in the otherwise flat northern terrain.

### Relationship to Other Sites
- **40km west:** [Buchrior Hollows](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Buchrior%20Hollows.md)
- **65km southwest:** [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md)
- **70km west:** [Slada](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md)
- **90km southwest:** [Borugham](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Borugham.md)
- The coastline lies directly east, with cliffs dropping to the sea

### Terrain
Rugged granite peaks, steep valleys, and dense alpine forest on lower slopes. The eastern face drops precipitously to the ocean via sheer cliffs. The western slopes are more gradual, allowing forest growth. Hidden valleys and cave systems riddle the interior.

## History

### Pre-Tharnic Era
Before human expansion, dwarven clans inhabited the Meriver Mountains, mining iron, copper, and precious stones. These were free clans who traded with lowland settlements. Their mountain holds were modest but defensible — built for mining, not war.

### Tharnic Conquest (circa 650 C.C.)
When the Tharnic Empire expanded northward, they conquered the lowlands quickly but struggled with the mountains. The original dwarven clans resisted, but superior Tharnic numbers and organization eventually prevailed. The mountain holds fell. The survivors were enslaved and sent to lowland mines at [Plalisle Den](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Plalisle%20Den.md), [Wynlants Cavity](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Wynlants%20Cavity.md), and other sites.

The mountains were considered "pacified" — stripped of resources, emptied of population, no longer of interest to the Empire.

### The Retreat (circa 710 C.C.)
When the rebellion began, escaped slaves needed refuge. The lowland forests were patrolled; the coastline was watched. But the Meriver Mountains were forgotten. Rebels climbed into the peaks, found the old dwarven holds, and made them habitable again.

For twenty years, the Meriver Mountains sheltered the heart of the rebellion:
- Hidden forges produced weapons
- Secret paths connected to lowland forests
- Scouts watched Tharnic movements from high ground
- Councils of war met in ancient halls

### The Liberation Campaign (720-730 C.C.)
The final push against Tharnic forces was coordinated from the Meriver Mountains. High Thane Broldir Flameforged established his headquarters in the largest hold, now called **Broldir's Fastness**. From here, messengers traveled to every rebel cell. From here, the order went out for coordinated attacks that broke Tharnic power in the north.

After the victory, many dwarves returned to lowland settlements. The mountains were left to memory.

### Modern Era
The Meriver Mountains are wilderness again. The old holds exist but are unmaintained. Some dwarves visit to honor the past; some young radicals dream of resettling the peaks. The Crown ignores the range — there's nothing there worth governing.

## Notable Sites

### Broldir's Fastness
The largest of the ancient holds, carved into the highest peak. During the rebellion, this served as the command center. Today, the halls are empty but intact. Dwarven pilgrims visit to walk where Broldir walked.

Key features:
- The War Room, where the liberation campaign was planned
- The Forge of Freedom, where rebellion weapons were made
- Broldir's personal quarters, now a shrine
- Signal platforms for coordinating with lowland forces

### The Cliffwatch
A series of lookout points along the eastern cliffs, where dwarven scouts watched for Tharnic ships during the rebellion. Today, these offer stunning views of the coastline. Some dwarves camp here for spiritual reflection.

### The Hidden Forges
Scattered through the mountains, these small complexes produced weapons for the rebellion. Some contain abandoned equipment; others were deliberately collapsed to hide them from Tharnic searches. A few may still hold valuable tools or artifacts.

### The Memorial Cairns
Throughout the mountains, stone cairns mark where dwarves fell — in the original conquest, during the rebellion, or in the years between. Pilgrims add stones to the cairns when they visit.

### The Sea Caves
At the base of the eastern cliffs, accessible only by boat or dangerous climbing, natural caves extend into the mountains. During the rebellion, these served as supply caches. Today, they're largely forgotten — and may still hold hidden materials.

## Modern Significance

### Pilgrimage Destination
Dwarves from across northern [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) visit the Meriver Mountains to honor the rebellion's heroes. The journey is difficult — no roads, steep terrain, harsh weather — which makes the pilgrimage more meaningful.

### Symbol of Resistance
When independence-minded dwarves speak of "the Mountains," they invoke the entire history of resistance: the original conquest, the years of suffering, the successful rebellion, the promise that dwarves will never be conquered again.

### Radical Dreams
Some young dwarves in [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) and [Slada](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md) speak of resettling the Meriver Mountains, establishing an independent dwarven territory beyond Crown authority. This remains a dream — the mountains are harsh, resources are limited, and provoking the Crown would bring reprisal.

### Scholarly Interest
The old holds contain Tharnic-era artifacts, pre-Tharnic dwarven craftsmanship, and records of the rebellion. Scholars occasionally mount expeditions, usually with dwarven guides who control what outsiders see.

## Dangers

### Natural Hazards
- Steep terrain with frequent rockslides
- Unpredictable mountain weather
- Predators (wolves, bears, mountain cats)
- Limited food and water in higher elevations

### Navigation
No marked trails exist. Finding the old holds requires local knowledge or extensive exploration. Getting lost in the mountains can be fatal.

### Protective Dwarves
Dwarves from [Slada](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md) and [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) consider the mountains sacred. Uninvited visitors — especially humans — may be "encouraged" to leave. This discouragement can be forceful.

## Access

### Getting There
From [Buchrior Hollows](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Buchrior%20Hollows.md): 40km east through dense forest, then into the foothills. The journey takes 2-3 days.

From the coast: Theoretically accessible by boat to the sea caves, but the cliffs are treacherous and the currents dangerous.

### Who Visits
- Dwarven pilgrims, especially from [Slada](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md) and [Yotherdon](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md)
- Independence movement leaders for private meetings
- Scholars with dwarven permission
- Adventurers seeking Tharnic artifacts
- Crown officials do not visit
