# Wynlants Cavity

---

## Geography

### Location
Wynlants Cavity lies 40km east of Plalisle Den, a few kilometers south of the main road. Unlike Plalisle, there is no path leading to the Cavity — reaching it requires wilderness travel through dense forest. The site sits approximately 42km west of Buchrior Hollows.

### Relationship to Other Sites
- **40km west:** Plalisle Den
- **42km east:** Buchrior Hollows (25km to the river, 15km past it)
- **30km northeast:** Borugham
- No direct path to any settlement

### Terrain
The Cavity is a natural cave system that Tharnic miners expanded when they discovered ore deposits within. The main entrance is a gaping hole in a forested hillside, partially concealed by undergrowth. Multiple secondary entrances exist — collapsed tunnels, natural fissures, and deliberate escape routes dug during the rebellion.

---

## History

### Tharnic Era (pre-730 C.C.)
The Tharnic Empire discovered the natural caves and expanded them into a mining complex. Dwarven slaves worked the deeper tunnels while the natural chambers served as storage and living quarters. The remote location made escape difficult — dense forest in every direction, no paths, no landmarks.

### The War of the Sundered Chains (710-730 C.C.)
When rebellion swept the north, Wynlants Cavity became a refuge. Escaped slaves from multiple mines converged here, using the caves as a staging ground. Dwarven rebels dug hidden passages connecting to the surface. Human sympathizers brought supplies from distant settlements.

The Tharnic garrison was overwhelmed by coordinated attacks from within and without. The overseers were killed; their equipment was repurposed for the war effort.

### Post-Empire Period
After the Tharnic collapse, Wynlants Cavity served various purposes:
- **Refuge:** During famines and wars, people sheltered in the upper caves
- **Storage:** Nearby settlements used the dry caverns for grain and goods
- **Hideout:** Bandits, fugitives, and rebels have used the Cavity over centuries
- **Pilgrimage:** Some dwarven families visit to honor ancestors who died here

### Modern Era
The Cavity is officially abandoned. The Crown considers it a curiosity, not a concern. In practice, the site sees occasional use:
- Dwarven pilgrims from Borugham and Yotherdon pay respects to the dead
- Smugglers cache goods in the deeper tunnels
- Young rebels meet in secret to discuss independence
- Scholars occasionally explore, seeking Tharnic artifacts

## Structure

### Upper Caves
Natural caverns near the surface, relatively accessible. These chambers feature:
- Old slave quarters, now collapsed
- Storage rooms with traces of ancient goods
- A natural spring providing fresh water
- Graffiti in Dwarvish — names of the dead, curses against slavers

### The Mining Tunnels
Deeper passages carved during Tharnic operations:
- Ore veins long since exhausted
- Cart tracks and rusted equipment
- Collapsed sections requiring careful navigation
- Cool, damp, and dark year-round

### The Hidden Passages
Escape routes dug during the rebellion:
- Narrow tunnels sized for dwarves
- Surface exits concealed in the forest
- Some connections to other sites are rumored but unconfirmed
- Deliberately confusing layout to trap pursuers

### The Shrine of Chains
A chamber deep within where rebels gathered before the final assault on the garrison. Today, broken chains hang from the walls — placed there by liberated slaves. Dwarven pilgrims add new chains, symbolizing continued remembrance.

---

## Current Uses

### Dwarven Pilgrimage
Families from Borugham, Yotherdon, and Slada visit Wynlants Cavity to honor ancestors. The Shrine of Chains is a sacred site. Pilgrims bring offerings — food, drink, tools, weapons — and recite names of the dead.

### Smuggler's Cache
The remote location and maze-like tunnels make the Cavity ideal for hidden storage. Goods moving between Kluimont and Braewood sometimes pass through here, avoiding Crown inspectors at Barge.

### Rebel Meetings
Young dwarves from moderate settlements sometimes gather in the Cavity to discuss independence, away from Crown observation. These meetings rarely produce action — mostly talk, drinking, and singing old liberation songs.

### Scholar's Interest
The Cavity contains Tharnic-era artifacts: tools, records, perhaps magical items. Academics occasionally mount expeditions, though the remote location and lack of paths discourages casual exploration.

## Dangers

### Natural Hazards
- Unstable tunnels that collapse without warning
- Deep shafts that drop into darkness
- Underground streams that flood during rains
- Bats, cave insects, and occasional predators

### Other Threats
- Smugglers who dislike witnesses
- Dwarven pilgrims who resent non-dwarven intrusion
- Whatever might lurk in the deepest, unexplored passages
- The forest journey itself — no path means easy disorientation

---

## Local Knowledge

Wynlants Cavity is known to:
- **Borugham:** As a pilgrimage site and place of ancestral significance
- **Barge:** As an old mine, supposedly empty, not worth investigating
- **Slada/Yotherdon:** As sacred ground where liberation began
- **The Crown:** As an abandoned curiosity, no strategic value

Most maps don't mark it. Finding the Cavity requires local guidance or determined exploration.
