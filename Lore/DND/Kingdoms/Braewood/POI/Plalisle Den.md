## Geography

### Location
Plalisle Den lies approximately 85km north of [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md), just off the main road to [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md). The area is less densely wooded than the surrounding forest, with exposed rock formations marking the old mine entrance. It sits about 20km from the northern coast.

### Relationship to Other Sites
- **40km east:** [Wynlants Cavity](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Wynlants%20Cavity.md) (another former Tharnic mine)
- **~70km northwest:** [Buchrior Hollows](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Buchrior%20Hollows.md)
- **~85km south:** [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md)

### Terrain
The Den occupies a hillside clearing where ancient excavations exposed bedrock. Scrubby vegetation has reclaimed much of the surface, but the main entrance — a carved stone archway — remains visible to those who know where to look.

## History

### Tharnic Era (pre-730 C.C.)
Like many sites in northern [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md), Plalisle Den began as a Tharnic mining operation. Dwarven slaves extracted iron ore from deep tunnels while human overseers maintained brutal discipline. The mine was abandoned during the War of the Sundered Chains when workers escaped to join the rebellion.

### Post-Empire Period
After the Tharnic collapse, the mine sat empty for decades. Local settlements occasionally used the upper tunnels for storage or shelter during harsh winters. Rumors of hauntings kept most away from the deeper levels.

### Balthazar's Arrival
At some point — the exact date is lost to history — the great wizard Balthazar claimed Plalisle Den for his own purposes. He transformed the abandoned mine into a magical vault, using the existing tunnel structure as the foundation for elaborate defenses.

Balthazar stored something precious here: **The Chart of the Threefold Way**, a star-charted codex of immense arcane significance. To protect it, he created a dungeon that responds to the intentions of those who enter.

## The Dungeon

### Magical Properties
Plalisle Den is no ordinary ruin. Balthazar enchanted the site with powerful detection magic that reads the intentions of anyone crossing the threshold:

- **Those seeking the Book:** The tunnels reconfigure to lead toward Balthazar's dungeon — a series of trials testing worth, sacrifice, and wisdom. This is the path adventurers must walk to reach the vault.

- **Those with other purposes:** The Den appears as a mundane abandoned mine. Collapsed tunnels, rusted equipment, perhaps some old bones. Nothing of interest. The magical dungeon simply doesn't exist for them.

This enchantment has protected the codex for centuries. Treasure hunters who've heard rumors find nothing. Scholars seeking the book must prove themselves worthy.

### Known Chambers
Based on accounts from those who've entered seeking the book:

**The Bat Room**
The first chamber features trapped floor tiles and an unlocked door. Stepping on the tiles triggers a swarm of bats including a larger guardian creature. Killing the guardian opens the way forward.

**The Counterweight Puzzle**
A mechanism involving chains and weights. The solution requires placing objects of sufficient mass in the correct receptacle. Many have failed this test through impatience or violence.

**The Peacebinding Hall**
A chamber that demands visitors leave weapons and magical foci behind. Stone guardians flank the passage, motionless unless the rule is violated.

**The Quad Fountain**
Four fountains where seekers must make personal sacrifices. The value of the sacrifice — measured in personal meaning, not material worth — determines what rewards are granted. Greater sacrifices unlock higher tiers of the vault's treasures.

**The Vault**
Where the Chart of the Threefold Way was kept, along with other artifacts Balthazar created or collected. A teleportation rune activates upon claiming the book, returning visitors to the surface.

## Current Status

### The Book Has Been Claimed
Recent events have seen a group of adventurers successfully navigate the dungeon and retrieve the Chart of the Threefold Way. They were teleported to [Sheyton](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Sheyton.md) upon completing the trial.

Whether the dungeon remains active — whether new treasures might appear, or whether Balthazar's magic has finally faded — is unknown. Some speculate the Den will collapse without the book's presence. Others believe Balthazar left contingencies.

### Local Knowledge
The nearest settlements know Plalisle Den as an old mine, possibly haunted. Few have reason to explore it. The party that claimed the book arrived by wagon from the south, fought wolves on the road, and left an injured companion with their horse before entering.

---

## Connections to Balthazar

### Who Was Balthazar?
Balthazar was a wizard of immense power who lived during or shortly after the post-Tharnic chaos. His works include:
- **The Chart of the Threefold Way** (the codex protected here)
- **Compass of Safe Passage** (also recovered from the Den)
- Various magical artifacts "gifted" to worthy individuals

The book can only be opened when submerged in water that has touched a magical artifact Balthazar created. This fail-safe ensures only those who understand his legacy can access its contents.

### Balthazar's Legacy
Information about Balthazar is fragmented. His work predates most recorded archives. Some believe he was a Tharnic-era mage who turned against the empire. Others claim he came later, building on ruins. The truth may be lost — or waiting in his codex.

## Adventure Notes

### For Those Who Came Before
The dungeon has been completed. The book is gone. But:
- Other artifacts may remain in untouched chambers
- Balthazar's magic may have reset, creating new trials
- The intention-detection spell might lead different seekers to different places
- The deeper Tharnic mine levels were never part of the dungeon — what lies below?

### Reaching Plalisle Den
- 85km north of [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) along the [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) road
- The turn-off is unmarked; locals may not know it
- The area is less wooded, exposed rock visible
- Wolves and other predators roam the surrounding forest
