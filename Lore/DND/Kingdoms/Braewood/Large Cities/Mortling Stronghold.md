>_All star ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

### **Name:**
Mortling Stronghold

### **Kingdom:**
[Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)

### **Settlement Type:**
Large City

### **Leader:**
**Lord-Marshal Caelric Ironward** — A stern human in his late 50s, [Ironward](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Lord-Marshal%20Caelric%20Ironward.md) is more general than governor. Appointed directly by the Crown, he holds both civil and military authority—a rare combination that speaks to Mortling's strategic importance. His word is law within the fortress walls, and he answers only to [King Tarvin Braeden IV](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/King%20Tarvin%20Braeden%20IV.md) himself.

### **Population:**
**80,000** (smallest of [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s large cities; Mortling prioritizes garrison space over civilian population)

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Human (68%):** Military families dominate, many tracing lineage to the fortress's founding. Officers, soldiers, and support staff.
- **Dwarf (18%):** Primarily engineers, siege weapon smiths, and fortification experts. The Stoneguard Engineers Guild maintains the walls.
- **Halfling/Gnome (6%):** Quartermasters, provisioners, and scouts. Essential to supply chain management.
- **Orc/Half-Orc (5%):** Mercenary companies and reformed raiders who proved their loyalty. Stationed in the outer districts.
- **Other (3%):** Specialists of various races—mages, physicians, diplomats.

### **Wealth:**
★★★☆☆ (3 stars)

- Wealth is secondary to defense—most resources funnel into fortifications, armaments, and military research.
- Crown subsidies keep the city functioning; Mortling produces little export beyond trained soldiers.
- Merchants here cater to military needs: weapons, armor, preserved rations, medical supplies.
- What luxury exists belongs to officers and visiting dignitaries; common folk live frugally.

### **Military:**
★★★★★ (5 stars — the most militarized large city in the kingdom)

- Serves as the first line of defense against [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md), with massive walls, barracks, and siege weapon stockpiles.
- The **Iron Legion** (12,000 active soldiers) maintains permanent garrison, with 30,000+ reserves callable within days.
- Nearly every family has military ties; children train from age twelve.
- Fortifications include triple walls, runed ballistae, underground bunkers, and a moat fed by diverted river waters.
- The **War College of Mortling** trains [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s officer corps here.

### **Happiness:**
★★★½ (3.5 stars)

- Despite financial limitations, pride in purpose and duty keeps morale steady.
- Community spirit is strong, bolstered by shared values of service and resilience.
- Veterans are honored, widows are cared for, and the phrase "Mortling stands" carries genuine weight.
- However, constant vigilance breeds tension—relaxation is viewed with suspicion, and outsiders are treated coldly.

### **Geography & Climate:**

**Position:**
- Located 55 km northwest of [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City, accessible via the Western Military Road through [Cleamond](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Cleamond.md).
- Sits on elevated ground overlooking the [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) border—the river marking the frontier runs just 8 km west.
- Slightly north of the capital due to the river's northward bend before it reaches the eastern ocean.

**Distances:**
- 138 km to northern coast
- 170 km to southern coast
- 116 km to eastern coast
- 56 km west to the Great [Lavalto](../../../Kingdoms/Lavalto/Lavalto.md) Lake (the massive lake surrounding [Lavalto](../../../Kingdoms/Lavalto/Lavalto.md) Island)
- 55 km southeast to [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City (through [Cleamond](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Cleamond.md))

**Terrain:**
- Built atop a granite plateau, the city rises above surrounding farmland.
- The western approach is deliberately cleared of trees for 5 km—no cover for invading forces.
- Rolling hills to the east provide natural barriers and watchpost positions.

**Climate:**
- Continental, with harsh winters and hot, dry summers.
- The exposed plateau catches fierce winds from the west.
- Snow lingers longer here than in the capital, and soldiers train in all conditions.

### **Economy & Trade:**

- **Primary Industry:** Military—weapons manufacturing, armor smithing, siege equipment, training facilities.
- **Secondary Industries:** Leather working (for armor), preserved food production, medical herbs and alchemical supplies.
- **Trade Partners:** [Cleamond](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Cleamond.md) (supplies flow in), [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City (recruits flow out), various dwarven smithing enclaves.
- **Notable Guild:** The **Stoneguard Engineers** — dwarven-led guild maintaining all fortifications, holding secrets of runed construction.
- **Crown Subsidies:** 40% of Mortling's budget comes directly from royal coffers; the city cannot sustain itself on trade alone.

---

### **History:**

- **Founded (410 C.C.):** Built as a forward outpost during early conflicts with [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md)'s mainland territories.
- **Expanded (620-680 C.C.):** Grew from fortress to city as border tensions increased; three major expansions added the current wall rings.
- **The Siege of Mortling (892 C.C.):** [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) forces besieged the city for 47 days; held successfully, becoming a symbol of [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) resilience.
- **War College Founded (1045 C.C.):** King Tarvin II established formal officer training; Mortling became the kingdom's military academy.
- **Modern Era:** Uneasy peace with [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) has reduced direct conflict but not vigilance. Cross-border raids still occur, and the garrison remains at full strength.

---

### **Notable Locations:**

- **The Iron Keep:** Central fortress and seat of Lord-Marshal [Ironward](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Lord-Marshal%20Caelric%20Ironward.md). Contains command chambers, war rooms, and the famed Map Hall showing all border intelligence.
- **The Triple Walls:** Mortling's defining feature—three concentric walls, each higher than the last. The outer wall (the Gray Line) is 30 feet high; the inner wall (the Black Line) rises to 60 feet.
- **The War College:** [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s premier military academy. Young nobles and common soldiers alike train here for 2-4 years before assignment.
- **The Stoneguard Foundry:** Massive dwarven-run smithy producing everything from caltrops to siege towers. The heat never dies here.
- **The Hall of Names:** Memorial to every soldier who died defending Mortling. Walls are covered in bronze plaques; new names are added in annual ceremony.
- **The Western Watch:** Observation tower on the highest point, looking directly toward [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md). Manned around the clock, messages relayed by signal fire.
- **Soldier's Rest:** The largest tavern district, where off-duty troops drink, gamble, and brawl. Fights are common but lethal violence is rare.

### **Key Figures:**

- **Lord-Marshal Caelric Ironward:** Human, late 50s. Supreme authority in Mortling, both military commander and civil governor. Veteran of three border wars, walks with a limp from an old wound. Respected but feared.
- **Commandant Yara Steele:** Human, 40s. Runs the War College with iron discipline. Rumored to have killed a [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) spy with her bare hands.
- **Master Engineer Borin Hammerfall:** Dwarf, 180s. Head of the Stoneguard Engineers. Knows every weakness in the walls—and how to fix them.
- **Quartermaster-General Tobias Marsh:** Human, 50s. Controls all supplies entering Mortling. Nothing moves without his approval.
- **Father-Commander Aldous Valorian:** Human, 60s. High priest of the military chapel, blessing soldiers before deployment. His sermons emphasize sacrifice and eternal honor.
- **Captain Renna Voss:** Human, early 30s. Rising star in the garrison, leads the elite Nightwatch unit patrolling beyond the walls.

### **Current Events & Hooks:**

- **The Tunnel Rumor:** Scouts report unusual activity near the river border—excavation sounds at night. Is [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) digging under the defenses?
- **Deserter Problem:** Three soldiers vanished last month. Desertion is rare in Mortling; something drew them away. Their barracks-mates speak of strange dreams.
- **Arms Shipment Missing:** A weapons caravan from the Stoneguard Foundry never reached the southern barracks. Forty swords, gone. Internal theft or external raid?
- **Kluimont Envoy:** A [Kluimont](../../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) diplomat has requested entry—unprecedented. Lord-Marshal [Ironward](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Lord-Marshal%20Caelric%20Ironward.md) suspects a trap; others see opportunity for peace.
- **The Old Siege Tunnels:** Beneath Mortling lie tunnels from the 892 siege. Officially sealed. Workers report sounds from below.

---

### **Relationships:**

- **Cleamond:** Supply lifeline—food, goods, and recruits flow through this town. Mortling treats [Cleamond](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Cleamond.md) well but expects priority service.
- **Braewood City:** Political superior. Lord-Marshal [Ironward](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Lord-Marshal%20Caelric%20Ironward.md) chafes at court politics but follows Crown orders.
- **Kluimont:** Adversary. Centuries of conflict; current peace is a watchful truce, not friendship.
- **Dwarven Enclaves:** Strong ties through the Stoneguard Engineers. Dwarves are respected here more than in most [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) cities.
- **Sheyton:** Trade connection via [Cleamond](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Cleamond.md). Supplies flow from [Sheyton](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Sheyton.md)'s markets northward.
