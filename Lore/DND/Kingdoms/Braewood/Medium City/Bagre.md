_All star-ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

## **Name:**

**Barge**

---

### **Kingdom:**

**Braewood**

---

### **Leader / Mayor:**

**High Bailiff Odrin Varrun**, a pragmatic dwarf loyal to the Crown but protective of northern autonomy. Former stone legion captain—steady-handed but blunt, known for his distrust of southern inspectors.

---

### **Population:**

**~12,000** within the walls; **~18,000** including the surrounding holds, trade outposts, and mining settlements.  
While technically smaller than Shayton, it serves as **the last major southern checkpoint before the Northern Marches**, giving it an importance far beyond its size.

---

### **Wealth:**

★★★★ (4 stars)

- Barge’s economy is built on **transit, trade, and tariffs**—everything passing north or south through the Iron Gate checkpoint is taxed.
    
- Its location between **Braewood City** and the dwarven mountain enclaves makes it a lifeline for ore, gems, and crafted metal goods.
    
- Wealth concentrates in the hands of guilds and merchants—especially **caravan brokers, miners, and smelters**.
    
- The common folk live modestly but stably; dwarven craftsmanship ensures no one goes cold for lack of tools or stone.
    
- Seasonal freezes sometimes choke the flow of trade, threatening winter shortages—but the people endure with grim resilience.
    

---

### **Military:**

★★★★½ (4.5 stars)

- The **Barge Watch** is more akin to a militia than city guards—half of them are veterans of the **Stone Legions**, the rest trained locals.
    
- A **fortified barracks and border gate** dominate the northern wall, complete with runed ballistae and boiling oil ports.
    
- Patrols operate well beyond the walls, keeping roads clear of bandits and ice trolls.
    
- Barge can hold its own for weeks under siege, and the dwarves take pride in saying: _“The mountains bow to no army.”_
    
- However, tensions between human and dwarven officers can undermine coordination in times of crisis.
    

---

### **Happiness:**

★★½ (2.5 stars)

- Life in Barge is hard and cold—both in weather and temperament.
    
- The people are proud, hardworking, and self-reliant, but **distrustful of outsiders** and weary of southern oversight.
    
- Tensions between dwarves, humans, and occasional northern traders (orcish, goliath, or goblinfolk) often spill into tavern fights.
    
- Festivals are brief and subdued, centred on ale, firelight, and the hammer’s song rather than revelry.
    
- Locals find joy in endurance and community loyalty—but peace feels fragile.
    

---

### **Current Climate:**

- **Border Tensions:**  
    The **Iron Gate checkpoint** is heavily patrolled. Trade remains brisk, but tempers are high—**northern traders claim harassment**, while southern inspectors accuse dwarves of **“losing” shipments and tax records.”** Both are partially right.
    
- **Racial Strain:**  
    Prejudice cuts both ways. Humans whisper about dwarven arrogance; dwarves complain of southern greed. Orcish and goliath travellers face scrutiny from both sides. The guard maintains order, but fights in the lower taverns are routine.
    
- **The Deep Frost:**  
    A **late and unnatural chill** has swept the region. Crops from nearby valleys are failing early, forcing the city to rely on grain imports. Old miners claim something ancient has stirred beneath the stone, “breathing cold.”
    
- **The Hammer and the Mask:**  
    Two forces quietly compete for loyalty in Barge:
    
    - **The Hammer Brotherhood**, a dwarven guild of veterans preaching unity through tradition and hard work.
        
    - **The Masked Lanterns**, a shadowy order of human traders who smuggle goods and bribe officials to keep commerce smooth.  
        Both claim to act for Barge’s prosperity—but rumours say their clashes are turning violent.
        
- **An Uneasy Stillness:**  
    The forges burn late into the night, caravans line up at dawn, and the Iron Gate clangs open each morning—but everyone feels it: **something is shifting in the North**.
