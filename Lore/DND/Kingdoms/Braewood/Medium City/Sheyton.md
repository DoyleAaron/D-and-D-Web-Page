_All star-ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

### **Name:**

Shayton

### **Kingdom:**

Braewood

### **Leader / Mayor:**

Lord-Mayor Vellan Harcrow (Crown-appointed), stern and numbers-first.

### **Population:**

**~14,000** within the walls; **~22,000** including surrounding hamlets and roadside estates. It’s the second-largest **medium-sized** settlement in Braewood, after Eaveton.

---

### **Wealth:**

★★★★½ (4.5 stars)

- Shayton thrives on trade—it's a bustling crossroads for wagons carrying grain, timber, dried goods, and spices between **Braewood City** and the scattered southern settlements.
    
- Caravans stopping for tolls, stabling, and market purchases funnel steady stream of coin into town.
    
- Guilds of carters, chandlers, and stoneworkers keep the wheel of commerce turning.
    
- It’s richer than most medium towns—but still misses the opulence of the capital or a true city.
    

---

### **Military:**

★★★½ (3.5 stars)

- The Watch is disciplined and well-armed, with halberds and patrols through the market and lanes.
    
- A compact barracks houses a modest garrison—enough to keep order, but not to repel a siege.
    
- Recent surges in thieves’ activity have forced extra patrols and checkpoints—but manpower is stretched thin, especially as Crown inspectors start monitoring.
    
- Solid, reliable defense—but not overwhelming.
    

---

### **Happiness:**

★★★ (3 stars)

- A strong civic identity and steady commerce bring contentment—most folk enjoy stable work, fair trade, and the relative safety of guild protection.
    
- However, the recent rise of the Red Scarves (a local thieves’ guild) and increased Crown scrutiny have soured morale:
    
    - Shopworkers fear extortion.
        
    - Citizens tense about surprise audits and enforcement.
        
- Religion offers solace—three competing shrines and an altruistic almshouse provide community anchors. The tone is reverent and cautious, rather than festive.


---

### **Current Climate:**

- **The Red Scarves:** A **cultish religious movement** that has taken deep root in Shayton. Outwardly, they seem harmless—even comforting: their gatherings are filled with women, children, and men who wear red scarves with pride. They preach unity, charity, and community service. They run soup kitchens, sing hymns in the lanes, and offer comfort to the poor.  
  - But there is an undertone of control. Membership grows fast, and dissenters feel a subtle pressure—social exclusion, loss of customers, whispers of being “against the light.”  
  - Their leaders are calm and soft-spoken, but no one seems able to name where their doctrine came from.  

- **Robberies on the Rise:** Shayton has become a **hotspot for theft and banditry**—caravans lose stock, warehouses are mysteriously light, and homes have been burgled. It’s unclear if this wave of crime is linked to the Scarves, opportunists, or outside forces.  

- **Crown Intervention:** With Shayton sliding into unrest, **extra guards from Braewood City** have arrived, and a **royal envoy** is coming to investigate. Some citizens welcome them; others fear escalation if the Scarves are challenged openly.  

- **A Town on Edge:** The streets look normal—markets bustle, shrines are busy—but behind polite smiles, there’s tension. Are the Red Scarves a benevolent faith, a mask for something darker, or simply a convenient scapegoat for Shayton’s troubles?
