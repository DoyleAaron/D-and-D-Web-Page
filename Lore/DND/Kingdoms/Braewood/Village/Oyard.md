>_All star ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

### **Name:**
Oyard

### **Kingdom:**
[Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)

### **Settlement Type:**
Village

### **Leader:**
**Warden-Captain Halvard Grimstone** — A severe, humorless dwarf in his 90s, appointed by the Crown to oversee the prison facility. He believes in absolute order and shows no mercy to prisoners or staff who break rules.

### **Population:**
**~900** (plus ~400 prisoners)

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Humans (45%):** Prison guards, administrators, service workers
- **Dwarves (40%):** Guards (many are "reassigned" from other posts), some prisoners
- **Other (15%):** Mixed — including some half-orcs who find steady work as guards

**Prisoner Population (~400):**
- Tharnic extremists (those who refused to accept the Empire's fall)
- Dwarves who "went too far" in nationalist activities
- Corrupt nobles too dangerous to exile, too connected to execute
- Occasionally: political prisoners the Crown wants forgotten

---

### **Wealth:**
★☆☆☆☆ (1 star)

- The Crown pays to maintain the prison, barely
- No legitimate trade or industry
- Guards spend their meager wages locally, keeping a few merchants alive
- Everything is expensive because no one wants to supply this place

### **Military:**
★★★★☆ (4 stars)

- The entire village exists to contain dangerous prisoners
- Heavy guard presence relative to population
- Walls around the prison are formidable; the village itself is unwalled
- Guards are well-trained but morale is low
- Escape attempts are rare — the isolation is as effective as the walls

### **Happiness:**
★☆☆☆☆ (1 star)

- No one wants to be here — not the guards, not the villagers, certainly not the prisoners
- Assignments to Oyard are punishment for military failures or political mistakes
- The isolation breeds despair, paranoia, and strange rumors
- The shadow of what the prisoners have done hangs over everything

---

### **Geography & Climate:**

**Position:**
Oyard is deliberately isolated:
- **14km up the road from Braewood City toward Ardismouth**, then **35km down a side road** to the southeast
- Surrounded by rocky, barren hills — poor farmland, no resources worth extracting
- The only road in is the only road out
- Miles from any other settlement

**The Approach:**
The side road to Oyard is maintained just enough to be passable. It winds through increasingly desolate terrain — sparse trees, rocky outcrops, eventually bare hills. The prison complex is visible from miles away, a stone fortress built into a cliff face.

**Key Distances:**
- **To Braewood City:** ~45km (by road)
- **To Ardismouth:** ~50km (by road)
- **To anywhere else:** too far to matter

**Terrain:**
Rocky, dry hills. Little grows here. The soil is thin over stone. In summer it's dusty; in winter it's wind-scoured. The surrounding area is almost uninhabited — a few goatherds, nothing more.

**Climate:**
Harsh by [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) standards. The hills block maritime moisture; rain is rare. Cold winters, hot summers. The wind never stops.

---

### **Economy & Trade:**

**Primary Industries:**
- **The Prison:** The only reason Oyard exists
- **Guard Support Services:** Taverns, suppliers, a healer, a chapel
- **Goat Herding:** The only agriculture the land supports

**Supply Chain:**
Everything Oyard needs must be brought in. The Crown sends supply wagons monthly. Prices are high because no merchant wants to make the journey. Guards supplement their diet with goat meat and whatever they can hunt (not much).

**Prison Labor:**
Prisoners work the quarry on the cliff face — breaking rock for no particular purpose except to keep them busy and exhausted. The stone isn't good enough to sell. The labor is punishment, not production.

### **History:**

**Tharnic Era (pre-730 C.C.):**
Nothing. This land was considered worthless even by the Empire's road-builders.

**Post-Empire Period:**
A few herders used the hills. No permanent settlement existed.

**Founding (~820 C.C.):**
When the [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) kingdom needed somewhere to put prisoners too dangerous for regular jails — Tharnic loyalists, dwarven extremists, nobles who'd committed unforgivable crimes — they chose the most isolated place they could find. Oyard was built specifically as a prison.

**The Debourg Connection:**
Some of the original prisoners were dwarves captured from [Debourg Grotto](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md) after that settlement's... transformation. They were the lucky ones — removed before whatever happened to the others. Most have since died. The few survivors are ancient, mad, and never speak of what they saw.

**Modern Era:**
Oyard continues its grim purpose. The Crown sends its embarrassments here. Guards are assigned as punishment. Prisoners are forgotten. The village survives because the Crown pays for it to survive, nothing more.

### **Notable Locations:**

- **Grimstone Prison:** The fortress-prison built into the cliff, capable of holding 500, currently at 400
- **The Quarry:** Where prisoners break rock; overlooked by armed guards on platforms
- **The Warden's Tower:** [Halvard Grimstone](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Warden-Captain%20Halvard%20Grimstone.md)'s residence, the highest point in the complex, with views of the entire valley
- **The Bitter Cup:** The only tavern, serving guards and merchants; cheerless but functional
- **The Chapel of the Forgotten:** A small shrine where guards pray and prisoners are rarely allowed

---

### **Key Figures:**

- **Warden-Captain Halvard Grimstone:** Severe dwarf administrator, believes absolutely in order and punishment
- **Lieutenant Seren Valk:** Human, second-in-command, rumored to be the only person [Grimstone](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Warden-Captain%20Halvard%20Grimstone.md) trusts
- **Prisoner 7:** An elderly dwarf from [Debourg Grotto](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md), real name unknown, kept in isolation, sometimes screams in the night
- **Father Aldric Mourning:** The prison chaplain, a kind man destroyed by years of trying to save the unsaveable
- **Merchant Ulric Harren:** Runs the supply wagon from [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City, the only regular visitor, knows more than he should

---

### **Relationships:**

**With Other Settlements:**
- **Braewood City:** The Crown controls the prison directly; local nobles prefer not to acknowledge it exists
- **Ardismouth:** Some guards are dwarves who were "reassigned" after the [Ardismouth](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Ardismouth.md) tensions — a warning to nationalists
- **Debourg Grotto:** Prisoners from there are kept here; the connection is not discussed

**With the Crown:**
Oyard is an embarrassment the Crown needs. It holds the people too dangerous to release, too connected to execute, too extreme to ignore. The Crown funds it reluctantly and inspects it rarely.
