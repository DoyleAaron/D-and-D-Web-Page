>_All star ratings (Wealth, Military, Happiness) are in **perspective to other settlements of the same size category**._

### **Name:**
Vorton

### **Kingdom:**
[Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)

### **Settlement Type:**
Village (Large — borders on small town)

### **Leader:**
**Harbormaster Edric Salthollow** — A weathered human in his late 50s who inherited the title from his father. Despite the grand name, he's essentially the village elder, managing the docks, settling disputes, and organizing the annual Tide Festival. Practical, salt-crusted, and deeply superstitious about the western waters.

### **Population:**
**~1,200** (large for a village; historically was much larger)

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Humans (65%):** Fishing families, dock workers, descendants of Tharnic garrison
- **Halflings (15%):** Boat-builders and net-weavers, prized for nimble hands
- **Dwarves (8%):** Stoneworkers maintaining the old Tharnic walls, largely apolitical
- **Gnomes (7%):** Tinkerers who maintain the lighthouse mechanisms
- **Other (5%):** Occasional half-orcs, sailors who settled down, oddities washed ashore

*Note: Unlike eastern northern [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md), Vorton's isolation from dwarven territories means racial tensions are minimal. The sea doesn't care about your blood.*

### **Wealth:**
★★☆☆☆ (2 stars)

- Fishing is the lifeblood — cod, mackerel, and the prized blackfin tuna that runs in autumn
- Salt-curing and smoking keeps fish valuable for the long journey to [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) and beyond
- Once-thriving trade docks now handle only local traffic; major shipping bypasses via rivers to Mortling/[Phine](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md)
- Coin is scarce; barter is common — fish for grain, repairs for lodging
- The village survives, but prosperity is a memory whispered by elders

### **Military:**
★☆☆☆☆ (1 star)

- No formal garrison — the old Tharnic watchtower is crumbling
- A volunteer "Shore Watch" of twelve able-bodied folk patrol the docks and walls
- Weapons are fishing spears, gutting knives, and a few rusted swords from the old days
- In true danger, they light the beacon and pray [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) sends help
- The sea itself is their best defense — treacherous rocks and unpredictable currents discourage invaders

### **Happiness:**
★★★☆☆ (3 stars)

- Life is hard but honest — there's pride in self-sufficiency
- Strong community bonds; everyone knows everyone's business (for good and ill)
- Elders remember grander times and grumble; youth dream of leaving for [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) or beyond
- The sea provides meaning, rhythm, and occasional terror
- Superstition runs deep — rituals to appease "what sleeps beneath the waves"

### **Geography & Climate:**
- **Position:** Westernmost point in all of [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md), tip of the northern peninsula
- **10 km west of Strane** — the only nearby settlement
- **~180 km from Barge** — the nearest major hub (via [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md), then south)
- **Peninsula geography:** Southern coast is mere kilometers away; the village sits where land narrows to a rocky point
- **Climate:** Cold sea winds year-round, frequent fog, harsh winters with salt spray freezing on stone. Summers are brief and prized.
- **Coastline:** Rocky cliffs to the west, small protected harbor to the south-facing cove
- **The Western Waters:** Open ocean facing away from the continent — occasionally ships are spotted on the horizon, but they never stop here anymore

### **Economy & Trade:**
- **Primary Industry:** Fishing (cod, mackerel, blackfin tuna, shellfish)
- **Secondary:** Salt-curing, fish smoking, minor boat repairs
- **Trade Partner:** [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) (10 km) — Vorton's only real market
- **Historical Trade:** Once received ships from distant waters; now those routes use Mortling/[Phine](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md)
- **Local Specialty:** "Vorton Black" — heavily smoked tuna that lasts months, prized by travelers
- **Barter Economy:** Coin is rare; most trade is goods-for-goods

### **History:**

**Tharnic Era (Pre-730 C.C.):**
- Founded as **Fort Vortanus** — a western watchtower and naval outpost
- Designed as first line of defense for the larger settlement of [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md)
- Significant garrison, deep harbor accommodating warships
- The great stone walls and lighthouse date from this era

**Post-Empire Decline (730-900 C.C.):**
- With the Tharnic Empire's collapse, military importance faded
- [Braewood](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s new rulers focused resources on developing [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md)
- Garrison withdrew; fort converted to fishing village
- Population peaked around 3,000 before gradual decline

**Modern Era (900 C.C.-Present):**
- Trade routes shifted to river-access ports (Mortling, [Phine](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md))
- Vorton became a backwater — too far, too isolated, too small to matter
- The old walls crumble slowly; the lighthouse still burns
- Fishing families persist, proud and stubborn as the rocks themselves

### **Notable Locations:**

- **The Tharnic Lighthouse:** Ancient stone tower on the western cliff, still operational. Gnome mechanics maintain the flame. On clear nights, the light sweeps across empty waters.

- **The Broken Quay:** Once a grand military harbor, now half-collapsed into the sea. Small fishing boats use the protected inner section. The submerged ruins are said to be haunted.

- **Salthollow Hall:** The harbormaster's home and de facto town hall, built from reclaimed Tharnic stone. Smells permanently of fish and [brine](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Widow%20Selna%20Brine.md). Village disputes are settled here over bowls of chowder.

- **The Drowner's Shrine:** A small altar on the cliff edge dedicated to appeasing the sea. Offerings of fish, coins, and occasionally blood are left here. No one admits to believing, but no one stops the practice.

- **Old Vortanus Walls:** Crumbling Tharnic fortifications encircle the landward side. Gaps have been patched with driftwood and prayer. The Shore Watch pretends they could hold.

### **Key Figures:**

- **Harbormaster Edric Salthollow:** Village leader, 50s human male. Knows every rock and current within twenty miles. Lost his wife to the sea; now married to Vorton itself.

- **Maren Tidecaller:** Elderly halfling woman who serves as the village's informal priestess, healer, and keeper of superstitions. Claims to hear whispers from the deep — most think she's mad, but everyone seeks her blessing before long voyages.

- **Jorik Ironbolt:** Dwarf stonemason, 80s, one of the last who knows how to repair Tharnic construction. Refuses to leave despite better opportunities in [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md). "These walls stood for empires. They'll outlast me."

- **Kessa Vane:** Human woman, early 20s, best fisher in the village and desperate to leave. Dreams of signing onto a real ship and seeing the world. Represents the restless youth.

### **Relationships:**

- **Strane (10 km east):** Dependent but resentful. [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) is Vorton's market, protection, and lifeline — but also its neglectful overlord. Vorton sends fish; [Strane](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Strane.md) sends occasional grain and total indifference.

- **Barge (180 km via path):** Distant and irrelevant. Few Vortonians have ever been that far south. [Barge](../../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md) might as well be another country.

---

### **Village Atmosphere:**

*Salt-wind. Creaking docks. The cry of gulls and the eternal murmur of waves against stone. Vorton smells of fish and woodsmoke, of [brine](../../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Widow%20Selna%20Brine.md) and damp wool. The streets — such as they are — wind between squat stone buildings patched with driftwood and hope. Faces are weathered, hands are calloused, and conversations trail off when strangers approach. The lighthouse beam sweeps the western dark, faithful and unanswered. This is a place that time and trade forgot, clinging to the edge of the world, watching an empty horizon.*
