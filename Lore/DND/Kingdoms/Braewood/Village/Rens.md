### **Name:**
Rens

### **Kingdom:**
Braewood

### **Settlement Type:**
Village

### **Leader:**
**Harbor-Elder Brennan Cole** — A gruff human man in his early sixties, tanned nearly brown by decades at sea. Former fishing captain who lost his boat to a reef and reinvented himself as the village's leader and mediator. His practical wisdom keeps the peace.

### **Population:**
**~1,100** permanent residents. The southernmost settlement in Braewood, perched at the edge of the kingdom.

### **Racial Demographics:**
- **Human (75%):** Fishermen, farmers, and craftsmen. Tough, independent folk.
- **Halfling (12%):** Net-makers, cooks, and provisioners. Essential support roles.
- **Dwarf (7%):** Boat-builders and metal-workers. The village's builders and repairers.
- **Other (6%):** Mixed travelers, sailors between ports, and those who came to the end of the road.

---

### **Geography & Climate:**

**Position:**
- Located on Braewood's southern coast, Rens is the southernmost settlement in the kingdom.
- 69 km from Osegas via the eastern road (37km to Frore turnoff, 5km more to Dalo turnoff, 27km more to Rens).
- The road ends here—there is nowhere further south.

**Road Network:**
- **To Eastern Road Junction (27km N):** The only road out of Rens.
- **To Dalo Turnoff (32km N):** 27km to junction, then 5km further.
- **To Frore Turnoff (37km N):** 27km to junction, then 5km, then 5km more.
- **Via Osegas (69km total):** The main connection to the rest of Braewood.

**Distances (Straight Line):**
- North: 320 km to the northern coast (80 km to Eaveton)
- South: On the coast (0 km)
- East: 14 km to the eastern coast
- West: 40 km to the western coast

**Terrain:**
- Rocky coastline with natural harbor formed by a curved headland.
- Scrubland and tough grasses cover the surrounding hills.
- The sea dominates the landscape—nothing blocks the view south.
- Shallow reefs offshore are both fishing grounds and ship hazards.

**Climate:**
- Maritime climate; mild year-round with constant sea breezes.
- Wetter than the interior; rain is common.
- Storm season brings powerful swells from the open ocean.
- The southern exposure means full force of weather systems.

### **Wealth:**
★★☆☆☆ (2 stars)

- Rens is remote, and remoteness limits prosperity.
- Fishing provides sustenance; some surplus is sold to traders.
- The village survives but doesn't thrive—too far from major markets.
- Coral and shells harvested from the reefs have modest value.
- Most wealth comes from passing ships that stop for repairs or shelter.

### **Military:**
★☆☆☆☆ (1 star)

- Minimal military presence. A small village watch.
- Distance from threats provides natural protection.
- Fishermen are hardy but not trained fighters.
- In emergency, the village would flee north toward Osegas.
- Occasional pirate or smuggler activity, but Rens is too poor to be worth raiding.

---

### **Happiness:**
★★★½ (3.5 stars)

- Life at the end of the road breeds independence and resilience.
- Strong community bonds—neighbors depend on each other.
- The Osegas tradition of mutual aid keeps everyone fed during lean times.
- Some residents feel forgotten by the kingdom, but most prefer the isolation.
- Those who stay genuinely love the sea, the sky, and the end of the world.

### **Economy & Trade:**

**Primary Industries:**
- **Fishing:** The village's primary occupation. The southern waters are rich but dangerous.
- **Boat Repair:** The last stop before open ocean; ships pay for repairs and provisioning.
- **Coral and Shell Trade:** Decorative items from the reefs have value in northern markets.
- **Salt Fish:** Preserved catches are traded north when fresh fish can't travel.

**Trade Relationships:**
- **Osegas (69km N):** Cultural center and primary market. Traders make the long journey.
- **Frore (57km via road):** Neighboring village; some cooperation on fishing and trade.
- **Dalo (via eastern road):** Shared fishing waters sometimes cause disputes.
- **Passing Ships:** Occasional vessels stop for supplies, bringing news and coin.

### **History:**

**Tharnic Era:**
- Founded as the Empire's southernmost outpost, watching the open ocean.
- A signal station communicated with ships and with settlements to the north.
- When the Empire collapsed, the isolation became total—no supplies came.

**The Osegas Salvation:**
- Rens was the furthest saved village, nearly forgotten in the chaos.
- An Osegas relief caravan arrived when the village was weeks from starvation.
- The memory of that desperate winter has never faded.
- Rens sends its finest coral to Osegas festivals in eternal gratitude.

**Post-Tharnic Era:**
- Slow recovery under Braewood rule.
- The village never grew large—the location limits population.
- Developed a reputation for toughness and self-reliance.

**Recent History:**
- Harbor-Elder Brennan Cole has led for twelve years.
- A reef claimed his fishing boat; he rebuilt his life on land.
- Young people leave more often than they used to—the road to Osegas is well-worn.
- Rumors of strange lights on the southern horizon concern some; most dismiss them.

---

### **Notable Locations:**

- **The Harbor:** A natural inlet protected by a curving headland. Small but serviceable for fishing boats and coastal traders.

- **Cole's Hall:** The Harbor-Elder's home and the village meeting place. Built from ship timbers salvaged after storms.

- **The Reef Gardens:** Tidal pools where coral and shells are harvested. Dangerous to the inexperienced—the currents are tricky.

- **The Signal Tower:** An old Tharnic structure on the headland, now used as a lighthouse. Maintained by a rotating watch.

- **The Last Tavern:** Exactly what the name suggests—the southernmost drinking establishment in Braewood. Popular with visiting sailors.

### **Cultural Notes:**

**The End of the Road:**
Rens takes pride in being the last place—the southernmost point of Braewood, the end of the kingdom's reach. There's nowhere further to run, so residents learn to stand their ground.

**The Storm Tradition:**
When major storms approach, the village gathers in Cole's Hall. Stories are told, songs are sung, and everyone waits together. No one faces the storm alone.

**The Southern Lights:**
Old Stormcrow Yentl insists she sees lights on the southern horizon—unexplained glows that appear on certain nights. Most villagers dismiss her claims, but sailors from distant ports have reported similar sightings.

**The Coral Gift:**
Each year, Rens sends its finest coral piece to the Temple Mount in Osegas. This tradition commemorates the salvation and reminds everyone that even the furthest village is still part of the family.
