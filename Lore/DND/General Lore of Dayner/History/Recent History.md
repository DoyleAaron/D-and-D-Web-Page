# Timeline (975–1225 C.C.)

### Year 975–990: The Post-Binding Era

- **975**:
    - **Braewood** emerges from a minor succession crisis. King Roden II officially ascends the throne after quelling a brief rebellion by his uncle.
    - **Kluimont** re-establishes control over the **Rimdiff Isles**, which had become semi-independent pirate havens during Braewood’s internal turmoil. This marks the start of Kluimont’s renewed push for maritime security.
- **980**:
    - **Islefield** invests heavily in new farmland irrigation. Elven nobility from the Shercam Woods sponsor magical greenways that increase crop yields, boosting the kingdom’s economic power.

### Year 1000–1010: The Western Tensions

- **1000**:
    - The dwarves of **northern Braewood** negotiate **partial autonomy** for their clans. They gain rights to manage their mines and hold dwarven moot councils. Braewood’s monarchy endorses this to secure a steady supply of minerals for the royal armory.
- **1002–1005**:
    - A brief conflict, **the Western Tensions**, flares up between Braewood border barons and Kluimont’s mainland enclaves, sparked by arguments over fishing rights and trade tariffs. There’s **no formal war**, but small skirmishes and raids escalate.
    - Islefield brokers a **ceasefire** in 1005, granting Kluimont limited free-trade passages along certain coastal routes.

### Year 1050–1080: Rise of Darewaeton Influence

- **1053**:
    - The **vampiric family** ruling **Darewaeton** on the Lavalto Volcano Isle solidifies control over a newly established **medium city** near the volcano’s foothills. They quietly eliminate a local merchant’s guild that threatened their secrecy.
- **1061**:
    - Adventurers from Braewood claim to have discovered ominous “blood rituals” in a cavern near the Lavalto caldera. Their allegations reach the Braewood court, but no formal action is taken—diplomats fear losing the island’s vital trade route.
- **1072**:
    - Darewaeton’s vampiric lords strike a **trade accord** with Islefield, offering precious volcanic minerals in exchange for safe maritime routes. Tensions lessen, but rumors of dark magic swirl.

### Year 1100–1110: The Dragon Rumors & Kluimont’s Arcane Boom

- **1101**:
    - Stories begin circulating of an **ancient brass dragon** being “subdued” in the great forest on Kluimont’s western flank. Kluimont’s leadership denies these rumors, but sightings of unusual arcane experimentation multiply.
- **1105**:
    - **Arcane academies** in Kluimont expand rapidly, surpassing even some of Braewood’s famed colleges. Scholars from Islefield and even dwarven smith-mages from northern Braewood visit under strict security protocols.
- **1109**:
    - A small scandal erupts when a Braewood diplomat is **expelled** from Kluimont’s main city. Whispers claim they tried to spy on hidden research labs or the rumored dragon site.

### Year 1130–1150: The Great Resource Dispute

- **1132**:
    - **Islefield** and **Braewood** clash over farmland boundaries near their mutual border. Islefield’s expanding farmland projects push into territories claimed by Braewood’s local barons.
- **1135–1138**:
    - **The Great Resource Dispute** becomes a trade war. Tariffs spike, caravans are harassed, and tensions build into a short, sharp conflict with minimal actual battlefield engagements.
    - **1140**: A truce is negotiated. Braewood recognizes some of Islefield’s farmland expansions in exchange for reduced grain prices and permission to traverse the area freely.

### Year 1170–1180: Scandal & Dynastic Shakeups

- **1172**:
    - A major scandal in Braewood reveals that **Prince Ralston**, a cousin to the current monarch, conspired with certain dwarven iron lords to divert weapon shipments. The scheme aimed to undermine the monarch’s power.
    - The dwarves disclaim knowledge of the treasonous intent; tensions rise between the monarchy and dwarf clans. Prince Ralston is exiled, and the dwarves pay restitution.
- **1179**:
    - The Islefield monarchy transitions peacefully to **Queen Atheliana**, an elf from the Shercam Woods line. Her coronation cements the woods-based aristocracy’s influence over the broader kingdom.

### Year 1190–1200: Lavalto’s Night of Red Embers
- **1191**:
    - A disastrous **volcanic flare** ravages farmland near Darewaeton. Townsfolk speak of **blood-red embers** lighting the sky and claim a sacrificial ritual awakened the lava flows.
    - Despite fear and rumors, Darewaeton’s vampiric rulers rally to rebuild the damaged port, offering financial aid (possibly sourced from hidden coffers or unscrupulous deals).
- **1195**:
    - Kluimont quietly sends an emissary to Darewaeton, possibly to barter for volcanic reagents used in advanced arcane experiments. A new trade channel opens, though details remain secretive.

### Year 1205–1215: Strains on Multiple Fronts
- **1207**:
    - **Braewood** experiences a major internal revolt in its northern regions, led by a rogue dwarven clan lord who accuses the monarchy of reneging on certain mining-rights agreements. The revolt ends swiftly thanks to Braewood’s large standing army, but distrust festers.
- **1210**:
    - **Orcish enclaves** in Kluimont’s mainland territory mount small raids against nearby Braewood border towns, claiming harassment and forced relocations by local human lords. Kluimont officially disavows the attacks, but orcish leaders blame Braewood aggression for inciting them.
- **1212**:
    - Islefield intensifies **coastal patrols** to block an uptick in Rimdiff Isle piracy. The elven navy’s presence leads to friction with Kluimont, which sees it as an infringement on open seas.

### Year 1220–1225: Modern Day Conflicts & Uneasy Peace

- **1220**:
    - An **unexplained phenomenon** (some say an “arcane storm”) strikes near Kluimont’s forest interior. In its wake, rumors swirl of the ancient brass dragon stirring—or even **breaking free**.
- **1222**:
    - Islefield finalizes a **defensive pact** with certain dwarven clans from Braewood, aimed at fostering cooperation if Braewood and Kluimont’s conflicts escalate. Braewood’s monarchy condemns this act as meddling.
- **1224**:
    - Darewaeton’s leadership entertains envoys from both Braewood and Islefield, each seeking advantageous trade deals or alliances. The vampiric household plays both sides diplomatically, stoking tension.
- **1225** (Present Day):
    - The continent stands at a precarious balance. **Braewood’s** monarchy wrestles with dwarven unrest, **Islefield** grows in wealth and allies, **Kluimont** protects its magical secrets amid swirling dragon rumors, and **Lavalto** remains an enigmatic wildcard whose alliances could tip the scales in any conflict.
