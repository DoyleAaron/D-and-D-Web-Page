# Gods of Dayner

## Overview

The people of Dayner worship a variety of deities, drawing from traditions both ancient and imported. While no single faith dominates the continent, certain gods have emerged as patrons of particular nations, professions, or causes.

---

## Major Deities

### Bahamut — The Platinum Dragon
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Justice, Protection, Honor, Metallic Dragons |
| **Alignment** | Lawful Good |
| **Symbol** | A platinum dragon's head in profile |
| **Worship Centers** | Kluimont, scattered temples across all kingdoms |

The god of justice and honor, Bahamut is revered by paladins, metallic dragons, and those who seek to protect the innocent. His followers believe in confronting evil directly and defending those who cannot defend themselves. In Kluimont, his worship takes on special significance given the kingdom's rumored connection to an ancient brass dragon.

**Notable Followers:**
- **Varin** — A cleric from Sord, Kluimont, who carries Bahamut's blessing

---

### Tyr — The Maimed God
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Justice, Law, War, Courage |
| **Alignment** | Lawful Good |
| **Symbol** | A balanced scale resting on a warhammer |
| **Worship Centers** | Braewood military chapels, courts of law |

Tyr represents justice tempered by wisdom and the courage to uphold what is right even when it costs everything. Unlike Bahamut's protective nature, Tyr's followers actively hunt evil and enforce law. Many judges, soldiers, and paladins in Braewood follow his tenets.

**Notable Followers:**
- **Father Halden** — A priest who fell to corruption and was later slain by adventurers in Dawerton

---

### Shar — The Mistress of Night
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Darkness, Loss, Night, Secrets, Forgetfulness |
| **Alignment** | Neutral Evil |
| **Symbol** | A black disk encircled by a purple border |
| **Worship Centers** | Hidden shrines, Lavalto (secretly) |

Shar is the goddess of darkness, loss, and secrets. Her worship is often clandestine, attracting those who have suffered great loss or who seek power through shadow. She teaches that the void is the true nature of existence, and that all things—joy, love, memory—must eventually fade into darkness.

**Notable Aspects:**
- Her worship sometimes overlaps with vampiric cults on Lavalto
- Some of her followers seek to spread despair; others find peace in accepting loss

---

### Asmodeus — The Lord of the Nine Hells
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Tyranny, Contracts, Power, Devils |
| **Alignment** | Lawful Evil |
| **Symbol** | A red pentagram or ruby-tipped scepter |
| **Worship Centers** | Lavalto (openly among vampires), hidden cults elsewhere |

The archdevil Asmodeus is not technically a god, but his power rivals divine beings and he accepts worship from those who seek infernal contracts. His followers believe in absolute hierarchy, unbreakable contracts, and the acquisition of power at any cost. On Lavalto, his worship exists openly among the vampiric nobility.

**Notable Followers:**
- **Father Maltrius Kael** — A vampiric high priest who bridges infernal and undead powers on Lavalto
- His influence may be connected to corrupted creatures in the region, including the Spehlina of Lavalto Lake

---

### Selûne — The Moonmaiden
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Moon, Stars, Navigation, Wanderers, Dreams |
| **Alignment** | Chaotic Good |
| **Symbol** | A pair of eyes surrounded by seven silver stars |
| **Worship Centers** | Coastal settlements, travelers' shrines, Islefield |

Selûne is the goddess of the moon, protector of travelers and those who walk in darkness while holding to the light. Her followers include navigators, wanderers, and those who work the night shift. She is eternally opposed to Shar, her dark twin.

**Notable Aspects:**
- Popular among sailors and those who travel by night
- Her temples often serve as safe havens for travelers

## Minor or Regional Deities

### The Drowned God
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Ocean Depths, Drowning, Ancient Seas |
| **Alignment** | Chaotic Evil |
| **Symbol** | A barnacle-encrusted altar, tentacles from deep waters |
| **Worship Centers** | Hidden coastal cults |

A mysterious entity worshipped by small cults in coastal regions. Its followers believe drowning is a return to their "true god" and prepare rituals to "call the depths." Whether this is a true deity or something else entirely is unknown.

---

### The Steady Flame (Multi-Faith)
| | |
|---|---|
| **Domain** | Varied (multi-faith tradition) |
| **Alignment** | Various |
| **Symbol** | A flame that never wavers |
| **Worship Centers** | Temple of the Steady Flame in Strane |

Not a single deity, but a tradition practiced in isolated communities where multiple faiths share one temple. Different shrines under one roof, united by the symbol of a steady flame representing persistence through hardship. Popular in northern Braewood's peninsula communities.

## Religious Institutions

### The Military Chapels of Braewood
Braewood's military maintains chaplains who serve multiple faiths, though Tyr and similar war-gods are most prominent. These chaplains provide spiritual guidance to soldiers and blessing for the kingdom's many military endeavors.

### The Infernal Courts of Lavalto
On Lavalto Isle, worship of Asmodeus exists openly among the vampiric ruling class. Father Maltrius Kael serves as the primary bridge between vampiric and infernal powers, conducting rituals in his volcanic manor.

### Scattered Temples
Most settlements maintain at least a small shrine to the most common gods. Larger cities often have dedicated temples, while villages make do with traveling priests or multi-faith "Steady Flame" style arrangements.

---

## Religious Tensions

- **Good vs. Evil:** Followers of Bahamut and Tyr actively hunt worshippers of Shar and Asmodeus
- **Secrecy:** Evil faiths operate in hiding across most of the continent, openly only on Lavalto
- **Multi-Faith Communities:** Isolated settlements often develop tolerance out of necessity, pooling religious resources
- **Corruption:** Even faithful servants of good gods can fall, as demonstrated by Father Halden's tragic fate
