## 🏰 Kingdom Identities (Quick Reference)

### BRAEWOOD 🛡️
- **Identity:** Militaristic human-majority monarchy, largest army
- **Vibe:** Roman Empire meets medieval England
- **Racial Mix:** 55% Human, 20% Dwarf (north), 5% Elf, 5% Halfling/Gnome, 5% Orc/Goblin, 10% Other
- **Key Tensions:** Dwarven autonomy disputes, northern frontier dangers, class divide
- **Ruler:** King Tarvin Braeden IV
- **Economy:** Military contracts, dwarven metal trade, agriculture (south)

### ISLEFIELD 🌾
- **Identity:** Elven-led wealthy kingdom, trade and diplomacy
- **Vibe:** Rivendell meets Renaissance Venice
- **Racial Mix:** 40% Elf, 40% Human, 5% Dwarf, 5% Halfling/Gnome, 3% Orc/Goblin, 7% Other
- **Key Tensions:** Human-elf class dynamics, resource disputes with Braewood
- **Ruler:** Queen Atheliana Aeristel (Elf, ~230 years old)
- **Economy:** Grain, timber, fishing, bridge tolls, maritime trade

### KLUIMONT 🔮
- **Identity:** Secretive arcane island kingdom
- **Vibe:** Wizard's Venice meets Tortuga
- **Racial Mix:** 40% Human, 25% Orc/Goblin, 5% Elf, 5% Dwarf, 5% Halfling/Gnome, 20% Other
- **Key Tensions:** Dragon rumors, pirate autonomy, mainland raids
- **Ruler:** High Archon Lysanthe Kilmora
- **Economy:** Arcane exports, smuggling, black market

### LAVALTO 🌋
- **Identity:** Independent volcanic isle, secretly vampire-ruled
- **Vibe:** Dishonored's Dunwall meets Transylvania
- **Racial Mix:** 60% Human, 10% Orc/Goblin, 5% each (Elf, Dwarf, Halfling/Gnome), 15% Other
- **Key Tensions:** Vampiric control, blood taxes, volcanic dangers
- **Ruler:** Lord Adrian Silwynn (Vampire)
- **Economy:** Volcanic minerals, exotic herbs, "neutral" trade port

## 🎭 Thematic Consistency Guidelines

### Naming Conventions
- **Braewood:** Anglo-Saxon/Old English feel (Eaveton, Mortling, Gitstin)
- **Islefield:** Elvish-influenced, softer sounds (Aeristel, Shercam, Praso)
- **Kluimont:** Harder consonants, mystery (Qrey, Rimdiff, Slonmore)
- **Lavalto:** Italian/Latin volcanic feel (Darewaeton, Flison)

### Architectural Styles
- **Braewood:** Stone fortifications, timber halls, military efficiency
- **Islefield:** Marble, vine-wrapped towers, integration with nature
- **Kluimont:** Crystal spires, arcane orbs, secretive compounds
- **Lavalto:** Basalt spires, volcanic glass, smoky grandeur

### Economic Principles
- Larger cities = more diverse economy
- Coastal = fishing + maritime trade
- Border = military + toll collection
- Interior = agriculture or mining
- Capital = administration + luxury goods
