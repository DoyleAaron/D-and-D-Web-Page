# Campaign Group Rules

These are the standing house rules agreed on by the group.

---

## Multiattack Rolls

When a creature has **Multiattack**, each attack is rolled separately.

- Roll **each attack roll to hit** separately.
- Roll **each damage roll** separately.
- Do not roll one attack and apply it to the whole multiattack.

This applies to PCs, enemies, and NPCs.

---

## Starting Combat With An Unreadied Attack Or Spell

If combat starts because someone suddenly attacks or casts a spell **without having readied it first**, that creature gets **advantage on initiative**.

This still applies even if the other side was tense, armed, or clearly expecting trouble.

### Example

Two sides are in a hostile conversation. No one has declared a readied action. One character suddenly attacks first.

- That creature rolls **initiative with advantage**.
- Combat then proceeds in initiative order.

This rule is meant to reward actually striking first, even in a standoff.

---

## Readied Attacks Before Initiative

If a character has **specifically readied an attack or spell before combat begins**, that readied action resolves **before normal initiative order**.

- The readied attack or spell happens first.
- Then initiative is rolled.
- Combat continues in initiative order after that.

This means a properly readied opening action can effectively **jump ahead of initiative**.

### Important Clarification

If an attack or spell was already readied, use the **readied action rule**, not the **unreadied first-strike advantage rule**.

In other words:

- **Unreadied first strike:** gets **advantage on initiative**
- **Readied first strike:** happens **before initiative**

---

## Surprise

If one side is genuinely caught off guard, use **surprise** normally.

A surprised creature:

- cannot move or take an action on its first turn of combat
- cannot take a reaction until that first turn ends

---

## Readied Actions And Surprise Together

If a creature or group had a valid **readied action** and the opposing side is also **surprised**, the order works like this:

1. The readied opening attack or spell resolves first.
2. Initiative is rolled.
3. Surprised creatures still lose their first turn as normal.

So surprise does **not** cancel a properly readied opening action. It stacks with it.

---

## Unreadied First Strike And Surprise Together

If no action was readied, but one side attacks from a position that should count as surprise:

1. The attacker rolls **initiative with advantage**.
2. Combat begins in initiative order.
3. Creatures who are surprised still lose their first turn as normal.

So an ambush without a readied action can still gain both:

- **advantage on initiative**
- **surprise on the target's first turn**, if the DM rules the target was truly surprised

---

## Quick Summary

| Situation | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| **Multiattack** | Every attack and damage roll is separate |
| **Unreadied first attack** | Attacker gets advantage on initiative |
| **Readied first attack** | Resolve it first, then roll initiative |
| **Surprise** | Surprised creatures lose their first turn and reactions until that turn ends |
| **Readied action + surprise** | Readied action still happens first, then surprise applies normally |
