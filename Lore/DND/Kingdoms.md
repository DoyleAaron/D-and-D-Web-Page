## General Overview

# Braewood
### Geography & Climate

- **Largest & Most Populous Realm**
    - Braewood dominates the **eastern** and parts of the **central** mainland. It’s known for its sprawling territory, with a great **river** winding through key trade cities.
    - The **southern portions** of Braewood are fertile enough to support **cattle ranches** and **modest agriculture** (not as rich as Islefield, but still viable).
    - The **northern** expanse becomes more **dry and desolate**, featuring dusty hills, rocky outcrops, and scattered canyons.

### Politics & Culture

- **Continental Capital**
    - Braewood hosts the recognized **capital** (or at least the largest seat of power) for the entire continent, though it’s not necessarily recognized as an “imperial capital” by Islefield or Kluimont.
    - Its monarchy or ruling council presides over huge swaths of land, with local governors or barons in each major city.
- **Strength in Numbers**
    - Braewood boasts the **biggest standing army** by sheer manpower, drawing on a populous domain.
    - The realm’s cities are somewhat **independent** of one another due to long travel times, so local militias are common. Trade routes can be perilous, forcing each city to develop self-sufficiency.
- **Human Majority & Racial Tensions**
    - Humans form the **vast majority** of Braewood’s population. Other races are legally permitted, but prejudice runs high, particularly in **northern frontier** towns.
    - Elves and dwarves with strong ties to trade or government usually fare better, but half-orcs, tieflings, and other “less accepted” races may face real hostility in certain regions.

# Islefield
## General Overview

### Geography & Climate

- **Resource-Rich Heartland**
    - Islefield occupies much of the **southern mainland** of the continent. Rolling plains, mild coasts, and broad rivers provide ample farmland and fishing grounds.
    - Warm and **humid** climate near the coasts, with slightly drier conditions inland.
- **Shercam Woods & Prairie Kingfisher Grove**
    - In the **northeastern** part of Islefield’s domain lie the Shercam Woods—dense forests teeming with wildlife and strongly influenced by elven traditions.
    - The **Prairie Kingfisher Grove** sits just south of the main city Islefield, a transitional zone of tall grasses and sparse groves and trees.
    - Both areas are **heavily Elven** in culture and population—many settlements here feature elf-crafted architecture that blends into the environment.
-  **Irowarf Heights**
    - While it’s **not** the tallest range, it’s the largest in terms of overall span—creating travel obstacles and harsh desert-like conditions in its rain shadow.

### Politics & Culture

- **Elven-Run Kingdom**
    - Islefield’s ruling class is predominantly **elven**, with a well-established monarchy or council that traces its lineage through centuries.
    - The **southern portion** of Islefield (including two villages and a couple of medium cities linked by a main bridge to the continental mass) is about **60–70%** elves, which is notably high.
    - This doesn’t preclude humans and other races from living there—particularly around trade hubs and farmland—but elven influence permeates law, art, and governance.
- **Resource Leverage**
    - The kingdom’s abundant **lumber**, **grains**, and **coastal trade** routes bring it wealth and political sway.
    - Islefield is known for forging **alliances** through diplomacy and resource trade rather than brute force.
- **Human/Elven Relations**
    - Outside the heavily elven south, the rest of Islefield is more **human-centric**, though elves remain a respected, if sometimes distant, nobility.
    - Most humans in Islefield regard the elves with a mixture of awe and practicality—elves bring stability and prosperity, so day-to-day relations are generally **amicable**.

# Kliumont
## General Overview

### Geography & Climate

- **Core Island (Northwest)**
    - The kingdom’s namesake, **Kluimont**, sits on a large island off the continent’s northwest coast.
    - A **wide, dense forest** stretches across the western flank of the island—around 213 miles of old-growth trees, rumored to be home to fey, hidden enclaves, and a subdued ancient brass dragon.
    - The climate is **warm** and pleasantly breezy due to surrounding seas. Rain is moderate, and soil fertility is above average in the cleared coastal regions.
    - **City of Qrey** is located on the **northernmost tip** of this island. Although smaller in population compared to Kluimont itself, Qrey serves as a strategic harbor and lookout post.
- **Rimdiff Isles**
    - A chain of **five** smaller islands scattered to the east and slightly south of the core island.
    - Ecosystems here range from rocky shorelines to lush coastal jungles; many local fishers and pirates call these isles home.
    - These islands are partly claimed by Kluimont but maintain a degree of autonomy, with some islanders more loyal to local chieftains or pirate lords than to the crown.
- **Mainland Territories**
    - Kluimont retains a slender strip of land on the continent’s northwest edge. Though technically part of the kingdom, it’s often overshadowed by Braewood to the east and by the sea-lanes connecting the main island.
    - Orc and goblin settlements are notably tolerated here—an unusual policy in a land where such races often face hostility. This leads many outcast groups to settle in Kluimont’s lesser-patrolled frontier.

### Politics & Culture

- **Obscurity & Magical Advancement**
    - Kluimont’s main city is famously **secretive**. Outsiders need invitations or special permits to enter. Rumors attribute the kingdom’s advanced arcane knowledge to an ancient brass dragon kept under magical restraint somewhere in the western forest.
    - Because of this secrecy, the kingdom is often **underestimated** politically—other realms know little of Kluimont’s capabilities beyond hearsay and rumor.
- **Military Standing**
    - Widely considered the weakest kingdom **in raw military strength**. Kluimont fields fewer standing troops and focuses more on arcane experimentation and magical defense.
    - However, this perception can be deceptive; should Kluimont unleash its arcane projects or allied monstrous races, they could pose a serious threat.
- **Social Ties with “Monstrous” Races**
    - A relatively higher acceptance of goblins, orcs, and other typically marginalized folk. Many find refuge in the Rimduff Isles or mainland enclaves.
    - Tensions exist within the populace—some Kluimonters fear these “outsider” settlements, but the kingdom’s rulers exploit the mutual benefits of a broader labor force and alliance-building.

# Lavalto(Independent state)
## General Overview
### Geography & Key Settlements

- **Central Volcano Island**
    - The **Lavalto Volcano** towers in the center of the sea, surrounded by the three major kingdoms but **claimed by none**.
    - Volcanic soil can be surprisingly fertile along the lower slopes, supporting specialized crops and exotic wildlife.
- **Darewaeton**
    - The largest city on the Lavalto isle, ruled by a **powerful family**.
    - Two other settlements—a **medium city** and a **town**—are also under this family’s control, effectively giving them a small mini-kingdom on the volcanic isle.
    - They maintain neutrality from Braewood, Islefield, and Kluimont through **tight-lipped diplomacy** and rumored threats of sabotage or curses should any kingdom invade.

### Politics & Dark Secrets

- **Vampiric Elite**
    - The ruling family are **secret vampires**, conducting **dark rituals** within hidden caverns near the volcano’s caldera.
    - They present themselves as refined aristocrats or well-to-do merchants, and as the unchallenged authority on their island.
    - Rumors swirl of **occult gatherings** and strange lights on the volcano’s slopes, but none of the main kingdoms have enough proof—or nerve—to press an invasion.
- **Regional Influence**
    - Darewaeton profits by trading unique volcanic minerals and rare magical herbs. Passing trade ships often stop for supplies; in exchange, the vampiric lords keep the waters relatively safe from piracy.
    - Adventurers occasionally arrive, lured by legends of hidden catacombs or the possibility of confronting the island’s rumored monsters. Few return to confirm or deny these tales.

- Known for towering basalt spires, smoky streets lit by enchanted lanterns, and whispers of catacombs beneath the city that lead to ancient lava-choked chambers.
