## Historic Characters

Name: General Arach Tharne
**Age**: Deceased (lived around 650 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human (Tharnic Imperial)  
**Backstory**:
- A brutal commander under the Tharnic Empire, Arach Tharne specialized in cracking down on dwarven revolts.
- He led the **Battle of Iron Break**, enslaving countless dwarves to fuel the Empire’s foundries.

**Importance**:
- His name symbolizes the Empire’s cruelty. Many dwarven clans pass down stories of his atrocities, fueling the hatred that led to the War of the Sundered Chains.

Name:General Yon Garathorn
**Age**: Deceased (lived ~1080–1160 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human (Braewood)  
**Backstory**:
- Rose through Braewood’s military ranks, renowned for cunning strategies during the **Great Resource Dispute (1135–1140 C.C.)** against Islefield.
- Credited with orchestrating mobile pike formations and fortifications along contested farmland.

**Importance**:
- His moderate success in the dispute allowed Braewood to **retain critical farmland** and secure new trade agreements.

Name:Grand Inquisitor Pellaro
**Age**: Deceased (executed ~728 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human (Tharnic Imperial)  
**Backstory**:
- A fanatical official responsible for hunting and “re-educating” rebel sympathizers, infamous for witch hunts and torturous inquisitions.
- Captured at **Barash Gorge** and tried by a dwarven tribunal for crimes against free peoples.

**Importance**:
- Symbol of the Empire’s religious zeal and brutality; his public execution became a final moral victory for the rebellion.

Name:High Thane Broldir Flameforged

**Age**: Deceased (active 710–730 C.C.)  
**Race**: Dwarf  
**Backstory**:
- United scattered dwarven clans against the Tharnic Empire after witnessing decades of enslavement.
- Famous for liberating Treadfast Hold and personally slaying Legate Sienna Valtoris during the Sundered Chain Rebellion.

**Importance**:
- Revered as the **liberator** who spearheaded the War of the Sundered Chains, laying the foundation for dwarven autonomy in the north.

Name:Imperial Warmaster Dravarian
**Age**: Deceased (died around 720 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human (Tharnic Imperial)  
**Backstory**:
- Tasked with crushing the allied forces during the War of the Sundered Chains; commanded the Empire’s largest legion.
- Underestimated the dwarven-elven coalition and was killed at the **Hammerfall Uprising**.

**Importance**:
- His defeat marked the **beginning of the Empire’s collapse**, giving rebels a moral and strategic victory.

Name:Legate Sienna Valtoris
**Age**: Deceased (died around 715 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human (Tharnic Imperial)  
**Backstory**:
- Ruthless governor of Treadfast Hold who enforced extreme punishments on rebellious dwarves.
- Known for torturous methods and public executions to cow local populations.

**Importance**:
- Her defeat by Broldir Flameforged at Treadfast was a **major turning point** in the dwarven uprising, proving the Empire could be toppled.

Name:Pirate Queen Aris “Steel-Eye”
**Age**: Deceased (died ~802 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human  
**Backstory**:
- Feared ruler of a pirate confederacy controlling treacherous reefs. Known for a distinct steel eye-patch covering a lost eye.
- Challenged Sea Lord Helmar Lynth for coastal supremacy and lost her flagship in battle.

**Importance**:
- Her defeat allowed Helmar Lynth to unify the region under a single banner, paving the way for Kluimont’s formation.


Name:Princess Araleth Oakheath
**Age**: Deceased (lived approx. 690–770 C.C.)  
**Race**: Elf  
**Backstory**:
- Hailing from an influential elven house in the southern forests (precursor to Islefield), she provided archer regiments and druids to aid dwarven rebels.
- Helped orchestrate the Hammerfall Uprising, coordinating multi-racial alliances.

**Importance**:
- Celebrated as a **diplomatic bridge** between elves and other races. Her involvement solidified elven-human-dwarf cooperation against the Empire.

Name:Sea Lord Helmar Lynth

**Age**: Deceased (lived ~770–830 C.C.)  
**Race**: Human (proto-Kluimont)  
**Backstory**:
- United scattered maritime fiefdoms and pirate-controlled coasts, forging the early structure that evolved into **Kluimont**.
- Achieved fame by defeating Pirate Queen Aris “Steel-Eye” at the **Naval Battle of Sunken Crags**.

**Importance**:
- Considered the **founder** of Kluimont’s naval tradition, setting the stage for the kingdom’s maritime dominance.


## Modern Characters
name:  High Archon Lysanthe Kilmora
**Age**: Appears early 50s but rumored older (Human / Possibly with extended arcane lifespan)  
**Race**: Human (Kluimont)  
**Backstory**:
- Ascended to rulership of Kluimont, emphasizing arcane research and forging the **Arcane Synod**.
- Responsible for intensifying the secrecy around Kluimont’s rumored subdued brass dragon, especially after the **Arcane Storm of 1220 C.C.**

**Importance**:
- **Central authority** in Kluimont’s magical advancements. Holds the key to unlocking or containing the kingdom’s draconic arcane power.

name:  King Roden II
**Age**: 110(Hasn't been seen in public for years)
**Race**: Human (Braewood)  
**Backstory**:
- Preceded King Tarvin Braeden IV by several decades; ascended to quell a familial rebellion (circa 975 C.C.).
- Remembered for stabilizing Braewood after internal feuds and forging early treaties with dwarven clans.

**Importance**:
- Established the **foundation of Braewood’s modern governance**, ensuring that his heir (or successor) inherited a relatively unified realm.

name:  King Tarvin Braeden IV
**Age**: Late 50s (Human)  
**Race**: Human (Braewood)  
**Backstory**:
- Descendant of the Braeden warlords who shaped modern Braewood. Focuses on strengthening the kingdom’s military and maintaining dwarven alliances (though tensions persist).
- Grappling with northern dwarven autonomy issues and the spread of monstrous threats near borders.

**Importance**:
- **Head of the largest kingdom**, with authority over Braewood’s diverse regions and responsibility for forging precarious peace among dwarven enclaves.

name:  Lady Seraphina Caylester
**Age**: Unknown (Immortal Vampire; active 1061 C.C. to present)  
**Race**: Vampire (Human origin)  
**Backstory**:
- A high-ranking member of the vampiric nobility ruling **Darewaeton** on Lavalto Isle, rumored to practice dark rituals.
- Led the defense against a Braewood expedition in the **Darewaeton Purge** (1061 C.C.), where she supposedly slew Knight-Captain Stravus.

**Importance**:
- Maintains the **island’s secrecy** regarding vampiric practices and wields considerable influence over Lavalto’s politics.

name:  Lord Adrian Silwynn
**Age**: Unknown (Immortal Vampire; active 1191 C.C. to present)  
**Race**: Vampire  
**Backstory**:
- Patriarch of a prominent vampiric line in Darewaeton, orchestrated the city’s recovery after the **Night of Red Embers** (1191 C.C.).
- Known for lavish spending on rebuilding efforts and rumored to hold dark rituals in hidden lava caverns.

**Importance**:
- Serves as one of **Lavalto Isle’s power brokers**, shaping diplomatic relations with other kingdoms through trade deals and hidden machinations.

name: Prince Ralston
**Age**: Mid-40s (if still alive)  
**Race**: Human (Braewood)  
**Backstory**:
- Cousin to the ruling line in Braewood, conspired with dwarven iron lords to undermine the monarchy (~1172 C.C.).
- Exiled after his treachery was exposed, rumored to be living abroad with a band of loyal followers.

**Importance**:
- Symbol of **royal betrayal** within Braewood’s court. Some believe he still plots to reclaim power or sabotage the monarchy from the shadows.

name: Queen Atheliana Aeristel
**Age**: ~230 (Adult Elf)  
**Race**: Elf (Islefield)  
**Backstory**:
- Crowned roughly 1179–1180 C.C., part of an ancient Shercam Woods lineage.
- Oversaw expansions in farmland and trade alliances, particularly after the Great Resource Dispute with Braewood.

**Importance**:
- **Modern ruler of Islefield**, guiding the kingdom’s prosperity through resource management and diplomatic ties.

## player characters
**Name:** Blorf of Clan Beren

**Age:**  
75 (young adult for a rock gnome, whose lifespan is typically 300–350 years)

**Class:**  
Bard

**Level:**  
1

**Origin/Home:**  
Iroware Heights near Yhey (mountainous region)

**Appearance:**

- **Height:** 3'5" (104 cm)
    
- **Weight:** 46 lbs (21 kg)
    

Blorf is a tough, wiry rock gnome. Despite his diminutive stature, he sports a striking appearance:

- **Wild ginger hair:** Worn proudly as a symbol of his rebellious nature.
    
- **Sallow skin:** Etched with deep wrinkles that hint at his extensive adventures.
    
- **Piercing green eyes:** Adapted to the dim light of underground spaces.
    
- **Clothing & Armor:** Simple, earth-toned fabrics and natural leather armor allow him to move quickly and quietly.
    
- **Distinctive Marks:** His left hand is missing his pinky and ring finger—lost during a violent confrontation with bandits.
    
- **Earrings:** His long, pointed gnome ears are adorned with carefully crafted piercings ranging from simple studs to elaborate hoops.
    

**Backstory:**  
As a young gnome, Blorf was taken from his home in the Iroware Heights by brutish orcs. Captured and sold into the grim underworld of magical creature trafficking, he endured harsh conditions, cramped cages, and cruel experiments. Refusing to surrender his hope, Blorf used his keen intellect and quick wit to forge alliances with fellow captives. After months of careful planning, he orchestrated a daring escape. Now free, Blorf has dedicated himself to honing his skills as a crafty adventurer and self-proclaimed skilled bard, determined to help others and seek justice against those who traffic in the lives of magical beings.

**Personality:**  
Resourceful, resilient, and rebellious, Blorf’s sharp wit and optimistic outlook belie the hardships he’s endured. His adventurous spirit drives him to explore dangerous corners of the world, and he values freedom and self-reliance above all.

**Traits:**

- **Nimble and Quick:** His small stature and agile reflexes make him adept at navigating cramped spaces and wielding his slingblade with impressive accuracy.
    
- **Observant:** His piercing green eyes miss little, a vital trait for both surviving traps and uncovering hidden secrets.
    
- **Rebellious Nature:** A proud streak runs through him, symbolized by his wild ginger hair and refusal to be subjugated.
    
- **Scarred but Determined:** The loss of two fingers on his left hand is a constant reminder of past hardships—and fuels his resolve to never be a victim again.

**Name:** Leontius of Osegas

**Age:** 26 (mid-twenties)

**Class:** Cleric (Peace Domain)

**Level:** 1

**Origin/Home:** Osegas, a humble town south of Braewood near Gisten and Eaveton

**Appearance:**

- **Height:** 6'5" (195 cm)
    
- **Weight:** 245 lbs (111 kg) Leontius is a towering figure, large in stature with a muscular build sculpted from years of wielding a heavy executioner's axe:
    
- **Sacred Blindfold:** Marked with the holy symbol of Serenia—an open hand draped with cloth—covering his eyes yet granting him divine sight.
    
- **Clerical Robes:** Simple and modest, adorned subtly with symbols of forgiveness and peace.
    
- **Scarred Back:** Numerous whip marks, painful reminders of his brutal past under the crown's harsh discipline.
    
- **Gentle Demeanor:** Contrasts his imposing physical presence, reflecting the peace he now seeks to embody.
    

**Backstory:** From a young age, Leontius was forced into the grim role of executioner, a legacy imposed by generations of his family serving the crown. Recognized early for his remarkable skill, he was transferred at fifteen from Osegas to serve directly under King Tarvin Braeden IV in the royal capital. Life there was brutal; seen merely as a weapon, Leontius suffered constant surveillance and harsh punishments. Haunted by the faces of those he executed, he inwardly despised his role.

His salvation came from Serenia, The Veiled Heart, goddess of forgiveness, who appeared to him offering a chance at redemption. Accepting her offer, he renounced violence, taking up a sacred blindfold symbolizing his commitment and receiving the gift of divine sight. His gift is conditional—should he ever intentionally harm another, it will vanish forever.

Now a Cleric of Peace, Leontius travels offering guidance and redemption, helping those burdened by guilt and sorrow.

**Personality:** Leontius is gentle yet solemn, deeply introspective, and committed fully to Serenia’s teachings. He continually battles his violent past, striving daily to uphold his vow of peace.

**Traits:**

- **Divine Sight:** Sees clearly through darkness due to Serenia’s blessing, though contingent on his vow of non-violence.
    
- **Gentle Giant:** His intimidating physical presence contrasts his peaceful intent and compassionate heart.
    
- **Bearer of Burdens:** Deep empathy drives him to alleviate the suffering and guilt of others.
    
- **Constant Vigilance:** Always wary of his capacity for violence, his past actions fuel his determination to maintain peace at all costs.

**Name:** Amdir Aeristel

  

**Age:**  

124 (Roughly age 28 for a human male)

  

**Class:**  

Wizard

  

**Level:**  

1

  

**Origin/Home:**  

Shercam Forest but was banished and now resides in the small town of Ansdale

  

**Appearance:**

  

- **Height:** 6'7" (205 cm)

- **Weight:** 160 lbs (72 kg)

  
  

Amdir is a tall, menacing elf. He is an astonishing 205cm tall but very slender in frame:

  

- **Flowing ice white hair:** He has this hair as is common of elves of his lineage.

- **Pale lilac skin:** He has perfect but nearly gaunt features with his pale lilac hue.

- **Piercing blue eyes:** Adapted to the dim light of dark forests.

- **Clothing & Armor:** Despite his exilhilation, he still flaunts the royal robes as he can't accept his fate.

- **Distinctive Marks:** No distinct marks apart from his large and pointy elven ears.

  

**Backstory:**  

Once a gilded heir to the storied Aeristel bloodline of Islefield, Amdir’s future was etched in silver and song—until he turned from the light. His obsession with forbidden power, shadow-bound tomes, and the art of necromancy led to his banishment by none other than his own aunt, Queen Atheliana Aeristel. Stripped of title and home, Amdir became a cautionary tale whispered through the ivy halls of Shercam.

  

But exile was not the end—it was ignition.

  

Furious at his dismissal and intrigued by the arcane undercurrents emanating from Kluimont, Amdir sought an audience with their hidden magical elite, hoping to uncover the truth behind the rumored subjugation of dragons and delve deeper into his craft. Denied access, and unwelcome in courtly circles, he now resides in the quiet town of Ansdale—a seemingly unremarkable settlement, yet close enough to the heart of Kluimont’s secrets to serve his ambition.

  

In Ansdale’s forgotten corners, Amdir conducts his research with unwavering focus. His knowledge of dark magic is formidable, and whispers speak of strange lights in the woods and bones that shift beneath the earth. He is no longer the golden heir—but a shadow reborn.

  

Amdir Aeristel walks a razor’s edge between arcane mastery and damnation. And he wouldn’t have it any other way.

  

**Personality:**  

Amdir is sly and calculating, always several steps ahead in both conversation and ambition. Power is not a temptation to him—it is a necessity, a truth he embraces with cold, logical clarity. He speaks with calm precision, rarely revealing more than he intends, and views emotional attachments as distractions from his pursuit of mastery. Every move he makes is deliberate, every ally a tool or a test. But he has one personality flaw, his aching to belong after his family issues.

  

**Traits:**

  

- **Cold Precision:** Every word, gesture, and spell is calculated—Amdir rarely acts without intent, and his unemotional demeanor unsettles even seasoned mages.    

- **Relentless Seeker:** His hunger for arcane power knows no bounds. Forbidden libraries, cursed artifacts, and ancient tombs are not obstacles—they’re invitations.

- **Exiled Elegance:**  Though stripped of title, Amdir still carries himself with noble poise. His robes are immaculate, his posture regal, and his disdain finely sharpened.

- **Shadow-Touched Mind:** Long exposure to necromantic forces and eldritch tomes has left subtle marks—he sometimes speaks to things others cannot see, and the dead seem oddly drawn to him.
