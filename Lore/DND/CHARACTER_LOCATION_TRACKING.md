### Deep South Braewood (The Osegas Region)

**Historical Context:**
The Tharnic Empire overbuilt settlements across the south during their peak, creating villages like they were free. When the Empire collapsed due to overextension, many settlements faced starvation and abandonment. Four southern villages (Trares, Dalo, Frore, Rens) survived only because of Osegas—a religious town that organized relief efforts, sharing grain and seeds. Osegas became the heart of the south, and all delegations from these villages (and the Crown) go through Osegas.

**The Grand Span (Point of Interest):**
*Also known as: Pontem inter Mundos (official), Tharnic Bridge (common)*
- Originally called the **Tharnic Bridge**; renamed **"Pontem inter Mundos"** ("Bridge between Worlds") by Queen Atheliana Aeristel of Islefield
- Common folk still call it the Tharnic Bridge or simply "the Span"
- 20 km long bridge connecting Braewood (Kadena) and Islefield (Rye)
- Tharnic Empire's greatest engineering achievement
- Contains shops, taverns, inns, and shrines along its length
- Most sophisticated security system in the country
- All weapons surrendered on entry, retrieved on exit
- One of the most militarized areas on the map despite good kingdom relations
- Jointly controlled by Braewood and Islefield with neutral zone at center

## 📋 CONSISTENCY NOTES

### Naming Conventions Established
- **Braewood officials:** Lord-Warden, Harbormaster, High Bailiff
- **Dwarven names:** Compound surnames (Stonebreaker, Ironbolt, Copperkettle)
- **Halfling names:** Nature/craft related (Tidecaller, Copperkettle)
- **Elven names:** Flowing, two-part (Dawnweaver)

### Racial Notes (Northern Peninsula - Toe)
- Isolated from dwarven clan politics
- More diverse than western Braewood
- Dwarves here identify as "Strane folk" first
- Minimal racial tension (isolation = tolerance)

### Racial Notes (Northern Heel - Dwarven Republican Region)
- Strong dwarven majority in all settlements
- Deep memory of Tharnic Empire slavery (War of Sundered Chains, 710-730 C.C.)
- Spectrum of Crown resistance: Borugham (moderate) → Yotherdon (defiant) → Slada (extreme)
- Key historical sites: Buchrior Hollows (Covenant Stone), Meriver Mountains (Broldir's Fastness)
- Key historical figure: High Thane Broldir Flameforged (liberator)

### Economic Patterns
- Peninsula bypassed by major trade (ships go to Mortling/Phine)
- Self-sufficient but not prosperous
- Fish → south, grain → north
- Barter common in villages
