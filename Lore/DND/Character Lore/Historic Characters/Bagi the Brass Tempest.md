## Appearance
A colossal brass wyrm whose scales have weathered to the color of storm-clouds over copper. His eyes hold centuries of memory—patient, measuring, and touched with a thin spark of fury for those who break their word. When he speaks, his voice rumbles like distant thunder over calm seas. His wings, though bound by arcane chains, still carry the scent of salt spray and lightning.

## Backstory

### Before the Kingdoms (Pre-900s C.C.)
In the long, chaotic centuries after the Tharnic Empire fell, a brass wyrm began policing the western seas. Sailors learned his name—**Bagi**—and his three rules for safe waters: keep your oaths, keep tolls fair, and shelter the desperate. This "lighthouse law" quietly safeguarded the capes and coves that would later feed into [Kluimont's](../../Kingdoms/Kluimont/Kluimont.md) maritime rise.

### The Rise of Kluimont (900–975 C.C.)
As the three kingdoms took shape, the northwestern isles leaned into wards and seamanship. Kluimont's proto-navy and wizard councils valued any pact that kept routes open; Bagi's code fit perfectly. When Kluimont emerged as a distinct realm, it invested in arcane protection for shipping while mainland powers bickered—aligning with Bagi's "open lanes, honest tolls" stance.

### The Pact That Soured (1101–1109 C.C.)
Around **1101 C.C.**, rumors began: an ancient brass dragon was "subdued" somewhere in Kluimont's western old-growth—not slain, but caught in a net of names, stones, and sigils. Official denials followed even as odd experiments multiplied and the arcane academies surged by **1105**. 

Kluimont's rulers cut a baited oath with Bagi and then **looped his breath and will into a ward-grid**, turning a guardian into a living battery. He was chained in place—not by iron bars but by **meteoric standing stones, clockwork timings, and bound names**—and the forest itself was taught to sing the refrain that kept him compliant.

### The Storm of Unbound Chains (1220 C.C.)
Bagi never stopped working at the knots. Through patient, lawyerly draconic magic he taught oaks, stones, and way-markers to **mispronounce the binding names**. In **1220 C.C.**, enough failure cascaded at once to crack the air: an **arcane storm** ripped the sky above the western forest for **three nights**, scorching fields near Qrey and forcing evacuations. Kluimont's mages contained it, but the event made the rumor undeniable.

## Personality
- **Oath-bound:** Oaths are sacred. Swear truth under storm-clouds, and he believes you; break it, and even the wind turns against you.
- **Patient wrath:** Over a century of captivity has thinned his patience, but not consumed it with hatred.
- **Hospitable:** Those who share food and signal peace in his woods get a warning roar before wrath. Pirates, slavers, and toll-forgers get none.
- **Anti-monopolist:** He wants the aether grid severed from his heartbeats and replaced with a clean oath: lanes open, tolls honest, dragon-derived research **published** instead of hoarded.

## Role in the Kingdom
Bagi remains bound in the western forest, wary, quick-sparked, yet not consumed by hate. The [Arcane Synod](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/High%20Archon%20Lysanthe%20Kilmora.md) has doubled its containment efforts since the 1220 storm, but those who find him may discover a dragon willing to bargain: **freedom for a wish**.

## Relationships
- **Kluimont's Arcane Synod:** His captors. He despises their secrecy but respects their craft.
- **[Chris Meudec](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Chris%20Meudec.md):** The Synod's hatchet-man who studies the binding. Bagi finds him insufferable.
- **Sailors of the Western Seas:** Once his charges. Some still leave offerings at old lighthouses.
- **The Forest Itself:** Taught to sing his chains. It obeys, but reluctantly.

## Notable Quote
> "You mortals measure time in harvests. I measure it in broken oaths. Yours is coming due."
