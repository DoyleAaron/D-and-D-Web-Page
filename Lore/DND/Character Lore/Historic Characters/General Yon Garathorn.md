## Appearance
A lean, weathered soldier with calculating eyes and a perpetual frown. Garathorn dressed practically even at court, preferring functional armor to ceremonial dress. His most distinctive feature was his habit of sketching battle plans on any available surface while talking.

## Backstory
Yon Garathorn rose through [Braewood's](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) military ranks during a time of constant border friction. His genius lay not in grand charges but in logistics, fortification, and knowing exactly when to retreat.

His defining moment came during the **Great Resource Dispute (1135–1140 C.C.)** against [Islefield](../../Kingdoms/Islefield/Islefield.md). While other commanders wanted decisive battles, Garathorn understood that Braewood couldn't win a prolonged war against elven archers and Islefield's wealth. Instead, he developed mobile pike formations and built a network of small fortifications along contested farmland—too costly to take, too spread out to siege.

The result was a strategic stalemate that favored Braewood. When peace came, Braewood retained critical farmland and secured new trade agreements. It wasn't glorious, but it was victory.

## Personality
- **Calculating:** Never fought a battle he hadn't already won on paper.
- **Pragmatic:** Victory meant keeping what you had, not dying for what you couldn't hold.
- **Unglamorous:** Other generals wanted songs sung about them. Garathorn wanted supply lines.

## Role in History
Garathorn's defensive doctrine influenced Braewood military strategy for generations. His fortification network along the Islefield border still stands, and his writings on mobile warfare remain required reading at Braewood's military academies.

## Relationships
- **Braewood Crown:** Served faithfully, though his cautious strategies frustrated kings who wanted decisive victories.
- **[Islefield](../../Kingdoms/Islefield/Islefield.md):** His enemy during the Great Resource Dispute. He respected their archers enough to never give them clear targets.
- **His Soldiers:** They loved him—he brought them home alive.

## Notable Quote
> "The best battle is the one your enemy decides not to fight."
