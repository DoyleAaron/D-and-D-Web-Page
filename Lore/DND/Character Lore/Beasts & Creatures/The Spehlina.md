## Appearance

In its corrupted state, the Spehlina is a nightmare given form—a massive creature nearly sixty feet across and twenty-five feet tall. Its flesh, once translucent blue-white, has turned murky crimson-black, shot through with pulsing veins of sickly orange-red light like volcanic fissures. Where it once had gentle moonstone eyes, now dozens of bloodshot orbs weep dark ichor. Its tendrils, once soft filters that sifted the water clean, have become barbed and necrotic, leaving trails of corruption in the water wherever it passes.

Those who have survived encounters describe the water around it turning oily and dark, and a feeling of oppressive dread that presses down on the mind.

## History

### The Guardian of Lavalto Lake

For over eight hundred years, a Spehlina dwelled in the deep waters of Lavalto Lake. The species is rare—ancient aquatic guardians found only in volcanic lake ecosystems. They serve as living water purifiers, their massive forms drifting through the depths as they absorb toxins, pollutants, and harmful magical residue.

The fishermen of the lake's shores considered the Spehlina sacred. Villages left offerings of flowers at the waterline. Children were told stories of the "Gentle Shadow" that kept the waters clean and the fish plentiful. No one feared it. It was a blessing.

### The Corruption

Approximately thirty to forty years ago, something changed. The Spehlina began behaving erratically. Fishermen reported glimpses of a creature that looked *wrong*—darker, larger, angrier. Then the attacks began.

What corrupted the guardian remains unknown to most. Some blame volcanic activity. Others whisper of dark magic seeping from somewhere in the Lavalto mountain range. Whatever the cause, the Spehlina now attacks anything that enters its waters—ships, swimmers, even creatures that come to the shore to drink.

The creature's anguished wails can be heard on still nights, echoing across the lake. Those who study such things say it sounds less like rage and more like *pain*.

## Behavior

The corrupted Spehlina is territorial and aggressive, but its attacks follow patterns that suggest something other than mere predation:

- It attacks primarily at **dusk and night**, when visibility is poor
- It targets **ships and boats** first, suggesting it associates vessels with threat
- Survivors report it **hesitates** before killing—as if fighting itself
- It does not eat its victims; bodies are left floating or dragged to shore

Some scholars theorize the creature is not evil—merely suffering, driven mad by whatever corruption festers inside it.

## The Bounty

The **Wayfarers' Accord** has listed the Spehlina as an A-tier threat with a bounty of **10,000 gold pieces**—5,000 from Dawerton and 5,000 from the Crown of Braewood. Trade across Lavalto Lake has been devastated, and both powers want the creature eliminated.

Many have tried. Adventuring parties, monster hunters, even a small naval expedition. None have succeeded. The creature's lair lies somewhere in the lake's depths, and fighting it in its own element has proven fatal.

## Rumors and Speculation

- Some say the Spehlina can **phase through solid objects**, explaining how it catches ships by surprise
- Old texts from Islefield describe Spehlinas as **sacred to nature spirits**, and that killing one brings terrible consequences
- Fishermen claim that on certain nights, the creature surfaces and **screams at the mountains**—as if calling to something, or cursing it
- A druid passing through Dawerton reportedly wept upon sensing the creature, saying "It's not a monster. It's a prisoner."

## Notable Quote

> "We put six harpoons in it. Six. The thing just looked at us with those bleeding eyes, and I swear—I *swear*—it looked sad before it tore our ship apart."
> — Hennik Boatwright, sole survivor of *The Maiden's Fortune*
