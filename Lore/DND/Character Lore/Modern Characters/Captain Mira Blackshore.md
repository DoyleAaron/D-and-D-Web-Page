## Appearance
A lean, sharp-featured woman with black hair cut short for practicality and dark eyes that assess everyone as a potential threat. Carries herself with military bearing even off duty. A thin scar runs along her jawline — knife fight, she says, and doesn't elaborate. Armor always polished, sword always within reach. Never seems to relax.

## Backstory
[Mira Blackshore](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Captain%20Mira%20Blackshore.md) grew up on the eastern coast of [Braewood](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md), in a fishing village that was raided by pirates when she was twelve. Her father died in the attack. She joined the Crown's forces at sixteen, driven by a need for order in a chaotic world.

Twenty-five years of service taught her that chaos takes many forms. Pirates. Bandits. Smugglers. And, increasingly, dwarven nationalists who talk of "autonomy" but mean rebellion.

She was assigned to [Ardismouth](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Ardismouth.md) three years ago, as tensions began rising. She saw immediately what Portreeve [Dunholm](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Portreeve%20Garrett%20Dunholm.md) couldn't: the dwarven faction was organizing for conflict. She's spent three years preparing for violence she considers inevitable. The Portreeve calls her paranoid. She calls herself realistic.

## Personality
- **Hard-line on order** — sees compromise as weakness
- **Deeply suspicious of dwarven autonomy movement** — believes it leads to violence
- **Protective of human residents** — sometimes at expense of fairness
- **Professionally competent** — the garrison is well-trained
- **Unable to see nuance** — divides the world into loyal and disloyal

## Role in Ardismouth
- Commands the town garrison (~200 soldiers)
- Maintains public order
- Investigates crimes (with heavy suspicion toward dwarves)
- Coordinates with Crown military if needed
- Prepares contingency plans for "civil unrest"

## Relationships
- **Portreeve Garrett Dunholm:** Frustrated by his weakness; follows orders reluctantly
- **Guildmaster Brogar Flamevein:** Views him as an enemy in waiting
- **Elder Mordin Greymantle:** Was willing to work with him — suspects [Flamevein](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Guildmaster%20Brogar%20Flamevein.md) in his disappearance
- **Father Osric Tidewalker:** Respects his neutrality even if she doesn't share it
- **Her soldiers:** Loyal, well-trained, share her suspicions

---

*"The Portreeve wants peace. I want order. They're not the same thing. Peace can mean everyone's happy, or it can mean everyone's too scared to fight. I'll take either. But what we have now? [Flamevein](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Guildmaster%20Brogar%20Flamevein.md) makes demands, the Portreeve stalls, and my soldiers patrol streets where half the population looks at us with hate. That's not peace. That's a truce. And truces end."*
