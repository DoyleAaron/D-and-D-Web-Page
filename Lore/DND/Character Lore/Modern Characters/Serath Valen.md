# Serath Valen

## Title
Enforcer of the Pureblood Council

## Age
Unknown (Immortal Vampire)

## Race
Vampire (Human origin)

## Location
Darewaeton, Lavalto Isle

## Appearance
A predator in noble's clothing. Serath has the build of a warrior—lean, powerful, coiled for violence. His dark hair is swept back, his features sharp and aristocratic, but his eyes hold none of the diplomatic patience of Lord Adrian. They hold hunger and barely restrained aggression. He dresses in black and crimson, and always carries a blade.

## Backstory
Serath serves as the Pureblood Council's enforcer—the threat behind their diplomatic words. When negotiations fail, when examples must be made, when enemies need to disappear, Serath is dispatched.

He was turned centuries ago by a master who valued violence over subtlety. That master is long gone—destroyed by Serath himself when he grew powerful enough. Now he serves the council because it gives him purpose and permission to do what he enjoys: hunt, fight, and kill.

## Personality
- **Ruthless:** Enjoys his work. Doesn't pretend otherwise.
- **Loyal to strength:** Serves the council because they are powerful. If they weakened, his loyalty might waver.
- **Prefers action:** Dislikes politics, negotiations, and patience. Tolerates them because they lead to violence.
- **Protective:** Of the manor and the purebloods, in his own brutal way.

## Role in Lavalto
Protector and enforcer for the Pureblood Council. Handles threats that diplomacy cannot resolve. His presence at negotiations is a message in itself.

## Relationships
- **Lord Adrian Silwynn:** Respects his power. Finds his diplomacy tedious but effective.
- **Lady Seraphina Caylester:** Follows her orders. She is the head of purebloods; he is her weapon.
- **Enemies of Lavalto:** They learn to fear him. Those who survive, anyway.
- **Other Vampires:** Some see him as a blunt instrument. He doesn't mind. Blunt instruments break things.

## Notable Quote
> "Lord Adrian talks. Lady Seraphina plans. I handle what comes after talking and planning fail."
