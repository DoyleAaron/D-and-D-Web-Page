## Appearance
A nondescript man of medium height and build, with brown hair and brown eyes — the kind of person your gaze slides past in a crowd. Dresses like a moderately successful merchant, nothing flashy. Face forgettable by design. Drives a modest supply wagon that looks like a hundred other supply wagons. The only notable feature: eyes that miss nothing, cataloging every detail with professional interest.

## Backstory
[Ulric Harren](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Merchant%20Ulric%20Harren.md) runs the monthly supply convoy to [Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) Prison — food, medicine, replacement equipment, and the occasional special delivery the Crown doesn't document. He's been doing it for fifteen years. On paper, he's a merchant contracted for logistical services.

In reality, [Ulric Harren](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Merchant%20Ulric%20Harren.md) works for Crown Intelligence.

[Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) holds secrets the Crown wants monitored. Prisoners who know things. Guards who might be corrupted. A warden who has been in place for thirty years and answers to no one locally. Ulric's supply runs are cover for intelligence gathering. He reports what he sees, hears, and suspects. The supplies are real; the merchant is a mask.

He is very good at his job. No one in [Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) suspects him — not even the paranoid Lieutenant Valk, though she watches him carefully. He has cultivated an image of a slightly nervous, overly curious trader who asks too many questions because he's bored and lonely. It's an act. [Ulric Harren](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Merchant%20Ulric%20Harren.md) is neither bored nor lonely. He is watching.

Recently, his reports have become more urgent. [Prisoner 7](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Prisoner%207.md)'s ravings. The tunnel. The increased activity near [Debourg Grotto](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Debourg%20Grotto.md). Something is happening, and Ulric needs to find out what before it's too late.

## Personality
- **Professionally invisible** — blends into any environment
- **Genuinely curious** — the job suits his temperament
- **Colder than he appears** — empathy is a tool, not a weakness
- **Loyal to the Crown** — truly believes in [Braewood](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md)'s stability
- **Increasingly concerned** — the Debourg situation is beyond his expertise

## Role in Oyard
- Official: Monthly supply contractor
- Actual: Crown intelligence operative
- Observes prisoners, guards, and village
- Maintains contacts among staff
- Reports to Crown intelligence in [Braewood](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City

## Relationships
- **Warden-Captain Halvard Grimstone:** [Grimstone](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Warden-Captain%20Halvard%20Grimstone.md) sees him as a necessary nuisance; Ulric respects the warden's efficiency
- **Lieutenant Seren Valk:** She suspects something; he admires her instincts
- **Father Aldric Mourning:** A useful source of information, though Ulric feels guilty using him
- **Old Guildmaster Tomas Ashwell (Adaham):** Uses the [Adaham](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Adaham.md) stop for additional intelligence gathering
- **Crown Intelligence handlers:** His true employers

*"The road to [Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) is forty-five kilometers of nothing. Rock, scrub, more rock. No one takes that road unless they have to. I take it every month, bringing salt pork and lamp oil to a prison full of people the kingdom wants to forget. That's my job. Officially. Unofficially, I take notes. Who's talking to whom. What the prisoners are saying. Whether the guards are steady or slipping. Lately my notes have been... concerning. The old dwarf in isolation is screaming things that match reports from the Debourg area. The Warden pretends it's nothing. The Lieutenant knows it isn't. And someone is digging a tunnel. I don't know who or why, but I intend to find out. That's also my job."*
