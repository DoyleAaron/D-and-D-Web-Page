## Appearance
A young man whose armor is slightly too big for him, betraying his recent conscription. His face still holds the softness of youth, not yet hardened by years of service. He keeps glancing around with worried eyes, especially when orders seem harsh. His hands fidget with his spear when he's nervous—which is often.

## Backstory
[Alren](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Cadet%20Alren.md) is a baker's son from a small village who joined the army because he believed in oaths, honor, and protecting the innocent. He is learning—painfully—that military service is messier than the heroic tales suggested.

He wants to do right. He wants to believe his commanders are just. Every day, that belief is tested. He's seen prisoners treated harshly, civilians caught in the gears of military necessity, good people forced to do questionable things. He hasn't broken yet, but he's bending.

## Personality
- **Earnest:** Genuinely wants to be a good soldier and a good person.
- **Conflicted:** Sees the gap between military ideals and military reality.
- **Brave in small ways:** Will whisper warnings, quietly vouch for the innocent, bend rules for mercy.
- **Not yet hardened:** Still has a conscience. Time will tell if he keeps it.

## Role in the Military
A low-ranking soldier assigned wherever infantry is needed. Unremarkable in skill but notable for his persistent conscience.

## Relationships
- **His Commanders:** He follows orders. He doesn't always agree with them.
- **Fellow Soldiers:** Some think him naive. Others quietly appreciate that someone still cares.
- **Prisoners/Civilians:** He treats them like people. This is rarer than it should be.
- **His Faith:** He still believes in something. That makes him valuable—or dangerous.

## Notable Quote
> "I... I know we have orders, but... isn't there another way?"
