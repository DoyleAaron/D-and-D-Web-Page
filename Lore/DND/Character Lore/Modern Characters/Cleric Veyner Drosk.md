## Appearance
A gaunt man in simple robes, unremarkable except for the pendant that hangs at his chest—an iron symbol that seems to drink the light around it. His eyes hold the fervent gleam of absolute conviction. He speaks with the cadence of a preacher, every word laden with false compassion that masks something darker.

## Backstory
Veyner was a minor priest of a legitimate faith before he discovered the pendant in a ruined shrine. What whispered to him there changed everything. Now he serves the Red Scarves' inner circle, wielding an artifact that can dominate weak-willed souls and bend them to his command.

His role in the movement is control. While [Mother Selyse](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Mother%20Selyse.md) provides the public face of charity and community, Veyner ensures compliance among the lower ranks. Those who question the faith find themselves suddenly eager to conform. Those who resist... join his "congregation" regardless.

## Personality
- **Fanatical conviction:** Believes absolutely in whatever darkness he serves.
- **False compassion:** Speaks of love and community while enslaving minds.
- **Cruel efficiency:** Views people as resources to be used.
- **Hidden monster:** The gentle priest mask conceals something genuinely evil.

## Role in the Settlement
Manages the Red Scarves' mind control operations in [Sheyton](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Sheyton.md). His Pendant of Subjugation allows him to dominate the weak-willed, building a secret army of thralls hidden among the faithful.

## Relationships
- **Mother Selyse:** His superior in the Red Scarves hierarchy. He fears and serves her.
- **The Pendant:** His source of power. It whispers to him.
- **His "Congregation":** They are not followers—they are slaves who don't know it yet.
- **Father Clement Varro:** The legitimate priest who suspects something is wrong. Veyner considers him an obstacle to be removed.

## Notable Quote
> "The community embraces all who seek belonging. You will find your place among us. You have no choice."
