## Appearance
A tall, lanky young man with sun-darkened skin and the calloused hands of someone who's spent years working the ferry poles. His brown hair is permanently wind-tousled, and his clothes are practical river-wear—waterproofed leather and linen that can survive being soaked daily. He speaks with the accent of the river folk, dropping consonants and stretching vowels.

## Backstory
Jorl inherited the Vatton ferry operation from his father, who inherited it from his father before that. The Vattons have been moving people and goods across the River Vael for four generations. When the southern barony began claiming taxation rights over the ferries, Jorl found himself at the center of a dispute that could determine [Cleamond](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Cleamond.md)'s economic future.

He's not a politician or a negotiator—he's a ferryman. But circumstances have forced him to become something more.

## Personality
- **Stubborn about what's right:** The Vattons have worked this river for a century. He won't surrender to southern lords who arrived yesterday.
- **Out of his depth:** Knows how to run a ferry, not how to navigate legal disputes and political pressure.
- **Loyal to Cleamond:** This is his home. He'll fight for it—he just doesn't know how.
- **Surprisingly brave:** When tested, he stands his ground.

## Role in the Settlement
Controls the largest ferry operation on the River Vael. His cooperation or resistance determines whether the southern barony's tax claims hold water—literally.

## Relationships
- **Warden-Mayor Lysa Thorne:** She's trying to protect him; he's trying not to make her job harder.
- **Southern Barony:** They've threatened, bribed, and cajoled. He's said no to all of it.
- **The river folk:** His people. They trust him to represent them; the pressure weighs heavily.

## Notable Quote
> "My grandfather poled this ferry. My father poled this ferry. I'll be damned if some southern lord tells me who owns the river my family has worked for a hundred years."
