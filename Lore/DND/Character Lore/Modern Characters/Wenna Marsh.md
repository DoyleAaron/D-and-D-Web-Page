# Wenna Marsh

## Title
Harbor Manager of Dalo

## Age
Late 20s

## Race
Human

## Location
Dalo, Braewood

## Appearance
A young woman with sun-streaked brown hair usually tied back and practical brown eyes that assess everything they see. Her face is already weathering from outdoor work, and her hands are calloused from rope and wood. She dresses in practical working clothes and carries a small ledger where she tracks harbor business.

## Backstory
Wenna grew up watching her father, Cotter Marsh, fish the southern waters. She expected to marry a fisherman herself and live the same life her mother had—waiting on shore, mending nets, raising children.

Then the storm took her father's boat and nearly took her father. In the chaos that followed, Wenna discovered she had a talent for organization. She coordinated repair crews, tracked supplies, and helped her father lead the village through crisis. When things stabilized, she'd become essential to Dalo's harbor operations.

Now she manages the harbor—tracking boats, scheduling dock space, handling visiting traders, and keeping records that no one had bothered with before. She's young for the responsibility, but no one questions her competence.

## Personality
- **Organized:** Knows where every boat is, what every crew needs, when every tide turns.
- **Ambitious:** Wants more for Dalo than just survival. Sees potential for growth.
- **Devoted:** Loves her father and her village. Would never leave them.
- **Impatient:** Sometimes frustrated by the slow pace of village life. Youth's restlessness.

## Role in the Village
Manages Dalo's small harbor, handling dock assignments, trader negotiations, and record-keeping. She's modernizing operations that her father's generation ran by memory and handshake. She's also being groomed as his eventual successor as headman.

## Relationships
- **Headman Cotter Marsh:** Her father, mentor, and the man she's learning to follow. Their relationship mixes family love and professional partnership.
- **Widow Selna Brine:** Works with her on export logistics. The older woman has become a role model.
- **Visiting Traders:** Negotiates directly with merchants from Osegas and beyond. Building experience.
- **Young People Her Age:** Most have left. She chose to stay.

## Notable Quote
> "My father says the village survived on instinct and tradition. I'm not criticizing that—we're alive. But I want us to thrive. That takes planning, records, thinking ahead. That's what I bring."
