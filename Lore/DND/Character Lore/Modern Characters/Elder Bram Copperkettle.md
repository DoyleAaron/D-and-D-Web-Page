## Appearance
Small for a dwarf, stooped with age, with a magnificent white beard that reaches nearly to his knees. Deeply wrinkled face that crinkles easily into smiles. Rheumy brown eyes that are sharper than they appear. Gnarled hands from a lifetime of metalwork.

Wears the traditional garb of the northern dwarven clans — practical wools and leathers in earth tones. Always has a copper kettle-shaped pendant (his family symbol) around his neck. Walks with a cane carved from [Plalisle Den](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/POI/Plalisle%20Den.md) oak — a gift from an elven contact.

Smells of pipe smoke, metal polish, and strong tea.

## Backstory
Bram was born in the northern peninsula when it was even more isolated than today. He watched the dwarven clans here develop their unique character — neither fully part of the western clans nor the [Braewood](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) establishment. He helped shape that neutrality.

He was a metalsmith by trade, making his living crafting the fine copperware that gave his family its name. But his real work was diplomacy — keeping the peninsula dwarves out of wars, feuds, and political entanglements that didn't concern them.

Now ancient, he serves as Elder — a position of respect rather than power. Younger dwarves lead the quarter's daily affairs, but everyone still seeks Bram's counsel.

## Personality
- **Wise** — has seen enough history to recognize patterns
- **Neutral** — genuinely believes in staying out of other peoples' fights
- **Patient** — thinks in decades, not days
- **Welcoming** — the peninsula tradition of accepting anyone who works
- **Tired** — ready to pass his role to younger dwarves, but they're not ready

## Role in Strane
- Unofficial representative of the dwarven community
- Keeper of northern dwarven traditions and history
- Mediator in disputes (dwarven and otherwise)
- Connection to the old stories about the peninsula
- Living memory of a century and a half of regional history

## Northern Dwarven Philosophy
Bram embodies the peninsula dwarves' unique values:
- **Political neutrality** — they serve whoever pays, fight for no one
- **Openness** — any race is welcome if they contribute
- **Pragmatism** — survival matters more than ancient grudges
- **Connection to the land** — not miners, but craftsmen and farmers

This puts them at odds with the more traditional dwarven clans elsewhere.

## Relationships
- **Captain Hilda Stonebreaker:** Old friend; they share tea weekly
- **Lord-Warden Velthorpe:** Respects his competence; keeps him at arm's length
- **Jorik Ironbolt:** Knows him from decades ago
- **The Younger Dwarves:** Loves them; fears they don't understand what they're abandoning
- **The Old Stories:** He knows things he wishes he didn't
