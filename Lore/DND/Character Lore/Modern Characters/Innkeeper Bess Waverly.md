# Bess Waverly

## Title
Innkeeper of the Drowned Anchor

## Age
Mid-40s

## Race
Human

## Location
Adaham (Village), The Drowned Anchor Tavern


## Appearance
A sturdy woman with broad shoulders and capable hands, her dark hair streaked with grey and usually pinned up out of her way. Warm brown eyes that can shift from welcoming to warning in an instant. Dresses practically in apron and working clothes. Moves through her tavern with the efficiency of someone who has done this for decades. A small scar on her chin from breaking up a fight years ago.

## Backstory
Bess came to Adaham twenty years ago, a young widow with a small inheritance and nowhere else to go. She bought the failing tavern on the ghost docks for a pittance and renamed it the Drowned Anchor — a dark joke that the locals appreciated.

Against all odds, she made it work. The Crown sailors needed somewhere to drink, somewhere to eat, somewhere to feel like people instead of soldiers. Bess provided all three. Over twenty years, the Drowned Anchor became the heart of Adaham — the place where sailors, locals, and occasional travelers mixed.

Bess knows everyone's secrets. She knows which sailors are homesick, which are drinking too much, which are falling in love with local girls. She knows Old Tomas hasn't been eating properly. She knows the Harbor-Warden is lonelier than he admits. And she keeps it all to herself, doling out advice only when asked.

## Personality
- **Maternal but not soft** — cares deeply, tolerates no nonsense
- **Shrewd businesswoman** — the Drowned Anchor turns a profit against all odds
- **Keeper of secrets** — everyone talks to Bess, Bess talks to no one
- **Fiercely independent** — rebuilt her life once, could do it again
- **Quietly content** — has found peace in an unlikely place

## Role in Adaham
- Runs the only tavern in the village
- Unofficial counselor to sailors and locals alike
- Maintains social order through sheer force of personality
- Provides meals to those who need them (including Old Tomas)
- First point of contact for most visitors

## Relationships
- **Harbor-Warden Colm Ravencroft:** Mutual respect; he sometimes dines alone at her bar
- **Fleet-Captain Isolde Merrick:** Worries the young captain is too reckless; tries to slow her down
- **Old Guildmaster Tomas Ashwell:** Brings him food; listens to his stories; dreads the day he dies
- **The sailors:** Mother figure to dozens of young men and women far from home

---

*"The Crown built those docks to make Adaham great. The river disagreed. Now we've got rotting piers, empty warehouses, and two hundred of the finest sailors in Braewood with nothing to do but drink and wait. My job is to keep them drinking responsibly and waiting patiently. So far, so good. But I've seen what happens when idle sailors get desperate. That's why I keep a cudgel behind the bar."*
