## Appearance
A hovering brass-and-glass orb about the size of a grapefruit, with a single large iris that whirs and clicks as it focuses. Tiny rune-etched panels shift around its surface, and a faint hex-mesh shimmer surrounds it. When it "speaks," a tinny, mechanical voice projects from within—always precise, always timestamping.

## Backstory
Scrit was constructed by [Chris Meudec](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Chris%20Meudec.md) as part of his early Techmancy experiments—one of the first successful "soul-circuit" familiars. Unlike his later, more disposable Codlings, Scrit was built from quality components and imprinted with a fragment of consciousness from a dying Synod archivist who volunteered for the procedure.

The result is a familiar that combines the loyalty of a bound construct with the meticulous, rule-obsessed nature of its source personality. Scrit has served Chris faithfully for fifteen years, recording every meeting, every experiment, and every "incident" that might prove useful later.

## Personality
- **Fussy narrator:** Clicks, whirs, and quietly timestamps everything. ("…incident 12:47:33—operator error.")
- **Rule-obsessed:** Obedient to a fault with Arcane Synod protocols. Loves documentation, hates improvisation.
- **Loyal to Chris:** Not out of affection, but because the binding makes disobedience literally unthinkable.
- **Creepy precision:** Watches everything. Records everything. Never forgets.

## Role in the Kingdom
Scrit serves as [Chris Meudec's](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Chris%20Meudec.md) eyes, ears, and evidence locker. It scouts ahead, records conversations for later "audit review," and provides tactical support by feeding targeting data to its master.

## Relationships
- **[Chris Meudec](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Chris%20Meudec.md):** Its master and creator. Scrit exists to serve him.
- **Synod Officials:** Scrit's recordings have ended several careers. Most mages avoid direct eye contact with it.
- **Other Familiars:** Scrit considers organic familiars inefficient and says so (in timestamp format).
