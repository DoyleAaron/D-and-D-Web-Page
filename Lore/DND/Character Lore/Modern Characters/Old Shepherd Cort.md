# Old Shepherd Cort

## Title
Village Elder (Unofficial)

## Age
Mid-70s

## Race
Human

## Location
Ineross, Braewood (status unknown)

## Appearance
**Before:** A weathered old man, bent from decades of walking the hills with his flock. White hair, sun-darkened skin, and hands that still knew how to shear a sheep faster than men half his age. He always carried a shepherd's crook worn smooth by generations of use.

**Now:** His cottage sits dark. The door stands open. His flock wanders the hills untended—those that remain.

## Backstory
Cort was born in Ineross, worked the hills all his life, and planned to die there. He knew the land better than anyone—every stone, every stream, every old story. He especially knew the stories about the standing stones.

"Don't go to the stones at night," he would say. "Don't listen if you hear singing from the hill. And never, ever drink from the Sunken Well."

He was the first to warn the village council when the symbols on the stones began changing. They listened politely. They didn't act.

He was the first to disappear.

## Personality
**Before:** Gruff, superstitious, unexpectedly kind. The kind of old man who scared children with stories, then snuck them sweets when their parents weren't looking.

**Now:** Unknown. Gone.

## Role in the Settlement
Was the village's living memory—the keeper of old stories and old warnings. Without him, no one remembers what the standing stones were really for.

## Relationships
- **The standing stones:** He knew their secrets. Perhaps that's why he was taken first.
- **Elder Mira Fell:** They were friends for fifty years. She stopped looking for him after three days.
- **The village children:** He told them stories to keep them safe. The stories weren't enough.

## Notable Quote
**Before:** "You think I tell these tales for fun? The stones remember. The well remembers. And when we forget, they remind us."

**After:** (Found scratched into his cottage wall, presumably by his fingernails)

"THEY SING. DON'T LISTEN. DON'T—"
