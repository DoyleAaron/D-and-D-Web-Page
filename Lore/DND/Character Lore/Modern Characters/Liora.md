## Description

**Liora** is one of the most influential figures on the Dawerton Council, operating as both a political power and the head of an extensive intelligence network. She presents herself as an elegant, calculating woman who values information above gold and discretion above all else.

She shares only what serves the city and her long-term goals, often speaking in careful, deliberate terms. Rumors about her background are widespread, but she neither confirms nor denies them.

## Appearance

Liora carries herself with quiet authority, her presence commanding attention without demanding it. She studies visitors carefully, assessing their capabilities and intentions with a practiced eye that misses nothing.

## Methods

### Information as Currency
Liora's most notable trait is her unconventional approach to payment. Rather than gold, she offers **three truthful questions** answered by her network—a payment that can be far more valuable than coin to those who know how to use it.

### Private Dinners
She hosts elegant private dinners for those who interest her, using the intimate setting to probe intentions and assess potential allies or assets. These evenings are controlled and deliberate, with every word chosen carefully.

### Discretion
Above all, Liora values results achieved quietly. Those who work for her are expected to handle matters without creating public incidents.

## Relationships

- **Mayor Adrian:** Close ally on council governance and city security
- **The Party:** Hired them to investigate disturbances in Dawerton, paying with three questions. Rewarded them after they dealt with Father Halden
- **Kluimont:** Knows someone within the Council of Ten (a mage she does not name publicly)
- **Her Network:** Commands an extensive intelligence operation across multiple cities

## Notable Events

### Session 10 (1225 C.C.)
During the diplomatic meeting, Liora quietly studied the party's presence and their escort's military capability. She later invited them to a private dinner where she probed their intentions and proposed a job.

### Session 11 (1225 C.C.)
The party accepted Liora's job—investigating disturbances in Dawerton. She agreed to payment by three questions:
1. Information on Balthazar
2. Relations between Dawerton and Kluimont
3. Relations between Dawerton and Islefield

### Session 14 (1225 C.C.)
After the party defeated Father Halden, Liora granted them their three questions and offered to cover the remaining cost of a Kluimont spell as additional reward.

## Notable Quote
