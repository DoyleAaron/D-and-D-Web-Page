## Appearance
A thin, elderly woman with wild white hair and pale blue eyes that seem to look through people into something beyond. Her face is deeply weathered, her hands gnarled but steady on her lamp oil. She dresses in layers of grey and brown, and moves with surprising agility for her age. She's rarely seen without her brass spyglass.

## Backstory
[Yentl](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Old%20Stormcrow%20Yentl.md) has kept the lighthouse at [Rens](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Rens.md) for fifty years, since she was a young woman fleeing something in the north—she's never said what. The lighthouse suited her: isolated, purposeful, requiring vigilance but not conversation.

Over the decades, she developed an uncanny ability to predict storms, earning her the nickname "Stormcrow." Sailors learned to trust her warnings. But in recent years, she's begun claiming to see something else—strange lights to the south, flickering on the horizon beyond the Burning Hells. Most people think it's age. Some aren't sure.

## Personality
- **Eccentric:** Half a century alone has made her strange by village standards.
- **Perceptive:** Sees weather changes before anyone else. Some say she sees more.
- **Devoted:** The light must never go out. That's her only rule.
- **Haunted:** Something in her past drove her to isolation. She's never shared it.

## Role in the Village
Maintains the lighthouse that guides ships safely past the treacherous reefs near [Rens](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Rens.md). She keeps the light burning, maintains the building, and uses her uncanny weather sense to warn of approaching storms. Her predictions have saved countless lives.

## Relationships
- **Harbor-Elder Brennan Cole:** Sometimes joins her for evening watches. He listens to her stories.
- **The Sailors:** They respect her predictions. "When Stormcrow warns, you listen."
- **Young Apprentices:** Occasionally trains replacements. None have stayed long.
- **Something to the South:** She claims to see lights. No one else sees them.

## Notable Quote
> "Fifty years watching the sea, I've learned its moods. Storms announce themselves if you know how to listen. But lately... there's something else. Lights beyond the horizon. Flickering. No one believes me. But I see them."
