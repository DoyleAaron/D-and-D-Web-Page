## Appearance
Broad-shouldered and imposing even by dwarven standards. Iron-grey hair worn in elaborate braids that mark his clan and office. A full beard reaching to his belt, bound with rings of blackened steel — the [Steelhand](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Master%20Smith%20Durgan%20Steelhand.md) signature. Deep-set eyes the color of storm clouds, perpetually assessing. His face bears the scars of a century of political battles and one very real one in his youth.

Dresses in practical but fine clothing — dark wool, good leather, no Crown symbols anywhere. Always wears the High Elder's chain of office: interlocking steel links forged by his ancestors.

Carries himself like someone who has argued with kings and hasn't lost yet.

## Backstory
Korrin was born into the [Steelhand](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Master%20Smith%20Durgan%20Steelhand.md) clan, master smiths of [Yotherdon](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) for generations. He apprenticed at the forge, mastered the craft, and might have been content to spend his centuries making steel — but the clan had other plans. His father was High Elder before him, and his grandfather before that. When the old Elder died, the council elected Korrin unanimously.

That was ninety years ago. Since then, Korrin has walked the knife's edge between independence and survival. He believes passionately that dwarves should govern themselves. He also believes that a war with the Crown would destroy everything his ancestors built. His life's work is finding the path between those truths.

## Personality
- **Pragmatic** — independence is the goal; survival is the requirement
- **Patient** — thinks in dwarven timescales; willing to wait for opportunities
- **Stubborn** — once committed, nearly impossible to move
- **Diplomatic** — speaks carefully; every word is calculated
- **Burdened** — carries the weight of his people's hopes and fears

## Role in Yotherdon
- Elected leader of the Council of Elders
- Spokesman for dwarven interests in (rare) Crown negotiations
- Final arbiter of disputes the council cannot resolve
- Symbol of moderate resistance — defiance with prudence
- The voice that keeps [Yotherdon](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) from becoming [Slada](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Slada.md)

## Relationships
- **Elder Thora Ironvow:** Respects her passion; frustrated by her impatience
- **Durgan Steelhand:** His younger brother; trusts him absolutely
- **Warden Hella Stonebrow:** Relies on her military judgment
- **Thane Aldric Borugh:** Maintains communication; considers him too moderate
- **Elder Grunhild Chainbreaker:** Respects her commitment; fears her example
- **The Crown:** Negotiates, delays, deflects; never trusts

---

*"Every generation, someone asks why we don't simply declare independence. March on the Crown. Fight for what's ours. I ask them: what happens the day after? The Crown has armies. We have a town. They would burn [Yotherdon](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Yotherdon.md) to ash, and every dwarven settlement would follow. My ancestors didn't survive the Empire by dying gloriously. They survived by enduring. That's what I do. I endure. And every year we endure is a year our children grow stronger. Someday — not in my lifetime, perhaps not in yours — we'll be strong enough. Until then, I keep us alive."*
