# Warden-Mayor Lysa Thorne

## Title
Warden-Mayor of Cleamond

## Age
~80 (appears early 40s due to elven heritage)

## Race
Half-Elf

## Location
Cleamond, Braewood

## Appearance
A woman who carries herself like someone accustomed to command—straight-backed, sharp-eyed, with the kind of practical beauty that comes from confidence rather than vanity. Her auburn hair, lightly touched with gray, is usually pulled back for convenience. She favors well-worn traveling clothes over formal robes, and always keeps a knife at her belt—old habits from her caravan days.

## Backstory
Born to a human merchant father and an elven mother who passed through Cleamond, Lysa grew up knowing both the road and the town. She ran caravans for twenty years, learning every route, every danger, and every trick of the trade. When the previous Warden-Mayor vanished during a southern raid, the Crown needed someone who understood both commerce and security—Lysa was the obvious choice.

She's held the position for twelve years. The bandits and barons who thought a former caravan captain would be easy to manipulate learned otherwise.

## Personality
- **Practical and direct:** No patience for flattery or unnecessary words.
- **Sharp tongue:** Famous for cutting observations that end arguments.
- **Deeply loyal:** To Cleamond, to her people, and to the Crown—in that order.
- **Haunted by the roads:** Still sometimes wakes from dreams of attacks on her old caravans.

## Role in the Settlement
Governs Cleamond with combined civil and military authority—the unique "Warden-Mayor" position created specifically for this crossroads town. She manages trade, settles disputes, commands the guard, and negotiates with Mortling's constant demands.

## Relationships
- **Captain Marcus Dunley:** Her guard captain and closest advisor. Trusts him completely.
- **Guildmaster Orvyn Cartwright:** Respects his wealth but doesn't trust his motives.
- **Mortling Stronghold:** Cleamond exists to supply them; Lysa ensures the relationship stays mutually beneficial.
- **The Lost Convoy:** The disappearance weighs on her—it happened on roads she used to travel.

## Notable Quote
> "The road teaches you two things: how to move fast, and when to stand still. The trick is knowing which one keeps you alive."
