## Appearance
A surprisingly jovial man for someone who lives in [Borugham](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Borugham.md). Stocky build, warm brown eyes, a ready smile that actually reaches his eyes. Dark hair streaked with grey, usually messy from work. Calloused hands from years of hauling casks and chopping firewood.

Wears practical innkeeper's clothes — apron always on, sleeves always rolled. Moves with the energy of someone who genuinely enjoys their work. 

The warmest presence in the coldest town.

## Backstory
The Wheelocks have run the Crossroads Inn for three generations. Located at the road junction technically outside the town walls, the inn exists in a strange legal limbo — part of [Borugham](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Borugham.md) but not quite, essential but not respected.

Hagen inherited the inn from his mother and has run it for twenty years. Unlike most Borughers, he actually *likes* travelers. They bring news, stories, variety. In a town that treats change like a disease, Hagen treasures every glimpse of the wider world.

This makes him suspect to the true Borughers. It also makes him the only reason anyone willingly stops in this town.

## Personality
- **Genuinely friendly** — not an act; he actually likes people
- **Curious** — hungry for news and stories from elsewhere
- **Careful** — knows how to navigate [Borugham](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Borugham.md)'s politics
- **Conflicted** — loves his home but chafes at its restrictions
- **Trustworthy** — keeps his guests' business private (mostly)

## Role in Borugham
- Only innkeeper in town
- De facto ambassador to the outside world
- Source of news for anyone willing to listen
- Buffer between travelers and hostile locals
- More observant than anyone gives him credit for

## The Crossroads Inn
- Two-story timber building at the road junction
- 12 rooms, mostly for traders and travelers
- Common room with hearth, decent food, adequate ale
- Stables for horses and storage for goods
- Technically outside town walls — important legal distinction

## Relationships
- **Thane Aldric:** Fears and resents him; knows his place
- **Greta Borugh:** She's the one he actually deals with; almost friendly
- **His guests:** Genuine care; they're his window to the world
- **The Town:** Tolerated but never accepted; he's made peace with it

---

*"People ask why I stay here. A man with my disposition should be in [Barge](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Medium%20City/Barge.md), or the capital, somewhere with life and laughter. Maybe they're right. But this is my home. My mother ran this inn. Her mother before her. The Wheelocks have always been the ones who welcome strangers to [Borugham](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Borugham.md). Someone has to. Otherwise, this place would forget that the rest of the world exists. And maybe that's what the Borughs want. But it's not what I want."*
