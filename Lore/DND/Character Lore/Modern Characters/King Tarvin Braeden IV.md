# King Tarvin Braeden IV

## Title
King of Braewood

## Age
Late 50s

## Race
Human

## Location
Braewood City, Braewood

## Appearance
A broad-shouldered man who still carries himself like the soldier he once was. His dark hair has gone iron-gray, and his face is lined with the weight of rule. He favors practical clothing even at court—rich but unadorned, always ready to move. A ceremonial sword hangs at his hip, though those who've seen him train know it's no ornament.

## Backstory
Tarvin is the latest in the Braeden line of warrior-kings who forged Braewood into the continent's dominant military power. He spent his youth on campaigns—fighting border skirmishes with Kluimont, suppressing bandit lords, and earning the respect of soldiers who would later swear their lives to him.

He inherited a kingdom at war with itself. The dwarven clans of the north demanded recognition. The southern nobles chafed under Crown taxation. Kluimont's raids grew bolder each year. Tarvin has spent three decades holding it all together through military strength, careful alliances, and the occasional brutal example.

## Personality
- **Military mind:** Thinks in terms of strategy, resources, and defensible positions.
- **Pragmatic:** Does what works, not what's idealistic.
- **Burdened:** The weight of rule shows. He sleeps poorly and trusts few.
- **Protective:** Of his kingdom, his family, and especially his wife Maera.

## Role in the Kingdom
Absolute monarch of Braewood. Commands the largest army on the continent. His word is law, his judgment final, his responsibility total.

## Relationships
- **Queen Maera Braeden:** His wife and partner. They rule together—he handles war, she handles court.
- **The Dwarven Clans:** An alliance that requires constant maintenance. The north wants autonomy; he offers partnership.
- **Kluimont:** The enemy across the river. Decades of raids have built deep hatred.
- **His Generals:** Lord-Marshal Ironward and others. He trusts their competence, if not always their judgment.

## Notable Quote
> "A king who cannot hold a sword has no business holding a crown. My grandfather taught me that. I've never forgotten."
