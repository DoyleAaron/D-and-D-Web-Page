# Keeper Helga Saltblood

## Title
Keeper of the Longhouse, Guardian of the Chain Relics

## Age
180s

## Race
Dwarf

## Location
Slada (Village), Northern Braewood


## Appearance
Weathered by wind and age, with salt-grey hair worn in intricate traditional braids. Her face is deeply lined but kind, eyes carrying the weight of centuries of stories. Short and sturdy, even for a dwarf. Her hands are strong and gentle — the hands of someone who has spent a lifetime touching sacred things with care.

Dresses in the simple Sladan style but with careful attention: her clothes are always clean, her chain-link jewelry always polished. Moves slowly and deliberately, with the presence of someone who commands attention without demanding it.

Looks like everyone's grandmother — if everyone's grandmother had memorized five hundred years of resistance history.

## Backstory
Helga was born in Slada to the Saltblood clan — named for their generations of cliff-fishing. But she was never drawn to the sea; she was drawn to the stories. From childhood, she sat at the feet of the elders, listening to tales of the Tharnic era, the rebellion, the founding.

She became Keeper at 90, trained by the previous Keeper for thirty years before his death. In the century since, she has been the village's memory — recording names, teaching history, maintaining the relics. Every child in Slada has sat in the Longhouse while Helga told them who they are and why it matters.

## Personality
- **Gentle** — her strength is quiet, not aggressive
- **Wise** — has perspective that comes from knowing the whole story
- **Patient** — has spent her life teaching the young and the stubborn
- **Faithful** — believes in the cause with deep, unshakeable conviction
- **Worried** — sees the young ones like Torvald and wonders if they understand the cost

## Role in Slada
- Keeper of the Longhouse and its sacred contents
- Guardian of the broken chain relics from the Tharnic era
- Teacher of village history to every generation
- Advisor to Elder Grunhild (her oldest friend)
- The living memory of Slada

## Relationships
- **Elder Grunhild Chainbreaker:** Friends for two centuries; they grew up together
- **Warden Torvald Stormwatch:** Worries about his eagerness; tries to teach him patience
- **Trader Korgin Deepdelve:** Supports his careful engagement with the outside
- **The Children:** Every one of them has learned the stories from her
- **The Ancestors:** Speaks to them daily; keeps their names alive

---

*"Torvald asks me when the war will come. I tell him: the war never ended. It just... paused. Every story I tell, every name I remember, every chain hanging on these walls — that's the war. We fight by remembering. We win by not forgetting. The young ones want battle because battle is simple. Kill or be killed. Win or lose. But this? This slow resistance, this patient defiance, this choosing every single day to be free even when no one's watching? That's harder. That's the real war. And we've been winning it for five hundred years."*
