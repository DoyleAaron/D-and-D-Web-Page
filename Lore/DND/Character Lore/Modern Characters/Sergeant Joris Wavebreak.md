# Sergeant Joris Wavebreak

## Title
Watch Captain of Rens

## Age
90s (appears middle-aged by dwarf standards)

## Race
Dwarf

## Location
Rens, Braewood

## Appearance
A stocky dwarf with a braided red beard going grey and steady green eyes. His skin is weathered from years of sea air, unusual for a dwarf. He wears practical armor suitable for coastal patrol—lighter than typical dwarf plate—and carries an axe that's seen decades of service. A small anchor pendant hangs at his throat.

## Backstory
Joris came south from Kluimont sixty years ago, one of the few dwarves drawn to the sea rather than the mountains. He found work as muscle on fishing boats, then as a guard, and eventually settled in Rens when he realized this southernmost village was where he belonged.

He's served as watch captain for thirty years, building a small but effective force that protects the village from pirates, smugglers, and the occasional threat from the Burning Hells to the south. He's trained human watchmen to fight with dwarf discipline, and they're better for it.

## Personality
- **Steadfast:** Dwarf stubbornness applied to coastal defense. He doesn't give ground.
- **Unusual:** A sea-loving dwarf is rare. He's used to the questions.
- **Protective:** This village is his home now. He'll defend it to the last.
- **Watchful:** The south holds dangers. He never forgets that.

## Role in the Village
Commands Rens's small watch force—about a dozen guards who patrol the village, escort fishing boats in dangerous waters, and stand ready against any threat. He coordinates with Kadena's military when needed but prefers handling things locally.

## Relationships
- **Harbor-Elder Brennan Cole:** Works closely on village defense. Respects his judgment.
- **His Watchmen:** Has trained most of them personally. They're loyal.
- **Old Stormcrow Yentl:** Takes her warnings about southern lights more seriously than most.
- **Kadena's Military:** Maintains contact but values Rens's independence.

## Notable Quote
> "Folk thought a dwarf at sea was strange. But stone teaches you to watch, to endure, to hold your ground. The sea teaches the same lessons differently. I learned both. That's why I'm still here."
