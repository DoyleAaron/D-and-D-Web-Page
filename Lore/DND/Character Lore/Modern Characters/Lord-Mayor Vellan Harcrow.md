# Lord-Mayor Vellan Harcrow

## Title
Lord-Mayor of Sheyton

## Age
Early 50s

## Race
Human

## Location
Sheyton, Braewood

## Appearance
A thin, precise man with receding brown hair and the pallid complexion of someone who spends more time with ledgers than outdoors. His clothing is well-made but deliberately austere—no rings, no adornment, nothing frivolous. His eyes are a watery blue, and they seem to be constantly calculating costs, assessing value, measuring worth. He rarely smiles, and when he does, it doesn't reach his eyes.

## Backstory
Vellan rose through the Crown's bureaucracy as a tax collector, then assessor, then auditor. His talent for numbers caught the attention of officials who needed someone to manage Sheyton's growing wealth—and its growing problems. When the previous Lord-Mayor died of fever, Vellan was appointed.

He runs the city the way he ran his ledgers: balanced, efficient, and completely impersonal. Citizens are entries in columns; problems are discrepancies to be corrected. This approach has made Sheyton prosperous—and Vellan profoundly unpopular.

## Personality
- **Obsessively organized:** Everything has a number, a category, a place.
- **Efficient but cold:** Gets results; inspires no loyalty.
- **Suspicious of emotion:** Distrusts anything that can't be quantified.
- **Secretly anxious:** The Red Scarves and the rising crime rate are disrupting his careful order. He doesn't know how to fight what he can't measure.

## Role in the Settlement
Governs Sheyton as Crown-appointed administrator. Manages trade, taxation, civic services, and—increasingly—the investigation into both the Red Scarves and the crime wave.

## Relationships
- **Watch-Captain Brennan Gale:** His enforcement arm. Vellan gives orders; Gale makes them happen.
- **The Red Scarves:** They disturb him. Their growth can't be explained by his models.
- **Guildmaster Arlen Wain:** The only man in Sheyton with comparable power. They circle each other warily.

## Notable Quote
> "Sentiment is for poets. Numbers don't lie, don't cheat, and don't play politics. I trust numbers."
