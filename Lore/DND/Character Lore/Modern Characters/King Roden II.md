# King Roden II

## Title
Former King of Braewood (Deceased)

## Age
110 at disappearance (hasn't been seen in public for decades)

## Race
Human

## Location
Unknown (Last seen: Braewood City)

## Appearance
In his prime, Roden was a commanding presence—tall, broad-shouldered, with the Braeden family's characteristic dark hair and piercing eyes. Portraits show a man of stern dignity, dressed in military finery. In his later years, he became increasingly reclusive, and those few who saw him reported a frail figure, bent by age and some unspoken burden.

## Backstory
King Roden II ascended to the throne around 975 C.C. to quell a familial rebellion that threatened to tear Braewood apart. His cousins had allied with ambitious nobles; blood was already spilling in the streets. Roden ended the conflict through a combination of military precision and ruthless execution of traitors.

His reign stabilized Braewood after generations of internal feuds. He forged the early treaties with the dwarven clans that King Tarvin IV still maintains today. He built the foundations of modern governance that allow Braewood to function as more than a collection of warring provinces.

Then, decades ago, he simply... stopped appearing. Official pronouncements continued in his name. Edicts bore his seal. But no one saw the King. When King Tarvin IV took the throne, there was no public funeral, no body displayed, no clear moment of transition.

## Personality
- **Iron-willed:** Crushed a rebellion through sheer determination.
- **Builder:** Focused on systems and institutions that would outlast him.
- **Secretive in later years:** Whatever happened to him, he didn't want witnesses.
- **Haunted:** Some said he was troubled by what he'd done to secure peace.

## Role in History
The king who unified Braewood after civil war. The architect of dwarven alliance. The mystery that no one discusses.

## Relationships
- **King Tarvin Braeden IV:** His successor. The nature of their relationship—and the transition of power—remains unclear.
- **The Braeden Line:** He secured their dominance through blood.
- **The Dwarven Clans:** He earned their respect through fair dealing.
- **His Enemies:** All dead. Every single one.

## Notable Quote
> "Peace requires sacrifice. Those who cannot sacrifice have no right to peace." — From his coronation speech, 975 C.C.
