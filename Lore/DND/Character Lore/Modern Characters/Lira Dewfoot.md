## Appearance
A small, sturdy halfling woman with curly auburn hair usually tied back with a practical cloth and bright green eyes that miss nothing in the undergrowth. Her bare feet are tough as leather—she rarely wears shoes in the marshes—and her clothes are practical earth tones that blend with the landscape. She carries a gathering basket woven from local reeds.

## Backstory
Lira's family came to [Frore](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Frore.md) three generations ago, halflings from [Osegas](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Osegas.md) who recognized that their natural talents—keen senses, small size for navigating undergrowth, patience for careful work—made them ideal gatherers. The Dewfoot family has been leading gathering expeditions ever since.

Lira knows the marshes and scrublands around [Frore](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Frore.md) better than anyone alive. She can find rare herbs that others walk right past, navigate treacherous terrain without getting lost, and sense when weather is turning dangerous. The gathering parties depend on her expertise.

## Personality
- **Perceptive:** Notices details others miss—a subtle color variation, a faint scent, bent grass.
- **Quiet:** More comfortable listening than talking. The marshes teach patience.
- **Confident:** Knows exactly how good she is at her work. Doesn't boast, but doesn't doubt.
- **Protective:** The younger gatherers are her responsibility. She brings everyone home.

## Role in the Village
Leads all gathering expeditions into the marshes and scrublands. She knows every path, every plant, every danger. She trains new gatherers, decides when and where to harvest, and ensures sustainable practices that will keep the land productive for future generations.

## Relationships
- **Root-Mother Yseult Greenbough:** Works closely with her on what to gather. The Root-Mother identifies needs; Lira fills them.
- **Her Gathering Parties:** Leads groups of 6-10 gatherers. Knows each one's strengths and weaknesses.
- **Aelindra Mistwalker:** The elven healer sometimes joins expeditions to find specific plants. They work well together.
- **The Marsh Itself:** She knows it like a friend. It shelters and provides for her.

## Notable Quote
> "The marshes give freely to those who know how to receive. Take too much, you kill the source. Take too little, people go without medicine. Finding the balance—that's my job."
