## Appearance
A tall man gone slightly to paunch, with silver hair and the calm, steady eyes of someone who has given last rites to too many dying soldiers to count. He wears the robes of a military chaplain—practical gray wool with the Iron Crown symbol embroidered on the breast. His voice is deep and carries across parade grounds without effort. When he speaks of sacrifice, soldiers listen.

## Backstory
Aldous served as a field chaplain for thirty years before taking the permanent position at Mortling's military chapel. He has blessed soldiers before battle, held them as they died, and written letters to more families than he can remember. The weight of it shows in the lines on his face but not in his faith—if anything, the death he's witnessed has made him more certain that sacrifice has meaning.

He never asks soldiers to give what he wouldn't give himself. During the last border skirmish, he walked into the field hospital and stayed for six days straight, praying over the dying until he collapsed.

## Personality
- **Unshakeable faith:** Believes that duty, sacrifice, and honor are sacred—and that those who die in service are blessed.
- **Compassionate but not soft:** Comforts the grieving while reminding them that grief serves the dead only if it strengthens the living.
- **Respected by all ranks:** Common soldiers and generals alike attend his services.
- **Haunted:** Too many names, too many faces. He drinks alone some nights.

## Role in the Settlement
Spiritual leader of Mortling's military community. Conducts services, blesses departing units, provides counsel to soldiers and officers, and maintains the Hall of Names memorial.

## Relationships
- **Lord-Marshal Ironward:** Complex. Aldous provides spiritual guidance; [Ironward](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Lord-Marshal%20Caelric%20Ironward.md) provides the wars that require it. They respect each other but don't always agree.
- **Soldiers:** Beloved. He knows many by name; they trust him with confessions they wouldn't share with commanders.
- **The dead:** He speaks to them, still. In the Hall of Names, late at night.

## Notable Quote
> "Death is not the enemy. Dying without purpose is the enemy. Every name on these walls died for something. So will you—make sure it matters."
