# Father Mikkos Ashford

## Title
Priest of the Temple of the Eternal Light

## Age
Late 60s

## Race
Human

## Location
Strane (Medium City), Northern Braewood Peninsula


## Appearance
A gentle-looking man with kind brown eyes and a warm, weathered face. White hair worn in a simple tonsure. Modest in build, neither tall nor short. Wears the simple grey-and-gold robes of his order — unadorned except for the sunburst symbol at his breast.

His hands are a contradiction: soft from prayer but calloused from the carpentry he does to "keep useful." Always smells faintly of incense and sawdust.

Has a grandfatherly smile that puts people at ease. Also has a way of looking at you that suggests he sees more than he lets on.

## Backstory
Mikkos grew up in Braewood City, one of seven children in a laborer's family. He found his calling young — the priesthood offered stability, purpose, and an escape from poverty. He rose through the temple hierarchy quietly, competently, never seeking prominence.

He was sent to Strane thirty years ago to lead its modest temple. It was meant to be a retirement posting, a place to see out his years in quiet service. Instead, he found a community that needed him — not just for sermons, but for comfort, counsel, and practical help.

He's baptized generations of Strane's children. Buried their grandparents. Married them to each other. He knows this city's souls better than anyone.

## Personality
- **Genuinely kind** — not performed, not conditional; just good
- **Wise without pretension** — offers advice through questions, not lectures
- **Humble** — credits his god and community for any good he does
- **Quietly observant** — notices more than he comments on
- **Struggling lately** — the current events are testing his faith

## Role in Strane
- Leads the Temple of the Eternal Light (main temple in Strane)
- Performs marriages, funerals, blessings, and other religious services
- Provides counsel to anyone who asks — regardless of faith or station
- Runs the temple's charitable works (food for poor, shelter for travelers)
- Serves as moral compass for those who struggle with decisions

## The Temple of the Eternal Light
- Moderate-sized stone building in Warden's Quarter
- Worships a god of sun, hope, and perseverance
- Small staff: Mikkos plus two acolytes and a groundskeeper
- Open to all — the god's light falls on everyone
- Known for practical charity as much as spiritual guidance

## Relationships
- **His Congregation:** Loves them; they are his life's work
- **Lord-Warden Velthorpe:** Respects the man's burden; prays for his wisdom
- **Captain Stonebreaker:** She attends temple occasionally; he doesn't push
- **Magistra Liriel:** Theological differences; mutual respect anyway
- **Elder Copperkettle:** Close friends; share tea and philosophy often

*"People think faith means certainty. It doesn't. Faith means choosing to believe in goodness even when the evidence is unclear. Faith means lighting a candle when the darkness seems infinite. Faith means getting up each morning and trying again, even when you failed yesterday."*

*"Right now, my friends, I am lighting candles. And if I am honest with you — as I try to be — I am also afraid. But fear and faith are not opposites. Fear is human. Faith is choosing to act anyway."*
