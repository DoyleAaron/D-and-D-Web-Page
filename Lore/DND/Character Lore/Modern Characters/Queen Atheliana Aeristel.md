# Queen Atheliana Aeristel

## Title
Queen of Islefield

## Age
~230 (Adult Elf)

## Race
Elf (Shercam Woods lineage)

## Location
Islefield City, Islefield

## Appearance
Tall and elegant with the timeless beauty of elvenkind. Her silver hair flows like water, and her eyes hold the depth of centuries. She favors flowing robes in greens and golds that echo the forests of her ancestors. A crown of woven silver branches sits upon her brow—the symbol of the Aeristel line.

## Backstory
Atheliana was crowned around 1179-1180 C.C., inheriting a kingdom that had long been shaped by elven wisdom and human labor. Her lineage traces back to the ancient lords of Shercam Woods, giving her a claim that even the most ambitious human nobles cannot challenge.

Under her rule, Islefield has expanded its farmland, strengthened trade alliances, and navigated the dangerous aftermath of the Great Resource Dispute with Braewood. She is a diplomat first, a ruler second, and a warrior only when absolutely necessary—which, in two and a half centuries of life, has been rarely.

## Personality
- **Patient beyond measure:** Thinks in decades where humans think in years.
- **Diplomatically brilliant:** Has ended more conflicts with words than her armies ever could with swords.
- **Protective of her people:** Both elves and the humans who share her kingdom.
- **Quietly melancholic:** Has watched many human friends age and die. This shapes her.

## Role in the Kingdom
Supreme ruler of Islefield. Guides policy, manages the delicate balance between elven and human populations, and maintains the kingdom's position as the continent's wealthiest trading power.

## Relationships
- **Amdir Aeristel:** A distant relative through the Shercam Woods line. She watches his development with interest.
- **King Tarvin Braeden IV:** Respects his strength; distrusts his ambition. Their kingdoms are rivals as much as neighbors.
- **Her Court:** A mix of elven advisors and human administrators. She values competence over race.
- **The Shercam Woods:** Her ancestral home. She returns there when the weight of rule grows too heavy.

## Notable Quote
> "Humans burn bright and fast. It is our duty to preserve what they build before they forget why they built it."
