## Appearance
A tired-looking man whose face shows the strain of impossible responsibilities. Brown hair going grey too early, worry lines etched deep around hazel eyes. Dresses in Crown-appointed regalia that never quite fits right — he wasn't born to this kind of authority. Shoulders that seem permanently tensed. Dark circles under his eyes suggest sleepless nights are common.

## Backstory
[Garrett Dunholm](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Portreeve%20Garrett%20Dunholm.md) was a successful customs inspector in [Braewood](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City — efficient, honest, respected. When the previous Portreeve of [Ardismouth](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Ardismouth.md) resigned after a nervous breakdown, the Crown needed someone with administrative skills and no political baggage. Garrett seemed perfect.

That was five years ago. Garrett has aged fifteen years since.

[Ardismouth](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Town/Ardismouth.md) is a town on the edge of violence. The dwarven population (35%) wields significant economic power. Human residents feel increasingly marginalized. The Crown demands order and tax revenue. The dwarven nationalist faction demands autonomy. Garrett stands in the middle, trying to satisfy everyone, succeeding with no one.

He is not a politician. He is not a soldier. He is an honest man trying to keep the peace in a town that doesn't want peace. Some nights, he wonders if his predecessor had the right idea.

## Personality
- **Genuinely well-meaning** — wants what's best for everyone
- **Hopelessly overwhelmed** — the job is bigger than him
- **Stubborn in small things** — won't compromise on fairness
- **Conflict-averse** — delays hard decisions hoping problems resolve themselves
- **Quietly desperate** — needs help he's too proud to request

## Role in Ardismouth
- Crown-appointed administrator
- Chief magistrate and mediator
- Manages trade disputes between humans and dwarves
- Coordinates (loosely) with the town garrison
- Reports to the Crown on the deteriorating situation

## Relationships
- **Guildmaster Brogar Flamevein:** His primary headache; the dwarf demands concessions Garrett can't grant
- **Captain Mira Blackshore:** Values her military advice; struggles with her hard-line approach
- **Elder Mordin Greymantle:** Was his best hope for compromise — now missing
- **Father Osric Tidewalker:** The only neutral party; Garrett leans on him heavily

---

*"Every morning I wake up hoping today will be the day it all makes sense. The dwarves want recognition. The humans want security. The Crown wants stability. I want everyone to stop shouting at me for five minutes. It never happens. Elder [Greymantle](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Elder%20Mordin%20Greymantle.md) was the only one who could bridge the gap, and now he's gone. I don't know if he's dead or just ran away. Some days I envy him either way."*
