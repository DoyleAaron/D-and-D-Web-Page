# Father Wren Stormwright

## Title
High Priest, Temple of Steel

## Age
190s (elderly for a dwarf)

## Race
Dwarf

## Location
Barge (Medium City), Central-Northern Braewood


## Appearance
A study in dignified age. White beard worn in three elaborate braids, each decorated with small steel rings representing decades of service. Deep-set brown eyes that radiate warmth and patience. Face weathered like well-cared-for leather.

Wears the formal robes of his order — grey and silver, simple but well-made. Always carries a small steel hammer pendant, the symbol of his god. Hands bear the calluses of a former smith; even priests of Steel work the forge in their youth.

Moves slowly, with the careful steps of someone conserving strength. Speaks softly, with the certainty of someone who has nothing left to prove.

## Backstory
Wren Stormwright was a smith before he was a priest. He worked the forges for fifty years before hearing his god's call. The Maker wanted more than metal from him — he wanted someone who could shape communities the way a smith shapes steel.

Wren came to Barge 120 years ago. He's served as High Priest for eighty of those years. He's outlasted four Magistrates, three Guildmasters, and countless other leaders. He's buried friends, married their grandchildren, and blessed their great-grandchildren.

He is Barge's institutional memory. He is its conscience.

## Personality
- **Wise** — has seen too much to be fooled by surface appearances
- **Patient** — thinks in decades; rushes nothing
- **Compassionate** — genuinely cares about people, all people
- **Neutral** — refuses to align with any faction; serves everyone
- **Worried** — current events trouble him more than he shows

## Role in Barge
- Spiritual leader of the dwarven community
- High Priest of the Temple of Steel (craft, labor, honest work)
- Neutral mediator trusted by all factions
- Keeper of centuries of city history
- The one person everyone respects

## The Temple of Steel
A faith centered on:
- The sanctity of honest labor
- The spiritual value of craftsmanship
- The divine nature of creation and building
- The importance of community and mutual support
- The belief that work is prayer made physical

The temple serves all workers, not just smiths. It's the spiritual home of Barge's working class.

## Relationships
- **Guildmaster Ironhand:** Old friend; they've known each other for decades
- **Magistrate Hallas:** Respects his burden; offers counsel
- **Lady Silke:** Knows what she is; prays for her anyway
- **Commander Ashworth:** She's visited him, asking about history
- **Everyone:** He's married, buried, or blessed most of the city

## What He Can Offer
For those who seek him:
- Historical knowledge about Barge, the region, and the Tharnic era
- Neutral ground for negotiations between hostile parties
- Spiritual guidance and genuine wisdom
- The truth about the Deep Frost, if the asker proves trustworthy
- A blessing that might actually help against what's coming

---

*"I've served the Maker for 140 years. I've learned that faith isn't about certainty. It's about working anyway. We forge steel knowing it might break. We build knowing time will erode. We love knowing loss will come. The act of creation is sacred not because it lasts, but because we choose to create despite knowing it won't."*

*"Something is coming. I feel it in my bones, in my dreams. I don't know if we can stop it. But I know we have to try. That's what the Maker asks of us: not success, but effort. Not victory, but purpose."*
