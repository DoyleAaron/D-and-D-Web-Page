## Appearance
A thin man with hollow cheeks and eyes that have seen too much suffering. Once-dark hair gone entirely white, though he's only sixty. Wears simple priest's robes, patched and worn. Moves slowly, weighed down by invisible burdens. Hands that never stop trembling slightly. A kind face that has forgotten how to smile fully.

## Backstory
[Aldric Mourning](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Father%20Aldric%20Mourning.md) was once Father Aldric [Thornwood](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Guildmistress%20Petra%20Thornwood.md), a promising young priest in [Braewood](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Braewood.md) City. He was assigned to [Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) as "missionary work" — a chance to save souls among the damned. That was thirty years ago. The church has forgotten him. He's forgotten his old name.

The first decade broke him. The prisoners in [Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) are not petty criminals seeking redemption — they are monsters who laugh at salvation. Tharnics who still worship the dead empire. Dwarves who describe their atrocities with pride. Nobles who consider themselves above divine judgment. Aldric offered grace; they offered contempt.

But he stayed. Not because he believes he can save them anymore — but because someone must witness their suffering. Someone must pray for souls that cannot pray for themselves. Someone must be kind in a place where kindness has been outlawed.

He has performed hundreds of last rites. He has heard confessions that gave him nightmares for years. He has watched men die and wondered if any god was listening. He no longer knows if his faith is real or just habit. But he continues. What else is there?

## Personality
- **Compassionate beyond reason** — tries to find humanity in monsters
- **Spiritually exhausted** — faith worn thin by decades of horror
- **Gentle even with the cruel** — it's all he has left
- **Haunted by what he's witnessed** — the confessions never leave him
- **Desperately lonely** — no one comes to [Oyard](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Oyard.md) for friendship

## Role in Oyard
- Prison chaplain (only spiritual authority)
- Provides last rites for dying prisoners
- Offers counsel to guards who need it
- Maintains the small chapel
- Occasionally allowed to visit [Prisoner 7](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Prisoner%207.md)

## Relationships
- **Warden-Captain Halvard Grimstone:** [Grimstone](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Warden-Captain%20Halvard%20Grimstone.md) tolerates him; Aldric sees the warden as a soul in need
- **Lieutenant Seren Valk:** She respects his kindness; he worries about the weight she carries
- **Prisoner 7:** Visits him regularly; the only person who tries to comfort the ancient dwarf
- **The other prisoners:** Most mock him; a few, near death, reach for his hand

---

*"I came here to save souls. I was young enough to think that was possible. Thirty years later, I've heard confessions that would curdle your blood. Men who describe murder like housework. Women who sold their children and feel nothing. Dwarves who ate their neighbors and called it justice. Can they be saved? I don't know. I pray for them anyway. What else can I do? [Prisoner 7](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Prisoner%207.md) is the worst and the best — worst because of what he carries, best because he's the only one who seems to suffer for it. He screams now, every night. I sit outside his cell and pray. I don't know if anyone is listening. But I have to try."*
