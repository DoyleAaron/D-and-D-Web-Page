## Appearance
A weathered man with a salt-grey beard, sun-darkened skin, and deep blue eyes that still hold the look of the sea. A long scar runs across his left cheek—a souvenir from the reef that took his boat. He walks with a slight limp from the same incident. His clothes are practical sailor's wear, and he always carries a carved whalebone pipe.

## Backstory
Brennan was the finest fishing captain in [Rens](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Rens.md) for thirty years, until a hidden reef claimed his boat and nearly his life. He spent three days clinging to wreckage before other fishermen found him. The sea had taken his livelihood but left him alive.

The village made him Harbor-Elder—not out of pity, but because no one knew these waters better. His knowledge of reefs, currents, weather, and fishing grounds now serves everyone. He can no longer work the sea himself, but he guides those who do, and they come home safely because of him.

## Personality
- **Experienced:** Forty years on these waters have taught him lessons books can't hold.
- **Cautious:** Lost his boat to overconfidence. Now he errs on the side of safety.
- **Respected:** Earned his authority through decades of demonstrated skill.
- **Wistful:** Misses the sea. Watches boats go out each morning and wishes he was on them.

## Role in the Village
Leads [Rens](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Village/Rens.md) as Harbor-Elder, coordinating fishing activities, maintaining relations with other villages, and making decisions for the community. He also serves as the village's living chart, knowing every reef and current in the surrounding waters. Captains consult him before any voyage.

## Relationships
- **Marta Reef:** The coral mistress. They work together on sustainable harvesting.
- **Gulliver Knot:** The halfling harbor-master who handles practical port matters.
- **Old Stormcrow Yentl:** The lighthouse keeper. They share evening watches sometimes.
- **Sergeant Joris Wavebreak:** The dwarf watch captain. Coordinates on village security.

## Notable Quote
> "The sea gives and the sea takes. I gave it thirty years and a boat; it gave me wisdom and a limp. Fair trade, I suppose. At least I came back. Not everyone does."
