# Old Barnacle Gorm

## Title
Elder Fisherman of Dalo

## Age
Late 70s

## Race
Human

## Location
Dalo, Braewood

## Appearance
An ancient, weathered man whose face looks like leather left too long in the sun. His beard is tangled white, his eyes are rheumy grey, and his hands are gnarled with age and old injuries. He walks with a cane carved from driftwood and wears clothes so salt-stained they've turned a permanent grey. Small barnacles cling to his boots—he walks the tidal pools daily.

## Backstory
Gorm has fished these waters for over sixty years. He knows every reef, every current, every seasonal pattern. He's survived three great storms, two shipwrecks, and more close calls than he can count. The sea has tried to kill him dozens of times, and he's still here.

He stopped active fishing ten years ago when his joints gave out, but he never left the water. Now he advises younger captains, predicts weather with uncanny accuracy, and tells stories that everyone has heard a hundred times but somehow never gets old. He's the living memory of Dalo.

## Personality
- **Gruff:** Doesn't suffer fools. Has no patience for inexperience or excuses.
- **Wise:** Sixty years of experience has taught him things no book contains.
- **Superstitious:** Respects the old traditions, the offerings to the sea, the things you don't speak of at night.
- **Generous:** Shares his knowledge freely with those who listen properly.

## Role in the Village
Serves as an informal advisor and living repository of fishing knowledge. He no longer sails, but captains consult him on weather, fishing grounds, and sea conditions. His predictions are remarkably accurate—locals joke that he can smell storms before they form.

## Relationships
- **Headman Cotter Marsh:** Respects him as a fellow man who understands the sea. Offers advice when asked.
- **Young Captains:** They seek him out for guidance. He tests them with gruff questions before sharing wisdom.
- **Tillo Quickfingers:** Visits the Net Loft often. They argue about old techniques versus new.
- **The Remembrance Rocks:** Tends the stones where the lost are commemorated. Knows every name.

## Notable Quote
> "The sea doesn't hate you, but it doesn't love you either. It just is. Learn its ways, respect its moods, and maybe—maybe—it lets you grow old. Like me."
