## Appearance
A handsome man who has aged well, with dark hair silvering at the temples and warm brown eyes. Dresses expensively but not ostentatiously — quality fabrics, subtle tailoring. Quick to smile, quicker to laugh. Moves with the easy confidence of someone who has never worried about money. Wears several rings, each representing a different trading partnership.

## Backstory
The Salk family has traded in [Phine](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md) for four generations, growing from a single warehouse to the largest trading house in the city. Yoren inherited the business at twenty-five when his father died unexpectedly. Many expected him to fail. Instead, he tripled the family's wealth in two decades.

His success comes from understanding that [Phine](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md)'s economy runs on multiple levels. The legal trade in tolled goods provides steady income. The semi-legal trade in under-reported goods provides better income. And the entirely illegal trade — which Yoren never touches directly but certainly enables — provides the best income of all.

He is beloved in [Phine](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md) for his charitable works: he funds the orphanage, maintains the old temple, sponsors festivals. This generosity is genuine but also strategic. A beloved merchant-prince is a protected merchant-prince.

## Personality
- **Charming and generous** — knows how to make people like him
- **Ruthlessly competitive** — destroys business rivals without hesitation
- **Genuinely charitable** — but never forgets the political benefits
- **Morally flexible** — sees ethics as guidelines, not rules
- **Ambitious** — wealth is nice, but influence is better

## Role in Phine
- Largest private employer in the city
- Primary legitimate trader across the Westbridge
- Unofficial banker and moneylender
- Social leader — his parties set the city's calendar
- Discreet facilitator of things that need facilitating

## Relationships
- **Lord-Governor Callum Westmarch:** Carefully maintained; [Westmarch](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Lord-Governor%20Callum%20Westmarch.md) tolerates him, doesn't trust him
- **Harbormaster Pel Vance:** Business partners in some ventures; rivals in others
- **Bridge-Commander Serena Coldwell:** Avoids her; she asks uncomfortable questions
- **His wife Seraine:** A marriage of convenience that became genuine affection
- **Kluimontian traders:** Extensive contacts across the border

Every day, goods flow across that bridge — legally taxed, theoretically inspected, properly documented. And every day, [Phine](../../Kingdoms/Braewood/Large%20Cities/Phine.md) grows richer. Some call that hypocrisy. I call it commerce. The Crown takes its cut, I take mine, everyone prospers. What's the problem?"*
