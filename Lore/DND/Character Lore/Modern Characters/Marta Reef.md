# Marta Reef

## Title
Coral Mistress of Rens

## Age
Mid-40s

## Race
Human

## Location
Rens, Braewood

## Appearance
A lean, athletic woman with sun-bleached brown hair worn in tight braids and grey-green eyes that seem to reflect the sea. Her skin is perpetually tan, her hands rough from handling coral, and small scars mark her arms from a thousand tiny cuts. She dresses in practical diving clothes and often goes barefoot.

## Backstory
Marta learned to dive before she could read, following her mother into the shallow waters where decorative coral grows. By fifteen, she could free-dive deeper than any adult in the village. By twenty, she knew every coral bed within a day's sail.

When her mother died, Marta took over as coral mistress—the keeper of Rens's most valuable trade resource. She manages harvesting schedules, ensures sustainable practices, and trains the next generation of divers. The coral beds are her responsibility, and she treats them as sacred.

## Personality
- **Dedicated:** The coral comes first. Always.
- **Fierce:** Defends sustainable practices against anyone who wants quick profits.
- **Knowledgeable:** Knows coral like a mother knows her children. Every bed, every species.
- **Private:** The sea is her true home. She's more comfortable underwater than on land.

## Role in the Village
Manages all coral harvesting operations—deciding where, when, and how much to take. She trains divers, maintains harvesting tools, and ensures the coral beds remain healthy for future generations. Her decisions directly affect Rens's primary trade resource and long-term prosperity.

## Relationships
- **Harbor-Elder Brennan Cole:** Work together on resource management. He understands the sea.
- **Her Diving Teams:** Trains and leads groups of 4-6 divers. She's responsible for their safety.
- **Kadena Traders:** The city pays well for quality coral. She negotiates firmly.
- **The Coral Beds:** She knows them intimately. They're like family.

## Notable Quote
> "People see coral as decoration. To me it's alive—growing, breathing, dying. We take only what the beds can spare. Take more, and there's nothing left for our children."
