## Description

**Mayor Adrian** is the vampiric ruler of Dawerton, one of Islefield's most important port cities. He maintains a carefully cultivated image of civility and sophistication, often meeting visitors in the city's library where he appears as a cultured intellectual rather than the predator he truly is.

Unlike more aggressive vampire lords, Adrian operates through diplomacy, trade deals, and careful manipulation. His calm demeanor and polite conversation mask a calculating mind that has guided Dawerton for longer than most mortals realize.

## Appearance

Adrian presents himself as an aristocratic figure with refined tastes. His manner is cordial and measured, designed to put visitors at ease while revealing nothing of his true nature. Those who observe closely may sense something **concealed** beneath his calm exterior.

## Role in Dawerton

### Public Duties
- Overseeing city administration
- Managing trade agreements and port operations
- Receiving foreign delegations
- Maintaining Dawerton's reputation as a neutral trading hub

### Hidden Activities
- Coordinating with Lavalto's vampire aristocracy
- Managing information about ancient artifacts
- Exchanging notes with Liora regarding **Balthazar's gifted items**

## Relationships

- **Liora:** Close ally on the council; they share vampiric nature and exchange sensitive information
- **Lavalto Vampires:** Connected to the broader vampire network
- **Foreign Delegations:** Maintains careful diplomatic relationships with all kingdoms
- **The Party:** Met them in the library; cordial exchange, but the party sensed he was concealing something

## Notable Events

### Session 10 (1225 C.C.)
The party encountered Adrian in the Dawerton library while researching local history, volcanic trade, and ancient magic. The exchange was polite and civil, with mutual respect maintained, but the party recognized that Adrian was hiding something beneath his calm demeanor.

### Session 12 (1225 C.C.)
Amdir discovered written notes exchanged between Adrian and Liora, referencing one of Balthazar's gifted items and its connection to Dawerton. This confirmed that Dawerton's leadership knows more about Balthazar than they admit publicly.
