# Councilor Miriam

## Overview

| | |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Councilor of Dawerton (Public Affairs & Welfare) |
| **Age** | Early 40s |
| **Race** | Human |
| **Location** | Dawerton, Lavalto |

---

## Appearance
A warm, approachable woman with kind eyes and an easy smile. Her brown hair is often loosely tied back, and she favors comfortable, colorful clothing rather than formal attire. She has a habit of touching people's arms when she speaks—a gesture that puts visitors at ease.

## Backstory
Miriam came from one of Dawerton's established human families who have lived alongside vampires for generations. Her grandmother served on the council, as did her mother before her. She was raised to understand the delicate politics of the city and chose to focus on the welfare of its mortal population—ensuring that the humans of Dawerton are not merely food or servants, but citizens.

## Personality
- **Genuinely friendly:** Her warmth is not an act; she truly cares about people.
- **Curious about travelers:** Always asks about journeys, distant lands, and personal stories.
- **Quietly protective:** Advocates for human rights within the vampiric system.
- **Diplomatically shrewd:** Uses her friendliness to gather information without seeming to pry.

## Role in the Council
Miriam handles public relations, citizen complaints, and the welfare of Dawerton's human population. She ensures that the "blood tithe" system remains fair (or at least tolerable) and mediates disputes between mortal families and their vampiric overlords. Visitors often find her the most approachable council member.

## Relationships
- **[Liora](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Liora.md):** Works to ensure Liora's diplomatic faction treats humans fairly.
- **[Councilor Elara](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Councilor%20Elara.md):** Close ally; they often coordinate on issues affecting common citizens.
- **[Mayor Adrian](../../Character%20Lore/Modern%20Characters/Mayor%20Adrian%20of%20Dawerton.md):** Maintains careful respect; pushes for humane policies without overstepping.

## Notable Quote
> "Travelers always have the best stories. Tell me—what was the strangest thing you saw on the road?"
